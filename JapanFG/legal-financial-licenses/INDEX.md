# JapanFG legal / financial licenses domain

## TL;DR

This is the JapanFG horizontal domain for **law, regulation, and financial-license stack**. Company pages stay in [[JapanFG/INDEX|JapanFG]], while this page routes the cross-cutting legal layer: bank licenses, securities / FIEA registration, insurance regulation, funds transfer / prepaid / electronic-payment-agency registration, crypto-asset exchange registration, stablecoin / EPI regimes, lending / installment-sales law, and policy-finance special statutes.

Use this page when the question is **"what license / legal regime makes this financial activity possible?"** rather than **"which company is this?"**

This is a navigation and research-control page, not legal advice. Any actual license conclusion should be checked against the latest FSA list, e-Gov law text, supervisory guidelines, and counsel / regulator confirmation.

## License Stack Pattern

Japan financial products are usually not governed by one license. They are assembled from layers:

1. **Balance-sheet layer** — who holds deposits, securities, insurance liabilities, loan receivables, prepaid balances, or crypto assets.
2. **Instruction layer** — who receives customer instructions and passes them to the regulated balance-sheet holder.
3. **Distribution layer** — who markets, solicits, intermediates, or embeds the regulated product.
4. **Self-regulatory layer** — JSDA, JVCEA, industry associations, exchange rules, or internal control standards.
5. **AML / user-protection layer** — KYC, segregation, cold storage, complaint handling, dispute resolution, suitability, and disclosure.

This is why a fintech UX can look like a bank while the legal stack is split across a bank, electronic payment agency, funds-transfer operator, prepaid issuer, securities firm, or crypto-asset exchange operator.

## Business Trigger Map

Use this table when evaluating a product idea, target company, or M&A / partnership structure.

| If the service wants to... | First license question | Likely route |
|---|---|---|
| Hold customer cash as deposits | Is the user claim a bank deposit or only wallet balance / receivable? | Bank license if deposit-taking; funds transfer / prepaid if wallet balance |
| Move money between bank account and app balance | Who receives the user instruction and who touches funds? | Electronic payment agency + funds transfer / prepaid stack |
| Show bank account balance inside another app | Is account information obtained through bank API with user consent? | Electronic payment agency route |
| Issue a yen-pegged transferable token | Is it an electronic payment instrument, crypto asset, trust beneficiary right, or prepaid balance? | EPI / stablecoin / trust / crypto classification analysis |
| Let users trade crypto spot | Is the platform custodying assets or intermediating exchange? | Crypto-asset exchange registration + JVCEA |
| Offer crypto leverage / CFD | Is it derivative exposure rather than spot crypto transfer? | FIEA / derivatives route + JVCEA derivative layer |
| Sell investment products through a bank or app | Is it securities solicitation / brokerage / advisory? | Type I/II FIBO, registered financial institution, intermediary, or advisory registration |
| Underwrite or sell insurance | Is the entity taking insurance risk or only distributing products? | Insurer license vs insurance agent / broker |
| Provide BNPL / installment credit | Is it installment sale, credit purchase intermediation, lending, or prepaid? | Installment Sales Act / Money Lending Business Act / prepaid classification |
| Lend repeatedly for profit | Is it "business" lending to unspecified or repeated borrowers? | Money-lender registration analysis |
| Use a policy-finance institution in a deal | Is the finance commercial, ODA, export credit, resource security, or SME / domestic policy finance? | JFC / JBIC / JICA / NEXI / JOGMEC / DBJ / ODFC lane |

## Source-of-Truth Checklist

Before promoting a legal / license page to `likely`, check these in order:

1. **FSA licensed-operator lists**: confirm the exact license category and as-of date.
2. **FSA law / guideline pages**: confirm whether the regime is under a law, supervisory guideline, cabinet office ordinance, or Q&A.
3. **e-Gov law text**: verify the current statutory term, not only a market nickname.
4. **Entity disclosure**: confirm the entity name, registration number, group relationship, and service scope from the operator's official page.
5. **Self-regulatory body**: for securities and crypto, verify JSDA / JVCEA membership or self-regulatory route where relevant.
6. **Negative finding**: if a registration is not found, record that as "not found in checked source as of date" rather than "unlicensed" unless the regulator states it.
7. **Scope boundary**: separate legal registration from product capability. A firm may have a license but not currently offer a specific product.
