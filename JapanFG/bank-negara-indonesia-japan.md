# バンクネガラインドネシア日本 (Bank Negara Indonesia Japan)

## Wiki route

Bank Negara Indonesia Japan is the Indonesia-Japan corridor route in [[JapanFG/foreign-bank-branches-japan-index|the foreign-bank branch registry]]. Read it with [[JapanFG/bangkok-bank-japan|Bangkok Bank Japan]], [[JapanFG/dbs-bank-japan|DBS Japan]], [[JapanFG/uob-bank-japan|UOB Japan]], and [[payments/INDEX|payments domain]] for remittance context.

Use this page when the question is about BNI Tokyo, Indonesia-Japan remittance, Japanese companies investing in Indonesia, trade finance, or ASEAN corridor banking.

## Overview

BNI's public global-network page says BNI Tokyo was established as a representative office on 1959-12-01 and upgraded to a branch office in 1969. The same page describes the Tokyo branch as a bridge between Indonesia and Japan, including Japanese SMEs investing in Indonesia.

The page is worth standalone treatment because BNI combines a licensed foreign-bank branch, remittance services, trade finance, working-capital lending, investment loans, and treasury products. That is a materially richer story than a registry-only row.

## Public Context

Indonesia is a large ASEAN market for Japanese manufacturers, trading companies, logistics operators, energy businesses, and consumer firms. A foreign-bank branch such as BNI Tokyo is useful because it connects local Japanese clients and Indonesian banking rails.

The peer set is not the European / US investment-bank cluster. It is closer to [[JapanFG/bangkok-bank-japan|Bangkok Bank]], [[JapanFG/dbs-bank-japan|DBS]], [[JapanFG/uob-bank-japan|UOB]], and [[JapanFG/state-bank-of-india-japan|State Bank of India Japan]]: corridor banks that bridge corporate, trade, and remittance demand.
