# Corporate strategy domain

## TL;DR

This domain routes **tax-driven corporate-restructuring strategy** in Japan — currently anchored on the single entry for **partial spinoff tax deferral** (パーシャルスピンオフ税制), the 2023 reform that lets a parent retain a minority stake in a spun-off subsidiary while still qualifying for tax-deferred treatment.

The domain is currently single-entry by design: spinoff / split / merger tax regimes form the natural cluster, and each new entry should add a *decision-relevant tax-regime page*, not a generic corporate-finance write-up. For broader financial-system context see [[finance/INDEX|finance]] and [[INDEX|FinWiki index]]; for the policy-finance institutions that often co-finance restructurings see [[policy-finance/INDEX|policy-finance]].

## Start here

There is one entry. Read [[corporate-strategy/partial-spinoff-tax-deferral|partial spinoff tax deferral]] when the question is *"can a Japanese parent spin off a subsidiary while retaining a minority stake without triggering capital-gains tax?"* — that's the specific gap the 2023 reform addresses. Pair it with the broader [[finance/INDEX|finance INDEX]] for general capital-markets context.

## Expansion rules

- Each new entry should answer a **decision question** about Japanese corporate-restructuring tax treatment (株式分配 / 株式交付 / 合併 / 分割 / 完全子会社化 / TOB conditions).
- Cite NTA (国税庁) and METI directly. Avoid relying on Big-4 advisory whitepapers as primary sources because their interpretations change.
- Cross-link any spinoff or merger case study back to the specific [[corporate-strategy/partial-spinoff-tax-deferral|partial-spinoff tax deferral]] regime or its successor entries, not to generic M&A pages.
- Stay scope-disciplined: this is *Japan tax-driven corporate strategy*. Pure capital-markets pricing belongs in [[finance/INDEX|finance]]; HR or organizational-design content does not belong here at all.
