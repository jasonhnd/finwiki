# Corporate strategy domain

## TL;DR

This domain routes **tax-driven corporate-restructuring strategy** in Japan — anchored on the **partial spinoff tax deferral** (パーシャルスピンオフ税制) regime, the 2023 reform that lets a parent retain a minority stake in a spun-off subsidiary while still qualifying for tax-deferred treatment, and now spanning the adjacent restructuring tax regimes, an option-selection decision tree, and a flagship going-private case.

The domain currently holds **five content entries**. The natural cluster is Japanese corporate-restructuring tax regimes (会社分割 / 株式交付 / パーシャルスピンオフ) plus the legal structures and decision tooling around them, with flagship cases that exercise these regimes end-to-end. Each new entry should answer a *decision-relevant* question about Japanese restructuring — a tax-regime page, a structure-selection guide, or a worked case — not a generic corporate-finance write-up. For broader financial-system context see [[finance/INDEX|finance]] and [[INDEX|FinWiki index]]; for the policy-finance institutions that often co-finance restructurings see [[policy-finance/INDEX|policy-finance]].

Start from the decision you face:

- *"Which restructuring structure fits my goal?"* — open [[corporate-strategy/spinoff-decision-tree-japan|the spinoff decision tree]] to choose between 株式分配 / パーシャルスピンオフ / 会社分割 / IPO partial sell-down.
- *"Can a Japanese parent spin off a subsidiary while retaining a minority stake without triggering capital-gains tax?"* — read [[corporate-strategy/partial-spinoff-tax-deferral|partial spinoff tax deferral]], the specific gap the 2023 reform addresses.
- *"How is a corporate split (吸収分割 / 新設分割) taxed?"* — read [[corporate-strategy/japan-kaisha-bunkatsu-tax-regime|the 会社分割 tax regime]], the broader regime under which partial spinoffs sit.

## Expansion rules

- Each new entry should answer a **decision question** about Japanese corporate restructuring — a tax regime (株式分配 / 株式交付 / 合併 / 分割 / 完全子会社化), a legal structure or option-selection guide, or a flagship worked case (TOB / squeeze-out / going-private).
- Cite NTA (国税庁), METI, FSA, and the Companies Act (会社法) directly. Avoid relying on Big-4 advisory whitepapers as primary sources because their interpretations change.
- Cross-link any new regime or case back to the related entries already here — the [[corporate-strategy/partial-spinoff-tax-deferral|partial-spinoff regime]], the [[corporate-strategy/japan-kaisha-bunkatsu-tax-regime|会社分割 regime]], the [[corporate-strategy/spinoff-decision-tree-japan|decision tree]], or the [[corporate-strategy/toshiba-tob-squeeze-out-2023-2024-case|Toshiba case]] — rather than to generic M&A pages.
- Stay scope-disciplined: this is *Japan tax-driven corporate strategy* — restructuring tax regimes, the legal structures around them, and the cases that exercise them. Pure capital-markets pricing belongs in [[finance/INDEX|finance]]; HR or organizational-design content does not belong here at all.
