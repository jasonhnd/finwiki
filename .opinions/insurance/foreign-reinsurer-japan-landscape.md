# 主観内容の隔離 — Foreign reinsurer Japan landscape

> 出典エントリー: `insurance/foreign-reinsurer-japan-landscape.md`

## 推論 (from 1. Regulatory boundary: 再保険 license vs branch)

| Cross-border placement (no Japan license) | Foreign reinsurer underwrites Japanese risk from London / Bermuda / Singapore via a Japan-licensed broker | None (broker is licensed; underwriter is offshore) ^[inferred] | Smaller specialty reinsurers, Bermudian carriers, ILS / cat-bond sponsors |
**Key point.** A Japanese primary insurer can cede risk through any of these four pathways. The Japan-branch route gives FSA direct supervisory visibility and aligns with the [[JapanFG/legal-financial-licenses/insurance-license-and-solvency|insurance license and solvency]] framework; cross-border placement is faster and capital-efficient but pushes oversight to the broker channel and to the global parent regulator. ^[inferred]

## 推論 (from 2. The panel: who is here)

This list is FSA-anchored. Additional foreign reinsurers may participate in Japanese cessions via cross-border placement without a Japan branch — those are not on this matrix. ^[inferred]

## 推論 (from Proportional (quota share, surplus))

- new product lines where the cedent wants underwriting input ^[inferred];
- specialty risks where the cedent has limited data — Asia personal lines, cyber, parametric ^[inferred];
- capital-relief structures for life and savings products (life-insurance financial reinsurance) ^[inferred].

## 推論 (from Non-proportional (excess of loss, stop loss, cat XL))

- casualty / liability tail risk (D&O, product liability, cyber breach response) ^[inferred];
- aggregate stop-loss for whole-year underwriting volatility ^[inferred].
The Big 2 (Munich Re, Swiss Re) and Hannover Re dominate the cat XL panel for the 大手3社 ^[inferred]. SCOR and Gen Re typically participate at lower lines on the same towers.

## 推論 (from 5. Big 2 vs Big 4 vs US-2 vs life-specialist)

| Global Big 2 | Munich Re, Swiss Re | Largest balance sheet, deepest cat panel, most diversified line capacity, sole providers of certain very-large single-risk lines ^[inferred] |
| European 3rd/4th pillar | Hannover Re, SCOR | Lower-cost operating model; willing to take lead or co-lead positions; structured-reinsurance specialism (Hannover) and Lloyd's-adjacent specialty (SCOR) ^[inferred] |
| US Berkshire pillar | Gen Re | Berkshire-style discipline; cyclical participation; willing to step back in soft markets ^[inferred] |
| US life-specialist | RGA, Pacific Life Re | Life-only focus; cedent-partnership orientation; longevity & annuity reinsurance; underwriting / product technical support ^[inferred] |

## 推論 (from 6. Retro market: where the reinsurers' own risk goes)

- **Other reinsurers in the same panel** — Munich Re may retro to Hannover Re and vice versa on different perils ^[inferred].
- **Bermudian and Lloyd's specialist retro carriers** — RenaissanceRe, Everest Re, several Lloyd's syndicates ^[inferred].
- **Insurance-Linked Securities (ILS) / catastrophe bonds** — capital-market investors take peak Japan earthquake and typhoon risk in 144A cat-bond format; Munich Re, Swiss Re, and Hannover Re all sponsor or arrange Japan-peril cat bonds for cedents and for their own balance sheets ^[inferred].
- **Industry-loss warranty (ILW) market** — Japan typhoon / EQ ILWs trade in the London / Bermuda specialty market ^[inferred].

## 推論 (from 8. Why this landscape matters)

- **Cycle / pricing transmission.** Global reinsurance hardening (e.g., post-2017 Hurricanes Harvey / Irma / Maria; post-2020 COVID; post-2024 high-natcat-loss years) raises the cost of cession for Japanese primary insurers, even when Japanese-specific losses are benign. ^[inferred]
- **Underwriting technical input.** Munich Re's Geo Risks Research, Swiss Re's Sigma, and RGA's product / mortality work feed back into Japanese cedent pricing. ^[inferred]
- **Sole-source risk concentration.** When two firms (Munich Re + Swiss Re) lead most cat XL towers, primary insurers are exposed to panel concentration risk that the FSA does not directly capitalise. ^[inferred]

## Counterpoints

- The "panel of seven foreign reinsurers" reading is editorial. The FSA register also lists smaller foreign reinsurer branches and changes over time; readers should re-pull songai.pdf and seimei.pdf for the current set. ^[inferred]
- Branch-level Japan underwriting figures are not publicly broken out; group-level reporting is the primary public source, and Japan-specific market share is largely inferred from broker market commentary. ^[ambiguous]
- Lloyd's Japan ([[JapanFG/lloyd-japan]]) and the syndicate model are *adjacent* to but not *part of* this foreign-reinsurer landscape; the marketplace structure is fundamentally different. See [[insurance/lloyds-japan-syndicate-operating-model]].
