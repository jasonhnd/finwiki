# 主観内容の隔離 — Japan IAIG and ICS mapping

> 出典エントリー: `insurance/japan-iaig-ics-mapping.md`

## Counterpoints

- IAIG designation does not by itself change the prudential strength of a group. It changes the disclosure and supervisory perimeter, not the underlying balance sheet.
- ICS at the group level can produce a ratio that differs from the aggregated subsidiary-level ratios because diversification benefits at the group level and consolidation eliminations work differently across regimes.
- "ESR and ICS are basically the same" is an oversimplification. Calibration differences in correlation matrices, shock scenarios, and risk-margin computation can produce different ratios for the same balance sheet.
- The FSA's role as group-wide supervisor for Japanese IAIGs gives Japan an active seat at the IAIS supervisory-college table, but it does not eliminate the need to coordinate with foreign host supervisors of overseas subsidiaries.
- Internal-model approval is not a one-time event. Model use is subject to ongoing supervisory review, and internal-model approval can be partially withdrawn if model performance degrades.
- The Aggregation Method comparability assessment is a live IAIS process; its outcome affects how US-headquartered groups report and how cross-jurisdiction comparison works.

## Open questions

- Which Japan-headquartered groups will the FSA publicly designate as IAIGs and on what timeline as ICS 2.0 implementation rolls out from 2025?
- How will FSA internal-model approval for ICS work in practice for [[non-life-insurers/tokio-marine|Tokio Marine]], given its diversified overseas P&C portfolio, versus for [[life-insurers/dai-ichi-life|Dai-ichi Life]] with its overseas life subsidiaries?
- How does the dual-framework architecture (ESR at entity, ICS at IAIG group) interact with internal-model approval timelines and resource allocation at the FSA?
- How does the IAIS Aggregation Method comparability outcome affect the way Japan-headquartered IAIGs benchmark against US-headquartered IAIGs?
- How does [[megabanks/sony-fg|Sony Financial Group]] / [[life-insurers/sony-life|Sony Life]]'s perimeter under [[insurance/sony-life-group-life-operating-model|Sony Life operating model]] interact with IAIG-perimeter definitions given the ultimate non-insurance parent (Sony Group Corporation)?
- How does the Japanese implementation of ICS interact with [[insurance/foreign-life-affiliate-japan-positioning|foreign-life affiliates]] and foreign-non-life affiliates whose parent is a non-Japan IAIG?
