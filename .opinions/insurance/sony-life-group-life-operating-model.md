# 主観内容の隔離 — Sony Life group-life and Lifeplanner operating model

> 出典エントリー: `insurance/sony-life-group-life-operating-model.md`

## Counterpoints

- The "Lifeplanner channel persistency is higher" claim depends on cohort, year, and product. Period-specific disclosures should always be checked rather than assumed.
- Foreign-tech parent ownership is not automatically a governance weakness. It can deliver capital stability through cycles where a listed insurance peer might be pressured into pro-cyclical buybacks. The judgement is structure-specific.
- Group life is a margin-thin business. Treating it as a "growth engine" misreads the model; it is a stabilizer and a corporate-relationship asset.
- A Lifeplanner-only channel does not insulate Sony Life from interest-rate, FX, or equity shocks — those flow through the asset-side balance sheet regardless of distribution model.
- The 2020-2021 take-private removed Sony Financial Holdings as a separately listed equity, so direct equity-investor access to Sony Life economics now goes through Sony Group Corporation. That is a different investment thesis from owning a listed insurance holding.
