# 主観内容の隔離 — T-Point + V-Point post-2024 merger — dual-brand transition, CCCMK Holdings, points-liability transfer

> 出典エントリー: `loyalty/t-point-v-point-post-2024-merger.md`

## Counterpoints

- **Brand confusion**: customers had to understand "T Point became V Point" while also distinguishing **SMBC's older V Point (credit-card reward)** from **the new unified V Point common point**. The dual-brand reality (TSUTAYA-branded vs SMBC-branded cards) extends the brand-confusion window beyond the original migration timeline.
- **Merchant-acceptance long tail**: physical-merchant rollover took longer than the marketing timeline suggested; some former T-Point chains have not maintained equivalent V-Point participation. The acceptance gap vs PayPay / Rakuten Pay in long-tail retail remains material.
- **Data-marketing carry-over**: CCC's data business was historically a major commercial layer. How cleanly the data-marketing capability transferred into CCCMK Holdings (vs being run down or restructured) is still observable in CCCMK / SMFG segment disclosures.
- **Olive dependency**: V-Point's bank-led identity is strongest when paired with Olive Account adoption; if Olive growth stalls, V-Point reverts to "another card-issuer reward" rather than the unique bank-anchored common-point case.
- **Liability disclosure opacity**: CCCMK Holdings is not separately listed, so V-Point liability sits inside SMFG / SMCC segment notes with partial visibility. Analyst comparison vs Rakuten (separately listed) or NTT docomo (annual report disclosure) is harder.

## Open questions

- Will V-Point reach durable parity with PayPay Points and Rakuten Points on per-user activity, or plateau as a card-and-bank-anchored point with thinner online tail and lower-frequency wallet usage?
- How will the **SMBC × SBI investment-distribution alliance** evolve V-Point's role in fund and NISA promotions, and does this offset weaker wallet activity?
- Will CCCMK Holdings retain the historical data-marketing depth that made T-Point commercially significant, or will the data layer be subordinated to SMFG's bank-customer view?
- How will the **dual-brand transition** resolve over time — will TSUTAYA cards continue to be issued in T-card design indefinitely, or will a forced design migration occur on a specific date?
- Is Olive Account adoption tracking the trajectory needed to make V-Point a **"primary bank account anchor"** rather than a card-reward overlay, and how does it compare to PayPay Bank's deposit growth?
- How will the cross-program exchange relationships (V-Point ↔ ANA mileage and other airline / partner programs) evolve relative to the more mature Rakuten / dポイント exchange relationships? Settlement-leg mechanics here connect back to [[loyalty/point-liability-accounting-boundary]].
