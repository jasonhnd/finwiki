# 主観内容の隔離 — JETRO vs NEXI vs JBIC — Japan export-promotion / insurance / financing three-pillar comparison

> 出典エントリー: `trade/jetro-vs-nexi-vs-jbic-comparison.md`

## Counterpoints

- The clean three-way split is more textbook than messy reality — NEXI and JBIC products overlap on the edges (loan vs insured loan), and JETRO services sometimes touch areas that look like JICA's technical cooperation. Operating teams often coordinate informally rather than along the stated mandate lines.
- JBIC competes — uncomfortably — with private project-finance banks in some segments; whether it crowds in or crowds out commercial finance is debated by Japanese banking associations and MOF advisory councils.
- NEXI's pricing under the [[policy-finance/oecd-export-credit-arrangement|OECD Arrangement]] is constrained, which limits its ability to undercut competitors but also limits its ability to subsidise Japanese exporters in head-to-head bids against non-Arrangement competitors (China, India ECAs).
- "JETRO is not a customer-facing sales organisation" is true in principle but the [[trade/jetro-members-gateway-model|JETRO Members]] tier creates pseudo-customer dynamics that confuse partners.

## Open questions

- How does JBIC respond to the rise of Chinese policy lending (China Eximbank, CDB) on competing bids, given OECD Arrangement pricing constraints?
- Will NEXI's role expand into climate-risk / supply-chain-resilience cover (semiconductors, critical minerals) under the METI economic-security framework, and does that overlap [[policy-finance/jogmec|JOGMEC]]?
- How does Japan's policy-finance stack compare structurally to Korea (KOTRA + K-SURE + KEXIM) and Germany (GTAI + Euler Hermes + KfW IPEX)?
- Should JETRO's J-StarX startup acceleration sit alongside the trade-promotion mandate or be split into a separate innovation agency?
