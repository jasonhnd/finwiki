# 主観内容の隔離 — USD stablecoin issuer ten-way comparison matrix — USDT / USDC / PYUSD / RLUSD / USD1 / FDUSD / USDS / USDe / M / USDB

> 出典エントリー: `fintech/usd-stablecoin-issuer-eight-way-matrix.md`

## Counterpoints

- **MCap figures are point-in-time and volatile** — Tether USDT supply can fluctuate by $5B in a week depending on EM stablecoin demand; USDC has swung $10B+ on Coinbase quarterly flows; smaller issuers (USDe, FDUSD) can move 20%+ in a quarter.
- **The "passing GENIUS §501" framing is partly speculative** — Treasury and OCC have not yet published final guidance for several borderline cases (USDS decentralised governance, M^0 multi-issuer template, USD1 political-credibility concerns). Final compliance status for some issuers may shift by 2027.
- **The "double oligopoly hardens" pattern** assumes regulatory enforcement continues — a Trump-administration shift in §501 enforcement (especially given USD1's political-network proximity) could reshape the matrix.
- **USDT's regulatory home in BVI + El Salvador** is structurally protected — Tether is unlikely to comply with §501 because compliance would compress its profit margin by 20-40bps, undermining its $1.5T-annual-profit short-treasury-yield business model.
- **USDS / USDe / FDUSD reserves transparency** is materially weaker than USDC / PYUSD / RLUSD — the matrix assumes published disclosures are accurate, but historical patterns (FDUSD Sun-affiliate dispute; Tether's pre-2023 partial-collateralisation) suggest skepticism is warranted.
- **The "vertical integration" of USDB / Stripe** is unique but small ($800M MCap), and the "100% Stripe yield retention" model only works because Stripe owns the distribution rail end-to-end. Other large payment companies (Adyen, Block, Wise) have not replicated this stack.
- **Audit-firm tier rankings are qualitative** — Deloitte is the most-cited tier-1 firm but Withum + BDO are accepted institutional-grade auditors. The tier-distinction is partly about brand recognition rather than substantive audit quality.
- **The "8 challengers"** framing excludes some products (TUSD residual, USDP retail, GUSD, USDD, frxUSD) that have $100M-1B supply but are excluded from this top-10 matrix. The boundary between "live challenger" and "fading product" is fluid.
- **M^0 is a different category** (neutral infrastructure for white-label issuers) and including it in an issuer-comparison matrix is a slight category stretch — but the M^0 thesis (brand-layer competition + infrastructure consolidation) is so important to the post-GENIUS architecture that omitting it would obscure the matrix.

## Open questions

- Will **USDT remain at $145B by 2028** or will GENIUS Act enforcement + MiCA equivalent regimes compress USDT supply by forcing exchange delistings?
- Will **USDC cross $150B by 2027** as the GENIUS-compliant institutional benchmark, doubling from 2026-Q2?
- Will **Ripple's OCC National Bank Charter** (2026-Q1 application) be approved, and would this trigger a similar charter application from Circle to bypass the multi-state MTL structure?
- Will **Ethena USDe** survive a sustained bear funding-rate environment, or does the delta-neutral basis trade unravel and force USDe to collapse below peg?
- Will **Sky USDS** maintain its decentralised classification as Treasury/SEC issue final §501 guidance, or will the BUIDL routing be reclassified as making Sky a "regulated SC issuer"?
- Will **FDUSD receive HKMA license** in 2026-Q3 (current expectation) or be denied over the Sun-affiliate reserve-custody dispute?
- Will **USDB cross $5B by 2027** as Stripe completes Tempo L1 + Privy + agentic-commerce integration, displacing USDC in B2B merchant rails?
- Will **WLFI USD1** become the first stablecoin formally investigated by SEC for conflict-of-interest under a future administration, and how does this affect GENIUS §501 neutrality narrative?
- Will **M^0 cross $5B** managed across white-label issuers by 2028, displacing the long-tail "want our own SC" demand from standalone issuers to neutral infrastructure?
- Will any **new top-10 entrant** emerge by 2028 (e.g., Anchorage USAT as Tether's US-compliant cousin; Robinhood + USDG expansion; bank-led stablecoin like Citi or BofA)?
