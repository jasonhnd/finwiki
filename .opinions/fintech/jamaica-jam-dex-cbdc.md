# 主観内容の隔離 — Jamaica JAM-DEX — Bank of Jamaica retail CBDC, Lynk wallet adoption, eCurrency Mint vendor

> 出典エントリー: `fintech/jamaica-jam-dex-cbdc.md`

## Counterpoints

- **The J$2,500 enrolment bonus may have purchased numbers rather than durable use.** Critics argue early enrolment was incentive-driven; supporters point to post-incentive growth as evidence of organic demand.
- **Lynk dominance is a concentration risk.** If NCB / TFOB de-prioritises Lynk, JAM-DEX has limited fallback wallet infrastructure — the opposite problem to Sand Dollar's interoperable-AFI model.
- **Active-wallet metrics are not consistently public** — registered-wallet headline counts are higher than active monthly use; the BoJ has not been fully transparent on the gap.
- **Cross-border CBDC absence** — Jamaica has substantial remittance flows (US, UK, Canada diaspora); a cross-border CBDC corridor is a logical extension but not yet planned.
- **eCurrency Mint vendor concentration** — the success of JAM-DEX is partly a vendor success; alternative vendors (Bitt, Hyperledger-based, ConsenSys) are less proven on retail CBDC outcomes.
- **The legal-tender amendment is symbolically clean but practically narrow** — JAM-DEX is denominated 1:1 in J$ and shares the J$ inflation and FX-rate risk; legal-tender status does not change consumer choice if the wallet UX is weaker than peer apps.
- **Pilot-to-launch sequencing is the structural lesson, but small economies have small samples** — extrapolating to larger-economy retail CBDCs (digital euro, BoE digital pound) requires care.

## Open questions

- Does Lynk maintain its anchor-wallet dominance, or does the BoJ require additional wallet providers to widen distribution?
- Does the BoJ open a cross-border JAM-DEX corridor (US, UK, Canada remittance) over the late 2020s?
- Does the success of JAM-DEX vs the slow adoption of Sand Dollar and eNaira cement an **anchor-wallet-incentive model** as a recommended small-economy CBDC template?
- Does Jamaica retain eCurrency Mint as technology partner, or does it migrate to a different vendor stack in the next refresh cycle?
- Does the digital-pound consultation outcome (see [[fintech/boe-digital-pound-consultation|BoE digital pound consultation]]) reference JAM-DEX as a case study?
- Does the comparison with [[fintech/bahamas-sand-dollar-cbdc|Sand Dollar]] and [[fintech/nigeria-enaira-retail-cbdc|eNaira]] formalise as a **three-way small-economy retail CBDC comparison** in BIS / IMF literature?
