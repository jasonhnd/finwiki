# 主観内容の隔離 — eRupee India pilot status 2026 — retail + wholesale dual track, UPI interoperability, programmable-money use cases

> 出典エントリー: `fintech/erupee-india-pilot-status.md`

## Counterpoints

- The "**5M+ retail users**" figure is a cumulative wallet count from 2024; **active monthly users are materially lower** and not publicly aggregated.
- UPI's dominance means the eRupee retail pilot **lacks a clear consumer-side value proposition**. The strategic logic is shifting toward programmable / offline / wholesale use cases where the central-bank-money property matters.
- Cross-border CBDC integration has been **discussed but not delivered**. The UPI cross-border footprint is the easier channel for India's payment-internationalisation goals.
- The RBI's pace is **deliberately conservative**. There is no political mandate to compress the pilot timeline; RBI prefers to learn and adjust.
- The wholesale leg is **operationally routine** but its incremental value over the existing CBDC-equivalent RBI accounts at the central bank is debated; the experimental value (preparing for tokenised G-sec, integrated DvP) is the real differentiator.
- The Finance Ministry's interest in **private stablecoins** (in the GENIUS-Act-like direction) sometimes conflicts with the RBI's CBDC priority. The internal Finance-Ministry-vs-RBI tension is visible in policy commentary and shapes the broader payment-stack story (see [[fintech/india-anti-dollar-dpi-alliance|India anti-dollar DPI alliance]]).
- Despite UPI's cross-border expansion to 8+ countries, eRupee has **no live cross-border settlement** — the two stacks have not been bridged externally.

## Open questions

- Does RBI publish active-monthly retail eRupee user figures by 2027, and if so what is the active-rate level?
- Does the programmable-money use case scale into a flagship welfare-disbursement rail at population scale (e.g. PM-KISAN-style direct transfer in eRupee)?
- Does offline-CBDC tech leave pilot scope and ship to last-mile rural geographies in 2026-2027?
- Does RBI formally join mBridge as an observer or participant, or does India choose a separate bilateral arrangement?
- Does the wholesale-retail interoperability test in 2026-Q1 lead to a unified CBDC stack with tokenised G-sec / DvP settlement use cases?
- Does the Finance Ministry's interest in private stablecoins reduce political pressure on RBI's eRupee programme, or are the two complementary?
- Does any cross-border CBDC settlement go live between RBI and a counterpart (UAE / Singapore / Bhutan) in 2026-2027?
- Does the eRupee programme converge with Project Agorá-style tokenised-deposit infrastructure, or stay separate as a pure CBDC track?
