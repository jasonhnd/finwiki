# 主観内容の隔離 — US 2025 Crypto Regulatory Reset · Trump-Era Policy Pivot

> 出典エントリー: `fintech/regulatory-reset-2025-usa-crypto-policy.md`

## Counterpoints

- **Statutory backstops are partial.** GENIUS covers payment stablecoins, not market structure; CLARITY remains in legislative motion. An administration change could reverse the executive and SEC-staff legs while statutes survive — leaving an unevenly settled regime.
- **EOs are revocable.** EO 14178 and EO 14233 can be undone by a successor EO. The Bitcoin Strategic Reserve and the Digital Asset Stockpile are statutorily fragile until codified.
- **The Czar is not Senate-confirmed.** Continuity is tied to the presidential term, and the role has no direct rulemaking authority.
- **SAB 122 changes accounting, not Basel III risk weights.** Banks still face capital-charge friction for held crypto exposures; SAB 122 only removed the gross-up liability. Real custody at scale still depends on Basel III treatment evolving.
- **Dismissals are not precedents.** SEC dropped cases without judicial rulings, leaving future SEC chairs free to revive similar theories. The statutory wins (GENIUS) are the durable layer; the docket dismissals are not.
- **Enforcement rollback risks re-creating consumer-harm gaps.** Two of the high-profile dismissed defendants had specific consumer-loss fact patterns that future cycles could revisit.
- **The Strategic Bitcoin Reserve is small in market terms** (forfeited inventory only, no budget appropriation), and its market signal may not survive a future change of posture.
- **State-level fragmentation persists.** New York's BitLicense, California DFPI Digital Financial Assets Law, and Texas SB 1860 each survive the federal reset and continue to fragment the licensing surface (see [[exchanges/us-crypto-licensing-multi-layer-system|US crypto licensing multi-layer]]).

## Open questions

- Does **CLARITY pass the Senate** with 60 votes, or does it require parliamentary procedural alternatives? Without CLARITY, the market-structure leg remains executive and SEC-staff only.
- Does the **Bitcoin Strategic Reserve** receive a statutory appropriation, or does it remain an EO inventory of forfeited assets?
- Does the **CBDC prohibition** survive future court challenge, given the Federal Reserve's independence?
- Does the **Czar role institutionalize** beyond the 2029 transition, or does it dissolve as the National Cyber Director role nearly did?
- Does **bank custody at G-SIB scale** materialize given persistent Basel III risk weights even after SAB 122?
- Does the **next administration** preserve, reverse, or partially modify the reset — and how robust is the durable statutory layer (GENIUS) versus the revocable executive layer (EO 14178, EO 14233)?
- Does **Hester Peirce continue** at SEC after her current term, and what does that signal for continuity?
- Does the **Operation Choke Point 2.0 rollback** survive in supervisor practice, or do informal supervisory norms reassert themselves?
