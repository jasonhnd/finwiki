# 主観内容の隔離 — Tokenized MMF issuer comparison matrix — BUIDL, BENJI, ACRED, WTGXX, USYC, USTB, OUSG (eight-way)

> 出典エントリー: `fintech/tokenized-mmf-issuer-comparison-matrix.md`

## Counterpoints

- **AUM figures are point-in-time and lumpy** — large institutional commitments can move BUIDL AUM by hundreds of millions in a single quarter; second-tier products can swing meaningfully with single-LP additions.
- **The "BUIDL dominance" narrative may be overstated**: 32% market share is high but the market is small (~$8B); when the market grows to $50-100B by 2028 forecasts, that share may compress significantly. The 2030 forecast range is BUIDL 30-40% with second-tier 60-70%.
- **Securitize is itself a single-point-of-failure** but it is also extensively regulated (SEC-registered transfer agent + ATS); the operational-failure risk is bounded.
- **Reg D vs 2a-7 wrapper choice is a market-segment-design choice** — BUIDL's Reg D wrapper is not "worse" than BENJI's 2a-7; it serves a different segment (institutional treasury vs retail brokerage). Both wrappers will continue to exist.
- **ACRED is included for completeness but is not a MMF** — it is a private credit fund tokenized on Securitize. Including it in a MMF matrix is a slight category-stretch.
- **USYC AUM figures pre- vs post-Circle acquisition are not directly comparable** — pre-acquisition USYC was a standalone product; post-acquisition it is the yield engine inside USDC.YS, and AUM reporting is now bundled.
- **WisdomTree Prime's user base (~ hundreds of thousands)** is much smaller than mainstream brokerages (Schwab / Fidelity / Robinhood with tens of millions). WTGXX's growth is bottlenecked by Prime's customer acquisition rather than by product-market fit.
- **The "instant-redemption $1B facility"** (BlackRock + Goldman + DTCC + Janus, 2026-05-14) helps BUIDL but does not extend to second-tier products — entrenching the BUIDL advantage at the T+0 liquidity layer.
- **Moody's Aaa-mf** (BUIDL + Fidelity FYHXX, 2026-05-13) is a credit-rating signal not a comprehensive risk assessment; smart-contract risk and operational risk are not captured in fund-rating methodology.

## Open questions

- Will **USDC.YS** (Circle USDC + USYC bundle) cross **$10B AUM** by 2027 and become the de facto standard yield-bearing-SC wrapper post-§501?
- Will the **BUIDL market share** compress from ~32% to <25% by 2028 as second-tier issuers (BENJI multi-chain, OUSG Asia/LatAm, WTGXX Prime, USYC via USDC.YS) collectively scale?
- Will **PYUSD-MMF** (Paxos shelf-registered) actually launch and meaningfully compete in the 2a-7 retail segment that WTGXX and BENJI currently dominate?
- Will the **Securitize transfer-agent concentration** trigger an OCC / FRB / SEC concentration concern by 2027-2028, forcing diversification?
- Will **BlackRock launch BSTBL and BRSRV** (announced 2026-05-08 SEC filing) and graduate from "BUIDL only" to a multi-product 2a-7 + Reg D combined stack, potentially leapfrogging BENJI?
- Will the **$1B instant-redemption facility** (BlackRock + Goldman + DTCC + Janus, 2026-05-14) be opened to competitors or remain a BlackRock-only competitive moat?
- Will non-US-domiciled tokenized fund issuers (a European MiCA-compliant or Asian MAS-compliant tokenized MMF) emerge as the **fourth pole** beyond US Reg D / 2a-7 / Reg S structures?
- Will the **OCC or FRB** introduce a "single tokenized-MMF must not exceed X% of an SC issuer's reserves" cap that would compress BUIDL's "yield infrastructure" position?
