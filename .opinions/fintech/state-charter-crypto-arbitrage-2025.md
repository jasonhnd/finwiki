# 主観内容の隔離 — State-Chartered Crypto Arbitrage · 2025 US Federal vs State Race

> 出典エントリー: `fintech/state-charter-crypto-arbitrage-2025.md`

## Counterpoints

- **The Custodia / Fed master account litigation could flip the entire calculus** if a 2026 appellate ruling forces the Fed to grant state-chartered SPDIs Fedwire access. Probability appears low (district court already ruled for Fed discretion), but a Supreme Court grant of cert would reset the field.
- **GENIUS Act final rules could collapse state-charter advantage** if "national stablecoin issuer" requires federal supervision — relegating SPDIs to second-class issuance status (see [[fintech/genius-act-501-denylist-mandate|GENIUS Act §501]]).
- **NYDFS exodus narrative may be overstated**: Paxos, Gemini Trust, and Coinbase Custody remain large NY-anchored entities. The exodus is at the marginal new-entrant level, not the installed base.
- **OCC charter timing risk**: a hostile OCC administration could revoke conditional approvals or stall pipeline. State SPDI approvals are more politically insulated.
- **Texas pragmatic-regulator strategy is not codified into a unique charter** — it depends on continuing favorable interpretive guidance, which is more vulnerable to administration change than Wyoming's statutory SPDI.

## Open questions

- Does the Custodia Bank v. Fed Board appeal reach the Supreme Court, and if so, does it force a structural rebalancing of state vs federal crypto banking?
- Will GENIUS Act §501 implementation rules (expected 2026-Q3) preempt state SPDI stablecoin-issuance authority?
- Does Florida, Idaho, or another state launch a **third-generation digital asset bank charter** (post-Wyoming, post-Nevada) that solves the Fed master account access problem via political pressure?
- Does Anchorage Digital's existing **OCC national trust bank** charter become the reference template, or does Bridge's **OCC trust bank conditional** path remain distinct?
- Does NYDFS modernize the BitLicense framework enough to retain growth (proposed Greenlist 2.0 changes pending) or accept its incumbent-only role?
- Does the CFTC vs SEC jurisdictional resolution (covered in [[fintech/cftc-sec-crypto-jurisdiction|CFTC vs SEC 暗号資産管轄]]) push more issuers toward OCC-route preferred chartering?
