# 主観内容の隔離 — Bank of England digital pound consultation — 2023-2026 design phase, private-sector wallets, comparison with ECB

> 出典エントリー: `fintech/boe-digital-pound-consultation.md`

## Counterpoints

- **"Design phase, not commitment to launch"** is the canonical BoE / HMT framing — but it is also a politically convenient way to **defer the launch decision indefinitely** without formally cancelling. Sweden's e-krona has done the same.
- **The £10K-£20K cap is unsustainably high** by banking-industry argument — UK Finance and individual banks have argued for materially lower caps to protect deposit funding.
- **The £10K-£20K cap is too low** to be useful by some consumer-advocate argument — a meaningful retail CBDC for everyday use needs cap headroom; £10K is at the lower bound of useful for SMEs.
- **The privacy posture is strong on paper but weakly enforceable** — if a PIP is compromised or compelled by court order, transaction data is exposed; the BoE-blindness claim depends on PIP integrity.
- **The marginal benefit case is weak** — UK consumer payments already work well; the digital pound has to argue against the default of "no need."
- **Primary legislation is the genuine bottleneck** — even if BoE / HMT decide to launch, Parliament must pass primary legislation, which requires political consensus that does not yet exist.
- **The 50,000+ consultation responses skewed toward concerned respondents** — large-scale public engagement attracted privacy-conscious responses; this may not be representative of broader public sentiment.
- **Cross-border CBDC absence at retail** — UK retail digital pound has no cross-border integration planned; for a financial-centre economy this is a notable gap.

## Open questions

- Does the BoE / HMT enter a formal Decision Phase by 2027, or does the design phase extend further?
- Does primary legislation get introduced to Parliament, and on what timetable?
- Does the final holding cap end up materially below £10K-£20K under banking-industry lobby pressure?
- Does the BoE prioritise the wholesale-CBDC track (via Agorá / RTGS Renewal) over the retail digital pound?
- Does post-2024 UK political administration sustain the cautious-design posture or reframe the programme more aggressively?
- Does the UK digital pound design end up materially diverging from the EU digital euro on holding cap, privacy, or PIP/ESIP structure — or do they converge in practice?
- Does the PIP/ESIP terminology become the standard public-CBDC vocabulary for the private-sector-wallet retail-CBDC model?
- Does the digital-pound consultation outcome influence small-economy retail CBDC redesigns (Sand Dollar privacy upgrade, eNaira relaunch sequencing)?
