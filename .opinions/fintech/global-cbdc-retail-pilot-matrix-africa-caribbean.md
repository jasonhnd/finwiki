# 主観内容の隔離 — Global retail CBDC pilot comparison matrix — Africa, Caribbean, Asia, UK (eNaira / Sand Dollar / JAM-DEX / DCash / e-CNY / Bakong / digital pound)

> 出典エントリー: `fintech/global-cbdc-retail-pilot-matrix-africa-caribbean.md`

## Counterpoints

- **The seven-pole framing is partial**: live retail CBDCs also exist in Uruguay (e-peso pilot ended), Eastern Caribbean (DCash discontinued), India (eRupee pilot), South Korea (BoK CBDC pilot), and Brazil (DREX wholesale-first). The seven-pole matrix focuses on retail and on the most-published cases.
- **Bakong is not strictly a CBDC** under tight definition — it is a payment-system that tokenises commercial-bank balances and supports KHR and USD wallets, more akin to Brazil's Pix than to Sand Dollar. Including it in a CBDC matrix is a design choice for completeness; readers should be aware of the definitional ambiguity.
- **e-CNY pilot vs national launch**: PBoC has not declared e-CNY a "national launch" — it remains in extended pilot across 26+ cities. The "live" designation is conventional but not formal.
- **Adoption metrics are not standardised**: registered wallets, active wallets, monthly transacting users, percentage of adult population, percentage of cash-in-circulation — each programme reports different combinations. The matrix uses the most-cited public figure for each but cross-programme comparison is loose.
- **JAM-DEX's "cleanest success" framing is small-sample**: ~200K-300K Lynk users in a ~2M-adult population is meaningful percentage but small absolute count; extrapolating to large-economy programmes is uncertain.
- **The Bitt vendor cluster correlation is small-sample (n=3)** — it may not generalise beyond Caribbean / African-Atlantic small-economy contexts.
- **DCash discontinuation has structural lessons but is one programme** — generalising "small-economy CBDC will fail" from a single failure is premature.
- **The UK digital pound is in design phase**, not live — including it in a "retail CBDC pilot" matrix is partial; it functions more as a contrast point (what the high-income, well-banked, post-Brexit CBDC design space looks like).

## Open questions

- Will any small-economy retail CBDC achieve **>10% of adult-population active-wallet penetration** within five years post-launch (no programme has yet)?
- Will the digital pound enter a formal Decision Phase by 2027 or will design phase extend indefinitely?
- Will any Caribbean / African-Atlantic programme adopt **anchor-wallet + government-incentive sequencing** following the JAM-DEX template, and replicate the adoption percentage?
- Will Bakong's payment-system + dual-currency-wallet hybrid become the **template for ASEAN regional retail-CBDC interoperability**, contrasting with the bilateral CBDC-bridge approach (e-CNY/HK link)?
- Will the **next small-economy retail CBDC** (Ghana e-cedi, Kenya CBK eShilling, South African Reserve Bank Project Khokha extension) deliberately avoid Bitt as vendor based on the cluster pattern?
- Will the **DCash discontinuation post-mortem** become the canonical BIS reference case in CBDC operational-risk literature, alongside the IMF Working Papers on Sand Dollar (2022) and eNaira (2023)?
- Will the **digital pound holding-cap debate (£10K-£20K)** influence the eventual ECB digital euro cap upward from the proposed €3K-€4K?
- Will any programme other than e-CNY achieve material **cross-border CBDC integration** by 2030 (Sand Dollar / JAM-DEX / eNaira all currently absent from mBridge / Agorá / regional bridges)?
