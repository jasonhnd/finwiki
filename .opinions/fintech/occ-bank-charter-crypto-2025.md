# 主観内容の隔離 — OCC National Bank Charter for Crypto Firms · 2025 Update

> 出典エントリー: `fintech/occ-bank-charter-crypto-2025.md`

## Counterpoints

- **OCC discretion remains broad.** A future administration's Comptroller can re-impose IL 1179-style non-objection. The statutory anchor is GENIUS for PPSI specifically; for the broader IL 1170–1174 powers there is no statute, only OCC interpretation.
- **Federal Reserve master-account discretion is independent.** OCC can charter; Federal Reserve can still decline master-account access. The Custodia case is the live test of how much discretion Federal Reserve retains.
- **18–36 month approval timelines remain.** IL 1183 procedurally unlocks the pipeline but does not shorten the underlying examination process. Charters are still slow.
- **FDIC insurance for crypto-bank deposits is not automatic.** A national trust bank may not even seek FDIC insurance (Anchorage does not); a national bank seeking insured-depository status faces FDIC analysis that is independent of the OCC charter.
- **State regulators may resist.** State banking regulators have an institutional interest in defending state-charter relevance; expect supervisory friction in some states.
- **OCC charter does not eliminate §501 compliance burden.** Every chartered PPSI faces the [[fintech/genius-act-501-actual-implementation|§501 compliance overhead]] — $3M–$10M / year.
- **Concentration risk.** As crypto firms cluster under OCC supervision, the **single-regulator failure mode** becomes more meaningful — a critical OCC examiner shortage or interpretive flip could cascade.
- **Wyoming SPDI may remain attractive for speed-and-cost-sensitive firms** even after the federal pathway re-opens, sustaining a two-track structure indefinitely.

## Open questions

- Does **Custodia v. Federal Reserve** resolve in 2026 — and on what theory? The outcome determines whether the SPDI / OCC trade-off shifts.
- Does the OCC approve the **2025-Q3 charter-application backlog** within a 24-month window, or does the pipeline congest?
- Does **Federal Reserve master-account policy** crystallize into a formal rule under the post-2025 PWG-DAM coordination?
- Does **FDIC insurance eligibility** for stablecoin-issuer deposits get a specific framework, or remain case-by-case?
- Does **state-regulator pushback** materialize, and through what channels (litigation, Congressional hearings, supervisory friction)?
- Does **Tether's USAT product** (via Anchorage) become the template for foreign-incumbent stablecoin issuers entering the US market?
- Does **Bitwise / Coinbase / Ripple / Kraken** charter outcomes diverge — i.e., does OCC approve some and not others, signaling selection criteria?
- Does the **OCC IL 1183 framework survive** a future administration change, or get reversed by a new Comptroller?
