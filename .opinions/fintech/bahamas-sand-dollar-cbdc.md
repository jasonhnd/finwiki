# 主観内容の隔離 — Bahamas Sand Dollar — longest-running live retail CBDC, AFI distribution, small-economy lessons

> 出典エントリー: `fintech/bahamas-sand-dollar-cbdc.md`

## Counterpoints

- **"First nation-wide retail CBDC" is technically correct but small-economy-dependent.** The Bahamas's small population (~400K) means absolute adoption headcount is necessarily small, even at high percentage penetration. The Sand Dollar is not directly comparable to e-CNY or eRupee on absolute volume.
- **The Bitt vendor lineage** (Sand Dollar, eNaira, DCash) is now associated with limited-adoption cases — but the vendor is also chosen because it works at the technical level, and the adoption gap is downstream of distribution / incentive design, not vendor capability.
- **Tier-1 KYC underuse** — most users are tier-2 ID-verified, suggesting the inclusion targeting did not fully reach the unbanked at scale. The IMF Working Paper makes this point explicitly.
- **DCash discontinuation in 2024** is a cautionary parallel — the ECCU programme used the same Bitt vendor and shut down after operational difficulty and limited adoption. Sand Dollar's continuity is not guaranteed.
- **The "longest-running" narrative is partly a function of others not yet existing** — JAM-DEX and eNaira are 2-4 years younger; the next decade will reset the comparative ranking.
- **Cross-border CBDC absence** — Sand Dollar has no [[fintech/mbridge-bis-multi-cbdc-overview|mBridge]] / [[fintech/bis-project-agora-overview|Agorá]] integration; for a tourism-dependent economy with substantial USD circulation, the lack of a cross-border digital-money rail is a real strategic gap.
- **The legal-tender status is real but practical impact is limited** — the binding payment instrument for inbound tourism is USD physical / card / mobile, not Sand Dollar, despite legal-tender parity.

## Open questions

- Does the CBOB layer cross-border CBDC capability (Caribbean-regional bridge, Federal Reserve-coordinated USD-bridge experiment) onto Sand Dollar in the late 2020s?
- Does Sand Dollar achieve material penetration of the outer Family Island unbanked population over a 10-year horizon, or does it remain a Nassau-/Freeport-skewed payment rail?
- Does the AFI interoperability requirement get adopted by larger-economy CBDCs (digital euro, eRupee) as a design principle?
- Does the next CBOB technology refresh stay with Bitt, or does it shift vendor (given the DCash failure on the same vendor)?
- Does Sand Dollar adoption pick up if the CBOB adds an **interest-bearing tier** or a **programmable-payment tier** (welfare disbursement, hurricane-recovery transfers)?
- Does the comparison with [[fintech/nigeria-enaira-retail-cbdc|eNaira]] and [[fintech/jamaica-jam-dex-cbdc|JAM-DEX]] cement a "Bitt-vendor small-economy CBDC class" as a recognisable design archetype in the CBDC literature?
