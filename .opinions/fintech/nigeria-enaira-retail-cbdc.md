# 主観内容の隔離 — Nigeria eNaira — lowest-adoption-rate retail CBDC, CBN distribution, cash-substitution policy collision

> 出典エントリー: `fintech/nigeria-enaira-retail-cbdc.md`

## Counterpoints

- **"Adoption-failure" framing may be unfair to a 4-year-old programme.** The Sand Dollar (older) has comparably low penetration; eNaira may yet grow if distribution incentives are reformed.
- **The CBN under new leadership has not formally deprecated the eNaira.** It remains live; future re-launch with corrected distribution model remains possible.
- **The 2022-2023 cash-policy collision is partly exogenous** to eNaira design — a different sequencing of policies (eNaira first, cash redesign years later) might have yielded a different outcome.
- **Wallet-download numbers are not the same as active-wallet numbers** — the same caveat applies to e-CNY (where 180M+ registered wallets vs single-digit-million daily-active are the same gap).
- **The agent-banking explosion is itself a kind of digital-money adoption success**, just one that did not flow through the CBDC rail; the systemic financial-inclusion goal is partly being met outside the eNaira.
- The IMF Working Paper (Ree, 2023) is one-year-after; longer-horizon data may show different patterns and the Working Paper does not claim its findings are immutable.
- **Surveillance concern is plausibly under-counted as an adoption blocker** — Nigerian public discourse on cash-redesign / cashless-policy / eNaira mixed concerns about state visibility into financial flows.

## Open questions

- Does the eNaira receive any major programmatic relaunch under the Cardoso CBN, or does it remain live-but-dormant indefinitely?
- Does the agent-banking layer (Opay / PalmPay / Moniepoint) eventually integrate eNaira as one of several rails, lifting transaction volume without resolving the brand challenge?
- Does the CBN re-attempt a cashless-policy push, and if so does it sequence eNaira distribution improvements first?
- Does Nigeria join any cross-border CBDC corridor (regional African CBDC bridge, BIS Innovation Hub project) and does that change the wholesale picture independent of retail?
- Does the eNaira become a case study that other African central banks (Ghana e-cedi, South African Reserve Bank Project Khokha, Kenyan CBK eShilling consultation) use to **avoid** the same pitfalls?
- Does the comparison with the [[fintech/bahamas-sand-dollar-cbdc|Sand Dollar]] and [[fintech/jamaica-jam-dex-cbdc|JAM-DEX]] cement a "small-bank-vendor distribution failure pattern" as a sub-class within the [[fintech/cbdc-multi-tier-architecture-tradeoffs|CBDC architecture tradeoffs]] literature?
