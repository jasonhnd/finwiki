# 主観内容の隔離 — WAON vs nanaco: retail prepaid e-money comparison

> 出典エントリー: `payments/waon-nanaco-retail-prepaid-comparison.md`

## Counterpoints

- "WAON and nanaco will fade as code payment grows." Installed-base is sticky for senior demographics and frequent group-retail shoppers; group-retail economic case sustains investment in the FeliCa platform.
- "AEON Pay / Seven app QR will replace WAON / nanaco." The two are run in parallel rather than as substitutes — different consumer-segment fit, different POS integration, different cost economics.
- "Group bank tie-in gives WAON / nanaco unique funding cost advantage." Partially — but PayPay's bank-account-direct-charge from PayPay Bank achieves similar in-group funding for SoftBank / LY ecosystem.
- "Retail-prepaid is structurally inferior to platform-wallet code payment." Inferior for cross-merchant share, superior for group-retail integration depth and group-loyalty economics; comparing on cross-merchant share misses the strategic logic.

## Open questions

- Will AEON consolidate WAON, AEON Pay, and AEON Card into a single unified payment app, or maintain parallel surfaces?
- Will Seven & i make a second attempt at proprietary code payment (after Seven Pay's 2019 retirement) or continue relying on third-party wallet acceptance at 7-Eleven?
- How does breakage economics differ between WAON / nanaco (domestic-resident-heavy) and Suica (inbound-tourist-heavy)?
- Will FeliCa-prepaid retail acceptance shrink as new POS rollouts in micro-merchants default to QR code rather than FeliCa reader hardware?
- Does the 国税納付 tax-payment-via-nanaco route remain a meaningful feature, or has Seven Card Service further restricted credit-card-funded charge into nanaco?
