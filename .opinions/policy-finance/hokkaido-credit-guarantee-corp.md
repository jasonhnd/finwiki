# 主観内容の隔離 — 北海道信用保証協会 (Hokkaido Credit Guarantee Corporation)

> 出典エントリー: `policy-finance/hokkaido-credit-guarantee-corp.md`

## Counterpoints

- **Tourism concentration risk**: Hokkaido CGC's book is structurally more cyclical than urban-prefecture peers because tourism and primary-industry SMEs are both highly sensitive to external shocks (weather, currency, geopolitics, disease). The counterargument is that the diversification across agriculture / fisheries / tourism / food-processing creates more uncorrelated risk than a single-industry urban concentration.
- **Recovery-value problem**: rural commercial real-estate recovery values are structurally lower than urban, suppressing 求償 recovery ratios. This is partly offset by the federation reinsurance and JFC credit-insurance layer absorbing the residual.
- **JA Bank overlap**: the SME-vs-agricultural-cooperative boundary is fuzzier in Hokkaido than elsewhere because of the prefecture's primary-industry weight. Some agri-supply-chain SMEs route credit through JA Bank channels that bypass Hokkaido CGC guarantees entirely.
- **Niseko foreign-capital risk**: the Niseko-area resort SME segment is increasingly capitalized by Australian / Hong Kong / Singapore inbound investment. This brings in non-standard loan structures and creates a guarantee-segment that does not fit the federation's typical risk model cleanly.
- **Geographic operational cost**: covering 179 municipalities across 83,000 km² creates an operational cost-to-balance ratio that is structurally higher than urban peers like [[policy-finance/tokyo-credit-guarantee-corp|Tokyo CGC]].

## Open questions

- What share of Hokkaido CGC's guarantee balance is concentrated in tourism / hospitality vs. food processing vs. fisheries vs. general SME?
- How did the 2020-vintage ZeroZero loans perform through full payoff in 2026–2028 specifically in the tourism segment?
- How does the Niseko-region foreign-capital inflow affect Hokkaido CGC's 創業保証 product utilization?
- What is the long-run trend in fisheries-segment subrogation as marine-resource availability changes with ocean warming?
- How are food-processing-SME succession patterns evolving under Hokkaido's demographic decline?
