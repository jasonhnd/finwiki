# 主観内容の隔離 — Japan local-government bond market issuance matrix (prefectures / 政令指定都市 / municipalities / special districts)

> 出典エントリー: `policy-finance/japan-local-government-bond-market-issuance-matrix.md`

## Counterpoints

- **Implicit-support assumption is fragile.** Investors price local bonds as if 地方交付税 guarantees servicing; the legal reality is more nuanced and a stressed issuer is not automatically rescued. The statutory fiscal-soundness regime exists precisely because the assumption needs constraint.
- **Demographic divergence.** Tax-base erosion in shrinking-population municipalities can outpace fiscal-equalization support over long horizons; the credit profile of small municipalities is structurally exposed to depopulation that does not exist at the prefecture-aggregate level.
- **Public-private boundary.** Public-offering local bonds compete with private regional-bank lending for the same issuers; cheap [[policy-finance/japan-finance-organization-municipalities|JFM]] funding can dampen private-sector pricing competition at the local-government-finance lane.
- **Liquidity vs spread asymmetry.** Liquidity in non-benchmark named-issuer bonds can dry up sharply under stress while spread to JGB benchmarks widens modestly — the spread metric understates execution risk.
- **Yield-spread comparisons are indicative.** The spread ranges referenced in this matrix are stable-market orders of magnitude, not precise quotations, and shift materially with market regime, BoJ policy stance, and individual-issuer credit events. Always check current market data before any execution decision.

## Open questions

- How do public-offering local-bond spreads vs JGBs behave under monetary-tightening conditions and post-YCC normalization?
- What share of local-government total debt rests on [[policy-finance/japan-finance-organization-municipalities|JFM]] vs FILP vs market funding vs bank lending, and how is the mix evolving across issuer tiers?
- Will labelled (green / sustainability / SDG) local-government bonds grow into a structural lane, or remain a marginal subset of total issuance?
- How will disaster-recovery bonds (Tohoku 2011, Kumamoto 2016, Noto 2024 cohort) compare in pricing to ordinary refinancing bonds over their amortisation schedules?
- How will demographic decline pressure the small-municipality tier's reliance on JFM and FILP through the late 2020s and into the 2030s?
- Will foreign-investor participation expand beyond Tokyo Metropolitan Bond and JFM bonds into wider named-prefecture and designated-city issuance, or remain concentrated?
- How will the 不交付団体 set evolve over time — is Tokyo Metropolis structurally protected, or is its long-run demographic exposure greater than commonly assumed?
