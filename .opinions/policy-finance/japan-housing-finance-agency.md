# 主観内容の隔離 — Japan Housing Finance Agency (JHF)

> 出典エントリー: `policy-finance/japan-housing-finance-agency.md`

## Counterpoints

- **YCC-era relevance erosion**: during NIRP / YCC, private variable-rate mortgages priced off ultra-low short rates dominated new origination, and Flat 35's long-tenor fixed-rate proposition lost relative appeal. Counterargument: even in low-rate eras, a subset of buyers value rate certainty over the full mortgage life, and Flat 35 retained a structural niche; the 2024–2026 rate normalization is restoring its relative appeal.
- **Privatization-debate residual**: critics argued the 2007 reform did not go far enough — JHF still operates as a public institution rather than being fully privatized; defenders argue housing-finance policy infrastructure is structurally a public good in Japan because no private-market structure would supply 35-year fixed-rate mortgages at scale.
- **MBS market dominance concern**: the dominance of JHF MBS in yen-denominated structured paper could be argued to crowd out private RMBS market development. Counterargument: private RMBS still exists for non-conforming credit segments; JHF MBS sets the benchmark curve.
- **Property-standard rigidity**: Flat 35's technical-standard eligibility (insulation, earthquake resistance) excludes some properties from eligibility, particularly older or non-conforming homes — which may not match all buyer preferences.
