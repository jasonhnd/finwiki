# 主観内容の隔離 — JFC Agriculture, Forestry and Fisheries Division operating model (農林水産事業)

> 出典エントリー: `policy-finance/jfc-agriculture-forestry-fisheries-division.md`

## Counterpoints

- **Cooperative-finance overlap.** The case for a state direct-lender in agriculture / fisheries depends on a credit-supply gap above and beyond what JA / 信農連 / JF / 信漁連 / [[JapanFG/norinchukin|Norinchukin]] can deliver. Critics argue the cooperative pyramid has the funding and reach to absorb most of what the AFF Division does, especially with MAFF subsidy overlay on cooperative-originated loans. Defenders argue long-tenor forestry and certain transition-capex deals are genuinely outside what cooperative balance sheets will price.
- **Subsidy opacity.** Heavy use of 利子補給 and disaster-response supplementary-budget appropriations makes the true fiscal cost of AFF Division-anchored primary-sector finance less visible than if it were booked as a grant or budget line.
- **Demographics vs product design.** Most products are designed around 認定農業者 / 担い手 / 集落営農 frames, but the underlying farmer population continues to age out. How much of the lending book is genuinely supporting expansion versus subsidising managed decline is contested.
- **Forestry tenor risk.** Multi-decade forestry-loan tenors are economically meaningful only if forest-management institutions and successor 経営体 remain in place across the loan life. Forest-management succession is itself a live policy issue.
- **Food-industry boundary with SME / DBJ.** Large food-industry M&A and capex deals sit at the boundary of the AFF Division, the [[policy-finance/jfc-sme-division-operating-model|SME Division]], and [[JapanFG/dbj|DBJ]] mid-cap finance; the right perimeter is periodically revisited.

## Open questions

- How food-security framing changes the long-term composition of AFF Division lending (feed / fertiliser / domestic-production capex vs traditional smallholder support).
- Whether forest-management transition (under the 森林経営管理制度) will drive a structural increase in long-tenor forestry lending or destabilise existing books.
- How the export-target push interacts with overseas-investment finance routed through this division vs through [[JapanFG/jbic|JBIC]].
- Whether the cooperative-banking sector's structural pressures (Norinchukin investment-portfolio stress; JA membership decline) will increase or decrease reliance on the AFF Division as a backstop.
- The pace and scope of 6 次産業化 lending and how it interacts with private mid-cap food-industry finance.
