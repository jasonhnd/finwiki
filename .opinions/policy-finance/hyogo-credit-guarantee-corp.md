# 主観内容の隔離 — 兵庫県信用保証協会 (Hyogo Credit Guarantee Corporation)

> 出典エントリー: `policy-finance/hyogo-credit-guarantee-corp.md`

## Counterpoints

- **Heavy-industry decline**: Hyogo's traditional heavy-industry SME base is in long-run secular decline as Japanese steel, shipbuilding, and rolling-stock production capacity contracts. This erodes one of Hyogo CGC's largest historical guarantee pools.
- **Kobe Port share loss**: Kobe Port has been losing share to Yokohama / Tokyo for decades. The SME logistics tail is structurally pressured.
- **Tourism geographic spread**: unlike Fukuoka or Hokkaido where tourism is concentrated, Hyogo's tourism SMEs are spread across Kobe / Awaji / Arima / Kinosaki, which adds operational complexity to disaster-response guarantee programs.
- **SMFG-Kansai-Mirai counterparty concentration**: the dominance of the SMFG / Kansai Mirai Bank ecosystem in Hyogo means a high single-group counterparty correlation in the guarantee book.
- **Banshu / Tajima rural decline**: the local-industry SME segments (Banshu textiles, Tatsuno leather, Tajima rural agriculture) are in secular contraction with founder-aging and wholesale-demand erosion, gradually shrinking the rural-Hyogo tail of the guarantee book.
- **Earthquake-doctrine cost**: maintaining the 1995-vintage disaster-response operational template carries a fixed operational cost that competes with day-to-day SME guarantee processing capacity.

## Open questions

- What share of Hyogo CGC's guarantee balance is concentrated in heavy-industry suppliers vs. logistics vs. tourism vs. local-industry SMEs?
- How is the Banshu textile / Tajima local-industry SME segment evolving under wholesale-demand decline and founder-succession pressure?
- How did the 2020-vintage ZeroZero loans perform through full payoff in 2026–2028 specifically in the heavy-industry-supplier segment?
- What is the long-term trajectory of Kobe Port logistics SMEs under continued port-share loss to Yokohama / Tokyo?
- How does the 1995 earthquake-recovery doctrine continue to inform Hyogo CGC's disaster-response protocols, and how was it adapted for COVID-era operations?
