# 主観内容の隔離 — Fisheries credit guarantee system in Japan

> 出典エントリー: `policy-finance/fisheries-credit-guarantee-system.md`

## Counterpoints

- **Geographic concentration risk**: the heavy coastal concentration means a single regional marine-environment shock can correlate losses across many guarantees simultaneously, raising portfolio-risk concerns. Counterargument: JAFFIC reinsurance and national federation pooling diversifies risk across regions and product types.
- **ALPS-water export shock**: the 2023 China seafood ban demonstrated how a single geopolitical / regulatory event can produce a sudden cash-flow shock to scallop / sea-cucumber-exporting operators, requiring a rapid guarantee-system response. The system absorbed the shock but at meaningful subrogation cost.
- **Cooperative-system dependence**: like the agriculture system, the fisheries guarantee system is integrated with the JF / 信漁連 / Norinchukin cooperative-banking stack, which may limit competitive alternatives for borrowers outside the cooperative-system reach.
- **Aquaculture environmental risk**: marine heatwaves and disease outbreaks (e.g., scallop disease, kelp-bed collapse) create non-traditional default vectors that the guarantee system must increasingly accommodate.
- **Demographic fisher decline**: Japan's fisher population is declining and aging faster than the farmer population; long-run scale of the guarantee book is structurally pressured.

## Open questions

- What is the consolidated guarantee outstanding across all prefectural / 広域 漁業信用基金協会?
- How did the 2023 ALPS-water China-ban-related guarantee absorption resolve through 2024–2025?
- What is the long-run default trajectory of vessel finance vs aquaculture vs processing segments?
- How will marine-heatwave-driven aquaculture failure rates change the guarantee-fee economics?
- What is the relationship between JF Marine Bank deposit base contraction and fisheries-guarantee lender mix?
