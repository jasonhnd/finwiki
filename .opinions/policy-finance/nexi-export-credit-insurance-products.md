# 主観内容の隔離 — NEXI export credit insurance products

> 出典エントリー: `policy-finance/nexi-export-credit-insurance-products.md`

## Counterpoints

- **OECD Arrangement constraint vs Chinese competition.** Chinese policy-insurance and policy-finance (Sinosure, CDB, China EXIM) do not sit inside the Arrangement, allowing terms NEXI cannot match. The structural disadvantage in some emerging-market tenders is real, especially against unconstrained Chinese cover.
- **Climate-finance vs Asian-energy reality.** NEXI's posture on transition-fuel LNG and on AZEC-frame ammonia co-firing is criticised by climate NGOs as life-extension of fossil infrastructure, even as Asian energy ministries treat the same products as the only available decarbonisation pathway.
- **Public exposure concentration.** Stacked NEXI cover + [[JapanFG/jbic|JBIC]] direct loans + [[policy-finance/jogmec|JOGMEC]] equity on a single frontier-country project concentrates Japanese state exposure. A project failure cascades across multiple state balance sheets.
- **OII claims on creeping expropriation.** Defining "creeping expropriation" for OII claim purposes is contentious in cases involving tax / regulatory measures; claim adjudication carries political tail risk.
- **Private vs public PRI overlap.** NEXI OII coexists with MIGA cover and private political-risk insurance (Lloyd's market, AIG, Chubb, Sovereign). The boundary is project-specific and sometimes results in layered private + NEXI structures.
- **Reinsurance opacity.** NEXI's reinsurance role for private trade-credit insurers is less visible publicly than its direct-insurance products; aggregate public-exposure data on this slice is patchier.

## Open questions

- How rapidly OECD Arrangement reforms during the current negotiating cycle re-open sector understandings (climate, critical minerals, semiconductors) and re-shape NEXI's product menu.
- The cumulative claim-loss performance of post-2022 Ukraine-war exposures and how reinsurance / state-loss-sharing handles outcomes.
- Whether AZEC-frame products generate material new NEXI underwriting volume in Southeast Asia.
- How NEXI's reinsurance role for the private trade-credit-insurance market evolves under tightening insurance-market capacity in some segments.
- The structural depth of NEXI's coverage of friend-shoring-driven semiconductor and supply-chain projects.
- The forward shape of OII claims under sanctions-impacted jurisdictions.
