# 主観内容の隔離 — Japan interest-rate derivatives overview

> 出典エントリー: `derivatives/japan-interest-rate-derivatives-overview.md`

## Counterpoints

- "Daily turnover figures are precise" — They are aggregate snapshots subject to revision and dependent on the survey methodology (BIS triennial vs ISDA weekly). Different sources may report different numbers for the same period.
- "FIEA mandate covers everything" — FIEA covers Japan-located dealers and trades involving regulated counterparties. Some bilateral, offshore, or non-mandated trades sit outside scope.
- "JSCC clearing eliminates risk" — It removes bilateral counterparty risk but concentrates risk at the CCP; the CCP's default-fund waterfall, capital adequacy, and stress-testing are the relevant risk surface.
- "TONA replaces TIBOR everywhere" — TONA is the RFR and dominant new-IRS reference, but TIBOR persists for term-fix loan markets and a meaningful share of new IRS. The TIBOR-TONA basis trade reflects coexistence.
- "Japan is a small derivatives market" — JPY is one of the four largest IRS currencies globally; aggregate notional is in the trillions of USD-equivalent. The market is large; the dealer concentration is high.
