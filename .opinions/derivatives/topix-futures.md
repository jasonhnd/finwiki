# 主観内容の隔離 — TOPIX futures (OSE)

> 出典エントリー: `derivatives/topix-futures.md`

## Counterpoints

- "TOPIX futures volume is too small to matter" — False at the institutional level. While daily contract count is smaller than Nikkei 225 futures, notional value per contract is larger, and institutional rebalancing flow during quarter-ends and index reviews drives outsized volume bursts.
- "TOPIX and Nikkei 225 futures are interchangeable hedges" — Not for sector-sensitive portfolios. The price-weighting (Nikkei) vs market-cap-weighting (TOPIX) difference creates persistent sector basis risk.
- "BoJ ETF holdings dominate TOPIX pricing" — Holdings are large but in hold-phase; BoJ is not currently buying. Free-float reduction effect remains but no new purchase pressure.
- "TOPIX sub-index futures are dead products" — Mostly low-volume, but Banks Futures see episodic activity around bank-sector catalysts.
- "Retail participation in TOPIX futures equals Nikkei" — No, retail leans heavily toward Nikkei mini and Micro; TOPIX is dominated by institutional flow.
