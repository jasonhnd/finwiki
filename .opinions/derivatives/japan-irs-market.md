# 主観内容の隔離 — Japan yen interest-rate swap (IRS) market

> 出典エントリー: `derivatives/japan-irs-market.md`

## Counterpoints

- "All JPY IRS clears at JSCC" — No. A material share remains bilateral, particularly non-standard tenors, structured trades, and trades involving non-mandated counterparties.
- "TIBOR is being phased out" — No. TIBOR survives as a term-fix reference and continues to support a meaningful share of JPY-loan-hedging IRS; the TIBOR-TONA basis trade is itself a tradable market.
- "Notional outstanding equals exposure" — No. Notional is a stock measure of contract size; economic exposure is far smaller after offsets and netting. Cite gross market value and post-netting exposures separately.
- "IRS is a substitute for JGB futures" — Overlapping but distinct. IRS is OTC, bilateral or cleared, tenor-customizable, and CSA-collateralized. Futures (see [[derivatives/jgb-futures-curve]]) are listed, standardized, daily-margined, and physically deliver into a JGB basket. Both serve duration hedging; the choice depends on tenor, customization, and balance-sheet preference.
- "Dealer P&L from JPY rates is publicly disclosed" — Only at the aggregate rates / fixed-income line in dealer IR. JPY-IRS-specific P&L is not separately published.

## Open Questions

- How will the TIBOR-TONA basis evolve through a BoJ normalization cycle? Will TIBOR's credit-bank-funding premium widen if Japanese bank funding stress rises?
- What share of new JPY-IRS issuance will reference TONA-compounded vs TIBOR-referencing five years from now?
- How does the JSCC clearing share affect dealer-franchise economics — does it commoditize the IRS business, or do dealers retain pricing power on the structured / bilateral residual?
- How will rising JPY rates change corporate end-user demand for IRS hedging? Higher rates increase the value of fixed-rate locking.
- How does the JPY IRS market interact with the JPY-USD cross-currency basis market (see [[derivatives/yen-basis-swap-market]]) during stress?
