# 主観内容の隔離 — JGB inflation-linked bond (JGBi)

> 出典エントリー: `derivatives/jgb-inflation-linked-bond-jgbi.md`

## Counterpoints

- "JGBi is too small to matter for inflation hedging" — Small in absolute size relative to nominal JGBs, but the BEI signal is widely watched and meaningful flows exist.
- "Deflation floor makes JGBi a sure thing" — The floor only applies at maturity; intermediate coupon income reflects the inflation-adjusted principal which can sit below par during deflation.
- "BoJ holdings make JGBi dysfunctional" — Holdings are large but the market continues to function; BEI continues to be a useful signal albeit with some distortion adjustment needed.
- "Foreign investors will exit JGBi as Japan rates normalize" — Possibly partial; but the diversification appeal and JPY real-return appeal persist for global inflation-asset mandates.
- "JGBi BEI is equivalent to JPY inflation swap breakeven" — Persistent gaps exist due to liquidity premia and CSA differences; arbitrage closes only partially.

## Open Questions

- Will MoF expand JGBi issuance (or add new tenors) post-2024 as inflation hedging demand becomes more institutionally embedded?
- How does the BoJ JGBi balance roll down as the BoJ normalizes its JGB-purchase program?
- Will a JGBi futures contract emerge to support hedging and arbitrage between cash JGBi and derivatives?
- How does foreign investor share of JGBi evolve through Japan rate-normalization cycles?
- Will the JGBi BEI / swap BEI gap compress as the JGBi liquidity premium changes with market maturity?
