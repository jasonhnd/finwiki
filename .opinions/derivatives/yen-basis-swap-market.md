# 主観内容の隔離 — Yen-USD cross-currency basis swap market

> 出典エントリー: `derivatives/yen-basis-swap-market.md`

## Counterpoints

- "Basis is an arbitrage" — In a regulated, capital-constrained world, basis is the equilibrium price of balance-sheet rental and USD-funding scarcity, not a free-money trade. Numerous BIS papers document this since 2014.
- "Quarter-end basis is just window dressing" — Partly. But window dressing reveals real balance-sheet costs that exist all the time; quarter-end just makes them visible.
- "BoJ-Fed swap line fixes the problem" — It caps catastrophic dislocation but does not eliminate ordinary basis. The swap line is priced at a penalty rate (OIS + fixed spread) precisely to discourage routine use.
- "Hedge cost is the same as the basis" — No. The all-in cost of hedging a USD asset back to JPY also includes the cross-currency interest differential and the FX-forward curve, not only the basis spread.
- "Basis collapse to zero means CIP is restored" — In rare windows the basis approaches zero, but persistent zero would require regulatory cost of FX-swap balance-sheet usage to fall, which has not occurred since 2008.

## Open Questions

- How does the BoJ rate-normalization cycle (post-March 2024) interact with the structural JPY-USD basis? Higher JPY rates reduce the JPY-asset yield gap and could reduce USD-bond demand by Japanese investors over time.
- How much does the basis widen if the BoJ-Fed swap line tenor or facility design changes?
- What is the cross-elasticity between the JPY-USD basis and JPY swap-spread (IRS - JGB) — both prices reflect dealer balance-sheet cost?
- How would broader USD asset reallocation (out of US Treasuries by Japanese life insurers) change the structural basis level?
- How does JSCC's expanded clearing scope (if it covers more bilateral CCBS) change the dealer franchise and warehousing economics?
