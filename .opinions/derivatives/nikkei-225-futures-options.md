# 主観内容の隔離 — Nikkei 225 futures and options (OSE)

> 出典エントリー: `derivatives/nikkei-225-futures-options.md`

## Counterpoints

- "Nikkei 225 futures are essentially a retail product" — False. While mini and Micro have very large retail participation, the Large contract is institutional-dominated, and foreign macro and HFT flow drive much of the price discovery, especially in night session.
- "OSE night session is illiquid" — Increasingly false. The night session has grown materially and now captures a meaningful share of foreign-overlap flow; it can have liquidity comparable to day session during US economic events.
- "CME Nikkei is a separate market" — Not really. OSE and CME pricing arbitrage continuously; the two listings function more like one global market with two listing venues.
- "Weekly options are a US-style innovation that won't take in Japan" — They did. Weekly options are now meaningful share of option volume.
- "Mini and Micro cannibalize Large volume" — Partially. They expand the addressable user base more than they cannibalize; Large remains the institutional benchmark contract.
