# 主観内容の隔離 — Japan inflation swap market (JPY CPI-linked)

> 出典エントリー: `derivatives/japan-inflation-swap.md`

## Counterpoints

- "JPY inflation swap is a tiny irrelevant niche" — Smaller than USD / EUR markets but has grown materially post-2022 and serves real end-user hedging needs.
- "BoJ 2% target makes inflation swap breakevens meaningless" — Breakevens reflect market-priced expectations, which can deviate from BoJ targets even when the central bank is credible; the gap itself is informative.
- "JGBi breakeven and swap breakeven should be identical" — They differ persistently because of liquidity premia, CSA collateral differences, and supply/demand imbalances in each market.
- "Inflation swaps are too complex for Japanese pension funds" — Standardized ZCIS structures are well-understood; ISDA documentation is mature; main barriers are governance and risk-budget rather than complexity.
- "Post-2024 the JPY inflation swap market will collapse as inflation moderates" — Even moderate above-zero inflation creates structural hedging demand; the market is unlikely to retreat to pre-2013 levels.

## Open Questions

- Will JPY inflation swap activity stabilize at a structurally higher level post-2024 than pre-2022, even if inflation moderates?
- How does the JGBi-swap breakeven differential evolve as MoF JGBi issuance changes and as JGBi liquidity premia shift?
- Will GPIF or other large public pension funds adopt more inflation-swap-based hedging if the inflation-linked liability share grows?
- How would a re-emergence of deflation pressure (e.g., from yen strength or external demand shock) reprice the swap curve, particularly long-tenor breakevens?
- Will JSCC extend clearing to inflation swaps in the medium term to support market growth?
