# 主観内容の隔離 — Japan market maker and liquidity provider landscape

> 出典エントリー: `securities/japan-market-maker-and-liquidity-provider-landscape.md`

## Counterpoints

- The global HFT tier's actual market share of Japan cash-equity volume is smaller than US Reg NMS market-share figures suggest; TSE retains dominant cash-equity flow and the domestic broker franchise is more entrenched than the US equivalent.
- Public disclosure of firm-by-firm market-share for MM activity is limited; survey / vendor estimates require methodology caveats.
- The narrative that HFT firms always tighten spreads is conditional; in stress events (rapid news, halt resumptions), MM withdrawal can widen spreads suddenly.
- Designated-market-maker scheme economics are venue-dependent; do not assume a global firm participates in every JPX MM scheme — verify the published MM list.
- Market-abuse cases (spoofing, layering) periodically involve algorithmic and HFT participants globally; treat regulator findings as case-specific rather than generalizable.

## Open questions

- Will JPX expand formal cash-equity DMM coverage beyond selective schemes, or retain the order-driven default?
- Does the global HFT tier in Tokyo continue to grow (more firms, deeper teams), or stabilize at the current roster?
- How do tokenized-securities markets at [[securities/odx-start-stb-secondary-market|ODX START]] develop market-maker structures? Currently thin; the design path may import futures / options-style DMM logic or evolve a different model.
- Will JGB market structural reforms (e.g. shorter settlement cycles, expanded JGB repo participation) change the MM economics for JGB futures / options?
- How does AI / ML-driven quoting infrastructure shift the competitive map between domestic broker MMs and global HFT firms?
