# 主観内容の隔離 — Japannext PTS SOR routing deep dive

> 出典エントリー: `securities/japannext-sor-routing-deep-dive.md`

## Counterpoints

- The PTS price-improvement story is sometimes overstated. For many liquid large caps, PTS displayed top-of-book equals or is fractionally worse than TSE NBBO because TSE remains the reference market and arbitrageurs keep PTS quotes pinned.
- Iceberg / hidden orders can be reverse-engineered by adverse-selection-seeking flow; the protection a hidden order offers is partial and asymmetric.
- "Dark pool" language imported from US debates often misleads Japanese audiences; Japannext's product is mostly a lit alternative venue, not a US-style ATS dark pool.
- Without standardised Rule-605-style execution-quality disclosure, broker SOR comparison in Japan relies on policy reading rather than measured outcomes.
- Broker preferencing concerns are structural but should not be conflated with regulatory non-compliance unless an FSA inspection or JSDA self-regulatory action has actually found one.

## Open questions

- How will execution-quality disclosure standardise (if at all) under continued FSA customer-best-interest implementation reviews?
- Will Japannext extend midpoint / cross-style segments to compete more directly with US-style ATS designs?
- Does the share of PTS executed value continue to rise after the 2025 PTS Information Network transition to JSDA-published statistics?
- How do tokenized securities at [[securities/osaka-digital-exchange|ODX]] and START affect cash-equity routing priorities (mostly orthogonal so far, but worth monitoring)?
- What is the likely steady-state number of PTS operators in Japan: two, three, or further consolidation?
