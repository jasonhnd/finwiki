# 主観内容の隔離 — Progmat tokenized securities infrastructure

> 出典エントリー: `securities/progmat-tokenized-securities-infrastructure.md`

## Counterpoints

- The ST issuance market in Japan remains small relative to listed equity, listed corporate bond, or 公募投信 markets; cumulative ST balance under JPY 1 trillion is a niche segment, not a mass-market product.
- Bank-led tokenization design is slower to market than US Reg D-style tokenized private placements; the trade-off is regulatory legibility and trust-bank-backed custody enforceability.
- The Progmat majority share by outstanding balance reflects an early-stage market with a small number of platform players; sustained share leadership depends on continued issuer pipeline.
- "Programmable corporate actions" is structurally promising but production usage is constrained by issuer disclosure obligations and trust-bank operational integration; do not overstate as fully automated.
- Atomic DvP with tokenized cash legs remains a stated architecture goal; many current ST trades still settle with the cash leg on conventional rails. Avoid framing the current state as a fully atomic DvP environment.

## Open questions

- Does the three-platform (Progmat / BOOSTRY / ibet for FIN) architecture consolidate further, or does sector-utility framing keep ibet neutral while Progmat and BOOSTRY remain bank- vs securities-firm poles?
- How quickly does Progmat Coin / SC integration translate into production atomic DvP at ST PTS scale?
- Does Progmat extend into tokenized investment-trust beneficial interests at scale, opening a bridge to [[securities/japan-asset-manager-landscape-matrix|asset manager]] distribution?
- Does the JSDA / FSA framework expand specified-investor scope toward broader retail accessibility, or remain narrowly scoped?
- How will the public-rail tokenized-cash-leg options (DCJPY vs Progmat Coin vs private bank tokenized deposits) shake out in the ST settlement design?
