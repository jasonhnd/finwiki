# 主観内容の隔離 — ODX START security token secondary market

> 出典エントリー: `securities/odx-start-stb-secondary-market.md`

## Counterpoints

- "Japan's first ST PTS" is a true statement of venue first, but the secondary-market depth was thin at launch and the active issue count remains far smaller than listed equity / corporate bond markets.
- ATR is not equivalent to TSE listing in regulatory weight or disclosure intensity; do not conflate them in cross-border investor communications.
- True atomic DvP for tokenized securities is still developmental; settlement-failure risk profile differs from JSCC-cleared cash equity.
- The five-party ST architecture (issuer / platform / securities firm / trust bank / venue) increases coordination complexity and slows time-to-market relative to cash-equity issuance.
- Specified-investor scope means ST is a structurally narrower retail product than 公募投信 / NISA-eligible funds; do not overstate ST as a retail mass-market democratization story without disclosure.

## Open questions

- Will ATR criteria converge with TSE listing standards over time, or remain a clearly narrower PTS-style framework?
- How quickly will atomic DvP with tokenized cash legs (Progmat Coin / DCJPY / private bank tokenized deposits) become standard in ST PTS settlement?
- Will START expand to host tokenized investment-trust beneficial interests at scale, bridging into the [[securities/japan-asset-manager-landscape-matrix|asset manager landscape]]?
- Will a second ST PTS launch as a competitive pole, or will START hold the entire secondary-market function for the foreseeable future?
- How will cross-border ST distribution (Japan-issued ST to non-Japanese investors and vice versa) develop under FIEA scope?
