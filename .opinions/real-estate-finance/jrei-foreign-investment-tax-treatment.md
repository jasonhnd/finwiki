# 主観内容の隔離 — Foreign investment in Japan real estate tax treatment

> 出典エントリー: `real-estate-finance/jrei-foreign-investment-tax-treatment.md`

## Counterpoints

- Tax treatment for foreign real-estate investors in Japan is structure-specific and treaty-specific; general descriptions in this page are NOT applicable to any individual transaction.
- TK / GK-TK and TMK are well-established but require ongoing compliance discipline; failure to maintain qualifying status risks loss of pass-through.
- PE risk is not always obvious; certain asset-management activities or sponsor-level involvement can trigger PE under some interpretations.
- Treaty-shopping anti-avoidance rules (LOB, principal-purpose test, beneficial-owner) have tightened materially in recent years; older structures may face DTT-claim challenges.
- Capital-gains source rule for real-estate-rich entities is broader than direct real-estate-sale rule; indirect-sale exits require careful structuring.
- J-REIT 90% distribution rule limits J-REIT-level capital retention; this is a feature for investors, not a bug.
- Tax guidance and treaty interpretation rotate; NTA / MoF guidance and CBCR / digital-economy taxation context should be re-confirmed before use.
- Some structures used historically by foreign investors have been challenged under newer anti-avoidance rules; legacy-structure review is a recurring discipline.
