# 主観内容の隔離 — Private REIT Japan vs listed J-REIT comparison

> 出典エントリー: `real-estate-finance/private-reit-japan-vs-listed-j-reit-comparison.md`

## Counterpoints

- Private REIT illiquidity does not eliminate market-cycle risk; it just deferrs the price recognition. NAV smoothing is not the same as low risk.
- Appraisal-based pricing can lag fast market moves, which makes private REIT look more stable in stress but does not insulate the underlying real-estate cash flow from the same shocks that hit listed J-REIT.
- Aggregate ARES private-REIT data hides large per-fund variation. Outside the qualified-investor disclosure circle, public analysis is limited.
- The "match with pension liability duration" framing assumes that the pension plan actually has long-duration liabilities and is not facing near-term outflow pressure; not all pension allocations satisfy that condition.
- Listed J-REIT and private REIT are both subject to the same sponsor / asset-management-company conflict-of-interest issues outlined in [[real-estate-finance/j-reit-sponsor-structure-conflict|sponsor structure and conflict of interest]].

## Open questions

- How private-REIT NAV converges to listed-J-REIT-implied yields during a sustained rate-normalization cycle.
- Whether redemption-window discipline holds across all managers in a stressed redemption-demand scenario.
- Allocation-policy direction for major corporate pension plans and [[insurance/INDEX|life insurers]] as Japan rates and yields shift.
- Sponsor strategy when running both a listed J-REIT and a private REIT in the same asset class — pipeline-allocation tension is structural.
- Public-disclosure direction: whether ARES or FSA push for more public-facing aggregate disclosure of private REIT segment metrics.
