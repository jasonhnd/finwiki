# 主観内容の隔離 — Japan real estate cap rate compression 2026

> 出典エントリー: `real-estate-finance/real-estate-cap-rate-compression-2026.md`

## Counterpoints

- Cap rates are appraisal- and survey-derived; transactional cap rates can diverge from published expected cap rates by ±50bp.
- "Tokyo prime office cap rate" hides asset-level heterogeneity; trophy-asset rates can sit below the class range, while sub-prime asset rates sit above.
- Foreign-buyer demand can reverse quickly; cap-rate floor is not structural in a hard-stop sense.
- Hospitality cap-rate compression in 2024 - 2025 may overshoot once new-supply pipeline absorbs; reversion risk is asset-specific.
- Appraisal cap rates lag transaction reality by 2 - 4 quarters; reading appraisal-NAV alone is misleading near inflection points.
- Cap-rate decomposition treats risk premium as residual; cleaner decomposition requires explicit equity-risk-premium and asset-risk-premium models that public surveys do not always publish.
- Rate-path uncertainty (post-NIRP normalisation pace) matters more than the headline policy rate itself for cap-rate path.

- How does data-centre / niche-asset cap-rate development reshape investor allocation among asset classes?

- Will JREI / ARES survey methodology adjust to reflect more transparent decomposition (rent growth, exit cap, leverage)?
