# 主観内容の隔離 — J-REIT sponsor structure and conflict of interest

> 出典エントリー: `real-estate-finance/j-reit-sponsor-structure-conflict.md`

## Counterpoints

- The sponsor model has clear benefits: stable pipeline, brand recognition, operating capability, and long-term commitment that internalization would not necessarily replicate at the same cost.
- Many sponsor-supported transactions have third-party-appraisal protection that materially reduces the practical conflict-of-interest risk versus the worst-case framing.
- Independent / financial-sponsor J-REIT (Kenedix-style) also face conflict-of-interest issues — they just look different (cross-fund allocation, fee structure) rather than absent.
- Foreign-sponsor J-REIT (GLP, Prologis) are not free of conflict; the sponsor group exists outside Japan but the pipeline-priority and cross-fund concerns are similar in structure.
- US REIT internal-management is not free of conflict either; executive compensation, related-party board members, and strategic misallocation cases exist there too.

## Open questions

- Whether FSA inspection priorities push J-REIT toward stricter related-party-transaction governance or toward partial internalization options.
- Whether activist investors who have engaged [[finance/japan-listed-financial-groups-investable-universe|listed financial groups]] extend their attention to J-REIT in a sustained way.
- How sponsor-stake levels move during a [[banking/INDEX|bank-credit]] tightening cycle, especially for sponsors with leveraged balance sheets.
- Whether the foreign-sponsor model develops materially different governance posture from the domestic-developer model.
