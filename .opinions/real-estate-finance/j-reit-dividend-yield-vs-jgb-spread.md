# 主観内容の隔離 — J-REIT dividend yield vs JGB spread

> 出典エントリー: `real-estate-finance/j-reit-dividend-yield-vs-jgb-spread.md`

## Counterpoints

- Spread is a coarse metric; sector-mix shifts within the J-REIT index can move yield independent of fundamentals.
- 10Y JGB is not the only relevant risk-free; super-long JGB (30Y) is more relevant for some long-duration buyers (life insurers).
- Dividend yield uses recent / forward distributions; one-off special distributions distort the trailing number.
- Spread compression alone does not signal overvaluation; cap-rate-level support from underlying real-estate market matters more.
- Foreign-buyer flow can drive J-REIT price independent of spread reading.
- BoJ FSR commentary lags real-time spread reading by 1 - 2 quarters; near-real-time spread monitoring requires live MoF / BoJ data plus ARES J-REIT publications.
- Spread reading does not capture J-REIT leverage / refi risk at vehicle level; vehicle-level analysis still required for any allocation use.

## Open questions

- Will post-NIRP spread settle structurally below pre-NIRP band (because real-rent growth is higher) or above (because risk premium reset wider)?
- Does life-insurer post-NIRP ALM re-allocation to super-long JGB reduce J-REIT secondary-market support meaningfully?
- How does foreign-flow sensitivity to FX hedge cost shape spread floor through cycles?
- Will sub-sector spread bifurcation (logistics vs office vs hospitality) widen further as cap-rate compression unwinds unevenly?
- Does the J-REIT 90% distribution rule continue to support yield-based investor base or shift toward total-return reading?
- How does BoJ policy-rate-path uncertainty translate into spread-volatility regime for J-REIT secondary market?
