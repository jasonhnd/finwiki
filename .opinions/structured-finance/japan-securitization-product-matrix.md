# 主観内容の隔離 — Japan securitization product matrix

> 出典エントリー: `structured-finance/japan-securitization-product-matrix.md`

## Counterpoints

- The matrix groups products at a single sub-segment level; in practice each product class has internal sub-variants (e.g., auto-loan ABS splits into prime / sub-prime / used-car pools; CMBS splits into office / logistics / hotel / retail; project finance splits into solar / wind / biomass / geothermal).
- Issuance volume ordering is approximate; live JSDA / JHF statistics may show different rank ordering in specific years (especially when JPY appreciation moves cross-border CLO holdings in JPY-equivalent terms).
- Some products operate in hybrid regulatory zones (synthetic securitization especially) — the regulatory-home column reflects the dominant regime but multiple regimes can apply simultaneously.
- AAA-tranche labels reflect typical outcomes; specific deals may achieve only AA or A senior ratings depending on pool quality, credit enhancement depth, and structural protections.
- "Distributor" identification is for primary distribution; secondary-market trading is broader and includes life insurers, asset managers, regional banks, JPost, and sometimes retail investors via repackaged structures.
- The covered-bond category covers only the UCITS-compliant EUR / USD megabank programs; there is no domestic Japan covered-bond statutory regime equivalent to European covered-bond law, so the structure is contractually-engineered rather than statutory.
- Synthetic securitization in Japan is reported episodically in megabank Pillar 3 disclosures and is less publicly visible than US / EU synthetic SRT (significant risk transfer) deals; see [[structured-finance/synthetic-securitization-japan-bank-rwa-relief|synthetic securitization Japan bank RWA relief]] for the framework.
- The CLO row treats Japanese banks as **investors** in US / EU CLO; there is no domestic Japan-domiciled BSL-CLO issuance market in any meaningful scale. This is a deliberate Japan-market-shape feature, not an oversight.
- JOL / JOLCO (Japanese Operating Lease with Call Option) for aircraft are tax-leveraged investor structures rather than rated ABS; they appear in the aircraft / ship-finance row but operate on different mechanics than rated ABS.

## Open questions

- **Domestic CLO origination market**: does any structural development (large-scale Japan-domiciled leveraged-loan market emergence) ever support a domestic Japan-CLO origination market, or does Japan remain a CLO-buy-side participant only?
- **CMBS revival**: does the post-2020 revival of single-borrower CMBS (logistics, office) ever scale to anything approaching the 2006-2007 peak, or do J-REIT and private real-estate funds permanently displace CMBS as the structured real-estate-debt vehicle?
- **Synthetic securitization scale**: how much further does megabank synthetic-securitization activity scale in the 2026-2030 window as Basel III RWA optimization becomes more urgent under the finalized framework?
- **NPL cycle**: under what macro conditions does NPL securitization re-emerge at scale (post-rate-normalization stress, corporate-credit deterioration, post-zombie-firm shakeout)?
- **Covered-bond program scale**: do MUFG / SMBC scale their covered-bond programs as a stable foreign-currency funding source, or does the absence of a statutory regime cap the program size?
- **Cross-border CLO concentration risk**: does concentration of Japanese-bank CLO holdings (Norinchukin, JPost, regionals) ever trigger a regulatory-driven divestment cycle if US BSL CLO market stress emerges?
- **Project-finance ABS / green securitization**: does the renewable-energy project-finance pipeline shift from bank-loan to securitized-bond format, and what role do green-securitization labels play? See [[structured-finance/japan-green-securitization|Japan green securitization]].
- **Rating-agency competition**: do JCR / R&I face competitive pressure from S&P / Moody's / Fitch expanding domestic Japan structured-finance coverage, or does the JCR / R&I domestic franchise hold?
