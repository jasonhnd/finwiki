# 主観内容の隔離 — 公益財団 vs 一般財団 vs 一般社団 vs NPO法人 — Japan non-profit / public-interest legal form comparison

> 出典エントリー: `non-profit/japan-koeki-vs-general-zaidan-comparison.md`

## Counterpoints

- The "endowment vs membership" framing is clean but rigid; some 一般財団 foundations operate de facto member-driven, and some 一般社団 associations are essentially endowment-funded — the legal form does not always match the governance reality.
- 公益認定 is sometimes treated as a one-way ratchet, but it can be revoked, and 2018-2025 high-profile revocations of 公益財団 status (governance failures, misuse of assets) show the status carries enforcement risk that is sometimes underweighted at setup.
- The donor-side deduction advantage of 公益 status is materially smaller in Japan than in the US 501(c)(3) ecosystem because individual giving rates are lower; the case for going 公益 is sometimes weaker on pure fundraising math than founders assume.
- NPO is sometimes treated as the "easy" option, but the 4-month public-notice (公告) requirement and ongoing public-disclosure burden mean NPO is slower and less private than 一般 forms once running.

## Open questions
