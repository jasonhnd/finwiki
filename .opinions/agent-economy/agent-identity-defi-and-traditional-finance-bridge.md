## Counterpoints / open questions

- **KYA may never become a distinct regulatory term**: existing KYC + AML on the deployer may be sufficient. The 2026 indication: regulators are mostly avoiding new terminology; the operational requirement is unchanged.
- **Skyfire issuer model centralization risk**: a single Skyfire failure could disrupt 30%+ of card-rail agent commerce. Diversification is starting (Stripe Issuing, Marqeta agent-issuance products) but Skyfire has first-mover advantage.
- **On-chain identity + AAIF convergence**: if AAIF ratifies a standard for VC-based agent attestation, the on-chain patterns (Pattern 1-3) and the issuer pattern (Skyfire) may converge into a single "issue VC, present at point of transaction" pattern that regulators can supervise uniformly.
- **mDL + eIDAS + agent identity** depends on whether EU member states ship EUDI Wallets with agent-delegation features by their 2026 deadline. Many states are behind schedule.
- **GENIUS Act treatment of "non-natural-person stablecoin holders"** — the final rules may require explicit registration / disclosure of agent-controlled stablecoin balances. This is the most likely 2026-2027 regulatory hot spot.
- **Will WebAuthn extensions for agent delegation standardize?** FIDO Alliance working group is exploring; no formal proposal yet.
