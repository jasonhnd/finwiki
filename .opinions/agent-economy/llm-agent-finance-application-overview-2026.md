## Counterpoints / open questions

- **"LLMs don't really do anything novel in finance"**: partly true — most production deployments are ML / NLP problems that were already being solved with smaller models. LLMs widen the addressable feature set (unstructured documents, conversational interfaces) but don't yet move the regulator-permitted decision boundary.
- **The PILOT → PROD transition for trading and underwriting** depends on whether regulators accept LLM explainability artifacts (e.g. chain-of-thought traces, structured-output justifications) as equivalent to traditional model-card / SR 11-7 model documentation. The 2026 indications: not yet, but the FCA + MAS sandbox programs are testing this.
- **Vendor concentration risk**: FSB explicitly flags the concentration of finance AI on Anthropic + OpenAI + Google as a systemic concern. If one vendor goes down (or is sanctioned), 30%+ of the G-SIB AI tooling fails simultaneously. Diversification is starting (BBVA running both Claude and GPT internally) but slowly.
- **Domestic-vendor push**: Japan's METI AI initiative funds NEC cotomi, NTT tsuzumi, PFN PLaMo to reduce dependence on US AI. Mizuho / SMBC / MUFG have committed to evaluating domestic options. EU has Mistral / Aleph Alpha analogously. The economics rarely justify the switch yet (US frontier models 2-3x more capable per dollar), but the sovereignty argument is shipping policy.
