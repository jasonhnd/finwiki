# JASSO (Japan Student Services Organization)


## Wiki route

This entry sits under [[policy-finance/INDEX|policy-finance index]]. Read it with [[policy-finance/japan-policy-finance-system|Japan policy finance system]] for adjacent context and [[banking/INDEX|banking index]] for the broader system boundary.

## TL;DR

JASSO is Japan's student-support agency under MEXT. In FinWiki policy-finance terms, it is the education-finance node: scholarships, student loans, international-student support, and student-life support. It should not be merged into [[JapanFG/jfc|JFC]], but it sits adjacent to JFC because both touch household / education-related public finance.

## Role map

- **Institution**: 独立行政法人日本学生支援機構 / Japan Student Services Organization.
- **Established**: 2004-04-01.
- **Main businesses**: scholarship programs, international-student support, and student-life support.
- **Finance relevance**: grant and loan-type scholarship schemes, repayment management, and education-access policy.

## Strategic read

JASSO is policy finance through the household / human-capital channel. It does not support corporate investment like JBIC or JFC SME finance. Instead, it allocates public-credit and scholarship resources to students, which affects household leverage, education access, and labor-market human-capital formation.

The connection from [[JapanFG/jfc|JFC]] is useful because JFC also has education-loan adjacency, but JASSO is the dedicated student-services institution. In a policy-finance map, JASSO is a separate lane from SME, export, resource, or regional development finance.

## Counterpoints

- JASSO's loan programs are social-policy finance, not bank-style commercial credit.
- The risk profile depends on repayment support, income-contingent mechanisms, government support, and household-income conditions.
- Program rules change over time; scholarship details should be verified against current JASSO pages before operational use.
