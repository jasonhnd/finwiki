# Japan credit guarantee system


## Wiki route

This entry sits under [[policy-finance/INDEX|policy-finance index]]. Read it against [[policy-finance/national-federation-credit-guarantee-corporations|Japan Federation of Credit Guarantee Corporations]] for peer / contrast context and [[policy-finance/japan-policy-finance-system|Japan policy finance system]] for the broader system / regulatory boundary.

## TL;DR

Japan's credit guarantee system is a public-credit infrastructure for SME and small-business finance. Local credit guarantee corporations guarantee eligible SME borrowing from financial institutions, while the credit insurance layer is connected to [[JapanFG/jfc|Japan Finance Corporation]]. It is a system, not a single lender.

Use this page for the system mechanics; use [[policy-finance/national-federation-credit-guarantee-corporations]] for the national federation entity.

## 1. System boundary

| Layer | Role | FinWiki route |
|---|---|---|
| SME / small-business borrower | Applies for business funding through a financial institution and guarantee route. | System route here |
| Financial institution | Extends the loan and shares responsibility under the applicable scheme. | Bank / shinkin / credit-cooperative pages as relevant |
| Local credit guarantee corporation | Guarantees eligible SME obligations and handles subrogation / recovery when needed. | System route here; local long-tail pages only if strategic |
| Credit insurance layer | Reinsures / insures guarantee risk through public credit insurance tied to [[JapanFG/jfc|JFC]]. | [[JapanFG/jfc]] |
| National federation | Supports and coordinates the 51 local credit guarantee corporations. | [[policy-finance/national-federation-credit-guarantee-corporations]] |

## 2. Operating logic

- Credit guarantee corporations are public institutions created under the Credit Guarantee Corporation Act.
- They support SME and small-business funding by guaranteeing loans from banks and other financial institutions.
- The credit supplementation system combines local guarantees with public credit insurance.
- The responsibility-sharing system normally leaves a defined share of risk with financial institutions, with exceptions for specific policy schemes.
- Eligibility, guarantee limits, guarantee fees, and crisis-related exceptions vary by program.

## 3. Why it matters

- The system is a core hidden layer behind Japan SME lending.
- It affects regional banks, shinkin banks, credit cooperatives, JFC, and local governments.
- It is crucial for reading crisis credit support, startup support, business succession, and regional revitalization policy.
- It should not be counted as one company; it is a public-credit guarantee architecture.

## 4. Boundary cases

- **System vs federation**: this page explains the mechanism; the federation page explains the national coordinating body.
- **Guarantee vs loan**: the guarantee corporation generally supports borrower credit; the loan is still made by a financial institution.
- **SME vs agriculture / fisheries**: agriculture and fisheries use separate guarantee-insurance systems: [[policy-finance/agriculture-credit-guarantee-system]] and [[policy-finance/fisheries-credit-guarantee-system]].
