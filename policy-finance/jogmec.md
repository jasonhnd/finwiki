# JOGMEC (Japan Organization for Metals and Energy Security)

## TL;DR

JOGMEC is Japan's public agency for energy and mineral resource security. It combines oil / gas, metals, stockpiling, resource exploration, and newer energy-security functions, making it the resource-security counterpart to [[JapanFG/jbic|JBIC]] and [[policy-finance/nexi|NEXI]] in overseas project finance.

## Role map

- **Institution**: Japan Organization for Metals and Energy Security.
- **Established**: 2004-02-29.
- **Origins**: integrated functions of the former Japan National Oil Corporation and Metal Mining Agency of Japan.
- **Mission area**: stable supply of oil, natural gas, metals and mineral resources; mine pollution control; stockpiling; resource and energy security.
- **Scale snapshot**: official overview lists equity capital of 1,828 billion yen and 1,147 employees as of 2026-04-01.

## Strategic read

JOGMEC is not simply a research body. It is a policy-finance and risk-sharing node for projects that secure upstream resources. In resource-import-dependent Japan, energy and critical minerals projects often need a combined stack: JOGMEC for exploration / equity / guarantees / resource-security support, [[JapanFG/jbic|JBIC]] for project finance, [[policy-finance/nexi|NEXI]] for insurance, and trading companies or utilities as sponsors.

This makes JOGMEC important for economic-security analysis. Critical minerals, LNG, hydrogen, CCS, offshore wind geological surveys, and stockpiles are not just industrial topics; they are financing and state-capacity topics.

## Counterpoints

- JOGMEC support can de-risk strategic resources, but public risk may rise when projects are technically difficult or geopolitically exposed.
- Resource-security mandates can conflict with decarbonization priorities when fossil-fuel security remains part of the mission.
- Project-level economics require separate verification; the agency overview is only the institutional layer.

## Open questions

- Which JOGMEC-supported projects feed into JBIC / NEXI financing packages.
- How critical-mineral policy changes JOGMEC's allocation between hydrocarbons and metals.
- Whether hydrogen, CCS, and offshore wind functions become core or remain policy additions.
