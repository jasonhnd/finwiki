# Japan Housing Finance Agency (JHF)

## TL;DR

Japan Housing Finance Agency is Japan's housing-finance policy institution. It supports private housing finance through securitization support, housing loan insurance, and direct lending in areas where ordinary private finance is incomplete, including disaster recovery and housing-related public-policy lanes.

Use this page as the housing-credit lane in [[policy-finance/japan-policy-finance-system]], separate from SME guarantees and cooperative agricultural / fisheries credit.

## 2. Function map

| Function | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Securitization support | Connects housing loans originated by private financial institutions with capital-market funding. |
| Housing loan insurance | Supports private lenders' ability to extend eligible housing credit. |
| Direct lending | Fills policy gaps such as disaster recovery, housing safety, and areas where ordinary private finance is insufficient. |
| IR / MBS route | JHF MBS and bonds are public-credit market instruments, not ordinary bank deposits. |

## 3. Why it matters

- JHF is the missing housing-finance anchor in the public-credit map.
- It complements [[JapanFG/jfc|JFC]] and [[policy-finance/okinawa-development-finance-corp|ODFC]] but is not an SME or regional-development bank.
- Its securitization role makes it relevant to banks, insurers, asset managers, and public-credit investors.
- It should be mapped with policy finance, not with ordinary mortgage banks.

## 4. Boundary cases

- **Not a retail bank**: JHF supports housing finance and capital-market funding; it is not a deposit-taking bank.
- **Not the old Housing Loan Corporation page**: legacy history can be mentioned, but current routing should use JHF.
- **Not SME credit guarantee**: SME credit guarantees belong to [[policy-finance/japan-credit-guarantee-system]] and [[policy-finance/national-federation-credit-guarantee-corporations]].
