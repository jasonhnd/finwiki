## Mechanism / How it works

The decision logic of the independent route: **discern what the mainstream consortium covers + what it does not cover**. [[payment-firms/progmat|Progmat]] = trust-type + Japan's 3 megabanks + B2B large-lot settlement, with the **out-of-coverage area = the cross-border-compliance path** (Project Pax solves SWIFT compatibility but [[fintech/genius-act-501-denylist-mandate|§501 denylist]] does not). Kitao's judgment: the core of Japan's stablecoin value lies in cross-border compliance, not domestic B2B → non-participation in Progmat → pre-empt the JV with Circle and secure the cross-border path via exclusive USDC distribution in Japan (contrast: the strategically multi-front deployment in [[megabanks/sbi-hd|SBIホールディングス]]).

Diagnostic template: when a "mainstream consortium" emerges in an industry, identifying the independent-route opportunity requires judging the 3 points of **1) the value axis the consortium explicitly covers** (domestic B2B), **2) the value axis the consortium implicitly does not cover** (cross-border / retail / gray), and **3) whether that uncovered axis carries large value over the long term** (depending on the direction of global regulatory evolution). Once the 3 points align, the independent route reverses the consortium in 5-10 years. Kitao's case is the most vivid specimen in 2025-2026, and because he **publicly declared his refusal** (rather than silence), the motive of the independent route is verifiable.

## Origin & evolution

The starting point is Kitao's founding of SBI Holdings in 1999年 (a carve-out from SoftBank). The early Ripple investment (the largest Japanese backer of XRP) showed a preference for "global cross-border settlement rails." When Japan's 3 megabanks + Datachain advanced the Progmat consortium in 2023-2024年, Kitao **publicly refused to participate** (a clear show of cards, rare in the industry). In 2025-08 SBI Circle Holdings was established = exclusive USDC distribution in Japan + JPYSC + after Circle's IPO, SBI Holdings + Shinsei Bank jointly hold $50M in shares. In 2026-05 SBI additionally invested in the Arc private placement → a full-stack deployment of the cross-border-compliance path. Follow-on candidates: Korea's Shinhan (what if it does not join a BOK consortium?), Singapore's DBS (what if it does not join the MAS Partior consortium?).
