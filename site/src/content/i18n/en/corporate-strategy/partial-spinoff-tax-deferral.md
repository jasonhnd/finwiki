# パーシャルスピンオフ税制 — partial spin-off tax deferral

## Wiki route

This entry sits under [[corporate-strategy/INDEX|corporate-strategy index]] and [[INDEX|FinWiki index]]. Read it with [[corporate-strategy/japan-kaisha-bunkatsu-tax-regime|会社分割 tax regime]] for the legal-plumbing peer, [[JapanFG/sony-fg|Sony FG]] as the main public transaction example, and [[finance/INDEX|finance index]] for adjacent capital-markets context.

## TL;DR

The partial spin-off tax regime (パーシャルスピンオフ税制) is a system under which, even in a spin-off where the parent distributes subsidiary shares in kind to shareholders while retaining a portion of the subsidiary's shares, the transfer gains/losses tax at reorganization and the deemed-dividend tax for shareholders are deferred or excluded if certain requirements are met. In Japan it was created in the 2023年 fiscal-year tax reform, and the 2024年 fiscal-year tax reform revised the requirements and extended the application deadline. The re-listing / separation of [[JapanFG/sony-fg|sony-fg]] is the leading domestic example.

## 1. Regime Structure

| Item | Content |
|---|---|
| Scope | Share-distribution-type spin-off carving out a wholly-owned subsidiary |
| Feature | Parent can retain a partial stake even after the carve-out |
| Stake cap | Basic condition: parent holding ratio below 20% |
| Tax effect | Deferral of transfer gains/losses tax at reorganization; exclusion of deemed-dividend tax for shareholders |
| Policy purpose | Promotion of corporate-group business-portfolio reorganization |
| Related policy | Business-reorganization plan certification under the Industrial Competitiveness Enhancement Act |

METI's Q&A frames the partial spin-off tax regime as a system that, under certain requirements, also permits tax-qualified treatment for a "spin-off that leaves a partial stake with the former parent." However, the scope is spin-offs carving out a wholly-owned subsidiary; it cannot be used for any and all company splits / business transfers.

## 2. Typical transaction flow

| Step | Processing | Practical points |
|---|---|---|
| 1  | Organize the business to be carved out into a wholly-owned subsidiary | Transfer of business, assets, liabilities, personnel |
| 2  | Business-reorganization plan, listing preparation | METI certification, TSE review, audit |
| 3  | Parent distributes subsidiary shares in kind | Distributable source, Companies Act procedures |
| 4  | Parent continues to hold a partial stake | Below-20% requirement, brand/alliance retention |
| 5  | Subsidiary lists independently | Shareholders hold both parent shares and subsidiary shares |

## 3. Sony FG case

Sony Group published a dedicated IR page on the partial spin-off of its financial-services business, explaining the structure of distributing more than 80% of SFGI shares in kind to Sony shareholders. Shareholders will hold both Sony shares and SFGI shares based on a 2025-09-30 record point, and Sony retains a partial stake.

METI announced on 2024-02-14 that it had certified Sony Group's business-reorganization plan under the Industrial Competitiveness Enhancement Act. The stated purpose was for Sony Group and Sony Financial Group each to concentrate management resources on their core businesses and to enhance corporate value.

## 4. Strategic reading

- **Separation that is not a full sale**: because the parent can retain a partial stake, it is easier to preserve the brand, customer base, and collaboration relationships. As an option on the full-acquisition side, reading it against [[finance/japan-mbo-and-squeeze-out-process|MBO・squeeze-out のプロセス]] or [[finance/japan-tender-offer-process|公開買付（TOB）プロセス]] reveals its position on the continuum of separation methods.
- **Resolving the conglomerate discount**: when heterogeneous businesses such as finance, insurance, manufacturing, and IT sit within the same group, capital markets find it hard to value individual businesses. A spin-off raises valuation clarity.
- **Tax regime governs transaction feasibility**: once deemed-dividend tax or transfer gains/losses tax arises, the burden on both shareholders and the parent becomes heavy and feasibility drops. For comparison with cases where the acquirer arranges borrowing, see [[finance/japan-acquisition-finance|acquisition finance Japan]].
- **Listing preparation is heavy**: the company being carved out must, as a standalone listed company, put in place financial statements, internal controls, governance, IR, and minority-shareholder protection. The listing procedure itself proceeds along [[securities/japan-ipo-listing-disclosure-route|IPO listing・disclosure ルート]].

## 5. Regulation and policy

- The tax regime involves the Ministry of Finance and the NTA; the business-reorganization plan under the Industrial Competitiveness Enhancement Act involves METI; listing involves the Tokyo Stock Exchange; disclosure involves the FIEA and the Companies Act.
- The 2024年 fiscal-year tax reform explains the revision of the certification requirements of the partial spin-off tax regime and the extension of the application deadline.
- METI's spin-off guide was also revised in 2025年7月, reflecting the 令和7年 fiscal-year tax reform and the TSE listing-rule revisions.
