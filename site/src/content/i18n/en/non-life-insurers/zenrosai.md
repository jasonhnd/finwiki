# 全労済 / こくみん共済 coop (National Federation of Workers and Consumers Insurance Cooperatives)

## Wiki route

This entry sits under [[non-life-insurers/INDEX|non-life-insurers INDEX]] as a **workers' / consumer-cooperative-based mutual-aid (共済) federation** outside the JA / JF / shinkin / shinkumi axis. Read with [[non-life-insurers/ja-kyosairen|JA 共済連]] for the agricultural cooperative kyosai peer, [[non-life-insurers/kyoritsu-fire-marine|共栄火災海上保険]] for cooperative-system non-life adjacency, [[insurance/INDEX|insurance domain]] for the kyosai-vs-insurance regulatory boundary, and [[cooperative-banks/rokin-renraku|労働金庫連合会]] for the workers' cooperative finance peer in banking.

## TL;DR

**A national federation that carries out the consumer-cooperative-system "kyosai" (共済) business for workers and consumers.** Established 1957 , it is a **consumer-cooperative federation** based on the Consumers' Cooperative Act (Co-op Act) (the Co-op Act, not the Agricultural Cooperatives Act). Under the **"Kokumin Kyosai coop (formerly Kokumin Kyosai)"** brand, it provides life kyosai + non-life kyosai (fire, automobile, accident, etc.) to labor unions, workers, and local residents. **Members = regional workers' co-ops + workplace co-ops + prefectural workers' co-ops nationwide.** As a **cooperative-system insurance provider with over 1000 万 in policies in force / cooperative members**, it is a large-scale domestic mutual-aid organization on a par with JA Kyosairen. **Its jurisdiction is the Ministry of Health, Labour and Welfare** (the Co-op Act has jurisdiction; consumers' cooperatives are under MHLW jurisdiction), and its regulatory system differs from insurance companies subject to the Insurance Business Act. ^[extracted]

### Key chronology ^[extracted]

| Year | Event |
|---|---|
| 1948 | The Consumers' Cooperative Act (Co-op Act) was enacted |
| 1950 年s | **Workers' co-ops** were organized nationwide as one with the postwar labor movement |
| **1957** | **The National Federation of Workers and Consumers Kyosai Living Cooperatives (全労済) was established** |
| 1960〜70 s | Developed life kyosai / automobile kyosai under the "全労済" brand |
| 1980 年s | Expanded fire kyosai / housing (Kokumin Kyosai) |
| 2002 | The "Kokumin Kyosai" brand became the mainstay |
| 2010 年s | Digitization / expansion of web applications |
| **2019** | **Brand unified to "こくみん共済 coop"** (the common name of 全労済) ^[extracted] |

### Sales channels

- **Prefectural workers' co-ops** (47  prefectural organizations) — directly operated branches + agencies
- **Via labor unions** — close relationships with Rengo and each industry-specific labor union
- **Convenience-store collection / automatic transfer** — efficiency in premium collection

### Sense of scale (FY2024 年 settlement / end of 2025-05-31 period)

- **Number of members**: 58  members (47  prefectural workers' co-ops + 8  workplace workers' co-ops + 3  co-op federations)
- **Number of policies**: 2,893 万 policies (38.7 億 policy units)
- **Policies in force**: approximately 791 兆円
- **Total assets**: 4 兆 27 億円 (4,002,681 百万円)
- **Kyosai premiums received**: 5,299 億円 (529,928 百万円)
- **Kyosai benefits paid**: 3,175 億円 / **Ordinary revenue**: 6,461 億円

### "こくみん共済 coop" brand strategy

- **2019  brand unification**: the "全労済" name is the old name; externally it advertises and sells under **"こくみん共済 coop"** ^[extracted]
- "coop" = bringing to the fore its character as a consumers'-cooperative federation
- Capturing young people / urban workers + raising name recognition

### Main competitors

- **JA Kyosairen** ([[non-life-insurers/ja-kyosairen]]) — the largest same-type institution by kyosai scale
- **Prefectural citizens' kyosai (National Federation of Workers and Consumers Co-ops)** — another co-op-system kyosai federation
- **Life-insurance Big 4** ([[life-insurers/nippon-life|日本生命]] / [[life-insurers/dai-ichi-life|第一生命]] / [[life-insurers/meiji-yasuda|明治安田]] / [[life-insurers/sumitomo-life|住友生命]]) — competition in life-system products
- **The 3  major non-life insurers** ([[non-life-insurers/tokio-marine|東京海上]] / [[non-life-insurers/msad|MS&AD]] / [[non-life-insurers/sompo|SOMPO]]) — competition in automobile / fire
- **Online insurance** ([[life-insurers/lifenet|ライフネット生命]], etc.) — price competition with a direct-sales model

### Combination with the labor movement

- A close relationship with **Rengo (Japanese Trade Union Confederation)** — utilizing the via-labor-union route as a sales channel for kyosai
- A strong base in the **workplace field** such as public-employee labor unions / teachers' labor unions

### Digital strategy

- Web applications / a policy-management app

## 4. Supervision / regulation

- **Jurisdiction**: **Ministry of Health, Labour and Welfare** (jurisdiction over the Co-op Act) + FSA (cooperative supervision in financial soundness / consumer protection)
- **Legal basis**: Consumers' Cooperative Act (昭和 23 年 Act No. 第 200 号) + co-op kyosai regulation
- **Recent policy topics**:
  - **Kyosai = insurance regulatory gap** (competitive fairness between the Insurance Business Act vs the Co-op Act)
  - The direction of strengthening **co-op kyosai regulation** (MHLW / FSA cooperative supervision)
  - Digitization of tax deductions, etc. via **My Number linkage**
  - Voluntary disclosure of **ESR-like indicators**
