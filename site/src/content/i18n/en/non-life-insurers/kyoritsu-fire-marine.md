# Kyoei Fire & Marine Insurance

## Wiki route

This entry sits under [[non-life-insurers/INDEX|non-life-insurers INDEX]]. Read it against [[non-life-insurers/sompo|SOMPO ホールディングス (Sompo Holdings)]] for peer / contrast context and [[banking/INDEX|banking index]] for the broader system / regulatory boundary.

## TL;DR

Kyoei Fire & Marine is a mid-sized non-life insurer whose largest shareholder is JA Kyosairen (**74.20%**). It was founded through a wartime merger in 1942-07-01 (Daito Marine + Daifuku Marine), became a **mutual company in 1946 年, an unusual case in the non-life insurance industry**, and converted to a stock company in 2003-04 . It does not belong to the 3  major non-life groups ([[tokio-marine]] / [[msad]] / [[sompo]]), instead following an independent route in the **cooperative / cooperative-organization line**. It has relatively strong share in Hokkaido, Tohoku, Hokuriku, and Kyushu, and is positioned as a complement to areas JA Kyosai cannot cover, such as auto and fire insurance. From 2025-04  it began the new medium-term plan "**SHIFT: toward sustainable growth**." ^[extracted]

> Note: the original brief said "Mitsui Sumitomo Insurance about 14%" was a major shareholder, but public information (Wikipedia "Kyoei Fire & Marine Insurance" shareholder structure) does **not confirm MS&AD-line shareholding**. The largest shareholder is JA Kyosairen 74.20%, followed by Norinchukin Bank 6.63% and Shinkin Central Bank 6.08%, all cooperative-organization line entities. In 2001 年 it once decided to join the Millea Insurance Group (predecessor of current [[tokio-marine]] HD), but in 2002-08  it withdrew and chose conversion to a stock company with JA Kyosairen as the largest shareholder. The current structure is therefore **complete within the JA Kyosairen / cooperative-organization line**.

### Regional characteristics ^[extracted]

**Enrollment share is relatively high in Hokkaido, Tohoku, Hokuriku, and Kyushu**. This overlaps with regions where agricultural cooperative bases are strong, and the structure with JA Kyosairen as largest shareholder is reflected in the sales network.

#### GO TO THE NEXT STAGE (2022-2024 年) ^[extracted]
- "To become a company chosen more by customers"
- 2024 年 is the final year

#### **SHIFT: toward sustainable growth (2025 onward)** ★ ^[extracted]
- "SHIFT" means not mere change, but **moving the center of gravity** while adapting to environmental changes
- Priority themes:
  1. Strengthening **relationships of trust** with cooperative / cooperative-organization bodies and closely connected companies, dealers, and repair shops
  2. Strengthening Kyoei Pro Club and direct-sales employee channels
  3. Establishing a business structure that can sustainably fund growth investments such as **human-capital investment and system investment**
  4. Building an organization able to survive an era of high uncertainty

## 4. Regulation / policy

- **Supervisor**: Financial Services Agency (FSA)
- **Industry body**: General Insurance Association of Japan
- **Regulation**: Insurance Business Act, supervisory guideline for non-life insurance companies
- **Recent policy issues**:
  - Auto insurance premium rates (loss-ratio rise → rate-hike phase)
  - Natural disasters (typhoons, heavy rain, earthquakes) and reinsurance cost
  - Cooperative financial-system debate around the JA Kyosairen line
  - Spillover of the non-life insurance cartel issue (2023-2024  major 4 社) to mid-sized non-life insurers
