# Zurich Insurance Company (Zurich Japan)

## Wiki route

This entry sits under [[non-life-insurers/INDEX|non-life-insurers INDEX]]. Read it against [[life-insurers/axa-japan|アクサ・ジャパン (AXA Japan)]] for peer / contrast context and [[banking/INDEX|banking index]] for the broader system / regulatory boundary.

## TL;DR

The Japan base of Switzerland's Zurich Insurance Group. One of the pioneers among foreign-capital non-life insurers that entered Japan with the **1986 年 opening of its Japan branch**. In Japan it gained recognition through the 2  major products of "**overseas travel insurance**" and "**online auto insurance (direct type)**," and in 2010 年 it established **Zurich Life** as a separate legal entity, expanding its business domain into the online direct sale of medical insurance and cancer insurance. Holding no sales branches, its consistent feature is a **direct-sales model** centered on call center + web; among domestic direct-type non-life insurers it is a leading player alongside Sony Assurance of [[megabanks/sony-fg]], AXA Direct and SECOM General Insurance of [[life-insurers/axa-japan]], and SBI Insurance of [[megabanks/sbi-hd]].

## 1. Group structure

**Ultimate parent**: Zurich Insurance Group AG (Zurich, Switzerland; SIX Swiss Exchange: ZURN; SMI constituent)
**Principal entities in Japan**:
- **Zurich Insurance Company Japan Branch** (non-life insurance, Japan-branch form of a foreign insurance company)
- **Zurich Life Insurance Company Ltd.** (life insurance, Japanese legal entity)

### Principal entity structure

Zurich Insurance Group AG（スイス、SIX: ZURN）

### Key history (timeline)

| Year/month | Event |
|---|---|
| 1872 | **Zurich Insurance** founded in Switzerland (starting from marine insurance) |
| **1986** | **Zurich Japan Branch opened** (entry into Japan as a foreign-capital non-life insurer) |
| 1990 年s | Brand established with **overseas travel insurance** (reaching individual customers via travel agencies + telemarketing) |
| Early 2000 年s | **Entry into online auto insurance (direct type)** ─ forming the "direct non-life" segment together with Sony Assurance, AXA Direct, etc. |
| Around 2002  | Channel mix of telemarketing × web application established in overseas travel insurance |
| Late 2000 年s onward | Strengthened direct-type competitiveness through auto-insurance risk segmentation (mileage / age / region-based rating) |
| **2010** | **Zurich Life Insurance Company Ltd.** established (incorporated as a Japanese legal entity) from "**Zurich Life Insurance Company Limited Japan Branch**" ── full-scale entry into online direct sale of medical / cancer / income-protection products |
| 2010 年s | Product lineup expanded with relaxed-underwriting medical insurance (for the elderly / those with pre-existing conditions) |
| 2020 年s | Under the medium-term strategy of the Zurich Insurance Group (customer-centricity, digital transformation), Japan also advanced UX improvement and digitalization of policy administration |

### Positioning

- **"Not a single-leg direct-sales operation"**: Unlike the fully online-only [[life-insurers/lifenet]] (Lifenet Insurance), a hybrid type that also leverages the travel-agency channel in overseas travel insurance
- **Foreign-capital company combining life and non-life**: Whereas [[life-insurers/axa-japan]] has a 3 -entity structure of "life and non-life + online direct life" and also holds a sales-staff channel, Zurich **holds no face-to-face sales-staff channel and is consistent with a direct-sales model**

### Global leverage

- **Zurich Insurance Group's reinsurance platform**: Absorbs large contracts and natural-catastrophe risk through intra-group reinsurance
- **Global overseas-travel-insurance network**: Provides local assistance / medical-evacuation networks for Japanese travelers abroad via Zurich Assistance (a group function)

## 4. Regulation and policy

- **Supervisor**: Financial Services Agency (FSA).
  - **Zurich Insurance Company Japan Branch**: based on Article 185  of the Insurance Business Act (Japan-branch license of a foreign insurance business)
  - **Zurich Life Insurance Company Ltd.**: ordinary license as a life insurance company (Japanese legal entity) under the Insurance Business Act
- **Solvency**: Japan's solvency margin ratio regulation applies to the Japan branch and the Japanese life insurance company respectively. The response to the **introduction of economic-value-based solvency regulation (ESR) from 2025** is the same issue as for other life and non-life insurers
- **Recent policy issues**:
  - **Auto insurance's response to autonomous driving / advanced safety equipment**: redesign of the risk-rating system, the degree of telematics-insurance rollout
  - **Accountability of online direct life insurance**: compliance with disclosure obligations / solicitation regulations in the online application flow
