## TL;DR

**JA Kyosairen is the core mutual-aid insurer among JA Group's four national federations, spanning life-equivalent and non-life-equivalent protection.** Established in 1951, it is a **federation under the Agricultural Cooperative Act**. Its life-type in-force policy amount is approximately 200 trillion yen, and it also operates large automobile, building, and other non-life-type mutual-aid lines. It provides life, annuity, automobile, building, accident, and related mutual-aid products to members, including farmers and local residents, making it one of Japan's largest insurance-sector organizations. It is **not a stock-company insurer under the Insurance Business Act**; it is regulated as **mutual aid under the Agricultural Cooperative Act**, a separate legal regime. Although its scale is comparable to the [[insurance/japan-life-insurance-big-four|life insurance big four]] (Nippon Life / Dai-ichi Life / Meiji Yasuda / Sumitomo Life), it remains a cooperative organization outside the listed-equity model. It is also the **74.20% shareholder** of [[non-life-insurers/kyoritsu-fire-marine|Kyoei Fire and Marine Insurance]], making it the parent of a non-life insurer that conducts non-cooperative insurance business through Kyoei Fire. ^[extracted]

### Mutual aid vs insurance channel

- **Sales channel**: National JA 497  union + prefectural union + Zenkyoren direct management
- Mainly **face-to-face/door-to-door sales** to member households—similar to the “sales lady” type in the life insurance industry
- **Policy amount in force (total of comprehensive life insurance and building rehabilitation mutual aid) is 216 兆 9,652 億円** (as of FY2023 year-end) — A scale comparable to the big 4  in the life insurance industry

### Parent company functions for non-life insurance supplements

- **74.20% shareholder of [[non-life-insurers/kyoritsu-fire-marine|Kyoei Fire and Marine Insurance]]** ^[extracted]
- Kyoei Fire is a non-life insurance company compliant with the Insurance Business Act** = Non-life insurance sales to non-association members + Transactions with general corporations possible
- Kyoei Fire handles areas that cannot be handled by JA Kyosairen's own mutual aid (high-value cases such as marine insurance and liability)
- **Hybrid model between cooperative and non-life insurance company**

### One of the largest insurance company positions in Japan

- **Total asset size**: 58 兆 4,751 億円 (FY2023 year-end, year-on-year change 101.4%) — Scale comparable to [[life-insurers/nippon-life|Nippon Life]] (more than 90 兆円)
- However, because mutual aid is based on membership under the Agricultural Cooperative Act, it is not normally included in the size ranking based on the Insurance Business Act (regulatory reports are different).

### product competition

- **Building Rehabilitation Mutual Aid (Construction Rehabilitation)**: **Integrated product** of fire + earthquake + death protection = Unique strength of mutual aid, unprecedented in the private insurance industry
- **Agricultural cooperative brand/community-based approach** is directly linked to premium (insurance premium) competitiveness

### competitive structure

- **Life insurance big 4**: [[life-insurers/nippon-life|Nippon Life]] / [[life-insurers/dai-ichi-life|Dai-ichi Life]] / [[life-insurers/meiji-yasuda|Meiji Yasuda Life]] / [[life-insurers/sumitomo-life|Sumitomo Life]] — Competing with the same type of life products

- **Inclusion of customers within JA** is the fundamental differentiation from other financial institutions (close contact with members + JA Bank account linkage)

- **Supervisor**: **Ministry of Agriculture, Forestry and Fisheries** (supervision of cooperatives) + **Financial Services Agency** (cooperative supervision of financial soundness and consumer protection)
- **Legal basis**: Agricultural Cooperative Act (mutual aid business provisions) + mutual aid business guidelines
