# The Bank of Iwate, Ltd. (株式会社岩手銀行)

## TL;DR

The Bank of Iwate is a first-tier regional bank headquartered in Morioka, Iwate Prefecture. In northern Tohoku, placed alongside [[regional-banks/fidea-hd|フィデア HD]], [[regional-banks/proclair-hd|プロクレア HD]], and [[regional-banks/77-bank|七十七銀行]], it serves as a reference point for observing regional finance in a population-declining area. Its core is deposits within Iwate Prefecture, corporate transactions, earthquake-recovery finance, and regional-industry support.

## 3. Strategy / competition

- **Core regional bank of Iwate Prefecture**: Centered on Morioka, it covers the entire prefecture and serves as the principal receptacle for intra-prefecture deposits and loans.
- **Medium-term plan**: The 21th medium-term management plan runs from 2023-04  to 2026-03 . Over the long term it targets consolidated net income of 100億円 and ROE of 5%, and sets as its 2025年 fiscal-year targets consolidated net income of 70億円 and ROE of 4% or more.
- **Tohoku-wide comparison**: By comparing with Miyagi's [[regional-banks/77-bank|77-bank]], Aomori's [[regional-banks/proclair-hd|proclair-hd]], and Akita/Yamagata's [[regional-banks/fidea-hd|fidea-hd]], one can see the difference between a standalone bank and an integrated holding company.
- **Distinctiveness of recovery finance**: Post-Great East Japan Earthquake recovery, disaster prevention, and coastal-area regeneration remain a distinctive theme in the Bank of Iwate's regional-finance story.

## 4. Regulation / policy

- A bank under the Banking Act, supervised by the FSA / Local Finance Bureau.
- As a listed bank, subject to the Companies Act, the FIEA, and TSE disclosure.
- In regional financial administration, business succession, regional decarbonization, disaster recovery, and regional revitalization are key themes.
