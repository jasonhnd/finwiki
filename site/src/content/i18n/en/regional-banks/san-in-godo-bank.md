# San-in Godo Bank

## Wiki route

This entry sits under [[JapanFG/INDEX|JapanFG index]]. Read it against [[regional-banks/tottori-bank|鳥取銀行]] for peer / contrast context and [[banking/INDEX|banking index]] for the broader system / regulatory boundary.

## TL;DR

San-in Godo Bank is the only major listed regional-bank name centered on the San-in region of Shimane and Tottori. It was created through wartime consolidation in 1941-07-01, combining Matsue Bank, Yonago Bank, and related predecessors in 7 行. The bank is commonly called **Gogin**. It is headquartered in Matsue, Shimane Prefecture, remains a standalone bank rather than a holding company, and is listed on TSE Prime under securities code 8381. It holds a high share in the two San-in prefectures, with shares around 4  and above 5  in key local markets. It also has branches outside the home region in Hiroshima, Okayama, Hyogo, and Osaka. Its regional economy combines tourism, agriculture and fisheries, manufacturing, and energy-related businesses, including Shimane nuclear-power adjacency, into a 1 行 regional structure. Local competition includes [[shimane-bank|島根銀行]], while outside the region it meets [[chugin-okayama]], [[hirogin-hd]], and [[yamaguchi-fg]]. ^[extracted]

### Merger history / predecessors

- 1922-04-01: **Matsue Bank** established.
- 1925 : **Yonago Bank** established.
- 1941-07-01: under wartime regional-bank consolidation policy, Matsue Bank, Yonago Bank, and the Matsue branch of Daisan Bank are combined through a **7 行 merger**, creating San-in Godo Bank.
- 1949 : listed on the Tokyo Stock Exchange, an early postwar listing after market reopening.

### Geographic strategy

- **San-in concentration**: strong share in Shimane and Tottori; local main-bank position around 40  and above 50% in key markets.
- **Outside-region footprint**: branches in Hiroshima, Okayama, Kobe, and Osaka support both San-in companies' expansion and inbound business to San-in.
- **International**: Asia offices such as Shanghai support trade finance.
- **Tourism dependence**: Izumo Taisha with annual visitors around 800 万, Tottori Sand Dunes, Matsue Castle, Tamatsukuri Onsen, Kaike Onsen, and the Oki Islands are pillars of the regional economy.

### Partnership / acquisition strategy

- **BlackRock asset-management alliance** (2009): partnership with the largest US asset manager to strengthen investment-trust lineup, an early overseas-major alliance case among regional banks. ^[extracted]
- **Local government, JA, and JF cooperation**: Shimane and Tottori prefectures, municipalities, agricultural cooperatives, and fisheries cooperatives form the local finance ecosystem.

### B2C branding

- **"Gogin" nickname**: deeply rooted in Shimane and Tottori; the bank name is synonymous with the local bank.
- **CSR / regional contribution**: sponsors local events around Izumo Taisha, Matsue Castle, festivals, scholarships, and cultural-property protection.
