## TL;DR

Daiwa Next Bank is the bank operating company of [[securities-firms/daiwa-sg|大和証券グループ]] (net bank / other bank), holding a bank license under the Banking Act and listed in the FSA's bank license list. It is a deposit / foreign-currency / asset-formation support bank premised on linkage with Daiwa Securities accounts. On 2026-04-27 its parent (Daiwa Securities Group Inc.) announced that it had taken [[trust-banks/orix-bank|オリックス銀行]] as a wholly owned subsidiary for approximately 3,700 億円 and that it plans a future merger of the two banks, so its importance in the expansion of the securities group's banking functions is increasing.

## 2. Business role

- As a net bank linked with Daiwa Securities accounts, it provides yen deposits, foreign-currency deposits, fund transfers, and asset-formation support.
- Because it connects with the nationwide network of Daiwa Securities branches and sales staff, it is a hybrid securities-bank model distinct from a fully online-only bank.
- If the subsidiarization and future merger of Orix Bank proceed, the expansion of the real-estate-loan and deposit base will become the focus (on a disclosed basis, the two banks combined aim for a substantial expansion of total-asset scale).
- It can be read as a "net bank originating from a business group" type, alongside the existing net bank [[regional-banks/sony-bank|ソニー銀行]].

## 3. Regulatory & system positioning

- **Supervision**: Supervised by the FSA ([[financial-regulators/fsa|FSA]]) under the Banking Act. It is a net bank belonging not to the Regional Banks Association or the Second Association of Regional Banks but to the "other banks" category in the FSA classification. Its soundness is disciplined by capital-adequacy-ratio regulation and the like.
- **Industry / system**: It participates in the nationwide bank fund-settlement network (Zengin System), and as for an industry body it connects to the framework of [[financial-regulators/zenginkyo|全国銀行協会（全銀協）]].
- **Depositor protection**: Deposits are covered by the Deposit Insurance Corporation (DIC).
