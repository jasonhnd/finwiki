# Juroku Financial Group

## Wiki route

This entry sits under [[regional-banks/INDEX|regional-banks INDEX]]. Read it against [[regional-banks/fukuoka-fg|ふくおか FG (FFG)]] for peer / contrast context and [[banking/INDEX|banking index]] for the broader system / regulatory boundary.

## TL;DR

Juroku FG is a regional-bank financial group based in Gifu Prefecture. It was established by **turning Juroku Bank into a holding-company structure through a sole share transfer in 2021-10-01**. Its core banking subsidiary is **Juroku Bank, founded as the Sixteenth National Bank in 1877-10 **, and it has long maintained the No. 1  share in Gifu Prefecture. The name "Juroku" comes from the Meiji-era national-bank numbering system (Sixteenth National Bank), making it one of the few surviving megabanks / regional banks that still retains a "number bank" name. It sits on the **northern edge** of the Chukyo economic area and shares Gifu Prefecture with [[ogaki-kyoritsu-bank]] (Ogaki Kyoritsu Bank), forming a two-strong-bank structure in the prefecture. As a single regional bank's move into an HD structure, the launch came relatively late nationally (2021). ^[extracted]

### Former Juroku Bank lineage

- **1877-10**: **Sixteenth National Bank** established in Gifu, the 16 th bank under the Meiji-era National Bank Act numbering system
- **1896**: incorporated as a stock company during the transition from national bank to ordinary bank
- **1898**: renamed **Juroku Bank** as part of the ordinary-bank transition
- **1949**: listed on the Tokyo Stock Exchange as part of the postwar regional-bank listing wave

### Holding-company transition

- **2021-10-01**: **Juroku FG established** — Juroku Bank shifted to a joint holding-company structure by sole share transfer ^[extracted]
  - A sole holding-company transition came **comparatively late** in the flow of regional-bank reorganization (megabanks and major regional banks completed holding-company transitions during the 2000 年s to 2010 年s)
- **2024**: Medium-term management plan formulated (details should be checked in Juroku FG official IR materials)

### Surrounding relationships

- Relationship with former **Gifu Bank** (separate legal entity with a history of financial distress) — **the institution rescued and absorbed by Juroku Bank**
  - After Gifu Bank fell into difficulty in the 1990 年s and became a close bank of former Tokai Bank, it requested capital support from Juroku Bank in 2009-02 . Juroku Bank underwrote 25 億円 of preferred shares
  - **2010-12-22**: Juroku Bank acquired all Gifu Bank shares with cooperation from Bank of Tokyo-Mitsubishi UFJ and made it a **wholly owned subsidiary**
  - **2012-09-18**: Juroku Bank **absorbed and merged Gifu Bank** with approval under Article 30  of the Banking Act; Gifu Bank disappeared. The Gifu regional-bank structure consolidated into the two-bank setup of "Juroku vs Ogaki Kyoritsu"

### Geographic position

- **All of Gifu Prefecture**: branch network in major cities such as Gifu, Ogaki, Tajimi, and Takayama
- **Expansion into western Aichi**: operating-base expansion into Ichinomiya, Bisai, and the northern edge of the Nagoya area
- **Northern edge of the Chukyo economic area**: positioned on the periphery of the Nagoya-centered Chukyo area, competing and dividing territory with Aichi regional-bank players (former Tokai Bank line [[mufg]] + Bank of Nagoya + Aichi Bank)

### Competition in the Chukyo economic area

- Competition in western Aichi with **Bank of Nagoya / Aichi Bank** ([[regional-banks/aichi-fg|aichi-bank]] and others)

### Industrial base

- **Manufacturing**: auto parts (peripheral suppliers in the Chukyo auto-industry area), electronic components, textiles, ceramics (Mino ware)
- **Tourism**: Takayama's old town, Gero Onsen, Shirakawa-go World Heritage site, Nagaragawa cormorant fishing, and others
