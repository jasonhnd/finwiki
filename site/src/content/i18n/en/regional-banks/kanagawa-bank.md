# The Kanagawa Bank

## TL;DR

The Kanagawa Bank, Ltd. (株式会社神奈川銀行) is a second regional bank headquartered in Yokohama, Kanagawa Prefecture, that holds a banking license under the Banking Act and is listed on the FSA's bank license list. In Kanagawa Prefecture, the first regional bank [[regional-banks/yokohama-bank|横浜銀行]] is the large one, but the Kanagawa Bank is treated as a separate-entity licensed second-regional-bank operating company. The substance of its business is the community-rooted second-regional-bank model of taking deposits and lending to SMEs and individuals in Yokohama and within the prefecture.

## 2. Operating model

The Kanagawa Bank is a second regional bank that, centered on Yokohama, handles local deposits, SME lending, personal loans, and community finance. On the in-prefecture market map, compared with the first regional bank [[regional-banks/yokohama-bank|横浜銀行]] (under [[regional-banks/concordia-fg|コンコルディアFG]]), it reads as a smaller, more community-rooted bank.

## 3. Regulatory & system positioning

- **Supervision**: Under the Banking Act, supervised by the FSA ([[financial-regulators/fsa|FSA]]) and the Kanto Local Finance Bureau. Its soundness is disciplined by capital adequacy ratio regulation (domestic standard) and the like.
- **Industry body**: A second regional bank belonging to the Second Association of Regional Banks (第二地銀協). It participates in the Zengin Data Telecommunication System (全銀システム) and, as an industry body, connects to the framework of [[financial-regulators/zenginkyo|全国銀行協会（全銀協）]].
- **Depositor protection**: Deposits are covered by the Deposit Insurance Corporation of Japan (DIC).
- **Consolidation context**: For the general context of second-regional-bank realignment and consolidation pressure in Kanto, see [[banking/regional-bank-consolidation-pattern|regional-bank consolidation pattern]].
