## TL;DR

The Bank of Nagoya, Ltd. (株式会社名古屋銀行) is a second regional bank headquartered in Nagoya, Aichi Prefecture, that holds a banking license under the Banking Act and is listed on the FSA's bank license list. In the Chukyo area, it is given a standalone operating-company page as a major local-bank peer alongside [[regional-banks/aichi-bank|あいち銀行]] ([[regional-banks/aichi-fg|あいちフィナンシャルグループ]]). The substance of its business is the standard second-regional-bank model of taking deposits and lending to SMEs and individuals within the prefecture and the Nagoya area.

## 2. Operating model

The Bank of Nagoya is a second regional bank that handles retail deposits, SME finance, corporate relationships for manufacturing and service industries, and local consulting in Aichi Prefecture and the Nagoya area. It is a local-bank peer of the in-prefecture second-regional [[regional-banks/aichi-bank|あいち銀行]], and it has Chukyo economic-area adjacency with the first regional banks [[regional-banks/juroku-bank|十六銀行]] and [[regional-banks/ogaki-kyoritsu-bank|大垣共立銀行]].

## 3. Regulatory & system positioning

- **Supervision**: Under the Banking Act, supervised by the FSA ([[financial-regulators/fsa|FSA]]) and the Tokai Local Finance Bureau. Its soundness is disciplined by capital adequacy ratio regulation (domestic standard) and the like.
- **Industry body**: A second regional bank belonging to the Second Association of Regional Banks (第二地銀協). It participates in the Zengin Data Telecommunication System (全銀システム) and, as an industry body, connects to the framework of [[financial-regulators/zenginkyo|全国銀行協会（全銀協）]].
- **Depositor protection**: Deposits are covered by the Deposit Insurance Corporation of Japan (DIC).
- **Consolidation context**: For the general context of regional-bank and second-regional-bank realignment in the Chukyo area, see [[banking/regional-bank-consolidation-pattern|regional-bank consolidation pattern]].
