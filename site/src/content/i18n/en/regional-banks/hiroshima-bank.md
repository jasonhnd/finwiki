## TL;DR

The Hiroshima Bank, Ltd. (株式会社広島銀行) is a regional bank (first-tier regional bank / 第一地銀) headquartered in Hiroshima City, Hiroshima Prefecture, holding a banking licence under the Banking Act and listed on the FSA bank-licence list. It is the core operating bank of [[regional-banks/hirogin-hd|ひろぎんホールディングス]] and a regional-bank operating-company anchor representing the Chugoku region. Its business follows the standard regional-bank model, centred on deposit-taking in the Setouchi area and lending to SMEs, individuals, and local governments.

## 2. Operating model

The Hiroshima Bank is a regional bank handling retail deposits, SME finance, Setouchi-area corporate banking, automotive and related supply-chain transactions, lending to local governments, and regional consulting in Hiroshima Prefecture. It is a cross-prefecture first-tier regional bank extending its operating base across the Setouchi area of Hiroshima, Okayama, Yamaguchi, and Ehime. The [[regional-banks/hirogin-hd|ひろぎんホールディングス]] page covers holding-company strategy and group companies (securities, leasing, IT), while this page covers the bank-licence operating boundary.

## 3. Regulatory & system positioning

- **Supervision**: Under the Banking Act, supervised by the FSA ([[financial-regulators/fsa|FSA]]) and the Chugoku Local Finance Bureau. Soundness is disciplined through capital-adequacy ratio regulation (domestic standard), etc.
- **Industry body**: A first-tier regional bank belonging to the Regional Banks Association of Japan (Chiginkyo). It participates in the Zengin System (the nationwide bank fund-settlement network) and, as an industry body, connects to the [[financial-regulators/zenginkyo|全国銀行協会（全銀協）]] framework.
- **Depositor protection**: Deposits are covered by the Deposit Insurance Corporation of Japan (DIC).
- **Consolidation context**: For the general context of regional-bank reorganisation / consolidation pressure in the Chugoku region, see [[banking/regional-bank-consolidation-pattern|regional-bank consolidation pattern]].
