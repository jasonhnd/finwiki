# Chiba Bank

## Wiki route

This entry sits under [[regional-banks/INDEX|regional-banks INDEX]]. Read it against [[regional-banks/gunma-bank|群馬銀行 (The Gunma Bank)]] for peer / contrast context and [[banking/INDEX|banking index]] for the broader system / regulatory boundary.

## TL;DR

A top-tier standalone regional bank rooted in Chiba Prefecture (consolidated deposits **16.3 兆円 scale** / consolidated total assets **approx. 21.6 兆円** (FY2025-03 ) / Chiba Prefecture share No.1). Founded in 1943-03  through the merger of Chiba Godo Bank, Omigawa Noshominato Bank, and the 98th Bank, renamed Chiba Bank the same month. Listed on the TSE in 1949 . A **standalone bank that has not adopted a holding-company structure**, securing management flexibility while remaining important at a scale comparable to regional bank FGs (such as [[regional-banks/concordia-fg]] / [[regional-banks/fukuoka-fg]] / [[regional-banks/hokuhoku-fg]]). Through **2015  lead establishment of the TSUBASA Alliance** ★, it gained economies of scale while avoiding holding-company formation via 11 行 collaboration (system co-development, overseas support). Full-scale overseas strategy launched with the opening of the Singapore branch in 2023-12 . ^[extracted]

### Predecessors / consolidation history

- **1943-03-31** Chiba Godo Bank, Omigawa Noshominato Bank, and the 98th Bank merged to newly incorporate **Chiba Bank** (wartime consolidation・intra-Chiba-Prefecture regional bank consolidation)
- 1944  Absorbed Chiba Savings Bank and acquired the business of Noda Shoyu Bank
- **1949** Listed on the Tokyo Stock Exchange
- Postwar high-growth period to the 80 s: established operating base in Chiba Prefecture and the eastern Tokyo metropolitan area
- Bubble period: non-performing loan disposal・branch-network optimization

### TSUBASA Alliance lead strategy ★

**Established 2015 **, a 11 行 alliance led by Chiba Bank (an alternative model that **gains economies of scale while avoiding regional-bank holding-company formation**):

| Bank | Base |
|---|---|
| **Chiba Bank** (lead) | Chiba Prefecture |
| Hokuyo Bank | Hokkaido |
| Musashino Bank | Saitama Prefecture |
| Shiga Bank | Shiga Prefecture |
| Daishi Hokuetsu Bank | Niigata Prefecture (under [[regional-banks/dai-shi-hokuetsu-fg|hokuetsu-fg]]) |
| Chugoku Bank | Okayama Prefecture |
| Iyo Bank | Ehime Prefecture |
| Toho Bank | Fukushima Prefecture |
| Hokkoku Bank | Ishikawa Prefecture |
| Kyoto Bank | Kyoto Prefecture |
| Gunma Bank | Gunma Prefecture |

**Strategic significance of TSUBASA**:
- **System co-development**: cost reduction through co-development of core and peripheral systems
- **Overseas support**: mutual use of member banks' local bases (Singapore, etc.)
- **Joint product development**: joint products such as investment trusts and insurance

### Non-holding-company strategy
