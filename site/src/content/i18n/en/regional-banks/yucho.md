## TL;DR

Japan Post Bank is a commercial bank with Japan's largest-scale retail deposit base, around the 190 兆円 level, with deposits of 190.5 兆円 as of 2025-03-31. It opened at the same time as postal privatization on 2007-10-01 and uses roughly 24,000 局 post-office outlets nationwide, operated under agency arrangements with Japan Post, as its retail base. The loan book is limited, so most investable assets have historically depended on securities investment, centered on JGBs, but recent strategy emphasizes diversification into foreign bonds and alternative / private funds in the 2025 年 public-material snapshot. The parent is Japan Post Holdings; voting-right ownership is gradually sold down and was **50.04% at 2025-03-31**, with a legal target of 50% or less. Japan Post Holdings, Japan Post Bank, and Japan Post Insurance conducted a **3 社-company simultaneous IPO on 2015-11-04**. ^[extracted]

### Digital strategy

- Japan Post Bank passbook app: the core channel for digitalizing passbooks and transaction history. ^[extracted]
- Key theme is reducing dependence on post-office windows and strengthening channels outside physical branches.

### Partnership / acquisition strategy

- **Business consignment to Japan Post**: Japan Post handles cash and counter operations at about 24,000 局 outlets nationwide, forming the backbone of the privatization scheme.
- **Sales-channel integration with Japan Post Insurance**: life-insurance sales through the same post-office network, with governance strengthened after the Japan Post Insurance improper-sales issue. 2019 年
- Individual partnerships with card, asset-management, and fintech companies.

### B2C branding

- **"Yucho" brand**: brand recognition built since postal savings; roughly from 1875 onward and at 150 -year scale.
- **Nationwide 24,000 局 network**: an unusually dense retail base that covers urban and rural areas.
- Deep access to local and older customers, including areas not covered by megabanks.

## 4. Controls / policy

- **Supervision**: FSA, Ministry of Internal Affairs and Communications, and Japan Post privatization framework.
- **Banking Act**: ordinary banking license.
- **Postal privatization law**: staged sale of Japan Post Holdings' ownership and limits on business expansion.
- **Private-sector complementarity principle**: business-scope constraints intended to avoid crowding out private financial institutions.
- **Deposit insurance**: member institution after privatization; ordinary deposits are protected under the deposit insurance system. 2007-10 

### Immediate policy issues

- Further government / Japan Post Holdings share sale and path toward privatization completion.
- Boundary of private-sector complementarity when deposit services expand.
- 2024: BOJ policy-rate normalization and both valuation losses on JGB holdings and improvement in investment yield.
- Governance strengthening after Japan Post Insurance sales problems.
