## TL;DR

The Nishi-Nippon City Bank, Ltd. (NCB; 株式会社西日本シティ銀行) is a regional bank (first-tier regional bank / 第一地銀) headquartered in Fukuoka City, Fukuoka Prefecture, holding a banking licence under the Banking Act and listed on the FSA bank-licence list. It is the core operating bank of [[regional-banks/nishi-nippon-fhd|西日本フィナンシャルホールディングス]] (NNFH) and, within Fukuoka Prefecture, is treated as a major regional-bank operating-company anchor alongside [[regional-banks/fukuoka-bank|福岡銀行]] ([[regional-banks/fukuoka-fg|ふくおかFG]]). Its business consists of deposit-taking in the Fukuoka metropolitan area and northern Kyushu and lending to SMEs, corporates, and individuals.

## 2. Operating model

The Nishi-Nippon City Bank is a first-tier regional bank handling retail deposits, SME finance, urban-area corporate transactions, and Asian business support, centred on the Fukuoka metropolitan area and northern Kyushu. The [[regional-banks/nishi-nippon-fhd|NNFH]] page covers holding-company strategy and group portfolio (including [[regional-banks/nagasaki-bank|長崎銀行]], etc.), while this page covers the bank-licence entity. Within Fukuoka Prefecture it holds a peer boundary with [[regional-banks/fukuoka-bank|福岡銀行]], [[regional-banks/kitakyushu-bank|北九州銀行]], and [[regional-banks/chikuho-bank|筑邦銀行]].

## 3. Regulatory & system positioning

- **Supervision**: Under the Banking Act, supervised by the FSA ([[financial-regulators/fsa|FSA]]) and the Kyushu Local Finance Bureau. Soundness is disciplined through capital-adequacy ratio regulation (domestic standard), etc.
- **Industry body**: A first-tier regional bank belonging to the Regional Banks Association of Japan (Chiginkyo). It participates in the Zengin System (the nationwide bank fund-settlement network) and, as an industry body, connects to the [[financial-regulators/zenginkyo|全国銀行協会（全銀協）]] framework.
- **Depositor protection**: Deposits are covered by the Deposit Insurance Corporation of Japan (DIC).
- **Consolidation context**: For the general context of regional-bank reorganisation in Fukuoka / Kyushu (the moves of NNFH / [[regional-banks/fukuoka-fg|ふくおかFG]]), see [[banking/regional-bank-consolidation-pattern|regional-bank consolidation pattern]].
