# Tomony Holdings

## Wiki route

This entry sits under [[JapanFG/INDEX|JapanFG index]]. Read it against [[regional-banks/fukuoka-fg|ふくおか FG (FFG)]] for peer / contrast context and [[banking/INDEX|banking index]] for the broader system / regulatory boundary.

## TL;DR

Tomony Holdings is a regional-bank FG with a cross-footprint across Shikoku (Tokushima and Kagawa) and Osaka (the former Taisho Bank lineage). It was launched in 2010-04-01 through the **management integration of Tokushima Bank and Kagawa Bank**, made 2017 Taisho Bank (former Hokuto Shoji lineage, Osaka) a rescue subsidiary, and in 2020-01  merged Tokushima and Taisho to create **[[tokushima-taisho-bank|徳島大正銀行]]**. Its current main subsidiaries are [[tokushima-taisho-bank|徳島大正銀行]] and [[kagawa-bank|香川銀行]], a 2 行-bank structure. ^[extracted]

### Merger history and predecessors ^[extracted]

- Former **Tokushima Bank**: founded in 1944  through wartime integration of former Tokushima Godo Bank and related institutions
- Former **Kagawa Mujin**: founded in 1943  -> converted to mutual bank in 1951  -> **converted to ordinary bank in 1989  and became Kagawa Bank**
- Former **Taisho Bank**: founded in 1922  (Osaka footprint) -> former Hokuto Shoji lineage -> management trouble
- **Tomony HD**: launched in 2010-04-01 through joint share transfer by Tokushima Bank and Kagawa Bank
- **Taisho Bank subsidiary acquisition**: 2017  rescue-type M&A, acquiring an Osaka footprint
- **Tokushima Taisho Bank**: born through Tokushima Bank + Taisho Bank merger in 2020-01

### Cross-footprint strategy

- Wide-area expansion combining **Shikoku (Tokushima + Kagawa)** with **Osaka (former Taisho Bank branch network)**, unlike other Shikoku regional banks
- Awa Odori economy (Tokushima) + udon economy (Kagawa) + Kansai economic-zone access (Osaka via Taisho Bank lineage)
- Tourism finance for the Shikoku pilgrimage plus manufacturing finance for Setouchi shipbuilding and Shikoku paper
- Agricultural finance for local products such as mikan, udon flour, and sweet potatoes

### Partnership and acquisition strategy

- **Tokushima + Kagawa management integration** (2010-04-01): equal-merger-style HD formation by adjacent Shikoku 2  prefectural regional banks; similar cases include Yamaguchi FG and Nishi-Nippon FH
- **Taisho Bank rescue subsidiary acquisition** (2017): strategic M&A to acquire an Osaka footprint by absorbing troubled Taisho Bank
- **Tokushima Taisho Bank merger** (2020-01): cost reduction and Osaka + Tokushima branch-network integration by moving from 2 行 to a 1 行 structure inside the HD

### Survival strategy in depopulating regions

- Shikoku's 4  prefectures have among Japan's higher population-decline rates, so the group supplements them with access to the Kansai economic zone through the Taisho Bank lineage
- A niche between megabanks and shinkin banks as the local SME main bank
- Focus on local-industry finance for tourism, agriculture, and manufacturing

### Position in regional-bank consolidation

- Shikoku regional banks: Tomony HD ([[tokushima-taisho-bank|徳島大正]] + [[kagawa-bank|香川]]) / [[iyogin-hd]] ([[iyo-bank|伊予銀行]]) / [[awa-bank|阿波銀行]] (Tokushima) / [[hyakujushi-bank|百十四銀行]] (Kagawa) / [[shikoku-bank|四国銀行]] (Kochi), etc.
- In Tokushima Prefecture: [[tokushima-taisho-bank|徳島大正銀行]] vs [[awa-bank|阿波銀行]] in a 2 行-bank structure
- In Kagawa Prefecture: [[kagawa-bank|香川銀行]] vs [[hyakujushi-bank|百十四銀行]] in a 2 行-bank structure
- In Osaka Prefecture: [[tokushima-taisho-bank|徳島大正銀行]] (former Taisho Bank branches) vs megabanks + [[smfg]] lineage + Kansai Mirai FG, etc.
