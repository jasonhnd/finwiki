## TL;DR

San ju San Bank, Ltd. is a first-tier regional bank headquartered in Yokkaichi, Mie Prefecture, and is the 100% subsidiary / single bank operating subsidiary of [[regional-banks/san-jusan-fg|三十三 FG]] (TSE PRIME 7322). It was created through the **2021-05-01 merger of the former Mie Bank and the former Daisan Bank**. The name "San ju San" is a double meaning that takes the "three" from Mie / Mie Prefecture and Daisan / "third" (English: San ju San = 33). In-prefecture share No.1  is held by [[regional-banks/hyakugo-bank|百五銀行]]; this bank sits at 2位 after the merger, while using its distinctive position between the Chukyo economic area around Nagoya and the Kansai-adjacent boundary. ^[extracted]

### Former Daisan Bank lineage

- **1895**: 112th National Bank established, forming a source lineage of the former Daisan Bank.
- **1912**: converted into an ordinary bank and established Matsusaka Bank.
- Through postwar reorganization, it became **Daisan Bank**, a regional financial institution based in southern Mie, especially Matsusaka and Ise.
- 1973 : joined the Second Association of Regional Banks and converted from mutual-bank status to ordinary-bank status (1989).

### Former Mie Bank lineage

- **1928**: Mie Bank established through the merger of the former Yokkaichi Bank, Kuwana Bank, and others, with the northern industrial belt as its base.
- Built relationships with companies tied to the Yokkaichi petrochemical complex.
- Historically competed with Aichi regional banks in the Chukyo economic area.

### Management-integration process

- **2018-04-02**: Mie Bank + Daisan Bank -> [[regional-banks/san-jusan-fg|三十三 FG]] established through joint share transfer, with both banks becoming 100% subsidiaries.
- **2021-05-01**: the two banks merged -> San ju San Bank launched, completing the single bank operating subsidiary structure.
- **2024**: medium-term management plan set, covering branch-network optimization, DX investment, and Nagoya-area expansion.

### Geographic position

- **Mie base + Chukyo economy + Kansai-adjacent boundary**: dual exposure to Nagoya / Chukyo economic spillover and proximity to the Kansai region.
- **Boundary regional-bank position**: sits between Chukyo / Aichi regional banks and Kansai / Kinki institutions.
- **Prefectural No.2**: smaller than Hyakugo Bank, but the merger (2018-2021) aimed to close the scale gap.

### Prefectural competitors

- [[regional-banks/hyakugo-bank|百五銀行]] (8368): prefectural No.1, headquartered in Tsu, covering all Mie and expanding into Chukyo.
- [[regional-banks/aichi-bank|あいち銀行]] ([[regional-banks/aichi-fg]] group): Aichi-centered competitor spilling into Kuwana and northern Mie.
- [[regional-banks/juroku-bank|十六銀行]] ([[regional-banks/juroku-fg]] group): Gifu-headquartered competitor crossing into Kuwana and northern Mie.
- [[regional-banks/ogaki-kyoritsu-bank|大垣共立銀行]]: competitor in Gifu / western Nobi and Kuwana areas.

### Integration effects (2018 -> 2021)

- **Branch-network overlap cleanup**: consolidation of overlapping Mie Bank + Daisan Bank areas, including Tsu, Matsusaka, and Ise.
- **System integration**: unified onto the former Mie Bank base.
- **Scale expansion**: deposit base secured around the 4 兆円 range, narrowing the gap with Hyakugo.

## 7. Regulation / policy

- **Primary supervisor**: FSA / Banking Act Article 4  license.
- **Industry association**: member of [[financial-regulators/zenchugin-kyo|全国地方銀行協会]].
- **Holding-company regulation**: Banking Act Article 52 -17 , with the holding company = [[regional-banks/san-jusan-fg]].
- **Current policy issues**:
  - 2024: Bank of Japan rate hikes -> improvement in domestic lending spread.
  - 2024: regional-bank consolidation expectations; a possible scenario with prefectural No.1  [[regional-banks/hyakugo-bank|百五]] has been officially denied.
  - Promotion of business-feasibility evaluation lending for SMEs.
  - Mie regional revitalization and tourism-recovery finance.
