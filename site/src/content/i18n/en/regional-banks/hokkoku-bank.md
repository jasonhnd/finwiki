## TL;DR

The Hokkoku Bank, Ltd. (株式会社北國銀行) is a regional bank (first-tier regional bank) based in Ishikawa Prefecture that holds a bank license under the Banking Act and is listed on the FSA's bank license list. It is the core operating bank of [[regional-banks/hokkoku-fhd|北國フィナンシャルホールディングス]]. Whereas the FHD handles the holding-company / digital-platform strategy, this page handles the licensed bank entity and the regional-lending surface. Its business foundation is the standard regional-bank model of deposit-taking in Ishikawa / Hokuriku and lending to SMEs, individuals, and local governments.

## 2. Operating model

The Hokkoku Bank, while handling retail / SME / municipal finance in Ishikawa Prefecture and the Hokuriku region, has also become the implementation base for the digital / BaaS platform strategy of [[regional-banks/hokkoku-fhd|北國フィナンシャルホールディングス]]. In addition to a regional bank's traditional deposit-and-lending business, it is known for its initiatives to provide IT / digital infrastructure externally. By separating the holding company from the bank operating company, it organizes matters so as not to conflate the platform strategy with the bank-license business.

## 3. Regulatory & system positioning

- **Supervision**: Under the Banking Act, supervised by the FSA ([[financial-regulators/fsa|FSA]]) and the Hokuriku Local Finance Bureau. Soundness is disciplined by capital adequacy ratio regulation (domestic standard) and the like.
- **Industry body**: A first-tier regional bank belonging to the Regional Banks Association of Japan. It participates in the nationwide bank funds-settlement network (the Zengin System), and as an industry body it connects to the framework of [[financial-regulators/zenginkyo|全国銀行協会（全銀協）]].
- **Depositor protection**: Deposits are covered by the Deposit Insurance Corporation of Japan (DIC).
- **Consolidation context**: For the general context of Hokuriku regional-bank reorganization and consolidation pressure, see [[banking/regional-bank-consolidation-pattern|regional-bank consolidation pattern]].
