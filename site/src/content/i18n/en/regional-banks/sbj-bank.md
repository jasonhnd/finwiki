# SBJ Bank (Shinhan Bank Japan)


## Wiki route

This entry sits under [[regional-banks/INDEX|regional-banks INDEX]]. Read it against [[trust-banks/mitsubishi-ufj-trust-bank|三菱UFJ信託銀行 (MUTB)]] for peer / contrast context and [[banking/INDEX|banking index]] for the broader system / regulatory boundary.

## TL;DR

SBJ Bank is a domestic Japanese bank corporation funded by Korea's Shinhan Bank 100%. In the FSA bank-license list it is listed as "SBJ Bank Co., Ltd."; rather than a Japan branch of a foreign bank, it has a retail / corporate bank model as a Japanese bank corporation covered by the deposit insurance system.

## 2. Business role

- As Shinhan Bank's local Japanese corporation, it provides banking services such as deposits, loans, and foreign exchange.
- With Shinhan Bank's Japan branch network after 1986 年 as its predecessor, it opened as a domestic bank in 2009 年.
- As a Japan onshore bank of a Korean financial group, it is treated separately from the foreign-bank branch list. Comparables include [[foreign-financial-institutions/woori-bank-japan|Woori Bank Tokyo]] and [[foreign-financial-institutions/korea-development-bank-japan|韓国産業銀行 東京支店]], and as a subsidiary pattern its structure is close to [[regional-banks/tokyo-star-bank|東京スター銀行]] (CTBC group).

## 3. #26 deepening: Shinhan corridor and domestic-bank treatment

- SBJ Bank's official company profile gives the trade name as SBJ Bank Co., Ltd., the English name as Shinhan Bank Japan, establishment as 2009-01-08, banking commencement as 2009-09-14, and the major shareholder as Shinhan Bank Co., Ltd. at 100%. The subject of this page is therefore not a Korean bank branch in Japan, but a Japan-incorporated bank with a Japanese banking license.
- Shinhan Group's history includes the founding of Shinhan Bank from the Korean community in Japan, and SBJ Bank can be read as its Japan onshore extension. It handles deposits, lending, FX, remittance, and online banking for corporate and individual customers, but unlike foreign-bank branches it is treated as a Japanese bank entity for deposit insurance, supervision, and disclosure.
- Its corridor role spans both Korea-Japan cross-border corporate banking and domestic Japanese retail / SME customer acquisition. Compared with branch pages such as [[foreign-financial-institutions/hana-bank-japan|Hana Bank Japan]] and [[foreign-financial-institutions/woori-bank-japan|Woori Bank Tokyo]], the license, capital, and customer-protection boundaries differ.
- Read beside [[regional-banks/tokyo-star-bank|Tokyo Star Bank]], SBJ shows the common pattern of a domestic bank subsidiary under a foreign parent. SBJ is Shinhan / Korea-linked, while Tokyo Star is CTBC / Taiwan-linked, so parent country, customer corridor, and deposit-product strategy should not be conflated.

## 4. Why standalone page matters

For foreign financial institutions, "Japan branch" and "Japanese bank corporation" differ in regulation, deposit insurance, and entity boundary. SBJ Bank is the latter and is important as a case that obtained a domestic Japanese bank license, in contrast to [[banking/foreign-bank-japan-retreat|foreign-bank Japan retreat]].
