# The Saikyo Bank

## TL;DR

The Saikyo Bank, Ltd. (株式会社西京銀行) is a second regional bank headquartered in Shunan City, Yamaguchi Prefecture, holding a bank license under the Banking Act and listed in the FSA's bank-license list. It is the standalone second-regional-bank operating-company anchor for Yamaguchi Prefecture, treated as a separate-legal-entity peer of the Yamaguchi Bank / [[regional-banks/momiji-bank|もみじ銀行]] / [[regional-banks/kitakyushu-bank|北九州銀行]] under [[regional-banks/yamaguchi-fg|山口フィナンシャルグループ]] (YMFG). The substance of its business is the standard second-regional-bank model: deposit-taking within the prefecture and lending to SMEs and individuals.

## 2. Operating model

The Saikyo Bank is a second regional bank that, centered on Yamaguchi Prefecture, handles retail deposits, SME finance, consumer loans, and local relationship banking. The [[regional-banks/yamaguchi-fg|YMFG]] page covers a multi-bank holding-company strategy (the 3-bank model), while this page covers the independent second-regional-bank license boundary. In the Chugoku region it has a peer boundary with [[regional-banks/hiroshima-bank|広島銀行]] / [[regional-banks/momiji-bank|もみじ銀行]].

## 3. Regulatory & system positioning

- **Supervision**: Under the Banking Act, the FSA ([[financial-regulators/fsa|FSA]]) and the Chugoku Local Finance Bureau supervise. Soundness is disciplined through capital-adequacy-ratio regulation (domestic standard) and the like.
- **Industry body**: A second regional bank belonging to the Second Association of Regional Banks (Daini Chigin Kyo). It participates in the Zengin System (the nationwide bank funds-transfer settlement network) and, as an industry body, is connected to the framework of [[financial-regulators/zenginkyo|全国銀行協会（全銀協）]].
- **Depositor protection**: Deposits are covered by the Deposit Insurance Corporation of Japan (DIC).
- **Consolidation context**: For the general context of YMFG's multi-bank model and second-regional-bank realignment in the Chugoku region, see [[banking/regional-bank-consolidation-pattern|regional-bank consolidation pattern]].
