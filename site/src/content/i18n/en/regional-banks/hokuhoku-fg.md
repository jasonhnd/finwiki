# ほくほく FG (Hokuhoku FG)

## Wiki route

This entry sits under [[regional-banks/INDEX|regional-banks INDEX]]. Read it against [[regional-banks/fukuoka-fg|ふくおか FG (FFG)]] for peer / contrast context and [[banking/INDEX|banking index]] for the broader system / regulatory boundary.

## TL;DR

A **2 -base wide-area regional-bank FG** spanning the 3  Hokuriku prefectures (Toyama・Ishikawa・Fukui) + Hokkaido. 2003-09-26 Hokuriku Bank became a holding company via a sole share transfer (the former "ほくぎん FG"), and after management integration with 2004-09-01 Hokkaido Bank, **Hokuhoku FG** was launched. Its biggest feature is the **geographic long-tail strategy** (the long-distance 2  bases of Hokuriku + Hokkaido), the pioneering model in Japanese banking history of a "wide-area alliance between regional banks of distant regions." Hokuriku Bank is of the former 第十二国立銀行 (founded 1877 ) lineage based in Toyama Prefecture, and Hokkaido Bank is a postwar-established (1951) bank based in Sapporo. In Hokkaido there is a competitive structure with **[[megabanks/ndfg]] (Hokuyo Bank)**, while in Hokuriku it holds the main-bank position within Toyama Prefecture. ^[extracted]

### 合併経緯・前身

- Former **Hokuriku Bank**: established as 第十二国立銀行 in 1877  (Toyama) → renamed Hokuriku Bank in 1943  (wartime consolidation)
- Former **Hokkaido Bank**: established in 1951  (a postwar newly-established regional bank based in Hokkaido. A separate legal entity from the former Hokkaido Bank = colonial bank of the Meiji era)
- **ほくぎん FG**: became a holding company via Hokuriku Bank's sole share transfer in 2003-09-26
- **ほくほく FG**: integrated with Hokkaido Bank in 2004-09-01 → renamed

### 地理ロングテール戦略

- A 2 -base wide-area alliance of the 3  Hokuriku prefectures (Toyama・Ishikawa・Fukui) + Hokkaido
- The forerunner of the "wide-area alliance between regional banks of distant regions" model in the history of Japanese banking integration
- Urban bases: regional cities such as Sapporo / Toyama / Kanazawa / Hakodate / Fukui
- Hokuriku Shinkansen (2015-03  Kanazawa opening, 2024-03  Tsuruga extension) → improved Hokuriku - metropolitan-area access creating opportunities for inflows of tourism and business money

### 競合構造

- **Hokkaido**: fierce competition with [[megabanks/ndfg]] (Hokuyo Bank). Hokkaido Bank is in the share 2 位 position
- **Toyama Prefecture**: main-bank position within the prefecture (differentiated from second regional banks such as Toyama Daiichi Bank)
- **Ishikawa Prefecture**: competition with Hokkoku Bank / Hokkoku FH
- **Fukui Prefecture**: competition with Fukui Bank / Fukuho Bank

### 観光・農業・水産金融

- Hokuriku: tourism (Kanazawa・Noto・Tateyama Kurobe) + manufacturing (Toyama aluminum・pharmaceuticals・machinery)
- Hokkaido: tourism (inbound) + agriculture + fisheries + logistics (Chitose・Tomakomai)
- Both bases have a high weight of primary industry / tourism, with deep linkage to the regional economy

## 4. 規制・政策

- **主管**：金融庁（FSA）+ 北陸財務局 / 北海道財務局
- **持株会社規制**：銀行法 第 52 条の 17
- **直近政策論点**：
  - 2024〜 BOJ policy-rate normalization → regional-bank margin improvement (both Hokuriku Bank / Hokkaido Bank benefit)
  - 2025〜 acceleration of regional-bank restructuring (population decline + digital-investment burden)
  - Hokuriku Shinkansen extension (2024-03  Tsuruga) → economic effect on the Fukui Prefecture base
  - 2024-01  Noto Peninsula earthquake → impact on Hokuriku Bank's Ishikawa Prefecture・Toyama Prefecture base and reconstruction finance
