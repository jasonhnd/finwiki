# Kyushu FG

## Wiki route

This entry sits under [[JapanFG/INDEX|JapanFG index]]. Read it against [[regional-banks/fukuoka-fg|ふくおか FG (FFG)]] for peer / contrast context and [[banking/INDEX|banking index]] for the broader system / regulatory boundary.

## TL;DR

A standalone regional-bank group based in southern Kyushu (the two core prefectures of Kumamoto + Kagoshima 2 ). It was launched through the 2015-10-01 management integration of [[higo-bank|肥後銀行]] + [[kagoshima-bank|鹿児島銀行]]. It is a **cross-prefecture "2 -strong alliance" integration model** (different from the broad-area franchise integration of [[fukuoka-fg]] and the mega broad-area linkage model of [[mufg]], etc.). The biggest recent topic is the **surge in semiconductor-cluster-related lending demand following TSMC's Kumamoto expansion (Kikuyo-machi from 2022)**. Within Kyushu, it is a hub FG alongside [[fukuoka-fg]] and [[nishi-nippon-fhd]], but its franchise is separated by focusing on **southern** Kyushu.

## 1. Company overview

**Legal name**: Kyushu Financial Group, Inc.
**English name**: Kyushu Financial Group, Inc.
**Securities code**: TSE Prime 7180  / Fukuoka Stock Exchange 7180

**Established**: 2015-10-01 (joint holding-company formation by Higo Bank + Kagoshima Bank)
**Headquarters**: Kumamoto City Chuo-ku + Kagoshima City (2  headquarters structure)

### Major subsidiaries / holdings

```
Kyushu FG (listed holding company 7180)
  ├── Higo Bank (100%) -- [[higo-bank]] / Kumamoto franchise, overwhelming prefectural share
  │     └── Kumamoto main-bank position, core lender for TSMC Kikuyo-machi-related finance
  ├── Kagoshima Bank (100%) -- [[kagoshima-bank]] / Kagoshima franchise, one side of the prefecture's 2 -strong structure
  │     └── Kagoshima main bank, agriculture / forestry / fisheries and tourism finance
  ├── Kyushu FG Securities (100%) -- group securities company
  ├── Kyushu FG Lease (100%) -- leasing business
  └── Related: Higo Card / Kagoshima Card, etc. -- bank-affiliated credit cards
```

### Integration background / predecessors

- Former **Higo Bank**:
  - 1879 : 135th National Bank established (Kumamoto, under the National Bank Act)
  - 1925 : converted to an ordinary bank and renamed **Higo Bank**
  - Reorganized after the war as a regional bank based in Kumamoto Prefecture
- Former **Kagoshima Bank**:
  - 1879 : 147th National Bank established (Kagoshima, under the National Bank Act)
  - After conversion to an ordinary bank, prefectural banks were consolidated under 1944  wartime controls, forming **Kagoshima Bank**
  - Postwar regional bank based in Kagoshima Prefecture
- **Kyushu FG**: launched through 2015-10-01 joint holding-company formation

> Integration background: population decline in southern Kyushu, pressure on low-interest-rate margins, and demand for adjacent-prefecture franchise integration. Higo Bank (Kumamoto) + Kagoshima Bank (Kagoshima) was a "mutually complementary" integration with adjacent but minimally overlapping franchises. Unlike intraprefecture competition-reduction cases such as Nagasaki Prefecture (the [[fukuoka-fg]] Eighteenth-Shinwa Bank integration = JFTC review delayed by 3  years), the antitrust hurdle was relatively low.

### TSMC-related special demand (largest topic in 2022-2024 ) ★

- **TSMC expansion into Kikuyo-machi, Kumamoto**: first 1  plant (JASM, opened in 2024-02 ) + second 2  plant (scheduled to operate in 2027 )
- [[higo-bank|肥後銀行]] is the **core local main bank for related lending**:
  - Entry finance for semiconductor supply-chain companies (land acquisition / capital investment)
  - Equipment-renewal loans for local subcontractors and related businesses
  - Real estate, housing, and commercial-facility loans tied to rapid expansion around Kikuyo-machi and Ozu-machi

### Franchise strategy

- Concentrated on the 2  prefectures of **southern Kyushu (Kumamoto + Kagoshima) + part of Miyazaki**
- Overlaps with [[fukuoka-fg]] (Fukuoka + Kumamoto + Nagasaki) in Kumamoto Prefecture -> Higo Bank has franchise superiority; [[kumamoto-bank|熊本銀行]] (FFG) has a second-regional-bank-like position
- Kagoshima Prefecture has a **[[kagoshima-bank|鹿児島銀行]] + [[minami-nippon-bank|南日本銀行]] 2 -strong structure** (Minami-Nippon Bank is the second regional bank, while Kagoshima Bank is much larger)
- Tourism, agriculture, and fisheries finance: lending know-how for southern Kyushu's local industries (shochu, farm products, fisheries, tourism)

### Competition / regional-bank restructuring

- **Kyushu competitors**: alongside [[fukuoka-fg]] (FFG) and Nishi-Nippon FHD (Nishi-Nippon City Bank), it is one of Kyushu's 3  major FGs, but direct competition is limited by north-south franchise separation
- **Megabanks** ([[mufg]] / [[smfg]] / [[mizuho-fg]]): megabanks dominate large corporates and nationwide coverage, while regional banks retain an advantage in local mid-sized businesses

## 4. Regulation / policy

- **Supervisor**: FSA + Kyushu Local Finance Bureau
- **Holding-company regulation**: Banking Act Article 52 -17

- **Kumamoto earthquake response** (2016-04): operational know-how in recovery lending and moratorium handling as a financial institution in the disaster area
- **Recent policy issues**:
  - From 2022: coordination with **METI / Kumamoto Prefecture semiconductor-cluster policies** tied to TSMC expansion (subsidy schemes / land development / talent acquisition)
  - From 2024: BOJ policy-rate normalization -> regional-bank margin improvement (greater benefit than megabanks)
  - From 2025: positioning of the southern Kyushu FG amid accelerated regional-bank restructuring (population decline + digital investment burden)
