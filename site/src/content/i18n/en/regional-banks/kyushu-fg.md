# Kyushu FG

## Wiki route

This entry sits under [[regional-banks/INDEX|regional-banks INDEX]]. Read it against [[regional-banks/fukuoka-fg|ふくおか FG (FFG)]] for peer / contrast context and [[banking/INDEX|banking index]] for the broader system / regulatory boundary.

## TL;DR

A standalone regional-bank group based in southern Kyushu (core in the 2  prefectures of Kumamoto + Kagoshima). Established through the management integration of [[regional-banks/higo-bank|肥後銀行]] + [[regional-banks/kagoshima-bank|鹿児島銀行]] in 2015-10-01. **A cross-prefecture "2 -strong union"-type integration (a different model from [[regional-banks/fukuoka-fg]]'s wide-area-base consolidation and [[megabanks/mufg]]-type mega wide-area consolidation)**. The biggest recent topic is the **surge in semiconductor-cluster-related lending demand accompanying TSMC's entry into Kumamoto (2022〜Kikuyo Town)**. Within Kyushu it is a hub FG alongside [[regional-banks/fukuoka-fg]] and [[regional-banks/nishi-nippon-fhd]], but with a niche structure focused on **southern** Kyushu.

## 1. Company overview

**Legal name**: Kyushu Financial Group, Inc. (株式会社九州フィナンシャルグループ)
**English name**: Kyushu Financial Group, Inc.
**Securities code**: TSE PRIME 7180 / FSE 7180
**Established**: 2015-10-01 (joint holding-company conversion of Higo Bank + Kagoshima Bank)
**Headquarters**: Chuo-ku, Kumamoto City + Kagoshima City (a 2 -headquarters system)

### Major subsidiaries / stakes

```
Kyushu FG (holding company / listed 7180)
  ├── Higo Bank (100%) ── [[regional-banks/higo-bank]] / based in Kumamoto Prefecture, overwhelming in-prefecture share
  │     └── Main-bank position in Kumamoto Prefecture, core of TSMC Kikuyo-Town-related lending
  ├── Kagoshima Bank (100%) ── [[regional-banks/kagoshima-bank]] / based in Kagoshima Prefecture, one corner of the in-prefecture 2 -strong
  │     └── Main bank in Kagoshima Prefecture, agriculture-forestry-fisheries / tourism lending
  ├── Kyushu FG Securities (100%) ── group securities
  ├── Kyushu FG Lease (100%) ── leasing business
  └── Affiliates: Higo Card / Kagoshima Card, etc. ─ each bank's affiliated credit cards
```

### Merger background / predecessors

- Former **Higo Bank**:
  - 1879 The 135th National Bank established (Kumamoto, under the National Bank Ordinance)
  - 1925 ordinary-bank conversion → renamed **Higo Bank**
  - Postwar, reorganized as a regional bank based in Kumamoto Prefecture
- Former **Kagoshima Bank**:
  - 1879 The 147th National Bank established (Kagoshima, under the National Bank Ordinance)
  - After ordinary-bank conversion, 1944 in-prefecture bank consolidation under wartime control → **Kagoshima Bank** established
  - Postwar, a regional bank based in Kagoshima Prefecture
- **Kyushu FG**: established through joint holding-company conversion in 2015-10-01

> Integration background: population decline in southern Kyushu / compression of low-rate margins + adjacent-prefecture base-consolidation demand. Higo Bank (Kumamoto) + Kagoshima Bank (Kagoshima) are a "mutually complementary"-type integration with adjacent bases and little overlap. Unlike an in-prefecture-competition-compression type such as Nagasaki Prefecture ([[regional-banks/fukuoka-fg]]'s 18th-Shinwa Bank integration = 3 -year delay in the JFTC review), the antitrust-law hurdle was relatively low.

### TSMC-related special demand (the biggest topic of 2022〜2024 ) ★

- **TSMC (TSMC) entry into Kikuyo Town, Kumamoto**: Plant No. 1  (JASM, opened 2024-02 ) + Plant No. 2  (scheduled to operate 2027 )
- [[regional-banks/higo-bank|肥後銀行]] is the **core of related lending as the local main bank**:
  - Entry financing for semiconductor-supply-chain companies (land acquisition / capital investment)
  - Equipment-renewal lending for local subcontractors / related operators
  - Real-estate / dormitory / commercial-facility-related lending (the rapid expansion around Kikuyo Town / Ozu Town)

### Base strategy

- Concentration on **southern Kyushu's 2  prefectures (Kumamoto + Kagoshima) + part of Miyazaki**
- Overlaps with [[regional-banks/fukuoka-fg]] (Fukuoka + Kumamoto + Nagasaki) in Kumamoto Prefecture → Higo Bank has the base advantage, with [[regional-banks/kumamoto-bank|熊本銀行]] (FFG) in a second-regional-bank-like position
- Kagoshima Prefecture is a **2 -strong structure of [[regional-banks/kagoshima-bank|鹿児島銀行]] + [[regional-banks/minami-nippon-bank|南日本銀行]]** (Minami-Nippon Bank is a second regional bank, with Kagoshima Bank overwhelming in scale)
- Tourism / agriculture / fisheries finance: lending know-how for southern Kyushu's intrinsic local industries (shochu, agricultural products, fisheries, tourism)

### Competition / regional-bank reorganization

- **In-Kyushu competition**: A Kyushu 3 -large FG alongside [[regional-banks/fukuoka-fg]] (FFG) and Nishi-Nippon FHD (Nishi-Nippon City Bank), but direct competition is limited due to the north-south division of bases
- **Mega-banks** ([[megabanks/mufg]] / [[megabanks/smfg]] / [[megabanks/mizuho-fg]]): They cede large corporates / nationwide expansion, but regional banks have the advantage with local mid-tier players

## 4. Regulation / policy

- **Supervision**: Financial Services Agency (FSA) + Kyushu Local Finance Bureau
- **Holding-company regulation**: Article 52 -17
 of the Banking Act- **Kumamoto earthquake response** (2016-04): operational know-how in recovery finance / moratorium response as a financial institution in an affected area
- **Recent policy issues**:
  - 2022〜 Coordination with the **Ministry of Economy, Trade and Industry / Kumamoto Prefecture semiconductor-cluster policies** accompanying TSMC's entry (subsidy schemes / land development / talent securing)
  - 2024〜 BOJ policy-rate normalization → regional-bank margin improvement (larger benefit than for the megas)
  - 2025〜 The positioning of southern-Kyushu FG amid accelerating regional-bank reorganization (population decline + the burden of digital investment)
