# ProCrea Holdings

## Wiki route

This entry sits under [[JapanFG/INDEX|JapanFG index]]. Read it against [[regional-banks/fidea-hd|フィデア HD (Fidea Holdings)]] for peer / contrast context and [[banking/INDEX|banking index]] for the broader system / regulatory boundary.

## TL;DR

Aomori-based regional banks 2 行 (**Aomori Bank + Michinoku Bank**) formed this holding company through a 2022-04-01 management integration (TSE PRIME **7384**). As the **first case applying the Antimonopoly Act special exception law (Regional Infrastructure Company Special Exception Act)**, it achieved in-prefecture 2 行 integration with national approval, making it a **model case for regional-bank survival in a depopulating region**. **In 2025-01-01, the two subsidiary banks merged and "Aomori Michinoku Bank" was launched** (total deposits over 5 兆円, 3 位 in Tohoku, and Aomori prefecture lending share above 7 ). The name "ProCrea" combines "Progress" and "Creation," and the group presents a wide-area North Tohoku strategy covering Aomori, Iwate, and Akita.

### Major subsidiaries (structure immediately after integration)

```
ProCrea Holdings (listed holding company 7384)
  ├── Aomori Bank, Ltd. (100%) -- largest prefectural share, core North Tohoku bank
  │     └── Affiliates (leasing, cards, consulting, etc.)
  └── Michinoku Bank, Ltd. (100%) -- prefectural 2 位, with branches also in southern Hokkaido
        └── Affiliates (leasing, cards, tie-ups with Hokkaido banks, etc.)

→ 2025-01-01 merger (completed):
  ProCrea Holdings
  └── Aomori Michinoku Bank, Ltd. (100%)
```

### Integration background (important)

- **2021-05** Aomori Bank and Michinoku Bank announced management integration
- **2022-04-01** ProCrea HD launched (joint share transfer)
- Because the integration created an in-prefecture 2 行 consolidation, a "geographic market" issue arose under antitrust law → the Fair Trade Commission permitted the merger under the **Antimonopoly Act special exception law**
- This was the **first application** of that special law and became a model case for regional-bank integration
- **2025-01-01** The two subsidiary banks merged and the trade name "**Aomori Michinoku Bank**" was launched (Aomori Bank's Keitaro Ishikawa as president, Michinoku Bank's Takayuki Fujisawa as chairman, about 2,300  employees and 148 branches)

### Integration synergies

- **Concentration of prefectural share**: After the merger, Aomori Michinoku Bank holds an almost monopolistic position in Aomori prefecture lending and deposit share → permitted under the Antimonopoly Act special exception law
- **System integration**: Avoids duplicated large-scale IT investment
- **Personnel-cost rationalization**: Consolidates overlapping administrative departments

### Regional strategy

- **North Tohoku strategy**: Treats the 3  North Tohoku prefectures of Aomori, Iwate, and Akita as one integrated market
- **Southern Hokkaido connection**: Michinoku Bank's Hakodate base gains value from the Shinkansen connection (Shin-Aomori to Shin-Hakodate-Hokuto)
- **Tourism, agriculture, and fisheries**: Apple exports to Taiwan / Hong Kong, scallops, sakuramasu salmon, and redevelopment around Shin-Aomori Station

### Issues (depopulating-region model)

- **Aomori population decline**: Among the highest decline rates nationwide, with youth outflow to Tokyo and Sendai
- **Deflation and aging**: Deposit-loan gap expands because deposits accumulate but borrowers are limited
- **Local business shrinkage**: Closures and lack of successors make business-succession support a core business
- The essence of the integration is not a "growth strategy" but **securing sustainability by reorganizing and integrating the earnings base**

## 5. Regulation & policy

- **Supervisor**: FSA Tohoku Local Finance Bureau
- **Holding-company regulation**: Banking Act Article 52 -17

- **Antimonopoly special exception**: **Antimonopoly Act special exception law** (Regional Infrastructure Company Special Exception Act, 令和 2 年 Act 第 32 号, effective 2020-11 , 10 -year sunset legislation)
  - **2021 年 JFTC approval** → **first application of the special law** ★
  - Policy shift allowing competition concerns from concentrated prefectural share to be outweighed by "sustainability"
  - Model for later regional-bank integrations, including Fukuoka FG-related cases

## 7. Open questions

- Speed of realizing **Aomori Michinoku Bank integration synergies** after the 2025-01-01 merger (progress on branch reductions, IT integration, personnel redeployment)
- Debate over extending or making permanent the **10 -year sunset** of the Antimonopoly Act special exception law (through 2030)
- Substantive progress of the wide-area North Tohoku strategy (whether lending share can expand in Iwate and Akita)
- Relationship with wide-area alliances such as the **TSUBASA Alliance**: further cross-prefecture management-integration scenarios
- Strategic positioning of southern Hokkaido bases (Michinoku Bank Hakodate, etc.) and relationships with Hokuyo Bank and Hokkaido Bank
- **The next domino in regional-bank reorganization**: whether other prefectural regional banks, especially in Kyushu, Shikoku, and Hokuriku, follow the ProCrea HD model
