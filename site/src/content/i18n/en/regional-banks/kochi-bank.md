# The Kochi Bank

## TL;DR

The Kochi Bank, Ltd. (株式会社高知銀行) is a second regional bank headquartered in Kochi City, Kochi Prefecture, holding a bank license under the Banking Act and listed in the FSA's bank-license list. It is the second-regional-bank operating-company anchor for Kochi Prefecture, treated as a separate-legal-entity peer of the first regional bank [[regional-banks/shikoku-bank|四国銀行]]. The substance of its business is the standard second-regional-bank model: deposit-taking within the prefecture and lending to SMEs and individuals.

## 2. Operating model

The Kochi Bank is a second regional bank that, centered on Kochi Prefecture, handles retail deposits, SME finance, local relationship banking, and regional customer support. Within Kochi Prefecture it has a first / second-regional-bank peer boundary with the first regional bank [[regional-banks/shikoku-bank|四国銀行]]. In the wider Shikoku area it is compared with [[regional-banks/iyo-bank|伊予銀行]], [[regional-banks/ehime-bank|愛媛銀行]], and [[regional-banks/awa-bank|阿波銀行]].

## 3. Regulatory & system positioning

- **Supervision**: Under the Banking Act, the FSA ([[financial-regulators/fsa|FSA]]) and the Shikoku Local Finance Bureau supervise. Soundness is disciplined through capital-adequacy-ratio regulation (domestic standard) and the like.
- **Industry body**: A second regional bank belonging to the Second Association of Regional Banks (Daini Chigin Kyo). It participates in the Zengin System (the nationwide bank funds-transfer settlement network) and, as an industry body, is connected to the framework of [[financial-regulators/zenginkyo|全国銀行協会（全銀協）]].
- **Depositor protection**: Deposits are covered by the Deposit Insurance Corporation of Japan (DIC).
- **Consolidation context**: For the general context of second-regional-bank realignment and consolidation pressure in Shikoku, see [[banking/regional-bank-consolidation-pattern|regional-bank consolidation pattern]].
