# The Towa Bank, Ltd.

## Wiki route

This entry sits under [[regional-banks/INDEX|regional-banks INDEX]]. Read it against [[regional-banks/jimoto-hd|じもとホールディングス (JIMOTO HD)]] for peer / contrast context and [[banking/INDEX|banking index]] for the broader system / regulatory boundary.

## TL;DR

The Towa Bank is a **second regional bank** headquartered in Maebashi, Gunma Prefecture and listed on TSE Prime 8558. It began as **1917-11-01 Gunma Mujin Co., Ltd.**, passed through the mutual-bank conversion route 1951, and was renamed The Towa Bank in line with the 1989  ordinary-bank conversion. It remains a **standalone bank structure, not a holding company**, and in Gunma it is positioned after the first 1  regional bank [[regional-banks/gunma-bank]] with an in-prefecture No.2  position. Its core business is SME-specialized local banking, including finance for the SUBARU Ota plant supply chain. It also has a broad strategic alliance with the 2003  former Dai-Ichi Kangyo Bank line, now [[megabanks/mizuho-fg]]. The name "Towa" is the current legal identity ^[extracted].

### Why the non-holding-company point matters

Within Gunma, both [[regional-banks/gunma-bank]], the first 1  regional bank, and Towa remain standalone bank structures; the prefecture's listed 2 行 banks therefore both have a distinctive "standalone bank / Prime listing" structure ^[extracted]. Across second regional banks more broadly, holding-company conversion has advanced through [[regional-banks/mebuki-fg]] / [[regional-banks/tomony-hd]] / [[regional-banks/fidea-hd]] / [[regional-banks/jimoto-hd]] / [[regional-banks/proclair-hd]], but Towa has kept the standalone form. The background is:

- a concentrated prefectural market and limited diversification benefit from holding-company conversion;
- related subsidiaries, such as leasing and guarantee companies, can be managed beneath the bank;
- a wide strategic alliance with the former Dai-Ichi Kangyo Bank line, now [[megabanks/mizuho-fg]], supplements coverage.
