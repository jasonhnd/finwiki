## TL;DR

[[regional-banks/aozora-bank|あおぞら銀行]] (8304 / TSE PRIME) is a **mid-sized independent bank descended from the former Nippon Credit Bank (NCB)**. 1998-12  failure -> special public management (temporary nationalization) -> 2000-09  sale to the SoftBank + ORIX + Tokio Marine consortium -> 2001-01 renamed "Aozora Bank" -> 2003  US PE **Cerberus Capital Management** became a major shareholder -> 2006-11  relisted on the TSE First Section -> Cerberus completed staged sale. This entry covers business segments, revenue structure, **2023-2024  large US CRE loss and strategic rebuilding**, international business, and regulatory response at **operating-level depth**. It is a "mid-sized independent bank" model that belongs neither to a megabank group nor a regional-bank group, with a business mix of **wholesale corporate banking + investment banking + international finance + retail customer**.

## 1. Corporate history and current ownership structure

- **Founded (1957-04)**: Japan Real Estate Bank established
- **Renamed (1977)**: trade name changed to Nippon Credit Bank (NCB)
- **Failure (1998-12)**: special public management under the Financial Reconstruction Act began
- **Sale (2000-09)**: sold to SoftBank + ORIX + Tokio Marine + domestic non-life insurer consortium
- **Renamed (2001-01)**: renamed "Aozora Bank"
- **Cerberus acquisition (2003)**: US PE Cerberus became a major shareholder
- **Relisting (2006-11)**: relisted on the TSE First Section
- **Cerberus sale (2017-)**: staged sale completed
- **Current major shareholders**: dispersed structure centered on institutional investors, with no specific controlling shareholder

## 2. License and regulatory boundary

- **Ordinary bank**: ordinary banking license under the Banking Act (Kanto Local Finance Bureau license)
- **International business**: designated financial institution under the Foreign Exchange and Foreign Trade Act
- **Industry association membership**: Japanese Bankers Association
- **Deposit Insurance Corporation**: member (deposit insurance covers 1,000 万円 + interest)
- **Bank holding company status**: listed as a standalone bank, not a bank holding company (similar standalone structure to [[regional-banks/sbi-shinsei-bank|SBI 新生銀行]] after dissolving the Shinsei HD structure)

## 3. Business segment details

### 3.1  Wholesale corporate banking

- **Targets**: loans to mid-sized and large companies, syndicated loans, corporate bond underwriting, M&A finance
- **Strengths**: corporate network, industrial finance, and real-estate finance from the former NCB long-term credit bank era
- **Competitors**: mega corporate divisions of [[megabanks/mufg-bank|三菱UFJ銀行]] / [[megabanks/smbc|SMBC]] / [[megabanks/mizuho-bank|みずほ銀行]]

### 3.2  Real-estate finance

- **Domestic real estate**: commercial real estate, residential development, REIT-related finance
- **US CRE**: commercial real estate investment and lending centered on office buildings — **epicenter of the 2023-2024  loss event**
- **Overseas real estate**: participation in European and Asian real-estate projects

### 3.3  Investment banking and marketable securities business

- **Trading**: proprietary investment in foreign bonds, interest rates, and credit
- **Asset management**: cooperation with Aozora Investment Management / GMO Aozora Net Bank
- **Securitization / structured finance**: CLO, ABS, and MBS origination and investment

### 3.4  International finance

- **Americas business**: US subsidiary and Americas CRE investment (risk factor)
- **Asia business**: Asian corporate lending centered on the Singapore base

### 3.5  Retail

- **Deposits / card loans / mortgages**: limited retail base
- **GMO Aozora Net Bank**: joint-venture online bank subsidiary with GMO Internet G (retail / corporate API banking)

## 4. US CRE loss event (2023-2024) - strategic issues

- **Background**: Aozora Bank had a high investment ratio in US CRE (centered on offices) relative to its size
- **Trigger**: post-COVID-19  structural change in US office demand + sharp rise in US interest rates + refinancing difficulty
- **Impact**:
  - **2024-05  full-year loss announcement** (2024-03  period) — US CRE-related provisions heavily damaged earnings
  - large stock-price decline
  - review of shareholder returns such as dividends / share buybacks
- **Management response**:
  - reduce and sell US CRE exposure
  - consider capital increase and capital reinforcement
  - rebuild management strategy and formulate a new medium-term plan
  - review board / management structure
- **Regulatory response**: ongoing monitoring and improvement requests by the FSA / Kanto Local Finance Bureau

## 6. Strategy and recent trends

- **Reduction of US CRE exposure**: sale and continued provisioning of office-building assets
- **Deepening domestic corporate business**: strengthen mid-sized company business, regional-bank cooperation, business succession / M&A finance
- **GMO Aozora Net Bank**: differentiation by providing API banking and corporate BaaS platform
- **Strengthening structured finance**: CLO / structured bonds / project finance
- **Review of overseas business**: shrink Americas exposure while strengthening Asian corporate lending
- **Capital policy**: capital increase, subordinated bond issuance, and dividend policy review (phased resumption of returns as earnings recover)

## 8. Risk and regulatory issues

- **Remaining US CRE exposure**: risk of additional losses if the US real estate market deteriorates further despite progress on sales and provisioning
- **Capital adequacy**: pace of recovery in capital damaged by CRE losses and possible need for additional capital reinforcement
- **Management strategy pivot**: sustainability of the "mid-sized independent bank" model — rebalancing overseas and domestic business ratios
- **Market-business volatility**: valuation losses on securities during rising-rate phases
- **Dividend sustainability**: after experience with dividend cuts / no dividend, predictability of dividends is a market focus
- **TCFD / sustainability disclosure**: climate-related risk disclosure and transition-risk management
