# The Akita Bank (株式会社秋田銀行)

## Wiki route

This entry sits under [[regional-banks/INDEX|regional-banks INDEX]]. Read it against [[regional-banks/fidea-hd|フィデア HD (Fidea Holdings)]] for peer / contrast context and [[banking/INDEX|banking index]] for the broader system / regulatory boundary.

## TL;DR

The Akita Bank is a first-tier regional bank based in Akita Prefecture. It faces the same Akita Prefecture market as Hokuto Bank under [[regional-banks/fidea-hd|フィデア HD]], but the Akita Bank is an independent, standalone listed bank that handles corporate, individual, and public finance within the prefecture. It is a representative example of a depopulating prefecture, used as a subject for observing regional-bank consolidation, in-prefecture manufacturing, tourism, and public-money transactions.

## 3. Strategy / competition

- **Defense as the No.1  independent within the prefecture**: In Akita Prefecture, because Hokuto Bank belongs to [[regional-banks/fidea-hd|フィデア HD]], the Akita Bank is compared as the "independent in-prefecture leader."
- **Depopulating-prefecture model**: Akita Prefecture is one of the regions with the strongest depopulation and aging nationwide, and the long-term contraction of deposit/loan volume constrains regional-bank earnings.
- **Observation point for wide-area Tohoku consolidation**: In neighboring prefectures, holding-company reorganizations such as [[regional-banks/proclair-hd|青森系統合]], [[regional-banks/fidea-hd|山形・秋田系統合]], and [[regional-banks/jimoto-hd|じもと HD]] are advancing. The Akita Bank's continued independence is a control group for observing consolidation pressure.
- **Industrial finance**: How it supports financing demand related to rice cultivation, sake brewing, timber, tourism, and renewable energy is important as regional finance.

## 4. Regulation / policy

- A bank under the Banking Act, supervised by the FSA / Local Finance Bureau.
- As a listed company, subject to TSE rules, the Companies Act, and FIEA disclosure.
- In regional-financial administration, the financial-intermediation function of depopulating areas, business-succession support, and regional-decarbonization support are the main points.
