# The Kiyo Bank

## Wiki route

This entry sits under [[JapanFG/INDEX|JapanFG index]]. Read it against [[regional-banks/ikeda-senshu-hd|池田泉州ホールディングス]] for peer / contrast context and [[banking/INDEX|banking index]] for the broader system / regulatory boundary.

## TL;DR

Kiyo Bank is a standalone regional bank based in Wakayama Prefecture, with an unusual reorganization history in which it **moved through a holding-company structure and then returned to a standalone bank structure**. Founded in 1895 (Meiji 28), the current legal entity traces the lineage of 1985-05-01 Kiyo Bank, Wakayama Bank, and related predecessors, and serves as a core bank in the prefecture. Its abbreviated name is "**Kiyo**." Its securities code is **TSE PRIME 8370** (inherited by Kiyo Bank through the former Ikeda Bank 8370  / Kiyo Holdings route). **Holding-company history: 2006-02-20 Kiyo Holdings established → 2006-10-01 Wakayama Bank integrated → 2013-10-01 holding company dissolved and integrated into the bank, returning to standalone bank structure**. Its earnings core is **overwhelming Wakayama Prefecture share (prefecture No.1) + expansion into southern Osaka (Senshu area) + tourism finance (Koyasan, Kumano Kodo, Nanki-Shirahama) + primary-industry finance (mandarin oranges, plums, forestry)**.

### Merger history / predecessors

- **Founded in 1895 (Meiji 28)**: launched in Wakayama as a Meiji-era regional bank, one of the oldest roots among Wakayama regional banks
- Prewar to postwar: multiple regional banks coexisted in Wakayama Prefecture → trend of regional-bank integration under the wartime control economy
- **1985-05-01**: current Kiyo Bank launched through financial reorganization
- Before 2006 年: Wakayama Prefecture had a **2 行-bank** structure of **Kiyo Bank** + **Wakayama Bank**, creating overlapping regional-bank coverage
- **2006-02-20 Kiyo Holdings established**: holding company established with Kiyo Bank under it, listed on the TSE 8370  ^[extracted]
- **2006-10-01 Wakayama Bank integration**: Kiyo HD made Wakayama Bank a wholly owned subsidiary; Wakayama Bank was absorbed into Kiyo Bank → achieved a prefecture-level 1 行 structure ^[extracted]
- **2013-10-01 Kiyo HD dissolved and integrated into the bank**: the holding company was dissolved and Kiyo Bank absorbed Kiyo HD, **returning to a standalone bank structure**; Kiyo Bank inherited listing code 8370  ^[extracted]
- **2022-04  TSE market-segment review** → TSE PRIME 8370 (transition from former TSE First Section)

> Integration background: the 2006  Wakayama Bank integration is a typical regional-bank reorganization from "prefecture 2 行 to 1 行." The notable point is the 2013  dissolution of the holding company — while many regional banks moved toward holding-company / FG structures, Kiyo did the opposite and **dissolved the holding company to return to a standalone bank**.

### Prefecture-base strategy

- **Wakayama Prefecture = tourism + primary-industry major prefecture**:
  - **Tourism**: many tourism assets in the prefecture, including Koyasan (head temple of Shingon Buddhism, founded by Kukai in 1200 年; World Heritage "Sacred Sites and Pilgrimage Routes in the Kii Mountain Range"), Kumano Kodo (Kumano Sanzan / World Heritage), Nanki-Shirahama Onsen, and Adventure World (pandas)
  - **Primary industry**: mandarin oranges national 1 位, plums (Nanko-ume) national 1 位, forestry (Wakayama part of the Yoshino-Kumano line), fisheries (Kishu-Katsuura tuna, etc.)
- **Primary-industry finance**: competition / division of roles with JA Bank ([[norinchukin]] line) is a structural issue. Supports business succession for mandarin-orange / plum farmers and lending to processed-food businesses such as pickled plums and juice producers
- **Southern Osaka (Senshu area) expansion**: because volume is limited within Wakayama alone, the bank has **strengthened expansion into southern Osaka (Kishiwada, Kaizuka, Izumisano, Sennan)**. It aims to win main-bank positions for logistics and tourism-related businesses around Kansai International Airport (Rinku Town, Izumisano City) and SMEs in the Senshu manufacturing area

### Competitive structure in the Kansai economy

- **Specificity of the Kii Peninsula**: Wakayama Prefecture occupies the western half of the Kii Peninsula, bordering Osaka to the north, Nara and Mie to the east, and the Pacific Ocean to the south. A prefecture-contained regional-bank operation is difficult given population scale, so it is structurally drawn into **competition across the Kansai economy + Kii Peninsula**
- **Competition with megabanks ([[mufg]] / [[smfg]] / [[mizuho-fg]])**: large-company deals in urban Osaka tend to flow to megabanks; Kiyo Bank counters through **main-bank positions for mid-sized and smaller companies in Wakayama + southern Osaka (Senshu area)**
- **Competition with Kansai regional banks**:
  - [[ikeda-senshu-hd|池田泉州 HD]] (Senshu Ikeda Bank): direct competition across Osaka, especially in southern Osaka's Senshu area, where the former Senshu Bank origin of the name "Senshu" collides with Kiyo's southern Osaka expansion
  - [[nanto-bank|南都銀行]] (Nara-based): contact at the prefectural border (northeastern Wakayama / Gojo area)
  - [[kyoto-fg|京都 FG]]: less direct contact, but parallel as a mid-sized regional bank in the same broad Kansai economy
  - [[resona-hd|りそな HD]] (including Kansai Mirai Bank): Kansai's largest regional-bank group, direct competition with Kiyo across Osaka
- **Contact with southern Mie / east coast of the Kii Peninsula**: the Mie side is the home market of [[san-jusan-fg|三十三 FG]] (former Mie Bank + Daisan Bank); there is prefectural-border contact on the east coast of the Kii Peninsula (Kumano / Owase)

### Strategic significance of dissolving the holding company

- The **2013-10-01 dissolution of Kiyo HD** is a rare example that went against the regional-bank reorganization trend ^[extracted]
- Benefits of standalone bank status: (1) simpler governance, (2) concentration of management resources in the bank, (3) no additional holding-company costs

### Position in regional-bank restructuring

- Because of the Kii Peninsula's geographic specificity (population decline + tourism dependence + high primary-industry weight), Kiyo's **tourism + primary-industry portfolio** is highly distinctive within Kansai and may contribute to bargaining power in any restructuring

## 4. Regulation / policy

- **Supervisors**: Financial Services Agency (FSA) + Kinki Local Finance Bureau
- **Banking regulation**: Banking Act (after 2013-10-01 it is a standalone bank, so bank holding-company regulation does not apply)
- **Recent policy issues**:
  - 2024 onward, BOJ policy-rate normalization → regional-bank margin improvement (relatively larger benefit than megabanks)
  - 2025 onward, acceleration of regional-bank restructuring (population decline + digital-investment burden) → decision on alliance / restructuring with Kansai regional-bank groups
  - Whether to remain a standalone bank or shift again to a holding-company / FG structure
  - Response to tourism portfolio (tailwind from inbound recovery vs vulnerability to external shocks)
  - Financial response to aging and succession problems in primary industries (mandarin oranges, plums, forestry), including business succession and farmland consolidation finance
  - Continuation of operating-area expansion into southern Osaka (Senshu area)
