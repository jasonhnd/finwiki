# Kyoto Financial Group

## Wiki Route

This entry sits under [[JapanFG/INDEX|JapanFG index]]. Read it against [[regional-banks/ikeda-senshu-hd|池田泉州ホールディングス]] for peer / contrast context and [[banking/INDEX|banking index]] for the broader system / regulatory boundary.

## TL;DR

Kyoto Financial Group is a **prestige Kansai regional-bank FG** based in Kyoto Prefecture. Securities code: **TSE PRIME 5844**. It was launched in **2023-10-02** when Bank of Kyoto became a holding-company group through a **sole-share-transfer** structure, a relatively late holding-company conversion among regional banks. **Bank of Kyoto maintains No. 1 share inside Kyoto Prefecture** and is known for transactions and **policy-shareholdings** with globally strong Kyoto-headquartered companies such as Nintendo, Kyocera, Murata Manufacturing, and Nippon Densan / Nidec. In Kansai it competes directly with [[resona-hd]] (Kansai Mirai Bank), [[ikeda-senshu-hd]], Shiga Bank, Nanto Bank, Kiyo Bank, and others. Against the megabank 3 行 cluster ([[mufg]] / [[smfg]] / [[mizuho-fg]]), it has a distinctive presence through its Kyoto base and the **financial strength** created by cross-shareholdings in listed companies. ^[extracted]

### Merger History and Predecessor

- Former **Bank of Kyoto** (Kyoto Prefecture base)
  - **1941-10-01** Bank of Kyoto established through wartime regional-bank consolidation in Kyoto, derived from combinations including **Kyoto Chuo Credit Cooperative + Nishijin Bank + Keihoku Bank + former Kyoto Bank** ^[extracted]
  - After the war, expanded from central Kyoto City to all Kyoto Prefecture and wider Kansai.
  - **1969** listed on the second sections of Osaka Securities Exchange and Tokyo Stock Exchange.
  - **1971** listed on TSE First Section (former securities code 8369) ^[extracted].
  - Built long-term transactions and **cross-shareholding** relationships with Kyoto-headquartered global companies such as Nintendo, Kyocera, Murata Manufacturing, and Nippon Densan / Nidec, making large **policy-shareholding unrealized gains** a signature feature.
- **Kyoto FG launched**: **2023-10-02** through Bank of Kyoto sole share transfer and HD conversion; one of the later regional-bank holding-company conversions 1  ^[extracted].

### Strategic Axes

- **No. 1  regional bank in Kyoto Prefecture**: overwhelming No. 1 share in Kyoto deposits and loans; uses local embeddedness plus the Kyoto brand ^[extracted].
- **Policy-shareholding unrealized-gain strategy** ★: long-term cross-shareholdings with Kyoto-headquartered global companies such as Nintendo, Kyocera, Murata Manufacturing, and Nippon Densan / Nidec have accumulated **several-hundred-billion-yen-scale unrealized gains**, the bank's largest financial feature and a source of capital strength and credit quality ^[extracted].
- **Wide Kansai expansion**: based in Kyoto Prefecture, while extending into parts of Osaka, Shiga, Hyogo, Nara, and Aichi as a midsize regional bank across Kansai ^[extracted].
- **Kyogin Direct Finance / DX**: digital investment, corporate internet banking, fintech partnerships ^[extracted].
- **Use of the Kyoto brand**: tourism, traditional industries, and links with Kyoto-headquartered companies are used as differentiating assets ^[extracted].
- **Medium-term management plan**: achieved the 1th plan (2023-03 to 2026-03, net-profit target 300 億円) ahead of schedule and announced new strategic targets in 2024-11-14. The next 2th plan (2026-04 to 2029-03) sets more ambitious goals, including profit attributable to owners of parent 600 億円 (2029-03), ROE above 8% (early 2030 年s), etc.

### Relationship with Kyoto-Headquartered Global Companies ★

- **Nintendo** (Minami-ku, Kyoto): Bank of Kyoto is a stable shareholder with long-term holdings and large unrealized gains in rising share-price periods ^[extracted].
- **Kyocera** (Fushimi-ku, Kyoto): same long-term cross-shareholding pattern ^[extracted].
- **Murata Manufacturing** (Nagaokakyo, Kyoto): same long-term cross-shareholding pattern ^[extracted].
- **Nippon Densan / Nidec** (Minami-ku, Kyoto): same long-term cross-shareholding pattern ^[extracted].
- These **policy-shareholding unrealized gains** support Bank of Kyoto's **capital strength** and **credit quality**, creating a balance-sheet structure unusual among regional banks ★ ^[extracted].

### Integration Synergy and Issues

- **Revenue diversification**: two pillars of loan-deposit interest income and share dividends / sale gains; the issue is year-to-year volatility in share-sale gains ^[extracted].
- **Pressure to reduce policy shareholdings**: response to FSA and TSE pressure to reduce **policy shareholdings** is a key issue, including explaining the rationale for cross-shareholdings and calibrating the reduction pace ^[extracted].

### B2C Branding

- **Bank of Kyoto = "Kyogin"**: extremely high awareness among Kyoto residents, with some awareness across wider Keihanshin ^[extracted].
- Brand asset as **"Kyoto's bank"**: links to tourism, traditional industries, and global companies differentiate the regional-bank brand ^[extracted].

## 4. Regulation & Policy

- **Supervisor**: Financial Services Agency (FSA)
- **Holding-company regulation**: Banking Act Article 52 -17 (bank holding companies) ^[extracted]
- **Recent policy issues**:
  - **2024 onward BoJ policy-rate normalization**: improvement in domestic loan margins, a major regional-bank benefit.
  - **Policy-shareholding reduction requests**: market focus is on compliance with FSA / TSE stewardship and corporate-governance codes and the pace of reducing policy shareholdings ★ ^[extracted].
  - **Regional-bank consolidation**: Kyoto FG was formed in 2023-10-02 through Bank of Kyoto sole share transfer and has not merged with another bank; it operates as a standalone group.
  - **2025 onward branch reduction**: standard regional-bank restructuring, slimming and consolidating the branch network.
  - **Business-feasibility lending for SMEs**: promoting lending not dependent on collateral or guarantees.
  - **Osaka / Kansai Expo (2025-04 to 10)**: expected activation of the Kansai economy and follow-tail effects for Kyoto tourism, services, and construction.
