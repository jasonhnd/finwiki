# Tokio Marine Asset Management (Tokio Marine AM)

## TL;DR

Tokio Marine Asset Management (TMAM) is the asset-management operating company of [[JapanFG/tokio-marine|東京海上ホールディングス]]. In the FSA financial instruments business operator list, it appears as "Tokio Marine Asset Management Co., Ltd." with Kanto Local Finance Bureau Director-General (FIBO) 第361号. It provides the investment-management function for [[JapanFG/tokio-marine|東京海上 HD]], the leading 3  mega non-life insurer group (Tokio Marine / [[JapanFG/msad|MS&AD]] / [[JapanFG/sompo|SOMPO]]). It is a non-life-insurer-affiliated asset manager centered on non-life-insurance policy-reserve management and pension management, while also expanding into public investment trusts and NISA products. It represents the **non-life-insurer-affiliated asset-manager** category, distinct from megabank-affiliated asset managers ([[JapanFG/mufg-asset-management|MUAM]] / [[JapanFG/asset-management-one|アセマネOne]]) and life-insurer-affiliated asset managers ([[JapanFG/nissay-asset-management|ニッセイ AM]]).

## 2. Business Role

- The asset manager of [[JapanFG/tokio-marine|東京海上 HD]], responsible for **non-life-insurance policy-reserve management**, **pension management**, **public investment trust management**, and **discretionary investment management**.
- As the investment function of a non-life insurance financial group, its core is duration-matching investment against long-term liabilities (non-life-insurance policy reserves).
- Also offers public investment trusts and NISA products, selling to individual investors through life-insurance and bank sales channels.

## 4. Why a Standalone Page Matters

[[JapanFG/tokio-marine|東京海上 HD]] is the leading 3  mega non-life insurer by market capitalization and overseas ratio, but **TMAM**, which handles its investment function, is important as a separate analytical unit:

1. **Representative of the non-life-insurer-affiliated asset-manager category**: it sits alongside asset managers affiliated with [[JapanFG/msad|MS&AD]] and [[JapanFG/sompo|SOMPO]], while Tokio Marine's overseas expansion and ESG leadership are reflected in its investment approach
2. A non-life-insurer-affiliated player in **NISA expansion + public investment trust competition**: competing with bank-affiliated ([[JapanFG/mufg-asset-management|MUAM]]), securities-affiliated ([[JapanFG/nomura-asset-management|野村アセット]]), and trust-affiliated ([[JapanFG/smt-am|SMT-AM]]) players
3. The investment policy for **non-life-insurance policy reserves (about 30 兆円 in scale)** affects mid-tier financial markets such as domestic bonds, equities, and alternatives
