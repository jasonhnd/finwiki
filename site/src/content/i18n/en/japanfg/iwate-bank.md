# The Bank of Iwate

##TL;DR

Iwate Bank is the first regional bank with its head office in Morioka City, Iwate Prefecture. Along with [[JapanFG/fidea-hd|フィデア HD]], [[JapanFG/proclair-hd|プロクレア HD]], and [[JapanFG/77-bank|七十七銀行]] in the northern Tohoku region, it serves as a reference point for observing regional finance in areas with declining populations. Its core services are deposits, corporate transactions, earthquake recovery finance, and local industry support within Iwate Prefecture.

## 3. Strategy/Competition

- **Iwate Prefecture's core regional bank**: Covers the entire prefecture, centered on Morioka, and serves as the main source of deposits and loans within the prefecture.
- **Medium-term plan**: The 21 medium-term management plan is from 2023-04  to 2026-03 . In the long term, the company aims for consolidated net income of 100億円 and ROE of 5%, and aims for consolidated net income of 70億円 and ROE of 4% or higher as 2025年 degree targets.
- **Tohoku wide area comparison**: By comparing Miyagi's [[JapanFG/77-bank|77-bank]], Aomori's [[JapanFG/proclair-hd|proclair-hd]], and Akita/Yamagata's [[JapanFG/fidea-hd|fidea-hd]], you can see the difference between a single bank and an integrated holding company.
- **Specialities of reconstruction finance**: Recovery, disaster prevention, and coastal region revitalization after the Great East Japan Earthquake are unique themes that remain in Iwate Bank's regional finance story.

## 4. Regulations and Policies

- A bank under the Banking Act under the supervision of the Financial Services Agency and the Finance Bureau.
- As a listed bank, subject to the Companies Act, Financial Instruments and Exchange Act, and TSE disclosure.
- In regional financial administration, important themes are business succession, regional decarbonization, disaster recovery, and regional revitalization.
