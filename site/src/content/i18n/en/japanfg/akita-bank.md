# The Akita Bank

## Wiki route

This entry sits under [[JapanFG/INDEX|JapanFG index]]. Read it against [[JapanFG/fidea-hd|フィデア HD (Fidea Holdings)]] for peer / contrast context and [[banking/INDEX|banking index]] for the broader system / regulatory boundary.

## TL;DR

Akita Bank is the first-tier regional bank based in Akita Prefecture. It faces the same Akita market as Hokuto Bank under [[JapanFG/fidea-hd|フィデア HD]], but Akita Bank remains an independent, separately listed bank serving corporate, retail, and public-sector finance in the prefecture. It is a representative case of a depopulating prefecture and a useful observation point for regional-bank consolidation, local manufacturing, tourism, and public-fund banking.

## 3. Strategy and competition

- **Defense of the independent No.1  position within the prefecture**: Because Hokuto Bank belongs to [[JapanFG/fidea-hd|フィデア HD]], Akita Bank is compared as the "top independent player in the prefecture."
- **Depopulating-prefecture model**: Akita is among the regions in Japan with the strongest population decline and aging, meaning long-term shrinkage in loan and deposit volume constrains regional-bank earnings.
- **Observation point for broader Tohoku consolidation**: Neighboring prefectures have seen holding-company-led reorganizations such as [[JapanFG/proclair-hd|青森系統合]], [[JapanFG/fidea-hd|山形・秋田系統合]], and [[JapanFG/jimoto-hd|じもと HD]]. Akita Bank's continued independence serves as a control case for analyzing consolidation pressure.
- **Industrial finance**: Supporting funding demand in rice production, sake brewing, timber, tourism, and renewable energy is a key regional-finance role.

## 4. Regulation and policy

- A bank under the Banking Act, supervised by the FSA and the Finance Bureau.
- As a listed company, subject to TSE rules, the Companies Act, and FIEA disclosure.
- In regional-finance policy, main issues include financial intermediation in depopulating regions, business succession support, and support for regional decarbonization.
