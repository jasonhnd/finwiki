## 1. Company overview

**Ultimate parent**: The Bank of New York Mellon Corporation (Delaware corporation, NYSE: **BK**, head office in New York City)
**Established** (parent): 2007 (merger of The Bank of New York + Mellon Financial Corporation) ^[extracted]
**Predecessors**: 1784 **The Bank of New York** (one of the oldest commercial banks in the US, one of the institutions founded by Alexander Hamilton) -> 2007 merged with Mellon Financial (Pittsburgh-based, founded 1869 ) -> BNY Mellon
**Ticker**: NYSE: BK

### Major Japanese entities (public-information basis)

```
The Bank of New York Mellon Corporation (US NYSE: BK; supervised by FRB / OCC)
  ├── BNY Mellon Trust Bank, Ltd. ── Japanese corporation holding trust-business license
  │     └── Custody, trust, and asset-servicing services at domestic bases
  ├── The Bank of New York Mellon, Tokyo Branch ── Japan branch directly connected to US head office
  │     └── Global custody and transaction banking
  └── BNY Mellon Asset Management Japan ── Asset-management business in Japan
        └── Sale and management of global investment products for institutional investors
```

**Features**:
- **No retail banking or individual deposits at all**. Fully specialized in B2B / B2  institutional investors

### Treatment of the trust-bank route

BNY's Japan official page explains that it acquired the Japanese trust-bank business in 2009 年, leading to the current **BNY Mellon Trust Bank, Ltd.** Another official disclosure page also describes BNY Mellon Trust Bank as a trust bank established in Japan and a registered financial institution whose main business is fiduciary services for corporate customers.

For this reason, FinWiki treats this page as the umbrella route for [[JapanFG/foreign-bank-branches-japan-index|foreign-bank branch registry]] and [[JapanFG/trust-companies-japan-index|trust-company registry]] rather than mass-producing a separate `bny-mellon-trust-bank` page. ## 2. History (selected timeline)

| Year | Event |
|---|---|
| **1968** | **The Bank of New York began Japan business** (banking operations started in Tokyo) ^[extracted] |
| 1970s〜80s | Expanded custody and transaction-banking operations during Japanese banks' global expansion phase |
| **1989** | **Supported many Japanese companies' overseas securities issuance as stock transfer agent (ADR agent)** (established position as ADR depositary for US-listed Japanese companies) ^[extracted] |
| 1990s〜2000s | Full-scale custody business as Japanese banks and pension funds expanded overseas asset management |
| **2007-07-01** | **The Bank of New York + Mellon Financial merger -> BNY Mellon launched** (creation of global bank specialized in custody and asset servicing) ^[extracted] |
| 2010s | **Expansion of global custody mandates from Japanese banks and pension funds (including GPIF)** (tailwind from yen depreciation and overseas investment shift) ^[extracted] |

## 5. Supervision and regulation

- **Lead supervisor**: Financial Services Agency (FSA) — as trust bank and Japan branch
- **US-side supervisors**: FRB / OCC / FDIC; subject to global stress test DFAST
- **Recent issues**:
  - **Rising overseas asset-management ratio at GPIF and public pensions** -> structural expansion in custody demand
  - **Yen depreciation and overseas investment shift** -> growth in assets under custody from Japanese banks and asset managers increasing overseas assets
  - Foreign Exchange and Foreign Trade Act / inward direct-investment screening -> regulatory response in overseas asset custody business
