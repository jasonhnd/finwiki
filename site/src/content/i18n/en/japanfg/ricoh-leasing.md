## TL;DR

Ricoh Leasing was established in 1976 年 as Ricoh's captive vendor-lease subsidiary and is now a TSE PRIME-listed 8566  general leasing company with meaningful independence, combining a Mizuho-side and Ricoh-side hybrid structure. It began with leases for Ricoh OA equipment and multifunction printers, then diversified into general equipment leasing, collection agency services, factoring, medical leasing, environmental leasing, and overseas leasing. It is one of the semi-major leasing companies, alongside [[JapanFG/fuyo-lease|芙蓉総合Lease]] / NEC Capital Solutions and others.

## 2. Business Lines

- **Information-equipment leasing (vendor lease)**: captive leasing for Ricoh multifunction printers, printers, and OA equipment
- **General equipment leasing**: machine tools, industrial machinery, OA equipment, and medical equipment
- **Collection agency services**: collection of utility bills and membership fees, one of the company's recurring revenue pillars
- **Factoring / purchase of trade receivables**: BtoB financial services
- **Real-estate-related leasing**: commercial facilities and offices
- **Environmental solutions**: solar PPA, LED, and other energy-saving equipment leasing
- **Overseas leasing**: leasing operations through Asian bases including China and Thailand
- **Credit / loans**: private cards and installment payment services

## 3. Strategy & Competitive Position

Ricoh Leasing occupies a distinctive hybrid position: Ricoh captive plus independent semi-major. Unlike the three major leasing groups ([[JapanFG/orix-corp|ORIX]] / Mitsubishi HC Capital / [[JapanFG/tokyo-century|東京Century]]), it has a vendor-lease base tied to Ricoh products while also securing stable non-lease revenue through collection agency services.

Compared with [[JapanFG/fuyo-lease|芙蓉総合Lease]], another Mizuho-side semi-major, Ricoh Leasing has a stronger captive color, collection agency business, and SME focus; Fuyo is more oriented toward large real estate, aircraft, and renewable energy, so the business mix differs. NEC Capital Solutions, as an NEC captive, is its peer in the "operating-company captive" category.

In recent years, **environmental solutions (solar / PPA)** and **overseas** expansion have been growth pillars, hedging against structural change in Ricoh's OA demand caused by paperless workflows. M&A has also been active, including factoring companies and regional captive-lease businesses.

In the [[finance/japan-acquisition-finance|Japan acquisition finance]] context, Ricoh Leasing can also support SME LBOs and equipment replacement finance.
