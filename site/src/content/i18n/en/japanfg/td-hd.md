## TL;DR

T&D Holdings is one of Japan's largest mid-tier life-insurance holding companies among **independent life insurers**. Its core feature is the **3 社-segment differentiation** among **Taiyo Life (founded 1893 ; household market), Daido Life (founded 1902 ; SME owner-manager market), and T&D Financial Life (bank counter sales)**. It was launched through the 2004-04  management integration and is listed on TSE PRIME 8795. It occupies a No. 5 -pole-style position after the major 4  life insurers ([[nippon-life]] / [[dai-ichi-life]] / Sumitomo Life / Meiji Yasuda Life). In 2020  it invested in Fortitude Group in the United States, moving toward a reinsurance platform, and its 2024  medium-term plan leaves room for overseas M&A expansion. The name "T&D" comes from **Taiyo + Daido**. ^[extracted]

### 3 -segment differentiation strategy (largest feature)

- **Taiyo Life**: household market focus, female sales-force channel, medical and cancer insurance, etc.
- **Daido Life**: specialized in the SME owner-manager market, including business succession and owner insurance; strong tax-accountant channel through its alliance with TKC National Federation ^[extracted]
- **T&D Financial Life**: specialized in bank counter sales, centered on savings-type and single-premium products

### Largest-class independent position

- A No. 5 -pole position after the major 4  life insurers ([[nippon-life]] / [[dai-ichi-life]] / Sumitomo Life / Meiji Yasuda Life)
- Operates as a **fully independent** insurer, neither under a megabank nor under a trading-company group

### Overseas strategy

- **Fortitude Group** (2020 onward): investment participation in a U.S. reinsurance platform, aiming to advance group-wide reinsurance and ALM sophistication
- **T&D United Capital**: expansion of alternative investment through a U.S. PE investment vehicle
- The 2024  medium-term plan explicitly sets a policy of **expanding overseas M&A** ^[extracted]

### DX / sales-force channel

- Bank counter sales are handled by T&D Financial Life, with functional specialization inside the group.

## 4. Regulation and policy

- **Main authority**: FSA
- **Insurance Business Act**: authorization as an insurance holding company under Article 271 -18 
- **Solvency-margin regulation**: applied to each of the domestic life insurers 3 社
- **Overseas regulation**: United States (Bermuda / New York, etc. for Fortitude-related operations), SEC and other regulation through related PE vehicles
- **Recent policy topics**:
  - 2024 onward: response to introduction of economic-value-based solvency regulation (ESR)
  - Bank counter sales fee-disclosure rules, affecting T&D Financial Life
