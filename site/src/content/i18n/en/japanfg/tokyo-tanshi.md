# Tokyo Tanshi

## Wiki Route

This entry sits under [[JapanFG/INDEX|JapanFG index]]. Read it against [[JapanFG/central-tanshi|セントラル短資株式会社 (Central Tanshi)]] for peer / contrast context and [[banking/INDEX|banking index]] for the broader system / regulatory boundary.

## TL;DR

Tokyo Tanshi is one of Japan's 3  major tanshi companies (unlisted, founded in 1908-09 , a long-established firm dating back to the late Meiji era). It handles inter-dealer functions in short-term money markets across 4  axes: **call-market brokerage + bill trading + Bank of Japan operation counterparty + corporate FX**. During the negative-rate-policy period from 2016-01 to 2024-03 , call-market functionality stagnated and the whole industry entered contraction, so the firm diversified revenue sources through foreign exchange and corporate FX brokerage. After the Bank of Japan ended negative interest rates in 2024-03-19 and raised the policy rate to 0.25% in 2024-07-31, short-term interest-rate markets normalized for the first time in 8  years, raising expectations for a tanshi-industry revival.

## 1. Company Overview

**Formal name**: Tokyo Tanshi Co., Ltd.
**English name**: Tokyo Tanshi Co., Ltd.
**Established**: 1908-09 (Meiji 41 , more than 117  years since founding)
**Form**: Unlisted stock company
**Head office**: Nihonbashi, Chuo-ku, Tokyo (traditional financial district around Kabutocho / Kayabacho)
**Business type**: Tanshi company (money market broker / inter-dealer broker)

### Position Among the 3  Major Tanshi Companies

The 3  major tanshi companies are the 3 社 that survived postwar tanshi-industry consolidation. They are recognized as counterparties for Bank of Japan open market operations and mainly broker inter-dealer transactions in the call market:

| Company | Short name | Founded | Features |
|---|---|---|---|
| [[central-tanshi]] | Central Tanshi | 1909 | Lineage of former Yamane Tanshi + Ueda Tanshi; regarded as the largest in the industry |
| [[ueda-yagi-tanshi]] | Ueda Yagi Tanshi | 1956 (merger) | Merger of former Ueda Tanshi + Yagi Tanshi; Kansai-linked capital |
| **Tokyo Tanshi** | TT | **1908-09** | **Oldest player, strengthening corporate FX brokerage** |

## 2. Business Segment Map

| Segment | Content | Customers |
|---|---|---|
| **Call-market brokerage** | Matching lenders and borrowers from unsecured / secured call O/N (overnight) to short tenors | Japanese banks, regional banks, trusts, life / non-life insurers, cooperative financial institutions, foreign bank Tokyo branches |
| **Bill trading brokerage** | Brokerage for CP (commercial paper), banker's acceptances, and similar instruments in circulation | Same as above + business corporations (issuers) |
| **BOJ operation counterparty** | Participation as counterparty in Bank of Japan open market operations (short-term JGB purchases, common-collateral funds-supplying operations, JGB repo / resale agreements, etc.) | Bank of Japan |
| **Corporate FX brokerage** | Interbank brokerage for large-lot FX and corporate hedging | Japanese banks, foreign banks, large-company treasury departments |
| **Short-term financial product services** | Repo transaction brokerage, bond lending transaction brokerage, etc. | Same as above |

Tanshi companies generally do not take large proprietary trading positions; **brokerage commissions are their main revenue source**. Under negative interest rates, interest-rate spreads disappeared and call-market balances collapsed, drying up call-brokerage commissions. Diversification into non-call businesses such as corporate FX and bond-related services became a common industry challenge.

### Difficulties During the Negative-Rate Period (2016-01 to 2024-03)

- Interbank surplus-fund investment needs were absorbed into the Bank of Japan current-account macro add-on balance / policy-rate balance 3  tier structure, structurally reducing call-market brokerage demand itself
- All 3  major tanshi companies responded through **headcount reduction, cost reduction, and strengthening non-call businesses (FX brokerage and bond-related services)**

### Negative-Rate Exit and Revival Expectations (2024-03 onward)

- **BOJ exit from negative interest rates in 2024-03-19** (from negative 0.1% to 0-0.1%) and abolition of YCC → first rate hike in 17  years
- **Policy-rate increase to 0.25% in 2024-07-31**
- Normalization of short-term rates → recovery of rate-formation function in the call market → expectations for larger call balances → revival scenario for the core business of the 3  major tanshi companies

### Competitive Structure

- **3 社 oligopoly**: consolidated into 3 社 through postwar restructuring; no new entrants and no exits
- **Competition with overseas IDBs**: global inter-dealer brokers such as TP ICAP and BGC handle some short-term financial products through Japan branches

## 4. Regulation / Policy

- **Primary supervisors**: Financial Services Agency (FSA) and Bank of Japan (as operation counterparty)
- **Industry association**: Tanshi Association (3  major tanshi companies + related tanshi-business institutions)
- **Extremely high linkage to BOJ policy**: changes in policy rates, YCC, and open market operation design directly affect industry revenue
- **Recent policy issues**:
  - 2024-03 onward BOJ policy-rate normalization (negative-rate exit → 0.25% → 0.50%) → expectations for call-market reactivation
  - Gradual reduction of excess reserves in BOJ current accounts (QT, quantitative tightening) → precondition for recovery of call-market balances
  - The **uncollateralized call O/N rate** is the market benchmark for the policy rate, making the brokerage function of tanshi companies part of the monetary-policy transmission channel
