# Iwaki Credit Cooperative

## TL;DR

Iwaki Credit Cooperative is a regional credit cooperative headquartered in Onahama Hanabatake-cho, Iwaki City, Fukushima Prefecture. It appears as FSA registry No. 20 under the Tohoku Local Finance Bureau and has corporate number 5380005005753. Its member base is small businesses and residents in Fukushima's Hamadori area, especially the Soma and Iwaki districts. It is one of the representative Tohoku credit cooperatives that continued operating in an area affected by the 2011 Great East Japan Earthquake and the Fukushima Daiichi nuclear accident. The cooperative is a member of [[JapanFG/zenshin-kumi|Zenshinkumiren]] and covered by [[JapanFG/dic|Deposit Insurance Corporation of Japan]]. Its core business is regional SME lending, reconstruction-related finance, Onahama Port fisheries finance, and Hamadori household / small-business banking. ^[extracted]

### Membership Eligibility ^[extracted]

As a regional credit cooperative, Iwaki Credit Cooperative uses the membership framework under the Small and Medium-Sized Enterprise Cooperatives Act:

1. **District requirement**: individuals and corporations that live, work, or maintain a business office in the district set by the cooperative's articles, mainly Fukushima Hamadori.
2. **Scale requirement**: small and medium-sized enterprises, generally 300 or fewer employees or ¥300M or less in capital, with industry-specific variations.
3. **Business field**: no single industry restriction; the cooperative is regional rather than trade-specific.

### Position Within Zenshinkumiren

- Member of the Tohoku block of [[JapanFG/zenshin-kumi|Zenshinkumiren]].
- One of 15 Tohoku credit cooperatives: Aomori 1, Iwate 2, Miyagi 3, Akita 1, Yamagata 4, and Fukushima 4.
- System ATM coordination and surplus-fund investment route through Zenshinkumiren.
- Belongs to the regional-credit-cooperative side of the industry's large-city versus regional-cooperative structure.

### Business Characteristics of a Regional Shinkumi

- **Onahama Port**: lending to fisheries, seafood processing, and port-logistics businesses.
- **Former Joban coalfield area**: finance for regional businesses navigating energy-transition effects.
- **Tourism**: finance for recovery and reputation-repair phases around areas such as Iwaki Yumoto Onsen.
- **Housing loans**: reconstruction public housing and self-rebuilt housing finance.

## 5. Supervision and Regulation

- **Supervisor**: FSA and Tohoku Local Finance Bureau.
- **Legal basis**: [[regulators-japan/financial-laws-stack|Small and Medium-Sized Enterprise Cooperatives Act]] and the Act on Financial Businesses by Cooperatives.
- **Deposit insurance**: covered by [[JapanFG/dic|Deposit Insurance Corporation of Japan]] under the ordinary financial-institution framework, up to ¥10M plus interest for protected deposits.
- **Current policy issues**:
  - rebuilding regional finance demand after the completion of the disaster-reconstruction phase;
  - shrinking member base caused by depopulation and aging;
  - cost of strengthened AML / CFT compliance.
