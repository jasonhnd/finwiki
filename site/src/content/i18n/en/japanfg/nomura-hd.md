# Nomura Holdings (Nomura HD)

## Wiki route

This entry sits under [[JapanFG/INDEX|JapanFG index]]. Read it against [[JapanFG/daiwa-sg|大和証券グループ本社 (Daiwa Securities Group)]] for peer / contrast context and [[securities/INDEX|securities index]] for the broader system / regulatory boundary.

## TL;DR

Japan's largest independent securities group by net revenue. It originated from "Nomura Securities," which became independent from the securities department of Osaka Nomura Bank in 1925-12-25, and was launched as a holding company in 2001-10-01. **Domestic retail (about 130 branches) + wholesale (IB / Markets) + investment management (Nomura AM, domestic No.2  class) + Instinet (U.S. electronic trading) are the 4  axes**. In 2008-10  it acquired Lehman Brothers' Asia / Europe / Middle East operations for about 2 億 dollars and accelerated globalization, while maintaining an "independent" position different from megabank-affiliated securities firms ([[mufg]] MUMSS, [[smfg]] SMBC Nikko, [[mizuho-fg]] Mizuho Securities). ^[extracted]

### Founding / predecessors

- 1872 年 Tokushichi Nomura I founded the money-changing business "Nomura Shoten" in Osaka
- 1904  Tokushichi Nomura II succeeded the family business
- 1918  Osaka Nomura Bank established (one ancestor line of today's [[JapanFG/resona-hd|rsh]] Resona HD)
- **1925-12-25** Osaka Nomura Bank's **securities department** became independent → **Nomura Securities** established by Tokushichi Nomura II ^[extracted]
- 1946  Postwar reconstruction, not subject to zaibatsu dissolution because it was in the securities business
- 1961  Listed on the Tokyo Stock Exchange (old Nomura Securities)
- 2001-10-01 Nomura HD established by share transfer, making Nomura Securities a wholly owned subsidiary

## 2. Business segment map

> **2025-04-01 divisional reorganization**: A Banking division was added to the previous 4  divisions (Retail / Wholesale / Investment Management / Others), shifting to a **5 -division structure** ^[extracted]

| Segment | Major operators | Features |
|---|---|---|
| Domestic retail (Retail division) | Nomura Securities domestic | About 130 branches; combined face-to-face + online; strong affluent and retiree base |
| Wholesale - Global Markets | Nomura Securities / Nomura International plc / Nomura Securities International | Equities, bonds, FX, derivatives; Tokyo / London / NY 3 -base operations |
| Wholesale - Investment Banking | Same as above | M&A advisory and DCM/ECM; direct competition with bank-affiliated IBs |
| **Banking division (newly established 2025-04 )** | Nomura Trust and Banking + related subsidiaries | **Consolidates corporate / affluent banking operations and integrates group-wide funding / credit functions** ^[extracted] |
| Electronic trading | Instinet, LLC | U.S. equity algorithms and dark pools for institutional investors |
| Investment Management | Nomura Asset Management | Public investment trusts No.2  class, strength in ETFs (NEXT FUNDS), **expanding global AUM through acquisition of Macquarie U.S. / European asset-management business (contract 2025-04 , 18 億 USD)** ^[extracted] |
| Trust / pensions | Nomura Trust and Banking / Nomura Fiduciary | Wrap accounts and pension consulting |
| Merchant banking | Nomura Capital Partners, etc. | PE investment and principal investment |
| Research | Nomura Research Institute (NRI, listed 4307, equity-method stake around 24%) | Think tank + IT, independently listed |

### Wholesale revival

- **"One Nomura" strategy** (from 2013-04〜): Eliminated divisional silos and integrated global management. Response to cultural friction after the Lehman acquisition
- **U.S. / Europe Equity shrinkage → focus on rates / FX / Macro** (from 2016〜): Cost reductions around 10 億 dollars and large European headcount reductions
- **Rebuilding after Archegos loss** (from 2021〜): Reduced prime brokerage and strengthened risk-management governance
- **Asia focus**: Singapore / Hong Kong / India as growth axes. The Asia network inherited from Lehman is the base

### Retail

- **About 130 branches**: Among the largest in Japanese securities. Competes with megabank-affiliated securities firms (MUMSS, etc.) in customer assets
- **New NISA (from 2024-01〜)**: Tailwind for inflows of individual money and expansion of assets under management
- **Online x face-to-face hybrid**: Nomura Net & Call plus face-to-face branches

### Investment Management

- **Nomura AM**: Domestic No.2  class in public investment trusts, top-tier domestic ETF franchise (NEXT FUNDS)
- **★ Acquisition of Macquarie U.S. / European asset-management business** (contract in 2025-04-22, **18 億 USD**): Takes over the U.S. + European public / private asset-management businesses of Australia's Macquarie Asset Management, expanding Nomura AM's global AUM at once. A rare "simultaneous U.S. and Europe entry" deal for a Japanese AM ^[extracted]
- **Wealth Management**: Expanding discretionary management, SMA / wrap accounts for affluent clients
- **Alternative investments**: PE / infrastructure / real-estate funds developed through group merchant banking

### Banking division (newly established 2025-04 )

- **2025-04-01 transition to 5 -division structure**: Added a **Banking division** to the previous 4  divisions, consolidating group-wide banking operations (trust / corporate lending / financing for affluent clients) ^[extracted]

### Global 3 -base model

- **Tokyo (head office)**: Japan's largest securities firm, retail + wholesale headquarters
- **London**: Nomura International plc, Europe / Middle East IB / Markets hub
- **New York**: Nomura Securities International, U.S. institutional investors, Instinet electronic trading
- **Asia**: Singapore / Hong Kong / India / Indonesia, based on the network inherited from Lehman

## 4. Regulation & policy

- **Supervisor**: FSA (securities business) + Japan Securities Dealers Association (self-regulation)
- **Overseas regulation**: U.S. SEC / FINRA, U.K. FCA / PRA, Hong Kong SFC, Singapore MAS, etc.
- **Recent policy issues**:
  - 2024〜 New NISA expansion (doubling investment limits) → individual money inflows
  - 2025〜 Stablecoin / tokenized securities regulation
  - 2025〜 Customer-oriented business conduct (revision to FSA "Principles for Customer-Oriented Business Conduct")
