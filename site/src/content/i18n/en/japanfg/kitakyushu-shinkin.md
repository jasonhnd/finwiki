## TL;DR

Kitakyushu Shinkin Bank (Kitashin) is a leading Kyushu shinkin bank headquartered in Kokurakita-ku, Kitakyushu City, Fukuoka Prefecture, with Kitakyushu City, eastern Fukuoka Prefecture, and part of western Yamaguchi Prefecture as its main operating base. It is a cooperative financial institution whose core businesses are loans to SME subcontractors and SMEs in the Kitakyushu industrial belt, including steel, chemicals, ports, and logistics, as well as housing loans and individual deposits. Within Fukuoka Prefecture, the financial market is divided between the Fukuoka City side, centered on [[JapanFG/fukuoka-bank|福岡銀行]] and [[JapanFG/nishi-nippon-city-bank|西日本シティ銀行]], and the Kitakyushu City side, including [[JapanFG/kitakyushu-bank|北九州銀行]] and Kitakyushu Shinkin Bank. This page is the cooperative-organization layer anchor for the Kitakyushu City side.

## 2. Operating model

Kitakyushu Shinkin Bank centers on SME loans, working capital, equipment finance, housing loans, and individual deposits for SMEs and sole proprietors in Kitakyushu City, including steel, chemicals, machinery, logistics, port-related businesses, construction, and services. Kitakyushu City is one of Japan's four major industrial belts, the Kitakyushu industrial belt, and hosts concentrations of large companies such as Nippon Steel & Sumitomo Metal, now [[JapanFG/nippon-steel|日本製鉄]], Mitsubishi Chemical, and TOTO. Loans to the secondary and tertiary SME suppliers of these large companies are a defining feature of the bank.

The structure of Fukuoka Prefecture's financial market can be organized as follows:
- **Fukuoka City side**: [[JapanFG/fukuoka-fg|ふくおか FG]] ([[JapanFG/fukuoka-bank|福岡銀行]] = Kyushu's largest regional bank) + [[JapanFG/nishi-nippon-fh|西日本 FH]] ([[JapanFG/nishi-nippon-city-bank|西日本シティ銀行]])
- **Kitakyushu City side**: [[JapanFG/kitakyushu-bank|北九州銀行]] (under Yamaguchi FG, second-tier regional bank) + Kitakyushu Shinkin Bank (cooperative organization)
- **Other regional banks in the prefecture**: [[JapanFG/sumitomo-mitsui-trust|三井住友信託]] Fukuoka base, etc.

The Kitakyushu metropolitan area is strongly affected by population decline and industrial restructuring, so responding to structural change in regional SME finance is a strategic issue for the bank.

## 5. Related / Regional Issues

### Connection with the Kitakyushu industrial belt

- Concentration of large companies such as [[JapanFG/nippon-steel|日本製鉄]] Yawata Works, [[JapanFG/toto|TOTO]] (headquartered in Kitakyushu City), and Mitsubishi Chemical.
- Many secondary and tertiary suppliers to these large companies are SMEs, making them core customers of Kitakyushu Shinkin Bank.
- The bank carries an SME portfolio affected by steel-industry structural adjustment and decarbonization related to [[banking/japan-decarbonization-financing-roadmap|Japan decarbonization financing roadmap]].

### Kitakyushu City's population decline and regional economy

Kitakyushu City is one of the ordinance-designated cities where population decline is especially visible, and financial responses to structural changes among local SMEs, including business succession, closures, and restructuring, are an important issue for the bank. Value-added businesses such as business-succession M&A brokerage and regional PE collaboration have also been strengthened in recent years.

### Inside Fukuoka Prefecture versus cross-border links with neighboring prefectures (Yamaguchi and Oita)

Kitakyushu City sits at the eastern edge of Fukuoka Prefecture and neighbors Shimonoseki City, Yamaguchi Prefecture, across the Kanmon Strait. Kitakyushu Shinkin Bank's operating district includes eastern Fukuoka Prefecture plus part of western Yamaguchi Prefecture, giving it a cross-prefectural operating structure.

### Kyushu city-anchor peer comparison

As a city-anchor shinkin in the Kyushu-Okinawa block, it is positioned alongside Fukuoka City-side Fukuoka City-system shinkin banks, the [[JapanFG/kumamoto-bank|熊本銀行]] customer shinkin bank on the Kumamoto City side, and district-anchor shinkin banks in Kagoshima, Miyazaki, and Okinawa. This page is positioned as the representative anchor for the Kitakyushu metropolitan area.
