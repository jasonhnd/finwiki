# Non-life Insurance Policyholders Protection Corporation of Japan

## Wiki route

This entry sits under [[JapanFG/INDEX|JapanFG index]]. Read it against [[JapanFG/seimei-hokensha-hogo-kiko|生命保険契約者保護機構]] for parallel safety-net peer and [[JapanFG/dic|預金保険機構]] / [[JapanFG/fsa|金融庁]] for the broader system / regulatory boundary.

## TL;DR

An **authorized corporation for policyholder protection whose members are all non-life insurance companies operating in Japan**. It was launched when the amended Insurance Business Act took effect in 1998-12 . It is the final safety net for **continuing the contracts of failed non-life insurers, paying insurance claims, and assuming contracts**. Its funding consists of member "contributions" and government "subsidies" when special measures apply. It responded to the **wave of non-life insurer failures from the late 1990 年s to the early 2000 年s, including [[JapanFG/dai-ichi-mutual|第一火災海上]] 2000-05 , [[JapanFG/taisei-kasai|大成火災海上]] 2001-11 , and [[JapanFG/taisho-kasai|大正海上]] 1996 **. Current members are roughly 24 社 domestic non-life insurers. Together with the life-insurance version, [[JapanFG/seimei-hokensha-hogo-kiko|生命保険契約者保護機構]], it is one of Japan's two major insurance safety nets.

## 1. Legal basis / establishment

**Official name**: Non-life Insurance Policyholders Protection Corporation of Japan
**English name**: Non-life Insurance Policyholders Protection Corporation of Japan
**Legal basis**: **Insurance Business Act, Article 259  and following** (amended in 1995 年 and fully effective 1998-12 )
**Established**: **1998-12-01**
**Head office**: Kanda-Awajicho, Chiyoda-ku, Tokyo
**Entity type**: Authorized corporation
**Supervisors**: [[JapanFG/fsa|金融庁]] and the Ministry of Finance, which has legal jurisdiction

### Membership obligation

- **All non-life insurance companies operating in Japan** are required to join, including Japan branches of foreign non-life insurers but excluding life-only insurers.
- Major 4 社 groups ([[JapanFG/tokio-marine-hd|東京海上日動]] / [[JapanFG/sompo-japan|損保ジャパン]] / [[JapanFG/mitsui-sumitomo|三井住友海上]] / [[JapanFG/aioi-nissay-dowa-insurance|あいおいニッセイ同和]]) + midsize insurers + foreign insurers + mutual-aid groups = **about 24 社**, depending on timing.

### Key timeline

| Date | Event |
|---|---|
| 1995-06  | Full amendment of the Insurance Business Act enacted; the old law was 1939 |
| **1998-12** | **New Insurance Business Act takes effect and the Non-life Insurance Policyholders Protection Corporation of Japan launches** |
| 2000-05  | **[[JapanFG/dai-ichi-mutual|第一火災海上]] fails** -> first full-scale rescue case for the corporation |
| 2001-11  | **[[JapanFG/taisei-kasai|大成火災海上]] fails** -> debate on strengthening the corporation's fiscal base |
| 2002-06  | Insurance Business Act amendment -> extension and expansion of special government-subsidy measures |
| 2010-04  | Review of contribution system amid frequent natural disasters |
| 2011-03  | Great East Japan Earthquake -> discussion of complementarity with the earthquake-insurance reinsurance scheme |
| 2024 onward | Midsize non-life insurer integration debate and fiscal stability of the corporation |

## 2. Operating scheme

| Function | Content |
|---|---|
| **Assumption of contracts of failed insurers** | If no rescue insurer appears, the corporation itself, or through a successor company, assumes the contracts |
| **Financial assistance to rescue insurers** | Provides **cash grants** when a rescue insurer assumes a failed insurer |
| **Compensation for insurance-claim payments** | **Compensates a fixed percentage** of insurance claims that had arisen at the time of failure |
| **Compensation for surrender values and similar items** | **Compensates a fixed percentage** of surrender values, maturity refunds, and similar claims |
| **Collection of contributions** | Collects from member non-life insurers each fiscal year, allocated by net premiums and similar measures |
| **Borrowing** | Borrows from financial institutions when needed, with government guarantee |

### Compensation levels by major line

| Insurance line | Compensation level |
|---|---|
| **Compulsory automobile liability insurance** | **100%**, prioritized because it is compulsory insurance |
| Earthquake insurance | **100%**, under the government reinsurance scheme |
| Voluntary automobile insurance | Accidents occurring within 3  months after failure are covered at 100%; thereafter **80%** |
| Fire insurance for individuals | **80%**, with 100% for the first 3  months after failure |
| Liability insurance | 80% |
| Marine, transportation, credit, and other corporate insurance | **80%** |

Compensation levels vary by contract type and timing of failure. See the corporation's official website for current institutional details.

## 3. Funding structure

```
Revenue
  ├── Contributions (paid by member 24 社, allocated by net premiums)
  ├── Borrowings (when needed, with government guarantee)
  └── Government subsidies (when special measures apply, under supplementary provisions of the Insurance Business Act)

Expenditure
  ├── Financial assistance to rescue insurers
  ├── Operating costs of successor insurers
  ├── Direct compensation payments to policyholders
  └── Corporation operating costs
```

### Contribution calculation

- Allocated based on each non-life insurer's **net premiums written**.
- Major 4  groups ([[JapanFG/tokio-marine-hd|東京海上]] / [[JapanFG/sompo-japan|損保ジャパン]] / [[JapanFG/mitsui-sumitomo|三井住友海上]] / [[JapanFG/aioi-nissay-dowa-insurance|あいおいニッセイ同和]]) contribute more than half.
- The corporation basically does not hold a thick permanent reserve fund and is structured to respond through ex-post burden sharing at the time of failure, similar to the life-insurance version.

## 4. Past rescue cases

| Date | Failed non-life insurer | Processing scheme |
|---|---|---|
| 1996  | Taisho Marine and Fire | Rescue merger, assumed by Sumitomo Marine |
| **2000-05** | **[[JapanFG/dai-ichi-mutual|第一火災海上]]** | First full-scale rescue by the corporation, with financial assistance |
| **2001-11** | **[[JapanFG/taisei-kasai|大成火災海上]]** | Corporation involvement; assumed by the Sompo Japan lineage, formerly Yasuda Fire |
| 2008  | Yamato Life, a life insurer and therefore an [[JapanFG/seimei-hokensha-hogo-kiko|生保機構]] case | — |

> Since the mid-2000s, failure cases have fallen sharply as midsize non-life insurers consolidated, including the formation processes of [[JapanFG/sompo-japan|損保ジャパン]] / [[JapanFG/aioi-nissay-dowa-insurance|あいおいニッセイ同和]].

## 5. International comparison / issues

- Similar to the U.S. **State Guaranty Fund** system under **NAIC** guidelines, but Japan has a single nationwide institution, while the U.S. structure is state-by-state.
- In contrast with the U.K. **FSCS (Financial Services Compensation Scheme)**, which integrates bank and insurance compensation, Japan separates deposit insurance, the life-insurance corporation, and the non-life-insurance corporation.
  - Reviewing contribution systems under climate-related risk, including more frequent and severe natural disasters.
  - Developing compensation schemes as new insurance lines such as cyber insurance and pet insurance expand.
  - Debates over member-count decline and fiscal base as non-life insurer consolidation progresses.
