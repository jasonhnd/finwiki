# Japan Post Holdings Co., Ltd.

## Wiki route

This entry sits under [[JapanFG/INDEX|JapanFG index]]. Read it against [[JapanFG/japan-post-bank|Japan Post Bank]] and [[JapanFG/kampo-life|Japan Post Insurance]] for its main financial subsidiaries, and against [[JapanFG/fsa|金融庁]] / [[JapanFG/boj|日本銀行]] for the broader system / regulatory boundary.

## TL;DR

**Japan Post Group's holding company, listed on the TSE Prime Market as 6178.** It integrates the postal ([[JapanFG/japan-post-co|日本郵便]]), banking ([[JapanFG/japan-post-bank|Japan Post Bank]]), and life-insurance ([[JapanFG/kampo-life|Japan Post Insurance]]) businesses under one group. The sequence **2005  Postal Privatization Act -> 2007-10  holding-company structure -> 2015-11  simultaneous listing of three companies (triple IPO)** made it one of Japan's largest postwar privatization projects. By law, the Japanese government, represented by the Minister of Finance, must hold **more than 1  of 3  of voting rights** and still holds a majority. For subsidiaries [[JapanFG/japan-post-bank|Japan Post Bank]] / [[JapanFG/kampo-life|Japan Post Insurance]], Japan Post Holdings has a policy of gradually reducing its ownership ratios. Recent issues include the 2019 [[JapanFG/kampo-life|Japan Post Insurance]] improper sales problem and the 2024-2025 [[JapanFG/japan-post-bank|Japan Post Bank]] ownership-ratio reduction / growth-strategy rebuild.

## 1. Entity structure

**Legal name**: Japan Post Holdings Co., Ltd.
**English name**: Japan Post Holdings Co., Ltd.
**Securities code**: **6178** (Tokyo Stock Exchange Prime Market)
**Established**: **2006-01-23** (as a special company under the Japan Post Holdings Co., Ltd. Act)
**Business start**: **2007-10-01** (dissolution of Japan Post and conversion to holding-company structure)
**Headquarters**: Otemachi, Chiyoda-ku, Tokyo
**Major shareholder**: **Minister of Finance** (must hold more than 1  of 3  = statutory duty)
**Business type**: Special company (stock company, listed)
**Legal basis**: **Japan Post Holdings Co., Ltd. Act** (平成 17 年 Act 第 98 号)

### Group structure

```
Japan Post Holdings Co., Ltd. (6178, Minister of Finance holds more than 1  of 3 )
  |-- [[JapanFG/japan-post-co|日本郵便株式会社]] (postal / logistics, 100%)
  |-- [[JapanFG/japan-post-bank|Japan Post Bank]] (7182, listed, majority owned)
  `-- [[JapanFG/kampo-life|Japan Post Insurance]] (7181, listed, majority owned)
```

Note: Japan Post Holdings plans to reduce its [[JapanFG/japan-post-bank|Japan Post Bank]] / [[JapanFG/kampo-life|Japan Post Insurance]] ownership ratios in stages.

### Statutory ownership duty

- **Japan Post Holdings Co., Ltd. Act**: The government must hold **more than 1  of 3 ** of Japan Post Holdings shares.
- Japan Post Holdings is gradually reducing its holdings of [[JapanFG/japan-post-bank|Japan Post Bank]] / [[JapanFG/kampo-life|Japan Post Insurance]] shares, aiming for full privatization of the financial 2 社.

### Government ownership ratio trend

| Timing | Government (Minister of Finance) ownership ratio |
|---|---|
| At 2015-11  IPO | 80.49% |
| After the 2017-09  2  secondary offering | About 57% |
| After the 2021-10  3  secondary offering | About 60.6% -> buybacks and offerings overlap |

### Triple IPO (2015-11-04)

- **One of Japan's largest postwar simultaneous IPOs**: parent-child simultaneous listing of Japan Post Holdings, [[JapanFG/japan-post-bank|Japan Post Bank]], and [[JapanFG/kampo-life|Japan Post Insurance]].
- Total offering size of about 1.4 兆円 (combined 3 社).
- Became a test case for parent-subsidiary listing governance and a starting point for later [[corporate-strategy/parent-subsidiary-listing|Parent Subsidiary Listing]] debate.
- Under the Reconstruction Funding Securing Act, part of sale proceeds was allocated to Great East Japan Earthquake reconstruction funding.

### Kampo improper sales problem (2019)

- Large-scale discovery of excessive sales and inappropriate policy-switch handling for elderly customers.
- More than 18 万 customer cases involving inappropriate contracts.
- 2019-12 [[JapanFG/fsa|金融庁]] business improvement order and senior-management resignations.
- Group-wide governance rebuilding and sales-culture transformation are in progress.

### Capital and business alliance with Rakuten (2021 onward)

- Japan Post Group, including [[JapanFG/japan-post-bank|Japan Post Bank]], invests in Rakuten Group (4755).
- Collaboration in logistics, mobile, and financial fields.

### Progress on financial 2 社 privatization

- Under the Japan Post Holdings Co., Ltd. Act, full privatization of [[JapanFG/japan-post-bank|Japan Post Bank]] / [[JapanFG/kampo-life|Japan Post Insurance]] is the goal.
- As of 2025 , Japan Post Holdings still holds majorities in both companies, so continued secondary offerings remain a policy issue.
- If full privatization is achieved, coexistence with universal-service obligations will become a key issue.
