# SBI Sumishin Net Bank -> Docomo SMTB Net Bank

## Wiki route

This entry sits under [[JapanFG/INDEX|JapanFG index]]. Read it against [[JapanFG/gmo-aozora-net|GMO あおぞらネット銀行]] for peer / contrast context and [[banking/INDEX|banking index]] for the broader system / regulatory boundary.

## TL;DR

A former internet bank that went through a dramatic parent-company replacement: **2025-09-25 TSE Standard delisting -> 2025-10-01 consolidation as an NTT Docomo subsidiary -> 2025-10-02 full dissolution of the SBI HD capital relationship -> scheduled renaming to "Docomo SMTB Net Bank" on 2026-08-03**. As a Sumitomo Mitsui Trust + SBI HD 50:50  JV, it opened on 2007-09-24, listed on PRIME on 2023-03-29, and after only 2  and a half years NTT Docomo acquired SBI HD's stake. With **FY2024 (period 2025-03 ) consolidated net income of 281 億円, ordinary revenue of 1,465 億円, deposits of 9 兆 8,216 億円, and 825 万 accounts**, it is second in scale in the internet-bank industry after [[banking/rakuten-bank|rakuten-bank]]. **Internet-bank No.1 -class home-loan handling volume** and **one of Japan's largest BaaS (NEOBANK) providers** are its 2  core axes. It is a rare case where the bank that had been the backend for "docomo NEOBANK / d Smart Bank" was absorbed by its partner [[ndfg]] itself.

> NEOBANK is an API that lets business operators (airlines / telecoms / retailers / real estate / life insurers) provide banking functions such as ordinary deposits, transfers, and card loans to customers under their own brands. SBI Sumishin supplies the banking license and backend, while the front surface is completed through the operator's app / brand.

### Strategic Implications of the Parent-Company Replacement (2025)

- **NTT Docomo side**: The acquisition motive was to "catch up with other telecom carriers in banking." It establishes an in-house financial base to compete with au Jibun Bank (KDDI line) and PayPay Bank (SoftBank line).
- **SBI HD side**: Recovers funds by selling the SBI Sumishin stake and concentrates retail strategy on SBI Shinsei Bank (former Shinsei Bank).
- **Sumitomo Mitsui Trust side**: The partner changes from SBI HD to NTT Docomo. With voting rights kept at 50:50 , management influence is unchanged.

### Core Differentiation

- **Home-loan specialization -> 2  axes of BaaS expansion**: In the early period, the bank captured real demand through home loans, including Flat 35  / variable-rate products; later it developed BaaS as a 2  axis.
- **BaaS as the largest differentiator from other internet banks**: [[banking/paypay-bank|paypay-bank]] / [[banking/rakuten-bank|rakuten-bank]] / au Jibun Bank are centered on cross-selling inside their own economic zones, while SBI Sumishin uses an external-sales model.
- **New parent NTT Docomo economic zone**: After delisting, deeper linkage with d Point and Docomo mobile-line customers 9,000 万 is expected.
- **Sumitomo Mitsui Trust-line backbone**: Access to trust, pension, and real-estate trust functions is a strength other internet banks do not have.

### Partnership / Acquisition Strategy (from 2025-10  onward)

- **NTT Docomo (new largest shareholder)**: From 2025-10-01 onward, a consolidated subsidiary and core financial function under [[ndfg]]. docomo NEOBANK is promoted from a "BaaS partnership" to an "in-house bank service."
- **Sumitomo Mitsui Trust Bank (joint parent company)**: The JV partner since establishment; provision of trust and pension infrastructure continues.
- **SBI HD (capital relationship dissolved)**: Fully dissolved on 2025-10-02. The future of "Hybrid Deposit" with SBI Securities and crypto-asset linkage APIs with SBI VC Trade is not yet fixed.
- **Past links with the former parent SBI line**: How existing API links with SBI Securities / SBI VC Trade continue or shrink is the issue.

## 4. Regulation / Policy

- **Primary regulator**: Financial Services Agency (FSA)
- **License**: Ordinary bank license under the Banking Act
- **2025  TOB**: NTT Docomo became the largest shareholder after Banking Act Article 52  9 approval for a bank major shareholder and prior Antimonopoly Act filing.
- **Crypto-asset linkage**: Continuity of the API with SBI VC Trade is an issue after dissolution of the SBI capital relationship.
- **BaaS regulation**: From 2022 onward, institutional development around bank agency business, electronic payment service providers, licensed financial instruments intermediary business, and related categories remains an ongoing issue.
- **Recent policy issues**:
  - From 2024 onward: BOJ policy-rate normalization -> improved loan-deposit spreads
  - From 2024 onward: NISA expansion -> accelerated fund inflow through online securities linkage
  - From 2025 onward: internet-bank response to the stablecoin framework, centered on megabank Progmat
