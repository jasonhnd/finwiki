# Japan Finance Organization for Municipalities (JFM)

## Wiki Route

This entry sits under [[JapanFG/INDEX|JapanFG index]]. Read it against [[JapanFG/jfc|日本政策金融公庫 (JFC)]] for peer / contrast context and [[banking/INDEX|banking index]] for the broader system / regulatory boundary.

## TL;DR

JFM is a **joint local-government corporation** specializing in long-term lending to local governments (prefectures, municipalities, and wards). It was established in 2008-08-01 as the Japan Finance Organization for Municipal Enterprises and reorganized under its current name in 2009-06-01. It inherited the operations of the former **Japan Finance Corporation for Municipal Enterprises** (established 1957-06 , abolished 2008-09-30). Its investors are all 47  prefectures plus all municipalities and wards through joint local-government investment, with no national-government equity. Its loan balance is **about 22.7 兆円** as of FY令和6年 end / 2025-03 end, per JFM IR, making it long-term funding infrastructure for local public finance. It has both **competitive and complementary relationships with private megabanks and regional banks**, and plays a distinct role in disaster-recovery lending and lending to local public enterprises such as water and sewerage businesses.

## 1. Corporate Structure

**Official name**: Japan Finance Organization for Municipalities ^[extracted]
**English name**: Japan Finance Organization for Municipalities (JFM) ^[extracted]
**Entity form**: Joint local-government corporation, a special corporation based on the Japan Finance Organization for Municipalities Act ^[extracted]
**Established**: 2008-08-01 as the Japan Finance Organization for Municipal Enterprises ^[extracted]
**Reorganized under current name**: 2009-06-01, renamed Japan Finance Organization for Municipalities and expanded in business scope ^[extracted]
**Head office**: Kojimachi, Chiyoda-ku, Tokyo ^[extracted]
**Investors**: All 47  prefectures plus all municipalities and wards, through joint local-government investment with no national-government equity ^[extracted]

### Governance Design ^[extracted]

- **President**: Selected from the local-government side; many past leaders have local-government backgrounds
- **Representatives meeting**: Supervised by representatives of the National Governors' Association, Japan Association of City Mayors, and National Association of Towns and Villages
- **Management deliberation committee**: External experts provide management review

### Business Scope ^[extracted]

| Business | Description |
|---|---|
| Local bond underwriting | Underwrites and manages prefectural and municipal local bonds at long maturities and low rates |
| Lending to local public enterprises | Long-term lending to water, sewerage, electric power, transport, hospital, and other local public enterprises |
| Disaster-recovery lending | Local-government lending for recovery from major earthquakes, typhoons, and other disasters |
| Funding | Issues bonds in domestic and overseas capital markets without a government guarantee: JFM bonds |
| Management support | Fiscal analysis and management advice for local governments and local public enterprises |

### Loan Balance

- About **22.7 兆円** at FY令和6年 end (2025-03 end), per JFM IR / JFM bond IR materials
- One of the three major funding sources for local-bond underwriting, alongside private banks and fiscal loan funds

| Date | Event |
|---|---|

## 3. Business Segment Map

| Segment | Main target | Features |
|---|---|---|
| General local-bond underwriting | Prefectures, municipalities, and wards | Competes with and complements private banks and fiscal loan funds |
| Water and sewerage lending | Local public enterprises | Long-term low-rate lending, lower than private-sector rates |
| Transport lending | Local public transport, including buses and subways | Supports low-profitability public transport maintenance |
| Hospital lending | Public hospitals and local independent administrative institutions | Long-term funding for local medical care |
| Electric / gas lending | Local public electric and gas businesses | Independent local public enterprises in some regions |
| Disaster-recovery lending | Disaster-hit local governments and public enterprises | Special lending for major earthquakes, typhoons, and other disasters |
| Funding | JFM bonds | No government guarantee. Issuer ratings Moody's A1 / S&P A+ / R&I AA+ (all stable, JFM IR)

### Competitive and Complementary Relationships

```
Funding for local governments and local public enterprises
  ├── Japan Finance Organization for Municipalities (JFM) -- long-term, low-rate, policy finance
  ├── Fiscal loan funds (Ministry of Finance) -- direct national-government lending, long-term and low-rate
  ├── Private financial institutions
  │     ├── [[mufg]] -- major metropolitan local governments and large local public enterprises
  │     ├── [[smfg]] -- major metropolitan local governments and Kansai-region local governments
  │     ├── [[mizuho-fg]] -- major metropolitan local governments and traditional local-government lending
  │     └── Regional and second-tier regional banks -- main banks for local governments
  └── Capital markets (local bond issuance) -- major cities are increasingly using market funding
```

### Background of Abolishing the Old Corporation and Moving to a Joint Local-Government Model

- **Koizumi administration special-corporation reform** from 2001- made national-equity policy finance institutions reorganization targets
- The **2008-09-30 abolition of the old corporation and 2008-08-01 establishment of the new organization** effectively continued the operations under a new structure

### Relationship with Private Financial Institutions

- **Complementarity**: Long-term lending to small local governments and public enterprises with low profitability, where private banks are less active
- **Rate advantage**: Joint local-government investment plus high issuer ratings (Moody's A1 / S&P A+ / R&I AA+) create low funding costs, allowing JFM to provide lower rates than private banks
- **Megabank strategy**: [[mufg]] [[smfg]] [[mizuho-fg]] handle major metropolitan local governments through syndicated loans and marketable local-bond underwriting

### Special Role in Disaster-Recovery Lending

- Funding needs at local governments and public enterprises rise sharply after major earthquakes, typhoons, and other disasters
- JFM provides long-term low-rate lending that private financial institutions alone would struggle to supply
- It has played an important role after the Great East Japan Earthquake (2011), Kumamoto Earthquake (2016), Noto Peninsula Earthquake (2024), and other disasters

### Recent Policy Issues

- **2024  Noto Peninsula Earthquake recovery**: full-scale response through earthquake-recovery lending ^[extracted]

## 5. Supervision and Regulation

- **Governing law**: **Japan Finance Organization for Municipalities Act**, enacted 2007 年 and effective 2008  ^[extracted]
- **Supervision**: Representatives meeting of JFM, made up of representatives of the governors', mayors', and towns / villages associations, plus a management deliberation committee
- **Audit**: Subject to Board of Audit review as a joint local-government corporation
- **Ratings**: Moody's A1 / S&P A+ / R&I AA+ (all stable, 2024-2025, JFM IR). Although its bonds are not government-guaranteed, the credit strength from joint local-government investment places it at one of the highest levels among Japanese public bond issuers

### Relationship with Public Financial Institutions

- **Japan Finance Corporation (JFC)**: SME and agriculture / forestry / fisheries lending; different borrower target from JFM
- **Development Bank of Japan (DBJ)**: Private large-company and infrastructure lending; different borrower target from JFM
- **Japan Bank for International Cooperation (JBIC)**: Overseas finance; JFM clearly focuses on domestic local governments
