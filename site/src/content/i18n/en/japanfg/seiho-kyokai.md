# Life Insurance Association of Japan (Seiho Kyokai / 生命保険協会)

## TL;DR

Seiho Kyokai (Life Insurance Association of Japan, LIAJ) is the industry body for Japan's life-insurance underwriters licensed under the Insurance Business Act. Its membership covers major domestic life insurers, mutual insurers, and Japanese subsidiaries of foreign-affiliated life insurers. Like other Japanese industry bodies, it is a general incorporated association, not the regulator and not a statutory SRO, but its joint guidelines on sales conduct, customer protection, ESR transition support, and disclosure operate as industry de facto standards.

## Legal route / statutory position

Life insurers underwrite under the Insurance Business Act (Act No. 105  of 1995),[^ins-act] and are licensed and supervised by the FSA under the insurance supervision guideline.[^fsa-ins] LIAJ is a general incorporated association of life insurers. It is **not** an FIEA self-regulatory organization, which the FSA lists separately,[^fsa-licensed] and it is not the Life Insurance Policyholders Protection Corporation; that statutory backstop is [[JapanFG/seimei-hokensha-hogo-kiko|Life Insurance Policyholders Protection Corporation of Japan]]. LIAJ's practical authority is membership-contractual: model rules, joint guidance, and industry consensus that feed into FSA consultations.

The supervisory cycle is bidirectional. FSA guidelines reference LIAJ-published industry standards in areas such as life-insurance sales conduct, customer-suitability assessment, anti-fraud measures, and elder-customer care. LIAJ in turn translates FSA expectations into operational templates that members can implement consistently.

## Function / scope

LIAJ's published outline groups its work as follows:[^liaj-overview]

1. **Policy advocacy and research**: joint positions on Insurance Business Act amendments, tax policy affecting life-insurance contracts, accounting and disclosure rules, capital-regulation transition (ESR / economic-value-based solvency referenced in [[JapanFG/legal-financial-licenses/insurance-license-and-solvency|insurance license and solvency route]]), and FSA / BOJ consultation responses.
2. **Industry self-discipline and customer-protection infrastructure**: model rules for sales conduct, suitability assessment, customer-information protection, anti-fraud / anti-financial-crime, elder-customer care, and the financial ADR contact body for life-insurance customer complaints.
3. **Statistics and disclosure**: monthly and annual statistics on premium income, new-policy counts, in-force-policy counts, surrender / lapse data, claim payments, and aggregated industry positioning data.
4. **Public communication and consumer education**: the seiho.or.jp portal, consumer-facing material on life-insurance products, and joint statements on industry positioning, including sustainable finance, ESG, digital transformation, and longevity products.

LIAJ also coordinates with [[JapanFG/sonpo-kyokai|GIAJ]] on cross-industry insurance topics, with [[JapanFG/zenginkyo|JBA]] where bancassurance themes intersect bank sales channels, and with [[JapanFG/jsda|JSDA]] where investment-linked life-insurance products intersect securities regulation.

## Membership / governance

Membership covers life insurers licensed under the Insurance Business Act.[^liaj-members] Member entities documented in this vault include, among others, [[JapanFG/dai-ichi-life-insurance|Dai-ichi Life Insurance]], [[JapanFG/asahi-life|Asahi Life]], [[JapanFG/sumitomo-life|Sumitomo Life]], [[JapanFG/sony-life|Sony Life]], [[JapanFG/daido-life|Daido Life]], [[JapanFG/aflac-japan|Aflac Japan]], [[JapanFG/sompo-himawari-life|Sompo Himawari Life]], [[JapanFG/dai-ichi-frontier-life|Dai-ichi Frontier Life]], [[JapanFG/dai-ichi-neo-life|Dai-ichi Neo Life]], [[JapanFG/nissay-wealth-life|Nissay Wealth Life]], [[JapanFG/sbi-life|SBI Life]], [[JapanFG/cardif-life|Cardif Life]], [[JapanFG/credit-agricole-life|Crédit Agricole Life]], [[JapanFG/zurich-life|Zurich Life]], and other life insurers across the [[insurance/INDEX|insurance index]].

Governance follows the general-incorporated-association model: a rotating chair selected from senior executives of major member insurers, with the chair seat typically rotating among the Big Four life insurers, and standing committees on legal, tax, accounting, sales conduct, customer protection, statistics, international policy, and ESR transition.

## Related industry adjacency

In the insurance industry-body stack, LIAJ is the life-side body. The non-life counterpart is [[JapanFG/sonpo-kyokai|GIAJ]]. The statutory policyholder-protection backstop for life insurers is [[JapanFG/seimei-hokensha-hogo-kiko|Life Insurance Policyholders Protection Corporation of Japan]]; for non-life insurers it is [[JapanFG/sonpo-hosho-kiko|Non-Life Insurance Policyholders Protection Corporation of Japan]]. The consumer-education sibling is [[JapanFG/seimei-bunka-center|Japan Institute of Life Insurance]] where added. For peers in other verticals see [[JapanFG/zenginkyo|JBA]], [[JapanFG/zenchugin-kyo|Regional Banks Association of Japan]], [[JapanFG/dai2-chigin-kyo|Second Association of Regional Banks]], [[JapanFG/zenshin-kyo|Zenshin-kyō]], [[JapanFG/shintaku-kyokai|Trust Companies Association of Japan]], and [[JapanFG/jsda|JSDA]].
