# Aozora Bank

## Wiki route

This entry sits under [[JapanFG/INDEX|JapanFG index]]. Read it against [[JapanFG/sbi-shinsei-bank|SBI Shinsei Bank]] for peer / contrast context and [[banking/INDEX|banking index]] for the broader system / regulatory boundary.

## TL;DR

Aozora Bank is a medium-sized independent bank descended from the former **Nippon Credit Bank (NCB)**. It is a representative case in Japan's post-failure bank rehabilitation history: bankruptcy in **1998-12**, special public management (temporary nationalization), sale in **2000-09** to SoftBank + ORIX + Tokio Marine, renaming to Aozora Bank in **2001-01-04**, Cerberus becoming a major shareholder in **2003-08**, and relisting on the First Section of the Tokyo Stock Exchange in **2006-11**.

The most important recent issue is the large loss on **U.S. CRE (commercial real estate)** loans in **2023-2024**, culminating in the **2024-05** announcement of a full-year deficit. The bank is now in a phase of earnings revision, U.S. CRE exposure reduction, and management-strategy restructuring. ^[extracted]

### "Medium-sized independent bank" model

- An independent position not belonging to either the megabank groupings or the regional-bank groupings. ^[extracted]
- Retail is centered on the BANK The Aozora Net Branch online franchise, with a limited physical-store network.

### U.S. CRE loss issue

- **Expanded U.S. office CRE lending in 2018-2023** as part of a yield-seeking strategy under Japan's low-rate environment. ^[extracted]
- In 2022-2024, the U.S. office market weakened due to remote-work normalization and Fed rate hikes, pressuring collateral values.
- **In 2024-05, announced about `320億円` of full-year deficit and the largest loss in its history**. ^[extracted]

### Past integration-review history

- **Considered integration with former Shinsei Bank in 2010, but withdrew**. ^[extracted]
- Both banks shared histories of public-fund involvement and overseas PE exits, so synergies had been expected.
- Former Shinsei Bank later became a wholly owned subsidiary under SBI following a TOB process in 2021-12 to 2022-02.

### Strategic positioning

- Often compared with the SBI Shinsei Bank model as a post-PE-exit strategic-partnering case.

## 4. Regulation / policy

- **Supervisor**: Financial Services Agency (FSA).
- **Historical positioning**: One of the few surviving independent lines from Japan's former long-term credit bank system, contrasted with lines that fed into [[mizuho-fg]] and [[sbi-hd]].
- **Deposit Insurance Corporation**: the entity associated with special public management at the time of the `1998-12` failure.
- **Current policy issues**:
  - normalization of the Bank of Japan policy-rate environment and its effect on domestic interest margins;
  - governance and strategic positioning for a medium-sized independent bank after the Cerberus exit.
