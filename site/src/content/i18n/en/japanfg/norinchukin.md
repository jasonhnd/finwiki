# Norinchukin Bank

## Wiki route

## TL;DR

Norinchukin Bank is one of the world's largest cooperative-system financial institutions. It was founded in 1923  as the Industrial Cooperatives Central Bank, renamed Norinchukin Bank in 1943, and moved toward modern banking functions through the 1986 / 2001  revisions of the Norinchukin Bank Act. It is **not a stock company**, but a special corporation whose capital base is provided by cooperative-system organizations such as JA agricultural cooperatives, JF fisheries cooperatives, and forestry cooperatives. It carries the national central-institution layer for [[banking/ja-bank-system-japan|JA Bank system]] and [[banking/jf-marine-bank-system-japan|JF Marine Bank system]], combining the top layer of the cooperative credit system with the role of a major domestic institutional investor. Consolidated total assets are **91 兆 7,243 億円** as of 2024-09-30. In the **2008  Lehman shock**, it booked around 1.5 兆円 of U.S. subprime-related losses; in 2024 年, unrealized losses on U.S. Treasuries and related assets triggered a capital-strengthening plan, first announced at roughly 1.2 兆円 in 2024-05-23 and then fixed in 2024-08-01 at a combined **roughly 1.3 兆円** through subordinated capital contribution 7,360 億円 plus about 6,000 億円 of dated subordinated loans. ^[extracted] This renewed governance scrutiny around large institutional-investment losses in 2 .

### Capital structure: cooperative-system capital ^[extracted]

- **Main capital providers**: JA agricultural cooperatives / federations, JF fisheries cooperatives / federations, forestry cooperatives, and other cooperative-system bodies.
- What would be "shareholders" in a stock company are capital contributors through upper-layer organizations such as JA-Zenchu, JA-Zennoh, and JA Kyosairen.
- **Dividends / returns** to cooperative-system bodies are part of the core model.
- It is not listed on TSE / JPX.

### Dual character: cooperative central bank + major domestic institutional investor

- **Upstream**: central bank for the JA Bank system, gathering abundant savings from JA organizations nationwide and investing them centrally.
- **Downstream**: institutional investor that deploys capital too large to be absorbed solely by domestic agriculture / forestry / fisheries credit demand into domestic and overseas securities, bonds, alternatives, and securitized products.
- This creates the structural feature that an "agricultural finance" institution is in practice one of the world's largest institutional investors.

### International diversified-investment strategy

- Focus on U.S. Treasuries, corporate bonds, MBS, and other foreign-currency assets.
- Investment / funding operations through three overseas bases: New York, London, and Singapore 3 .
- Large U.S.-market losses occurred in both the 2008  subprime episode and the 2024  U.S.-rate-surge episode, making them **2  distinct major loss cycles**.

### 2024-2025  portfolio restructuring ^[extracted]

- Policy announced in 2024 年: realize and sell U.S. Treasuries and other unrealized-loss positions, including a plan to sell around 10 兆円 of low-yielding assets.
- Capital strengthening, including subordinated capital contribution of 7,360 億円 and related measures, reallocated risk assets.
- Lower dividends to contributing JA and other bodies raise governance issues inside the cooperative system.

### Cooperative financial-system restructuring

- Central player in the JA Bank national system from 2002 onward.
- Rebalancing roles with prefectural credit federations.
- Similar central-bank functions for JF Marine Bank and JForest systems.

### Competitive position

- **Compared with megabanks, [[mufg]] / [[smfg]] / [[mizuho-fg]]**: commercial banking operations are limited; the core is cooperative finance plus institutional investment.
- **Compared with life insurers, [[nippon-life]] / [[dai-ichi-life]]**: similar as an institutional investor and comparable in asset-management scale.
- **Compared with policy financial institutions, [[jfc]] / [[dbj]]**: partly competes with or complements lending to agriculture, forestry, and fisheries.

## 4. Regulation / policy

- **Primary supervisors**: FSA and **MAFF**, reflecting the dual supervision of financial and cooperative-system functions ^[extracted].
- **Governing law**: Norinchukin Bank Act (昭和 61 年 law No. 第 93 号).
- **Current policy issues**:
  - Whether dividends / returns to contributing JA and cooperative-system bodies are appropriate.
  - Cooperative financial-system restructuring, including JA Bank integration and role allocation with credit federations.
  - ESG and climate-risk disclosure as an institutional investor.

### Representative office: president / chief director

- In 2024-2025 , the chief director was **Kazuto Oku**. During FY2025-03 , the bank expected to book a final deficit of about 1.9 兆円 due to foreign-bond sales, and Oku indicated his intention to resign, according to Bloomberg's 2025-02-20 report.
- For the latest officer lineup and terms, see Norinchukin's official IR / disclosure report.
