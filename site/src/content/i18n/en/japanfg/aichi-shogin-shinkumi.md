### Membership eligibility ^[extracted]

As an ethnic shinkumi, member eligibility has a dual structure of **business domain + ethnic community** (under the Small and Medium-Sized Enterprise Cooperatives Act):

1. **Business-domain requirement**: commercial and industrial operators (mainly resident Korean / Chosen business operators, with strict limits on non-member usage)
2. **Ethnic community**: resident Koreans / Chosen and their spouses, relatives, and related businesses
3. **Area requirement**: persons residing, working, or having a business office in the charter-designated area centered on Aichi Prefecture

### Historical background of shogin-kei shinkumi ^[extracted]

| Period | Event |
|---|---|
| 1950-60  decades | "Shogin" shinkumi were established across Japan as mutual-aid finance for resident Korean / Chosen merchants and industrialists |
| 1970-80  decades | Shogin shinkumi networks were established in major cities such as Nagoya, Tokyo, and Osaka |
| 1990 年 era | After the bubble collapse, loans to **real estate and construction** turned non-performing |
| 2002-2003 | **Chain-collapse problem of Kansai Shogin shinkumi** + failures among Chogin-kei shinkumi |
| 2003 onward | Business transfers to successor shinkumi + progress in **reorganization and consolidation of shogin-kei shinkumi** |
| From the 2010 年 era onward | Generational turnover in the resident community + stronger AML/CFT requirements |

### Main businesses ^[extracted]

| Business | Content |
|---|---|
| **Loans to SMEs** | Working capital and capital expenditure funding for resident Korean / Chosen SMEs (restaurants, retail, pachinko, real estate, construction, etc.) |
| **Personal deposits** | Time and demand deposits for member households |
| **Housing loans** | Home-acquisition funding for members |
| **International remittance** | Cross-border remittance to Korea (a demand specific to ethnic shinkumi) |
| **Community finance** | Living funds for members for education, weddings / funerals, etc. |
| **Asset-management consultation** | JGBs and investment-fund sales through the cooperative system |

## 6. Supervision / regulation

- **Supervisors**: Financial Services Agency and Tokai Local Finance Bureau
- **Legal basis**: Small and Medium-Sized Enterprise Cooperatives Act + Act on Financial Businesses by Cooperatives
- **Deposit insurance**: [[JapanFG/dic|預金保険機構]] general-financial-institution framework
- **Recent policy issues**:
  - **Stronger AML/CFT** (monitoring cross-border remittance) — a regulation-cost issue specific to ethnic shinkumi
  - **Member generational turnover** (risk that the 2 -generation and 3 -generation resident community moves away from shinkumi)
  - **Entry of Korean banks into Chukyo** — competition from Shinhan Bank Nagoya Branch and others
