# IB Lease (formerly known as Mizuho Lease integration)

## TL;DR

**IBJ Leasing** was established in 1969 as a leasing company affiliated with the former Industrial Bank of Japan (IBJ), and for many years it was a representative lessor in the Mizuho / former-IBJ corporate group. On 2019-04-01 it changed its name to Mizuho Leasing, effectively becoming [[JapanFG/mizuho-lease|Mizuho Leasing]]; IBJ Leasing as an independent business name ended. This page is therefore retained as a **historical entity and naming-integration note**. Refer to [[JapanFG/mizuho-lease|Mizuho Leasing]] for current operations and financial status.

## 2. Merger history & business lines (old)

The former IBJ Lease was a semi-major leasing company within the Mizuho group with strengths in **real estate, aircraft, and large-scale project finance**, and coexisted with [[JapanFG/fuyo-lease|Fuyo General Lease]], a member of the Fuyo Group, with some business overlap. It inherited the former IBJ's large-corporate and project-finance orientation, which supported large real-estate, ship, aircraft, and overseas project transactions.

Main former business areas:

- General equipment lease/industrial equipment lease
- Real estate lease/finance (large properties)
- Aircraft/ship operating lease/JOL
- Overseas (mainly in Asia) bases
- Environment/Renewable Energy PJ Finance

### 2019 - 04  Background of Mizuho Lease Integration

- The leasing functions within Mizuho FG have an overlapping structure with IBJ Lease + Fuyo General Lease, etc., and there is pressure to reorganize within the group.
- The company name change from IBJ Lease to Mizuho Lease (2019-04-01) is part of Mizuho FG's restructuring of non-bank functions.
- In 2024, Marubeni's strategic shareholder participation in [[JapanFG/mizuho-lease|Mizuho Leasing]] was announced, evolving the company into a hybrid Mizuho-affiliated and trading-company-affiliated platform.

## 3. Strategic significance of this transition

The merger of the former IBJ Lease was the result of Mizuho FG's decision that there was limited merit in maintaining multiple leasing subsidiaries. By contrast, [[JapanFG/fuyo-lease|Fuyo General Lease]], which is affiliated with the Fuyo Group, continues to exist as an independent listed company, so leasing integration within the Mizuho sphere has not been complete.

This history is important for understanding [[JapanFG/mizuho-fg|Mizuho FG]]'s non-bank strategy: the post-merger process of reconciling the former IBJ, former Fuji Bank, and former Dai-Ichi Kangyo Bank lineages.

In the context of [[finance/japan-acquisition-finance|Japan acquisition finance]], the former IBJ Lease's large-project know-how has been inherited by Mizuho Leasing and is now used in Mizuho-affiliated LBO and M&A financing.
