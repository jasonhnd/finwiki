# General Insurance Association of Japan (Sonpo Kyōkai / Japan General Insurance Association)

## TL;DR

Sonpo Kyōkai (General Insurance Association of Japan, GIAJ) is the industry body for Japan's non-life insurers: property, casualty, motor, fire, marine, liability, and accident insurance underwriters licensed under the Insurance Business Act. Its membership covers domestic non-life insurers, led by the three megagroups, and Japanese subsidiaries of foreign-affiliated non-life insurers. Like other industry bodies it is a general incorporated association, not the regulator and not a statutory SRO, but its joint guidelines on motor-claims handling, fraud detection, catastrophe response, and customer protection operate as industry de-facto standards.

## Legal route / statutory position

Non-life insurers underwrite under the Insurance Business Act, Act No. 105  of 1995,[^ins-act] and are licensed and supervised by the FSA under the insurance supervision guideline.[^fsa-ins] GIAJ is a general incorporated association of non-life insurers; it is **not** an FIEA self-regulatory organization, which FSA lists separately,[^fsa-licensed] and it is not the Non-Life Insurance Policyholders Protection Corporation, whose statutory backstop is [[JapanFG/sonpo-hosho-kiko|Non-Life Insurance Policyholders Protection Corporation of Japan]]. The statutory body for premium rating, including auto / earthquake / fire reference loss-cost calculation, is the separate Non-Life Insurance Rating Organization of Japan; see [[JapanFG/hokenryo-sanshutsu-kiko|hokenryo-sanshutsu-kiko]].

GIAJ's effective authority is membership-contractual: model rules, joint operational standards, financial ADR contact, statistics publication, and industry consensus that feeds into FSA consultations and coordinated post-disaster responses.

## Function / scope

GIAJ's published outline organises its work as:[^giaj-about]

1. **Policy advocacy and research** — joint positions on Insurance Business Act amendments, tax policy affecting non-life products, especially auto / earthquake / fire / liability lines, capital regulation transition, ESR / economic-value-based solvency referenced in [[JapanFG/legal-financial-licenses/insurance-license-and-solvency|insurance license and solvency route]], and FSA consultation responses.
2. **Industry self-discipline and customer-protection infrastructure** — model rules for sales conduct, suitability, customer-information protection, fraud detection, especially organised motor-claim fraud, elder-customer care, and the financial ADR contact body for non-life customer complaints, the Sonpo ADR Center.
3. **Catastrophe and disaster response** — coordinated industry response to large catastrophe events such as earthquake aftermath, typhoon clustering, and flood damage, joint claims-survey infrastructure, and public communication on natural-disaster insurance availability.
4. **Statistics and disclosure** — annual statistics on direct premiums, claims payments, line-level breakdowns, motor, fire, marine, casualty, liability, accident, market-share data, and reinsurance flows.
5. **Public communication and consumer education** — the sonpo.or.jp portal, consumer-facing material on motor and earthquake insurance, anti-fraud awareness, and post-disaster claim-filing guidance.

GIAJ also coordinates with [[JapanFG/seiho-kyokai|LIAJ]] on cross-industry insurance topics, with the rating organization on reference loss-cost updates flowing into auto / earthquake / fire premium scales, and with international peers through the Global Federation of Insurance Associations.

## Membership / governance

Membership covers non-life insurers licensed under the Insurance Business Act.[^giaj-members] Member entities documented in this vault include, among others, [[JapanFG/tokio-marine-nichido-fire|Tokio Marine & Nichido Fire]] where the page exists, [[JapanFG/sompo-japan-insurance|Sompo Japan Insurance]], [[JapanFG/aioi-nissay-dowa-insurance|Aioi Nissay Dowa Insurance]], [[JapanFG/sompo-direct|Sompo Direct]], [[JapanFG/sony-insurance|Sony Insurance]], [[JapanFG/au-insurance|au Insurance]], [[JapanFG/anicom-insurance|Anicom Insurance]], [[JapanFG/sbi-insurance|SBI Insurance]], [[JapanFG/sakura-insurance|Sakura Insurance]], [[JapanFG/daido-fire-marine|Daido Fire & Marine]], [[JapanFG/aig-japan|AIG Japan]], [[JapanFG/zurich-japan|Zurich Japan]], [[JapanFG/aioi-nissay-dowa-insurance|Aioi Nissay Dowa]], [[JapanFG/allianz-fire-marine-japan|Allianz Fire & Marine Japan]] where added, and other non-life insurers across the [[insurance/INDEX|insurance index]].

Governance follows the general-incorporated-association model: a rotating chair drawn from senior executives of major member insurers, typically rotating among the Big Three non-life groups, and standing committees on legal, tax, accounting, sales conduct, motor claims, fire / earthquake, catastrophe response, statistics, international affairs, and ESR transition.

## Related industry adjacency

In the insurance industry-body stack, GIAJ is the non-life side. The life counterpart is [[JapanFG/seiho-kyokai|LIAJ]]. The statutory policyholder-protection backstop is [[JapanFG/sonpo-hosho-kiko|Non-Life Insurance Policyholders Protection Corporation of Japan]], with life-side counterpart [[JapanFG/seimei-hokensha-hogo-kiko|Life Insurance Policyholders Protection Corporation of Japan]]. The statutory premium-rating body is [[JapanFG/hokenryo-sanshutsu-kiko|Non-Life Insurance Rating Organization of Japan]], where added. ## Why this page matters

JapanFG non-life-insurer entries frequently cite "GIAJ standard practice" or "industry-coordinated catastrophe response" without explaining the source. This page pins GIAJ's identity, its non-regulatory but operationally significant role, and its boundary with the statutory protection corporation and statutory premium-rating body. It is also the natural anchor for catastrophe-event references: the industry's coordinated response to large natural disasters is GIAJ-led, with member insurers operating under joint survey and claims-handling frameworks. Linking back here disambiguates statements like "the non-life industry recommended," which usually means GIAJ, not the statutory bodies.
