**Features**:
- **No retail banking or personal deposits at all**. Completely specialized in B2B / B2  institutional investors
- **SSGA is a differentiator from BNY Mellon**: it deploys SPDR ETFs, including SPY, the world's largest ETF, in Japan as well, giving State Street both ETF management and custody ^[extracted]
- **State Street Trust and Banking capital 25 億 1000 万円, employees 426 名 (as of 2024-04-30), R&I rating AA-** ^[extracted]
- **Representatives**: trust bank president **Keiko Terada**, Tokyo branch manager **Norihiro Wakabayashi**, SSGA president **Michihiro Echizenya** ^[extracted]

## 5. Supervision / Regulation

- **Primary supervisor**: Financial Services Agency (FSA) — as trust bank, Japan branch, and SSGA (financial instruments business operator)
- **U.S. supervisors**: FRB (Federal Reserve Board) / OCC (Office of the Comptroller of the Currency) / FDIC; subject to global stress test DFAST
- **Recent issues**:
  - **Rising overseas-asset allocation by GPIF and public pensions** → structural expansion of custody demand
  - **Weak yen and overseas-investment shift** → higher entrusted assets as Japanese banks and asset managers increase overseas assets
  - **AUC/A record highs in 2025-2026 **: 53.8 兆 dollars, but concerns about a "revenue paradox" due to rising costs (market remains cautious) ^[extracted]
