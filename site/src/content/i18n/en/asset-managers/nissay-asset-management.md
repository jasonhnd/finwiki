## TL;DR

Nissay Asset Management (Nissay AM) is the asset-management operating company of [[life-insurers/nippon-life|日本生命保険 (相互会社)]]. In the FSA list of financial instruments business operators it is listed as "Nissay Asset Management Corporation," Director-General of the Kanto Local Finance Bureau (Kinsho) 第369号. It carries the asset-management function of [[life-insurers/nippon-life|日本生命]], **Japan's largest life insurer** (total assets over 80 兆円, a mutual company), and is centered on life-insurance policy-reserve management (scale over roughly 75 兆円) + public investment trusts + pension management. It is the **largest player in the life-insurer-affiliated asset-manager category**, and its differentiator versus megabank-affiliated ([[asset-managers/mufg-asset-management|MUAM]] / [[asset-managers/asset-management-one|アセマネOne]]) and P&C-affiliated ([[asset-managers/tokio-marine-asset-management|東京海上 AM]]) managers is **the scale of its life-insurance policy-reserve management base + the independence of its mutual-company parent**.

## 2. Business role

- **Public investment-trust management** (for retail investors): expanding the retail market with low-cost investment trusts such as the "Nissay Index Series" and "Nissay Foreign Equity Index Fund"
- **Pension management** (DB / DC / iDeCo): discretionary management for institutional investors and corporate pensions
- **Discretionary investment / investment advisory** (for institutional investors)

## 3. Capital / governance structure

```
Nippon Life Insurance Company (mutual company, unlisted)
  ├── Nissay Asset Management (100% stake)
  ├── Taiju Life Insurance (subsidiary, former Mitsui Life)
  ├── Nissay Wealth Life Insurance (subsidiary)
  └── Affiliated operating companies (trust / agency / asset-management related)
```

- Not a JV structure like megabank-affiliated managers, but a life-insurer-standalone 100% model

## 4. Why standalone page matters

[[life-insurers/nippon-life|日本生命]] is Japan's largest mutual-company life insurer, and **Nissay AM**, which carries its asset-management function, is important as an independent unit of analysis:

1. **The largest life-insurer-affiliated asset manager**: it ranks alongside [[asset-managers/asset-management-one|アセマネOne]] (Mizuho + Dai-ichi Life JV) and [[asset-managers/tokio-marine-asset-management|東京海上 AM]] (P&C), but stands out in scale through a life-insurer-standalone 100% stake
2. **Low-cost / index investment-trust competition**: the "Nissay Foreign Equity Index Fund" and others sit at the industry's lowest trust-fee level and compete directly with [[asset-managers/sbi-asset-management|SBI AM]] / [[asset-managers/mufg-asset-management|MUAM]] (eMAXIS Slim) and others
3. A major beneficiary of **NISA expansion + retail investors' low-cost orientation** ([[securities/nisa-2024-flow|nisa-2024-flow]] context)
4. The impact of **life-insurance policy-reserve management (over 75 兆円)** on the domestic bond market, equity market, and alternative investment management
