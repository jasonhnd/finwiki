## TL;DR

Principal Financial Group, Inc. is a US-headquartered global financial services group focused on retirement, insurance, and asset management, headquartered in Des Moines, Iowa and listed on NASDAQ (PFG). In Japan, Principal's primary footprint has historically been on the institutional asset management side (Principal Global Investors) and pension-related advisory rather than retail life insurance distribution. Compared with retail-focused foreign life insurers like [[life-insurers/prudential-japan|Prudential Japan]] and [[life-insurers/metlife-japan|MetLife Japan]], Principal's Japan presence sits closer to the institutional / asset-management edge of the foreign financial group boundary.

## 2. Operating model

Principal Financial Group's global business spans:

- Retirement and Income Solutions (US-centric)
- Principal Global Investors (asset management, global)
- US Insurance Solutions (life and specialty benefits)
- Principal International (selected non-US markets, historically Latin America and Asia)

In Japan, Principal's institutional asset management and pension-advisory business has been the primary engagement model, with limited retail life insurance distribution compared with foreign retail life insurers. Principal Global Investors offers institutional investment management services to Japanese pension funds, financial institutions, and other institutional clients.

Compared with peers covered in JapanFG:

- [[life-insurers/manulife-japan|Manulife Life Insurance Japan]]: retail life with strong foreign-currency product focus
- [[life-insurers/nn-life-japan|NN Life Japan]]: SME / owner-protection retail life
- [[life-insurers/metlife-japan|MetLife Japan]]: broad retail life and accident & health
- [[life-insurers/aia-life-japan|AIA Life Japan]]: Hong Kong-headquartered pan-Asia life

Principal's solvency / capital framework follows US National Association of Insurance Commissioners (NAIC) risk-based capital, summarized comparatively in [[insurance/global-solvency-framework-comparison-matrix|global solvency framework comparison matrix]].
