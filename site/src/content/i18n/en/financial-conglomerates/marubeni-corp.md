| 1858  | Omi merchant Chubei Itoh founded the business in textile wholesaling; Marubeni and Itochu share this root. |

In addition, within the **Mizuho-group financial-services ecosystem**:

**Strategic shareholder entry into Mizuho Leasing (2024)** gives Marubeni an alternative route to an external leasing / asset-finance platform. Together, the structure is layered as: Marubeni Finance for internal CMS, Mizuho FG as the main-bank relationship, and Mizuho Leasing as the external asset-finance platform.

### Identity among the 5  major trading companies

- **Grain and agricultural trading company**: after the 2013  Gavilon acquisition, Marubeni entered the global top 5  grain-trading tier; after selling the grain business to Viterra (Glencore), it shifted toward an agriculture-distribution model centered on Helena.
- **Proximity to the Mizuho group**: historical ties to the former Fuji Bank line; among the major trading companies, Marubeni is the clearest Mizuho-affiliated trading-company case. Other lines are Mitsubishi to MUFG, Mitsui / Sumitomo to SMFG, Itochu between Mizuho and Mitsubishi, Toyota Tsusho toward Mitsubishi, and Sojitz closer to Mizuho (5 ).

### Competitors

- **Domestic**: 5  major trading companies ([[financial-conglomerates/mitsubishi-corp|三菱]] / [[financial-conglomerates/mitsui-co|三井]] / [[financial-conglomerates/itochu-corp|伊藤忠]] / [[financial-conglomerates/sumitomo-corp|住友]]).
- **Power IPP**: NextEra Energy in the U.S., Iberdrola in Spain, Engie in France, and IPP trading groups in various countries.
- **Grain / agriculture**: ADM, Bunge, Cargill, Louis Dreyfus (ABCD), and Viterra, the current owner of the former Gavilon grain business.
