### History highlights ^[extracted]

| Year | Event |
|---|---|
| 1919 | Osaka Hokko Kaisha established (origin in a Sumitomo zaibatsu urban-development company) |
| 1944 | Renamed Sumitomo Tochi Komu |
| 1945 | Renamed Nippon Kensetsu Sangyo under the GHQ zaibatsu-dissolution order |
| 1952 | Renamed Sumitomo Corporation after the peace treaty took effect; trading-company operations expanded in earnest |
| 1970s onward | Global expansion in steel pipes, transportation equipment, and construction machinery, mainly in the U.S. market |
| 1990s | Copper unauthorized trading incident (Hamanaka incident, 1996) -- massive loss and catalyst for governance reform |
| 2010s | Strengthened J:COM stake jointly with KDDI; integrated SCSK (former Sumisho Computer Systems + former CSK) |
| 2020s | Net income in the JPY 300-500 billion class, maintaining a middle position among the five major trading houses |

In addition, the **Sumitomo group financial-services ecosystem** has a three-layer structure:

"**SMFL = Sumitomo Corporation's external leasing and asset-finance arm** (50/50 JV with SMFG)," "**Sumitomo Mitsui Auto Service = auto leasing** (three-company joint arrangement)," and "Sumitomo Corporation Financial Management = internal CMS." Compared with [[financial-conglomerates/mitsubishi-corp|Mitsubishi Corporation]] (HC Capital equity-method stake + MCFS) and [[financial-conglomerates/mitsui-co|Mitsui & Co.]] (JA Mitsui Leasing + MBFS), Sumitomo Corporation's distinctive feature is the joint-control arrangement of a **50/50 JV with megabank SMFG**.

### Identity among the 5  major trading houses

- **Diversified trading-company positioning**: neither as resource-heavy as [[financial-conglomerates/mitsubishi-corp|Mitsubishi Corporation]] / [[financial-conglomerates/mitsui-co|Mitsui & Co.]] nor as non-resource-heavy as [[financial-conglomerates/itochu-corp|Itochu]], but a diversified middle position.
- **Media / IT trading company**: J:COM (cable TV) + SCSK (IT services) give it a uniquely large media / IT weight among the five major trading houses.

### Competitors

- **Domestic**: five major trading houses ([[financial-conglomerates/mitsubishi-corp|Mitsubishi Corporation]] / [[financial-conglomerates/mitsui-co|Mitsui & Co.]] / [[financial-conglomerates/itochu-corp|Itochu]] / [[financial-conglomerates/marubeni-corp|Marubeni]])
- **Media (J:COM area)**: NTT group, Rakuten Mobile, streaming video platforms (Netflix, Amazon Prime)
- **IT services (SCSK area)**: NRI, Fujitsu, NEC, TIS, ITOCHU Techno-Solutions
