# LifeNet Insurance (ライフネット生命保険)

## Wiki route

This entry sits under [[life-insurers/INDEX|life-insurers INDEX]]. Read it against [[life-insurers/dai-ichi-life|第一ライフグループ / 旧 第一生命 HD]] for peer / contrast context and [[insurance/INDEX|insurance index]] for the broader system / regulatory boundary.

## TL;DR

**Japan's first independent internet-only life insurance company**. Co-founded 2006-10  by Haruaki Deguchi (formerly Nippon Life) and Daisuke Iwase (formerly BCG), it began operations in 2008-05-18. Having no sales-staff channel, it sells **entirely online-completed** with "simple, transparent, cheap" as its pitch, targeting the young generation in their 20s-30 s. 2012-03-15 Listed on the TSE Mothers (the first postwar internet-life-insurer listing). With **KDDI [[megabanks/au-fh]] holding approximately 28% as a strategic shareholder**, it is linked with the au economic zone. Its mainstays are term life insurance, medical, cancer, and disability (income-protection) insurance. ^[extracted]

## 2. Business segment map

| Product | Features | Main target |

### Channel & contract flow

- **Online-completed**: quote → application → health-certificate / disclosure → underwriting → acceptance → monthly credit-card / account debit
- **No paperwork, no face-to-face** (electronic application) as a principle
- Holds no sales staff / walk-in shops → a structure with small fixed costs ^[extracted]

### Core strategy

1. **"Simple & Transparent"**: transparency of product design, premiums, and policy terms (the disclosure of premium breakdowns, etc., was a talking point at the time) ^[extracted]
2. **Lightweighting of the cost structure**: a model that holds no sales-staff personnel costs or store rents → thickening the net-premium portion
3. **Targeting centered on the young generation (in their 20s-30 s)**: capturing the "first insurance" layer that existing life insurers serve thinly
4. **Digital UX**: smartphone-completed, minimizing application time
5. **KDDI / au economic-zone linkage** [[megabanks/au-fh]]: routing customers into the au customer base, with flow linkage of au PAY points, etc. ^[extracted]
