# LifeNet Insurance (ライフネット生命保険)

## Wiki route

This entry sits under [[life-insurers/INDEX|life-insurers INDEX]]. Read it against [[life-insurers/dai-ichi-life|第一ライフグループ / 旧 第一生命 HD]] for peer / contrast context and [[insurance/INDEX|insurance index]] for the broader system / regulatory boundary.

## TL;DR

**Japan's first independent internet-only life insurance company**. Co-founded in 2006-10 by Haruaki Deguchi (formerly Nippon Life) and Daisuke Iwase (formerly BCG), it began operations on 2008-05-18. Without a sales-staff channel, it has used **online direct sales as the starting point** and pitched "simple, transparent, cheap" insurance to younger customers in their 20s-30s. It listed on TSE Mothers on 2012-03-15 as the first postwar internet-life-insurer listing. 2026 IR materials disclose it as TSE Prime 7157. KDDI Group / [[megabanks/au-fh]] remains an important alliance and shareholder relationship, but the 2026-03-31 top-shareholder table shows au Financial Holdings as the largest shareholder at 18.32%. Main products are term life, medical, cancer, and disability income protection; group credit life insurance (GCL) has also become a growth axis.

### Channel & contract flow

- **Online-completed**: quote → application → health-certificate / disclosure → underwriting → acceptance → monthly credit-card / account debit
- **No paperwork, no face-to-face** (electronic application) as a principle
- Holds no sales staff / walk-in shops → a structure with small fixed costs ^[extracted]

### Core strategy

1. **"Simple & Transparent"**: transparency of product design, premiums, and policy terms (the disclosure of premium breakdowns, etc., was a talking point at the time) ^[extracted]
2. **Lightweighting of the cost structure**: a model that holds no sales-staff personnel costs or store rents → thickening the net-premium portion
3. **Targeting centered on the young generation (in their 20s-30 s)**: capturing the "first insurance" layer that existing life insurers serve thinly
4. **Digital UX**: smartphone-completed, minimizing application time
5. **KDDI / au economic-zone linkage** [[megabanks/au-fh]]: routing customers into the au customer base, with flow linkage of au PAY points, etc. ^[extracted]

## 3. #26 deepening: direct-life economics and listed insurer metrics

LifeNet's business model is not just "selling insurance online." It should be read as a direct-to-consumer insurer without sales-staff or store costs, where the analysis turns on customer acquisition cost, policies in force, annualized premiums, insurance service results, and IFRS17 metrics such as CSM / comprehensive equity. The 2026 mid-term plan sets a FY2028 comprehensive-equity target of JPY 200-240 billion and describes individual insurance and GCL as separate growth engines.

From a regulation and solvency perspective, the life-insurance business licence and solvency margin ratio are the base layer. Company disclosures for the fiscal year ended 2025-03 show a consolidated solvency margin ratio of 1,722.2%, and fiscal 2026-03 IR materials present annualized premiums of policies in force, insurance service results, and GCL contracts value as key indicators. Reading only traditional J-GAAP premium income can understate the growth and profitability transition of an internet-origin life insurer after IFRS17.

For group relationships, the auFH / KDDI Group relationship should not be treated as a parent-company relationship. It is a combination of shareholder ownership, customer referral, and business alliance. As of 2026-03-31, auFH is the largest shareholder at 18.32%, while LifeNet is also expanding partnerships with JAL, financial institutions, and GCL channels. The page therefore treats LifeNet as moving from a pure internet life insurer toward a **digital-origin multi-partner life platform**.
