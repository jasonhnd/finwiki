## TL;DR

Manulife Life Insurance Japan is the Japanese subsidiary of **Manulife Financial Corporation**, Canada's largest life insurer (TSX/NYSE: MFC, headquartered in Toronto, founded 1887 ). The former Daihyaku Mutual Life, a mid-sized Japanese life insurer that traced its roots to Kyosai Life founded in 1880 , **failed in 1999-08 ** and was rescued by US GE Capital (GE Edison Life). It restarted in 2001-04  as **Manulife Century Life**, was renamed **Manulife Life Insurance** in 2003 , and has continued in its current form since 2009  as the integrated entity of Manulife Century and the former Manulife Century line. It is positioned as a mid-sized foreign life insurer, focusing on **foreign-currency insurance and variable insurance**, and operates through 3  channels: sales employees, agencies, and bank counter sales. It is one of the 1  core bases in global Manulife's Asia strategy. ^[extracted]

> Daihyaku Life's failure is positioned as 1 件 in Japan's **chain of life-insurer failures** from 1997 to 2001 年, alongside Toho Life (1999-06), Chiyoda Life (2000-10), Kyoei Life (2000-10), Taisho Life (2000-08), and Tokyo Life (2001-03). The common root cause was negative spread (guaranteed rates > investment returns). ^[extracted]

### Channel strategy ^[extracted]

- **Sales employees**: PlanRight Advisor system (consulting style)
- **Agencies**: storefront insurance shops and multi-insurer agencies
- **Bank counter sales**: alliances with major and regional banks for foreign-currency single-premium products
- **Online**: direct sales are limited, a typical structure for a mid-sized foreign insurer

### Main strategic axes

- **Foreign-currency insurance niche**: in a low-yen-rate environment, **USD / AUD whole-life insurance** became core products. High sales share in bank counter sales
- **Shift to variable insurance**: strengthened variable individual annuities and variable whole-life products in line with 2020s NISA expansion and rising asset-formation demand
- **Use of global asset-management capability**: incorporates Manulife Investment Management's global investment network into product design
- **Asia integration**: positions the Japan operation as one wing of the Hong Kong headquarters under parent Manulife Asia's regional strategy

## 5. Regulation / policy

- **Supervisors**: Financial Services Agency (FSA) and local finance bureaus
- **Industry body**: member of the Life Insurance Association of Japan
- **Regulatory frame**: Insurance Business Act
- **Overseas regulatory linkage**: parent Manulife Financial is supervised by Canada's OSFI, and Americas regulation also applies through US John Hancock
- **Recent policy issues**:
  - 2024 onward, stricter sales-suitability rules for foreign-currency insurance (the FSA continues strengthening fee disclosure and replacement-sale regulation) → headwind for mid-sized foreign insurers dependent on bank counter sales
  - 2025 onward, global application of IFRS 17 for insurance contracts → parent Manulife already applies it, with disclosure changes also at the Japanese subsidiary level
  - Responsibility to explain **foreign-exchange risk** in a persistent weak-yen environment for foreign-currency insurance sales
