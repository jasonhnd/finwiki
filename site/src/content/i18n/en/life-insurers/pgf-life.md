## TL;DR

プルデンシャル ジブラルタ ファイナンシャル生命保険株式会社 (**PGF生命**) is a life-insurance operating company in the US Prudential Financial group (the Japanese Prudential group) that holds a life-insurance company license under the Insurance Business Act and appears on the FSA's list of licensed life-insurance companies. It changed to its current trade name in 2010 年 4 月 1 日, and has used "PGF生命" as its abbreviation since 2013 年 4 月. With **bancassurance (bank-counter sales) as its main business**, it provides single-premium savings-type products such as USD-denominated single-premium whole life insurance through partner financial institutions, which separates it in channel and product from [[life-insurers/prudential-japan|プルデンシャル生命]] and [[life-insurers/gibraltar-life|ジブラルタ生命]], which are centered on face-to-face consultants.

## 2. Operating model

PGF生命 runs the standard life-insurer model—underwriting premiums, building policy reserves, long-term ALM, paying out claims—with a specialization in the **financial-institution agency channel** and **single-premium savings-type products**. Because it sells foreign-currency products centered on USD-denominated single-premium whole life insurance through bank and securities counters, it has a liability profile with high sensitivity to the FX and interest-rate environment. This is clearly different from [[life-insurers/prudential-japan|プルデンシャル生命]]・[[life-insurers/gibraltar-life|ジブラルタ生命]], which are centered on the Prudential-group face-to-face life-planner / consultant channel. [[life-insurers/ms-primary-life|MS Primary 生命]] and [[life-insurers/dai-ichi-frontier-life|第一フロンティア生命]], which share the same FI-channel・single-premium model, are direct comparison targets. For the revenue structure of bancassurance, see [[insurance/bancassurance-economics-japan|bancassurance economics in Japan]].
