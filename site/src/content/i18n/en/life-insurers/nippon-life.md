# Nippon Life Insurance Company

## Wiki route

This entry sits under [[life-insurers/INDEX|life-insurers INDEX]]. Read it against [[life-insurers/meiji-yasuda|明治安田生命保険相互会社]] for peer / contrast context and [[insurance/INDEX|insurance index]] for the broader system / regulatory boundary.

## TL;DR

Japan's largest life insurer. It is **domestic 1 位 in both premium income and total assets**, founded in Osaka in 1889-07  as Nippon Life Insurance Company, Limited, and organized as a **mutual company** (unlisted; members = policyholders). **FY2024 (2025-03  period) group core profit was 1兆109億円 (+32% YoY, first time above 1 兆円 and record high) / consolidated ordinary revenue 7兆2,937億円 / insurance and service revenue 8兆3,895億円 (consolidated basis, -6%) / consolidated net surplus 4,354億円 (+5.6%) / consolidated total assets 96.3兆円 / solvency margin ratio 861.9% (non-consolidated)**. Interest spread profit was 5,512 億円 (+94%, record high). The domestic share was long dominated by the sales-staff "Nissay auntie" model, but **sales-staff headcount has fallen below 2024 年 5 万 people**. 2015〜2018  acquisition of MassMutual Life Japan → renamed Nissay Wealth; 2016  integration of Mitsui Life as Taiju Life and consolidation as a subsidiary; 2024  establishment of a JV with U.S. Resolution Life. **Under Vision 2030  / the medium-term plan (2024〜), the FY2035 年 core-profit target is 1.4 兆円**, alongside building a life-insurance block acquisition platform. It is one of Japan's largest institutional investors, holding JGBs and equities.

### Form characteristics: mutual company

- It is not a stock company but a **mutual company** (a special corporation under the Insurance Business Act). Many major Japanese life insurers historically adopted this form, but conversion to stock-company form has progressed in recent years ([[dai-ichi-life]] 2010  / Daido Life 2002 , etc.); Nippon Life has **traditionally maintained the mutual-company form**.
- There are no shareholders, and **insurance policyholders = members** receive surplus dividends.
- There is no listing obligation, and external capital is raised through foundation funds (kikin, member accounts equivalent to equity) and subordinated debt.
- There is no securities report; disclosures are mainly "financial results announcements," "disclosure reports," and "basic figures."

### Subsidiary performance highlights

- **Taiju Life**: FY2024  revenue decline (lower sales of foreign-currency single-premium products). SMR 812.7%.
- **Nissay Wealth**: Revenue down due to lower reinsurance income. New policy annualized premium 2,043 億円.
- **Hanasaku Life**: SMR 2,946% (exceptionally high level).
- **Reliance Nippon Life** (India): FY2024-25  net profit +25%, steady expansion.

### Medium-term management plan / Vision 2030  milestones

- **FY2026  target**: Group core profit 8,600 億円 (+20% vs FY2021-23  average) → **already exceeded 1 兆円 in FY2024 , achieving the target ahead of schedule**
- **FY2035  long-term target**: Group core profit **1兆4,000億円** (domestic 1 兆円 + overseas 4,000 億円)
- Strategic investment frame: more than 2 兆円 through FY2035 , assuming single mega-deals above 1 兆円
- **Long-term continuation dividends from FY2026 年**: Paid every 5  years from 10  years after contract; funding source 100 億円

### Domestic base: mutual-company model x sales staff

- Meanwhile, **younger-segment and digital-agency markets** are growing through online life insurers (Lifenet Life, etc.) and independent-agency products → countermeasure is **Hanasaku Life** (launched 2019 , independent-agency specialist).
- For sales staff, **consecutive 6〜7% wage increases** in 2023〜FY2025 年 (talent-retention strategy; costs above 100 億円).
- Maintaining the mutual-company form secures long-term customer returns through policyholder dividends and independence from short-term shareholder earnings pressure. **Long-term continuation dividends start in FY2026 年**, extending the mutual-company return framework over a longer horizon.

### Overseas strategy: M&A platformization

- Against the backdrop of domestic market maturity, **overseas M&A accelerated after 2015 **:
  - United States: MassMutual Life acquisition → Nissay Wealth (2018) / NLI US / **Resolution Life JV (2024)** / TCW investment
  - Australia: MLC Life Insurance (policy to convert from NAB to 100%)
  - India: Reliance Nippon Life (FY2024-25  +25% profit growth)
  - Southeast Asia: Bangkok Life (Thailand)
- The **2024  JV with Resolution Life** is not a one-off acquisition; it is designed as a **continuous acquisition platform for life-insurance blocks (in-force policy portfolios)**, establishing a structural overseas capital-allocation tool.

### Domestic competitors

- Direct competitors: [[dai-ichi-life]] (Dai-ichi Life HD, listed stock company, FY2024  net income 4,296 億円) / Meiji Yasuda Life (mutual company) / Sumitomo Life (mutual company) / Japan Post Insurance ([[megabanks/nippon-post|japan-post]]-group listed company)
- **Nippon Life vs Dai-ichi Life**: In FY2024 , net surplus / net income was at almost the same level (Nippon Life 4,354 億円 vs Dai-ichi HD 4,296 億円). Nippon Life still leads in scale at 1 位, but Dai-ichi Life converted to a stock company in 2010  and led in M&A flexibility, while Nippon Life remains a mutual company and rapidly accelerates overseas M&A, making the strategic contrast clear.
- The **"Nissay auntie" model** resembles Meiji Yasuda and Sumitomo Life, but Nippon Life ranks first by scale.

### Position as an institutional investor

- As one of Japan's largest institutional investors, it is a **major holder of JGBs and listed equities**. Because it has long-term liabilities (insurance liabilities), allocation to long-term JGBs, infrastructure, PE, and other alternatives is large.
- One of the major investors in Japan Master Trust Bank (joint investment by MUFG Trust, Nippon Life, Dai-ichi Life, etc.).

## 5. Regulation & policy

- **Supervisor**: FSA / Insurance Business Act
- **Legal basis for form**: Insurance Business Act (mutual-company provisions)
- **Solvency regulation**: Solvency margin ratio (SMR, Nippon Life non-consolidated 861.9%) / **economic-value-based solvency regulation (ESR, phased introduction from 2025〜)**
- **Overseas regulation**: U.S. NAIC and state insurance regulators, Australia's APRA, India's IRDAI, Thailand's OIC, etc.
- **Recent policy issues**:
  - **ESR introduction from 2025〜**: Capital soundness disclosure for life insurers, including mutual companies, will change significantly. Nippon Life's SMR is trending down due to lower unrealized equity gains and increased subsidiary risk.
  - BOJ rate normalization → rising long-term interest rates affect both new contracts and existing policy reserves (**FY2024  interest spread profit +94% strongly reflected the benefit of rates and equity markets**)
  - Requests to reduce strategic shareholdings (FSA / Corporate Governance Code)
