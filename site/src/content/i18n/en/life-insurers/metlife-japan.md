# MetLife Insurance K.K. (MetLife Japan)

## TL;DR

**One of Japan's largest foreign-owned life insurers**. It is the Japanese subsidiary of U.S.-based **MetLife, Inc.** (NYSE: MET, one of the world's largest life-insurance groups). It opened in 1973-01  as **Alico Japan**, the Japan branch of U.S. American Life Insurance Company (ALICO) -> became a wholly owned subsidiary of U.S. AIG in 2001  -> AIG entered a management crisis after Lehman in 2008  -> **in 2010-04 , U.S. MetLife acquired ALICO from AIG for about 155 億 dollars** (one of the largest M&A deals in the U.S. insurance industry) -> in 2012-04 it changed names from "MetLife Alico" to **MetLife Insurance**. Head office is in Chiyoda-ku, Tokyo (formerly the foreign-insurer building, then Hamamatsucho Square). It ranks highly in Japan for foreign-currency insurance, including U.S.-dollar term and whole-life products, and uses a **3 -channel parallel model** across bancassurance, agencies, and FA channels. ^[extracted]
