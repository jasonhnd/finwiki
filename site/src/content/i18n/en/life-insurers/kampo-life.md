# Japan Post Insurance

## Wiki Route

This entry sits under [[JapanFG/INDEX|JapanFG index]]. Read it against [[life-insurers/dai-ichi-life|第一ライフグループ / 旧 第一生命 HD]] for peer / contrast context and [[insurance/INDEX|insurance index]] for the broader system / regulatory boundary.

## TL;DR

**The privatization-successor subsidiary to the former postal life insurance system, which began in 1916 **. It sits under Japan Post Group and is listed on TSE PRIME 7181 (2015-11-04 simultaneous parent-subsidiary 3 社 listings). It is a major domestic life insurer with consolidated total assets of about 59.6 兆円 (59兆5,556億円, end of 2025-03  term) and a retail base built on the nationwide post-office network (about 2 万 offices). It still carries the legacy of the historic **2019-06 to 2020  improper sales scandal** (double charging of old and new contracts, inappropriate sales to elderly customers, and more than about 18 万 cases), which led to 2019-12  business improvement orders, 2020-01  mass resignation of management, and 2020-04  business suspension orders (3  months of sales restraint). Under its medium-term management plan, it aims to rebuild trust and return to growth. Japan Post's staged sale of shares as privatization advances is gradually lowering the government ownership ratio. ^[extracted]

### Former Postal Life Insurance Brand and Post-Office Network

- The **postal life insurance brand founded in 1916 ** has extremely deep penetration among regional and elderly customers, and even after privatization the **nationwide network of about 2 万 post offices** is used as the retail base -> a physical-access advantage other life insurers do not have
- Bancassurance and internet sales are late compared with peers (such as [[dai-ichi-life]] Frontier Life)

### Product Diversification and Post-Privatization Constraints

- Under the former postal insurance era, because it was a state-operated business, **insurance amount limits and product-design constraints** applied -> products have diversified in stages after privatization (medical, cancer, higher-value whole-life contracts, etc.)
- However, under the privatization scheme, "consideration for other life insurers" is required, so product approvals are subject to cautious review by supervisory authorities (FSA and MIC)
- Coexistence of old contracts (postal insurance) and new contracts (Japan Post Insurance) became the **breeding ground for double-charging problems** ^[extracted]

### Improper Sales Scandal and Trust Restoration

- **2019-06 to 2020  improper sales scandal** (investigation into more than about 18 万 improper cases) ★ — simultaneous enrollment in old and new contracts (double charging), periods without coverage, inappropriate sales to elderly customers, failures to disclose disadvantages in contract switching, etc.
- 2019-12  business improvement orders, 2020-01  mass resignation of management, 2020-04  business suspension order (3  months of sales restraint)
- Under new management, **compliance strengthening, review of sales quotas, and stricter solicitation rules for elderly customers**
- Customer reimbursement and contract restoration programs implemented
- Governance rebuilding: strengthened whistleblowing, compliance framework, and monitoring

### Decline in Government Ownership Ratio

- Japan Post Holdings (parent company) is required by the privatization scheme to **sell Japan Post Insurance shares in stages**

### Digitalization and Customer-Centered Strategy

- Under the 2024  medium-term management plan, **digitalization and customer-centered service improvement** are core priorities
- Paperless processes, online procedures, chatbots, etc.
- Aims for both **digital + physical** channels through linkage with the nationwide post-office network

## 4. Regulation / Policy

- **Holding-company regulation**: Japan Post Holdings is subject to financial-holding-company regulation (both banking and insurance)
- **Privatization law**: Obligation to dispose of Japan Post Holdings shares and Japan Post Insurance shares in stages
- **History of business improvement orders**: 2019-12  / 2020-04 (**3 -month business suspension**) — unusually heavy administrative sanctions for a major domestic life insurer ^[extracted]
- **Universal service obligation**: Nationwide provision of financial services through the post-office network (requirement under privatization law) -> also affects maintenance of Japan Post Insurance's sales channel
- Recent policy issues:
  - Timeline for full privatization (elimination of government ownership)
  - Protection of former policyholders (handling long-term contracts from the postal-insurance era)
  - Sales-quota management and elderly-customer sales regulation (post-scandal regulatory tightening)
