# Visa Worldwide Japan

## Wiki route

This entry sits under [[JapanFG/INDEX|JapanFG index]]. Read it against [[card-issuers/jcb|JCB]] for peer / contrast context: Visa is a global pure scheme, while JCB is a Japan-origin hybrid. ## TL;DR

Visa Worldwide Japan is the **Japan hub legal entity** of U.S.-based Visa Inc. (NYSE:V), providing Visa brand licensing and scheme operations in Japan. Visa is generally estimated to hold the largest share among international card brands in Japan ^[likely], and it supplies the Visa brand to major issuers such as [[card-issuers/smbc-card|三井住友カード]], [[card-issuers/mufg-nicos|三菱UFJニコス]], and [[card-issuers/rakuten-card|楽天カード]]. Historically, Visa also had a domestic issuer association called **VJA (Visa Japan Association)**, centered on Sumitomo Mitsui Card as the secretariat. The distinctive feature is the 2  two-layer structure between the brand entity, Visa Worldwide Japan, and the association organization, VJA.

## 2. Business lines in Japan

- **Brand licensing**: Provides the Visa brand to major issuers such as SMBC-line issuers, MUFG-line issuers ([[card-issuers/mufg-nicos|三菱UFJニコス]]), Rakuten Card, AEON Card, and others.
- **Scheme operations**: Provides authorization, clearing, and settlement through VisaNet.
- **Interchange / merchant-fee setting and monitoring**: Sets and monitors Japan-market fee structures; details should be read through [[payments/japan-interchange-and-merchant-fee-stack]].
- **Coordination with VJA**: Joint domestic promotions, overseas-travel insurance, and other association services around the Visa brand. VJA runs cross-issuer campaigns for member issuers.
- **Merchant strategy / technology introduction**: Domestic spread of Visa touch payments (NFC contactless), Visa Click to Pay, tokenization, and 3-D Secure 2.0 .
- **B2B / commercial / cross-border**: Corporate purchasing cards, Visa B2B Connect, and related supply.

As a brand entity, Visa is the "face of Visa in Japan", but it does not directly perform consumer issuance or merchant acquiring and is not an Installment Sales Act registered merchant.

## 3. Strategy & competitive position vs JCB / domestic schemes

Visa is the international card brand with the leading domestic share in Japan ^[likely]. It has a broad domestic issuer base, including traditional VJA members with more than 100 行 regional banks and credit unions, merchant acceptance, and global interoperability. [[card-issuers/jcb|JCB]] claims "Japan brand" and "Asia strength"; Visa claims "usable in more than 200  countries worldwide" and "the largest merchant network." Visa competes globally with [[payment-firms/mastercard-japan|Mastercard Japan]] as a 4-party network, but Visa leads in Japan. New products such as Rakuten Card and Olive ([[card-issuers/smbc-card|三井住友カード]]) also frequently adopt Visa. VJA is Japan-specific: the global Visa entity centrally manages brand licensing, while a domestic issuer association runs shared businesses, a 2-tier structure.
