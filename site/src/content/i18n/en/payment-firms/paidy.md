## TL;DR

Japan's first full-scale **BNPL (Buy Now Pay Later)** provider. Established 2008-10  as the former **Exchange Corporation Co., Ltd.**, renamed to Paidy 2018-09 社. Founder **Russell Cummer** (from the US). **2021-09-07 PayPal Holdings announced an acquisition for about 27 億 dollars (approx. 3000 億円)**, completed 2022  → became a PayPal Japan subsidiary. A "pay-later" model that allows payment with only an email address + phone number, with payment made later via convenience store, bank transfer, or account debit. About 100 万 merchants (Amazon Japan / SHEIN / Apple Store / Mercari, etc.). The Japanese-version position of the US's Affirm / Klarna / Afterpay. ^[extracted]

## 2. Business segment map

| Segment | Main business | Characteristics |
|---|---|---|
| BNPL lump-sum | Paidy (pay-later) | Email + phone-number authentication, credit-screening AI, merchant-fee model |
| BNPL installment | Paidy pay-later plan | 3/6/12  installments, with a fee-free plan available (consumer side) |
| Card | Paidy Card / Card Plus | Visa virtual, Apple Pay supported, higher rank is a real card |

### Credit-screening model

- **Phone number + email authentication**: no bank account or credit card registration required, credit decision in as little as a few seconds
- **AI scoring**: calculates a dynamic credit limit from transaction history / device / behavioral data
- **Pay-later collection channels**: convenience store / bank transfer / account debit / Pay-easy
- **Fee model**: merchant-side fees (higher than credit cards) + consumer-side fees for installments (lump-sum is free)

### Global comparison

- **US Affirm**: listed BNPL, partnered with Amazon US
- **Sweden's Klarna**: Europe's largest BNPL, global expansion
- **Australia's Afterpay**: acquired by Block (formerly Square) 2022  (about 290 億 dollars)

### Merchant strategy

- **Amazon Japan official BNPL**: the largest customer-acquisition channel, permanently set up as an Amazon "pay-later" option
- **SHEIN / cross-border EC**: fashion EC for Gen Z
- **Apple Store**: penetrating the younger demographic as installment purchasing for iPhone / Mac
- **Mercari**: a cross of flea market + BNPL (note: Mercari itself also operates Merpay pay-later within [[payment-firms/mercari-hd]], both a competitor and a merchant)

## 4. Regulation / policy

- **Supervisory authority**: METI (registered as an Installment Sales Act / small-amount comprehensive credit purchase intermediary operator) + FSA (Payment Services Act related)
- **Installment Sales Act amendment response** (promulgated 2020-06 , enforced 2021-04 ): newly established the "small-amount comprehensive credit purchase intermediary operator" category → each BNPL company including Paidy became subject to registration. The AI credit-screening model was formally recognized ^[extracted]
- **Act on the Protection of Personal Information**: handling of personal data related to credit screening based on phone-number + email authentication
