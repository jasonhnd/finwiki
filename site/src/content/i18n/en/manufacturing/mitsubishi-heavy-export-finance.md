## TL;DR

Mitsubishi Heavy Industries (MHI, 7011) is one of Japan's largest heavy-industry makers, holding defense, aerospace, energy, industrial machinery, and logistics equipment. **Unlike Toyota / Sony / Hitachi, it does not hold a directly-affiliated bank / insurance / large-card captive at the parent level**, but **the export of large deals (defense equipment, commercial aviation, thermal / nuclear turbines, ships, chemical plants, rockets) always requires a combination of [[JapanFG/jbic|JBIC]] export finance + [[policy-finance/nexi|NEXI]] trade insurance + a megabank syndicate + ECAs**, and MHI's corporate treasury and project headquarters jointly handle their structuring and coordination. From the manufacturing perspective, it is a model where "a maker does not hold a captive but effectively operates an **export-finance platform combining policy finance / commercial banks / ECAs**." Recent topics are the **GIGO/GCAP program** for F-X (the future fighter; a Japan-UK-Italy joint development, on the extension line of the former "X-2  Shinshin"), the **Sapphire defense-equipment initiative**, AP1000 -series / Mitsubishi Heavy PWR overseas projects, and the commercialization of H-IIA / H3  rockets.

  │     └── equipment for the US military / Self-Defense Forces
  ├── Plants & Infrastructure Systems (chemical plants, logistics, transport)
  ├── Logistics, Thermal & Drive Systems (forklifts, HVAC, turbo)
  └── Corporate treasury (Treasury & Project Finance)
        ├── syndicate structuring for export deals (JBIC + megabanks + US-European ECAs)

        ├── project finance (IPP, thermal, offshore wind)
        └── supply-chain finance, receivables ABL

MHI functions not as a "captive that holds banks / insurance," but as a "**finance arm that designs a policy-finance + commercial-bank + ECA syndicate for each export deal**."

### Major programs (2024-2026)

- **GCAP/GIGO (Global Combat Air Programme / GCAP International Government Organisation)**: Japan-UK-Italy next-generation fighter joint development. On the technological extension line of the former Mitsubishi Heavy X-2 "Shinshin," it constitutes Japan's next mainstay fighter as F-X. The GCAP International Government Organisation was launched at its UK headquarters at the end of 2023 年, and a joint-venture business company by the 3  countries (Japan-UK-Italy) is being formed. MHI is the Japan-side prime. Commercial finance involvement is limited; it is defense-budget based.
- **"Sapphire" defense-equipment initiative**: an overseas defense-equipment expansion initiative under the MHI name, advancing exports of Anti-Air / naval / missile-defense systems toward Europe and Southeast Asia. After the revision of the operation of the 3  principles on equipment exports, it is gradually being turned into commercial deals, but the design of utilizing NEXI coverage is a point of discussion.
- **AP1000 -series / Mitsubishi Heavy PWR overseas projects**: large nuclear-export deals to Turkey, Eastern Europe, and the Middle East. A typical case combining JBIC + government guarantees + local-government support.
- **Commercialization of the H3  rocket**: expanding the JAXA-linked H3  rocket into the commercial launch market, requiring satellite-operator contracts + insurance structuring.

## 4. Strategy (Energy Transition + Defense + Project Finance)

- **Energy transition**: broadening investment areas from a thermal / nuclear base toward hydrogen, ammonia, offshore wind, and SAF (sustainable aviation fuel). Creating long-term orders with carbon-neutral products while maintaining capital efficiency.
- **Scale expansion of the defense business**: against the backdrop of the 2022  National Security Strategy and Defense Buildup Program (43 兆円 scale over 5  years), structurally expanding the defense-business segment. F-X / GCAP is the next-generation pillar.
- **GCAP/GIGO international collaboration**: entering the defense-equipment export market through JVs with UK BAE Systems and Italy Leonardo. Under a framework where the GCAP International Government Organisation is supported by the governments of 3  countries, the basis is intergovernmental finance rather than commercial-finance structuring.
- **JBIC / NEXI coordination**: energy and infrastructure exports are advanced with a set of [[JapanFG/jbic|JBIC]] export finance + [[policy-finance/nexi|NEXI]] trade insurance. Commercial banks ([[JapanFG/mufg|MUFG]] / [[JapanFG/smfg|SMFG]] / [[JapanFG/mizuho-fg|みずほ FG]]) participate in the syndicate.
- **Lightening the logistics / industrial-equipment captive**: at the group-company level such as Mitsubishi Logisnext (forklifts), vendor finance is structured via Mitsubishi HC Capital, reducing the burden on the parent's treasury.
- **Cash conversion cycle (CCC) improvement**: because the long-term-order business tends to inflate working capital, supply-chain finance and receivables ABL are used to improve the CCC.

## 5. Regulation / policy

- As a listed company, subject to disclosure under the TSE, FIEA, Companies Act, and IFRS.
- Heavy multi-layered regulation across defense, space, nuclear, and export control (Foreign Exchange Act, METI security trade control).
- Defense-equipment exports are affected by the 2014  Three Principles on Defense Equipment Transfer 3  and the 2023  revision of operational guidelines, with the exportable areas gradually expanding. International joint-development deals such as GCAP/GIGO are emblematic of the new operation.
- Nuclear exports require IAEA safeguards, review by the export-destination country's nuclear regulatory authority, and intergovernmental nuclear agreements (123  agreement / Japan's bilateral nuclear agreements).
- In the green field, there is expanding room to utilize the METI GX Promotion Act and the JBIC GX facility.
