# Toyota Motor Corporation (トヨタ自動車)

## Wiki route

This entry sits under [[manufacturing/INDEX|manufacturing index]]. Read it against [[manufacturing/seiko-epson|Seiko Epson Corporation (セイコーエプソン)]] for peer / contrast context and [[JapanFG/INDEX|JapanFG index]] for the broader system / regulatory boundary.

## TL;DR

Toyota Motor is one of Japan's largest automakers, established in 1937-08-28, and the parent of [[JapanFG/toyota-financial|トヨタファイナンシャルサービス]]. In FinWiki it is used as a manufacturing anchor for viewing "how the manufacturing body supports the finance subsidiary, sales finance, residual-value risk, and the EV/hybrid strategy."

With automobile manufacturing / sales at its core, Toyota forms a huge group including finance, mobility, parts, logistics, and a sales network. The finance subsidiary handles sales finance, leasing, insurance, and credit cards, integrating vehicle sales with residual-value management.

In the FY2025  Integrated Report, total sales for FY2025  are stated as 9,362,410  units and revenue as 48,036.7 billion yen. Toyota Financial Services covers more than 35  countries / regions.

## 3. Strategy and competition

- **Multi-pathway electrification**: Toyota does not stand on a BEV-only leg but combines HEV / PHEV / BEV / FCEV. On the finance side, residual-value setting and used-car price outlooks are important.
- **Integration with sales finance**: [[JapanFG/toyota-financial|toyota-financial]] connects vehicle sales, leasing, insurance, and the dealer network, supporting the sales cycle of the manufacturing body.
- **Supply-chain finance**: Toyota-affiliated parts companies, regional suppliers, and capex connect with the corporate finance of [[JapanFG/mufg|mufg]], [[JapanFG/smfg|smfg]], [[JapanFG/mizuho-fg|mizuho-fg]], and regional banks.
- **FX / trade risk**: as a global production / export company, it is heavily affected by yen depreciation/appreciation, tariffs, and US-China / US-Japan trade policy.

## 4. Regulation and policy

- As a listed company, subject to disclosure under the TSE, FIEA, and Companies Act.
- Affected by automobile safety / environmental regulation, CO2  emission regulation, and each country's EV-subsidy / tariff policy.
- The finance subsidiary is subject to each country's financial supervision and consumer-credit, leasing, and insurance-sales regulation.
