# FATF Grey List / Black List · AML/CFT Country Evaluation Mechanism Overview

> [!info] TL;DR
> FATF (Financial Action Task Force) evaluates each country's AML/CFT framework through 40  Recommendations + 11  Immediate Outcomes and updates the jurisdiction list 2  times per year: **Black list** (Call for Action · North Korea / Iran / Myanmar 2026-05), **Grey list** (Increased Monitoring · 23  countries 2026-05). Recommendation 15 (VASP) + Recommendation 16 (Travel Rule $1K threshold) are the 2  core provisions that directly bind the crypto domain.

## Key facts

- FATF was established in 1989 年 by G7  · Secretariat in Paris · 41  member countries + 9  regional bodies (FSRBs) ^[extracted]
- Legal status is non-binding, but de facto enforcement is achieved through IMF/World Bank Article IV + bilateral sanctions ^[extracted]
- Evaluation cycle: each country undergoes a Mutual Evaluation Report (MER) approximately every 7-10  years + interim follow-up ^[extracted]
- 2026-05  lists: black 3 (DPRK / Iran / Myanmar) + grey 23  ^[extracted]
- Hong Kong 2019  MER: TC 35/40  + Effectiveness 9/11  · Global top tier ^[extracted]
- Singapore 2016  MER: TC 36/40  + Effectiveness 10/11  ^[extracted]
- USA 2016  MER: TC 31/40  + Effectiveness 7/11 (mid-tier) · 2024-25  follow-up completed ^[extracted]

## Mechanism / How it works

FATF evaluation uses a dual-track architecture: **Technical Compliance (TC)** assesses legal implementation of 40  Recommendations, while **Effectiveness** assesses the real-world impact of 11  Immediate Outcomes. Each country completes a Mutual Evaluation Report (MER) approximately every 7-10  years, with follow-up reviews in between. Update logic for the 2  lists: **Call for Action (Black list)** triggers mandatory countermeasures + enhanced due diligence; **Increased Monitoring (Grey list)** triggers ongoing monitoring + remediation via action plan.

There are 2  core crypto-related provisions: **R.15  (Virtual Assets, revised 2019-10 )** requires VASPs (exchanges / wallet custodians / ICO platforms / SC issuers) to obtain a licence or register and applies the full AML/CFT set of R.10-21 . **R.16  (Travel Rule)** requires that VA transfers of USD/EUR 1,000  or more transmit originator + beneficiary information, mandating VASP-to-VASP information sharing (see [[fintech/fatf-travel-rule-overview|FATF Travel Rule 概要]] for details). For a global cross-jurisdictional VASP comparison, see [[exchanges/global-vasp-regulatory-comparison-matrix|グローバル VASP 規制比較マトリックス]].

## Origin & evolution

FATF was established at the 1989 年 G7  Paris Summit, initially focused on anti-money laundering. After 2001 年 911 , it expanded to counter-terrorism financing. In 2012 年, 40+9  were merged and 40  Recommendations were issued. At the 2019-10  G20  Osaka summit, the expansion to crypto assets (revision of R.15 + R.16 ) was announced, incorporating VASPs into the global AML/CFT framework. FATF's 2025-09  Targeted Update on Virtual Assets noted that 75% jurisdictions still had not fully implemented R.15/R.16 , focused on self-hosted wallet + DeFi risks, and promoted RegTech standards for cross-border Travel Rule communication. For Japan's VASP regulatory timeline, see [[exchanges/jp-vasp-regulatory-timeline|日本 VASP 規制タイムテーブル]].
