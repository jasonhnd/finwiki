# Issuer vs Distributor 50/50  Model · Coinbase ↔ Circle Interest Allocation Mechanism

## Wiki route

This entry sits under [[fintech/INDEX|fintech index]]. Read it with [[fintech/japan-financial-regulation|日本金融規制 — トークン・暗号資産・決済に関する法体系]] for adjacent context and [[fintech/japan-stablecoin-regulatory-landscape|日本 Stablecoin 法制度の三層構造（JPYC・USDC・Project Pax）]] for the broader system boundary.

> [!info] TL;DR
> The Coinbase-Circle USDC interest 50/50  allocation model (2018  Centre joint-issuance agreement → continued after 2023  Circle became independent) is **the largest issuer-distributor alignment structure in the stablecoin industry**. Q1 2025  Coinbase stablecoin revenue was $305M ≈ 12-15% of company-wide revenue. However, in the GENIUS Act §501  era, the structure of bargaining power has changed fundamentally, and 50/50  inevitably enter a renegotiation window.

## Key facts

- USDC circulating supply $40-45B (2024-2025) ^[extracted]
- Circle annual interest income $1.7-1.8B (Treasury 4.5% yield) ^[extracted]
- Of that, distribution to Coinbase is ~$905M (full-year 2024 ) ^[extracted]
- Coinbase stablecoin revenue Q1 2025  = $305M (annualized $1.2B) ^[extracted]
- Legal basis for the 50/50  split = 2018  Centre Consortium joint-issuance agreement; continued after 2023  Centre dissolution ^[extracted]

## Mechanism / How it works

**Historical rationale of 50/50 **: In the early stage, Coinbase was the main distribution channel for USDC (brand + user base + exchange liquidity), while Circle provided issuance + reserve management. The Centre Consortium 2018  joint-issuance model tied the two parties closely together. After 2023 年 Centre was dissolved, Circle recovered sole issuance authority, but the 50/50  revenue-sharing clause continues.

**Revenue flow**: USDC reserves of $40B+ hold short-term Treasuries → annualized 4.5% yield = $1.8B → issuance costs deducted → net $1.7-1.8B → Coinbase 50% ($905M) + Circle 50% ($895M).

**Change in bargaining power before §501  vs after §501 **:

| Axis | Before §501  (2024) | After §501  (2025.07+) |
|---|---|---|
| Issuer core cost | Regulatory uncertainty + reserves + listing channels | Reserves + Arc proprietary L1  |
| Distributor core value | User base + liquidity + compliance backing | User base + liquidity (compliance already standardized) |
| Issuer fallback | Must partner with major exchanges | Can build its own L1  ([[fintech/issuer-distributor-incentive-realignment-arc-strategy|Arc · 自社販売自循環]]) |
| Distributor fallback | USDC only (no substitute) | Can promote its own stablecoin (Base USDB-like) |

**Result**: The "distributor premium" in the 50/50  model is inevitably renegotiated after [[fintech/genius-act-501-denylist-mandate|§501]].

## Origin & evolution

2018  = Centre Consortium established; Coinbase and Circle jointly issued USDC; 50/50  were the original terms. 2020-2023  = DeFi summer + listing boom; USDC became DeFi's default stablecoin; in 50/50  the relationship was highly symmetrical (the distributor's value was real). 2023.08  = Centre dissolved; Circle moved to sole issuance; however 50/50  clauses continued (disclosed in the prospectus). 2025.07  = GENIUS Act §501  took effect; compliance became standard equipment; the distributor premium was diluted. 2025.09  = Arc announced, becoming Circle's renegotiation lever.
