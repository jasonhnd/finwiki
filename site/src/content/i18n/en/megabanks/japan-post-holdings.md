# Japan Post Holdings Co., Ltd.

## Wiki route

## TL;DR

**The holding company of the Japan Post Group (TSE Prime 6178)**. It integrates under its umbrella the three businesses of postal ([[megabanks/japan-post-co|日本郵便]]), banking ([[regional-banks/japan-post-bank|ゆうちょ銀行 7182]]), and life insurance ([[life-insurers/kampo-life|かんぽ生命 7181]]). A privatization deal of the largest scale in the postwar era: **the 2005  Postal Privatization Act → 2007-10  conversion to a holding company → 2015-11  simultaneous listing of three companies (triple IPO)**. The Japanese government (Minister of Finance) is, by law, **obligated to hold more than 1  of 3  of the voting rights** (a majority holding continues to this day). With respect to subsidiaries [[regional-banks/japan-post-bank|ゆうちょ銀行]] / [[life-insurers/kampo-life|かんぽ生命]], the policy is to gradually reduce Japan Post's holding ratio. The 2019  [[life-insurers/kampo-life|かんぽ生命]] improper-sales issue and the 2024-2025  [[regional-banks/japan-post-bank|ゆうちょ銀行]] holding-ratio reduction / growth-strategy reconstruction are recent issues.

## 1. Corporate structure

**Formal name**: Japan Post Holdings Co., Ltd.
**English name**: Japan Post Holdings Co., Ltd.
**Securities code**: **6178** (Tokyo Stock Exchange Prime Market)
**Establishment**: **2006-01-23** (as a special company under the Japan Post Holdings Co., Ltd. Act)
**Business commencement**: **2007-10-01** (dissolution of Japan Post Public Corporation / conversion to a holding company)
**Head office**: Otemachi, Chiyoda-ku, Tokyo
**Major shareholder**: **Minister of Finance** (holding more than 1  of 3  = legal obligation)
**Business type**: Special company (stock corporation, listed)
**Legal basis**: **Japan Post Holdings Co., Ltd. Act** (平成 17 年 Act No. 第 98 号)

### Group structure

```
Japan Post Holdings Co., Ltd. (6178, Minister of Finance holding more than 1  of 3 )
  ├── [[megabanks/japan-post-co|日本郵便株式会社]] (postal / logistics, 100%)
  ├── [[regional-banks/japan-post-bank|株式会社ゆうちょ銀行]] (7182, listed, majority holding)
  └── [[life-insurers/kampo-life|株式会社かんぽ生命保険]] (7181, listed, majority holding)
```

※ Japan Post's [[regional-banks/japan-post-bank|ゆうちょ]] / [[life-insurers/kampo-life|かんぽ]] equity-stake ratio is on a policy of gradual reduction

### Legal holding obligation

- **Japan Post Holdings Co., Ltd. Act**: The government is obligated to hold **more than 1  of 3 ** of the shares of Japan Post
- Japan Post is gradually reducing its holding ratio of [[regional-banks/japan-post-bank|ゆうちょ銀行]] / [[life-insurers/kampo-life|かんぽ生命]] shares (aiming for the full privatization of the financial 2 社)

### Government holding-ratio trend

| Period | Government (Minister of Finance) holding ratio |
|---|---|
| 2015-11  at IPO | 80.49% |
| 2017-09  after the 2  offering | approx. 57% |
| 2021-10  after the 3  offering | approx. 60.6% → ※ buybacks and offerings interweave |

### Triple IPO (2015-11-04)

- **A simultaneous IPO of the largest scale in the postwar era**: simultaneous parent-subsidiary listing of Japan Post・[[regional-banks/japan-post-bank|ゆうちょ銀行]]・[[life-insurers/kampo-life|かんぽ生命]]
- Total offering amount on the scale of approx. 1.4 兆円 (3 社 combined)
- It became a touchstone for the governance debate over "parent-subsidiary listing," and the starting point of the later [[corporate-strategy/parent-subsidiary-listing|親子上場ガバナンス]] discussion
- Based on the Reconstruction Funding Securing Act, part of the proceeds from the sale was allocated to Great East Japan Earthquake reconstruction funding

### Kampo improper-sales issue (2019)

- Excessive sales to the elderly and improper handling of switching contracts were uncovered in large numbers
- Over 18 万 improper customer contracts arose
- 2019-12  business-improvement order from [[financial-regulators/fsa|金融庁]] / resignation of management taking responsibility
- Group-wide governance reconstruction / sales-culture transformation is underway

### Capital and business alliance with Rakuten (2021~)

- The Japan Post Group, including [[regional-banks/japan-post-bank|ゆうちょ銀行]], invested in Rakuten Group (4755)
- Cooperation in the logistics / mobile / financial fields

### Progress of financial 2 社 privatization

- Under the Japan Post Holdings Co., Ltd. Act, the full privatization of [[regional-banks/japan-post-bank|ゆうちょ]] / [[life-insurers/kampo-life|かんぽ]] is the goal
- As of 2025 , Japan Post holds a majority of both companies → continued offering is a policy issue
- If fully privatized, compatibility with the universal-service obligation becomes an issue
