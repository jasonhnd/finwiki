# JA Mitsui Leasing

## TL;DR

JA Mitsui Leasing is a quasi-major general leasing company formed in 2008-04  through the merger of **JA Lease** (Norinchukin Bank / JA Zen-Noh lineage) and the **Mitsui leasing business** (Mitsui & Co. lineage). It has an unusual dual-capital structure spanning the **JA Group** (Norinchukin Bank [[cooperative-banks/norinchukin|農林中金]] / JA Zen-Noh / local JAs) and the **Mitsui Group** (Mitsui & Co., Sumitomo Mitsui Trust Bank, etc.). It serves both as a captive leasing function for JA Group agricultural machinery and logistics facilities, and as a general leasing company. It is unlisted, with strategic shareholders such as Norinchukin, Mitsui & Co., and Sumitomo Mitsui Trust.

## 2. Business Lines

- **Agriculture-related leasing**: tractors, combines, and agricultural machinery leasing through local JA branches.
- **Logistics-facility leasing**: JA-lineage warehouses and logistics facilities plus general logistics facilities.
- **General industrial equipment leasing**: machine tools and industrial machinery.
- **Real-estate leasing / finance**: office buildings and commercial facilities.
- **Environment / renewable energy**: project finance for solar, biomass, and similar assets.
- **Aircraft leasing**: operating leases and JOL (Japanese Operating Lease).
- **Overseas**: Asian offices, including China, Thailand, and Vietnam.
- **Medical / care**: medical equipment and care facilities.

## 3. Strategy & Competitive Position

JA Mitsui Leasing's biggest differentiator is **the JA Group's nationwide end-user base**. Through local JAs across Japan, about 600  unit agricultural cooperatives, it has access to agricultural machinery, logistics facilities, and agriculture-related equipment demand. This is a proprietary channel other leasing companies do not have.

Compared with [[leasing-firms/smfl|SMFL]] (SMFG + Sumitomo Corporation JV) and [[leasing-firms/fuyo-lease|芙蓉総合リース]] (former Fuji Bank lineage), it sits in the middle of the quasi-major tier by scale, but has overwhelming share in the JA / agriculture sector. Through Mitsui & Co., it also participates in aircraft leasing (JOL) and global projects, leaving its business balance as a three-pillar model: JA captive, general leasing, and global projects.

It is closely tied to [[cooperative-banks/norinchukin|農林中金]] through both capital relationships and deal flow. While Norinchukin manages large financial assets as the JA Group's investment institution, JA Mitsui Leasing takes the "leasing / real-asset" role.

In the context of [[finance/japan-acquisition-finance|日本アクイジションファイナンス]], it may be involved in agricultural M&A and equipment-investment finance in the food sector.
