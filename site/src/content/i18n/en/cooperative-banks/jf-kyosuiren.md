# JF Kyosuiren (National Mutual Insurance Federation of Fishery Cooperative Associations)

## Wiki Route

## TL;DR

**JF Kyosuiren (National Mutual Insurance Federation of Fishery Cooperative Associations) is the cooperative-insurance core of the JF / fishery cooperative group**. It is a **national federation** under the Fisheries Cooperative Act and is the fishery-cooperative equivalent of [[non-life-insurers/ja-kyosairen|JA 共済連]] in the JA Group. Its **members are nationwide JF fishery cooperatives plus prefectural fishery federations**, and it provides **life mutual aid, pension mutual aid, building endowment mutual aid, automobile mutual aid, fishing-vessel mutual aid, and fishery-equipment mutual aid** for members such as fishers and local residents. It is only several percent to around 10 % of JA Kyosairen's 200 兆円 scale because fishery production is smaller than agriculture, but it has distinctiveness in **fishery-specific mutual-aid products** such as fishing-vessel mutual aid and fishery-disaster mutual aid. Within the JF Group's three pillars ([[cooperative-banks/jf-zengyoren|JF 全漁連 = 代表 + 経済]] / **JF Kyosuiren = mutual aid, this page** / [[cooperative-banks/jf-shingyoren|JF 信漁連 = 信用]]), it is the national federation dedicated to mutual-aid business. ^[extracted]

### Scale Comparison with JA Kyosairen

- **JA Kyosairen = 200 兆円 scale vs JF Kyosuiren = several hundred billion to several trillion yen scale**, reflecting the economic-scale difference between fisheries and agriculture.
- However, it maintains differentiation from private non-life insurers through **distinctive products such as fishing-vessel mutual aid**.

### Fishery-Member Characteristics

- **Occupational risks at sea** (marine accidents, disappearance, weather) create special mutual-aid demand.
- **Seasonally concentrated income** based on fishing seasons requires premium-payment timing design.
- **Aging and succession issues** require responses to generational changes among mutual-aid participants.

## 5. Supervision & Regulation

- **Supervisors**: **MAFF** (primary) + **FSA** (co-supervision) + **Fisheries Agency** (fishery policy)
- **Legal basis**: Fisheries Cooperative Act (mutual-aid business clauses) + mutual-aid business guidelines
- **Recent policy issues**:
  - Debate over the **mutual-aid vs insurance regulatory gap**, similar to JA Kyosairen.
  - Possible application of economic-value-based solvency (ESR)-like regulation to mutual-aid entities.
  - Operation of the fishing-vessel mutual-aid legal system and JF Kyosuiren's role.
  - Redefinition of fishery mutual aid under food-security planning (from 2026).
  - Response to the 2018  Fisheries Act amendment and structural change among fishers.
