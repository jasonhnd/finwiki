# Shinkin Central Bank (信金中央金庫)

## Wiki route

This entry sits under [[cooperative-banks/INDEX|cooperative-banks INDEX]]. Read it against [[cooperative-banks/zenshin-kumi|全国信用協同組合連合会 (全信組連)]] for peer / contrast context and [[banking/INDEX|banking index]] for the broader system / regulatory boundary.

## TL;DR

The **central institution** of the nationwide shinkin (credit union) industry (254  unions as of 2025-11-06 in the FSA registry). Established as a cooperative-organization financial institution, its two main pillars are the management of surplus funds deposited by shinkin banks and lending to shinkin banks. With total assets on the scale of **47 兆円** (end of 2025-03 : total funds under management 46.9 兆円 · total funds raised 45.6 兆円; consolidated total assets 46 兆 953 億円 at end of 2023-03 ), it is one of Japan's foremost institutional investors. Together with [[cooperative-banks/norinchukin]] (Norinchukin Bank = the agricultural-cooperative system), it forms one of the twin pillars of "cooperative-organization-system central banks," holding a large-scale investment portfolio that includes overseas bonds, corporate bonds, equities, and alternative investments. It is unlisted and composed of capital contributions from members (254 行 shinkin banks). ^[extracted]

### Dual role as a central institution

1. **Liquidity pool**: absorbs surplus funds (deposits that cannot be fully lent out locally) deposited by shinkin banks, and manages or redistributes them among shinkin banks.
2. **Investment manager**: manages surplus funds as large-scale institutional investments and returns the investment income to member shinkin banks.

### Overseas strategy

- **New York · London bases**: the management base for overseas bond / credit investment. ^[extracted]

### Digital / settlement strategy

- **Shinkin Cash Service (SCS)**: joint operation of the shinkin bank ATM network. The infrastructure supporting mutual use among shinkin banks nationwide. ^[extracted]
