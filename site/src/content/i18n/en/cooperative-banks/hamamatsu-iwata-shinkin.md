## TL;DR

Hamamatsu Iwata Shinkin Bank (Hamashin) is a major regional shinkin bank headquartered in Chuo-ku, Hamamatsu City, Shizuoka Prefecture, with its main operating base in western Shizuoka Prefecture (Hamamatsu, Iwata, Kosai, Fukuroi, etc.). It is a cooperative financial institution with strength in lending to SMEs in the Hamamatsu region's transport-equipment, musical-instrument, and optical-technology manufacturing supply chains, including Suzuki, Yamaha, Yamaha Motor, and Roland.

## 2. Institution-type business model

As a **shinkin bank**, Hamamatsu Iwata Shinkin Bank is a *member-based cooperative deposit-taking institution* under the Shinkin Bank Act, not a joint-stock bank, formed by the merger of the former Hamamatsu Shinkin Bank and the former Iwata Shinkin Bank. Membership and lending are tied to a defined operating district (western Shizuoka Prefecture) and statutorily oriented to member SMEs — here the transport-equipment, musical-instrument, and optical-technology manufacturing supply chain — rather than the open corporate market. Surplus serves member return and reserves, the defining feature of the cooperative finance tier it shares with [[cooperative-banks/okazaki-shinkin|岡崎信用金庫]] across the Chubu manufacturing belt.

Hamamatsu Iwata Shinkin Bank's core business is SME lending, working capital, and capital-expenditure financing for manufacturing supply-chain SMEs centered on the Hamamatsu region's transport equipment (Suzuki, Yamaha Motor), musical instruments (Yamaha, Kawai Musical Instruments), and optical technology (Hamamatsu Photonics). Mortgage loans, retail deposits, and local business-succession support are also pillars. It formed its current base through the merger of the former Hamamatsu Shinkin Bank and the former Iwata Shinkin Bank, and in the Chubu shinkin landscape it is a representative shinkin of a manufacturing-cluster area alongside [[cooperative-banks/okazaki-shinkin|岡崎信用金庫]] (the Nishi-Mikawa automotive supply chain).

## 3. System & regulatory positioning

- **Central institution**: sits beneath [[cooperative-banks/shinkin-central|信金中央金庫 (Shinkin Central Bank)]] for liquidity, surplus-fund investment, and settlement — the shinkin analogue of [[cooperative-banks/norinchukin|農林中央金庫]] for the JA system.
- **Supervision**: licensed under the Shinkin Bank Act by [[financial-regulators/fsa|the FSA]] with day-to-day oversight delegated to the regional Local Finance Bureau; deposits are insured by [[financial-regulators/dic|the Deposit Insurance Corporation (DIC)]].
