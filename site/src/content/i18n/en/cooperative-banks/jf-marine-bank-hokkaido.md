# JF Marine Bank Hokkaido (Hokkaido Credit Federation of Fishery Cooperatives / Hokkaido Shingyoren)

## TL;DR

**Hokkaido Credit Federation of Fishery Cooperatives (Hokkaido Shingyoren / Doshingyoren) = Japan's largest fishery-cooperative credit-business federation within [[banking/jf-marine-bank-system-japan|JF Marine Bank system]]**. Based on the Fisheries Cooperatives Act, it aggregates surplus funds from coastal and offshore fishery cooperatives (JF) in Hokkaido, deposits them with [[cooperative-banks/norinchukin|農林中央金庫]], and provides liquidity to local JFs. It is **the largest by total assets and deposits among the 10  Shingyoren (as of 2025-04-01)** and provides the credit foundation for a **highly concentrated fishery region** in which Hokkaido accounts for about 25% of domestic fishery production value and about 20% of catch volume. Core businesses include pre-sale working-capital finance for **northern marine products such as salmon, scallops, kelp, Alaska pollock, and crab**, fishery equipment finance, and fishing-port infrastructure finance. It is a representative example embodying the regional-fishery safety net + system-finance function within the **three-stage structure of "71  JF -> 10  Shingyoren -> 1  Norinchukin Bank."** ^[extracted]

### Representativeness of Hokkaido Shingyoren within the 10  Shingyoren

- **Hokkaido area = 1  Shingyoren** = operates as a **single institution for the entire Hokkaido area 1 **, not by 47  prefecture (reflecting Hokkaido's geographic independence)

### Distinctive features of Hokkaido fisheries and Shingyoren strategy

- **Dominance in northern marine products**: Hokkaido accounts for nearly all domestic production of salmon, scallops, kelp, Alaska pollock, etc.
- **Mixed business forms** of **large-scale offshore fisheries + coastal + aquaculture**

### Impact of China's import suspension (from 2023 onward) ^[extracted]

- **China's full ban on Japanese marine products due to 2023-08  Fukushima Daiichi treated-water release**
- Significant contraction in Hokkaido exports of scallops, salmon, kelp, etc. to China

## 5. Supervision / regulation

- **Supervisors**: FSA / Hokkaido Local Finance Bureau (soundness of credit business) + MAFF / Fisheries Agency (cooperative supervision) + Hokkaido Government
- **Legal basis**: Fisheries Cooperatives Act (credit-business federation provisions)
- **Recent policy issues**:
  - **Countermeasures for the China import ban (from 2023 onward)** + financing for alternative market development
  - Implementation of the **2018  Fishery Act revision** and impact on allocation of fishery rights
  - **JF Marine Bank Basic Policy enhancement** + DX promotion
  - Impact on Shingyoren dividends from **Norinchukin Bank U.S. bond losses from 2024 onward**
  - Role of northern marine products under the **Food Security Plan (from 2026 onward)**
  - **Aging fishery workforce** + support for new entrants
