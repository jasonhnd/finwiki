# JA Zen-Noh (National Federation of Agricultural Cooperative Associations)

## Wiki Route

This entry sits under [[JapanFG/INDEX|JapanFG index]] as the **JA Group economic-business federation**, parallel to [[cooperative-banks/norinchukin|農林中央金庫 (信用)]] / [[non-life-insurers/ja-kyosairen|JA 共済連 (共済)]] / [[cooperative-banks/ja-zenchu|JA 全中 (代表)]]. Read with [[banking/ja-bank-system-japan|JA Bank system]] for the credit-side adjacency and [[banking/cooperative-banking-japan|cooperative banking domain]] for the system boundary.

## TL;DR

**The economic-business core of the JA Group's 4  major nationwide federations = a national-scale purchasing + sales business federation**. Established in 1972  through the merger of Zenhanren + Zenkoren, it is a **federation** based on the Agricultural Cooperatives Act. Its businesses are **(1) purchasing = procurement and supply of feed, fertilizer, agrochemicals, agricultural machinery, fuel, and daily-life goods**, **(2) sales = collection and distribution of rice, wheat, horticultural products, and livestock products**, and **(3) processing, logistics, and directly operated stores (A-Coop, etc.)**. **Annual transaction volume is on the scale of several trillion yen**, making it one of Japan's largest agricultural-products distribution organizations. [[cooperative-banks/norinchukin|農林中金]] handles finance, while JA Zen-Noh handles the **flow of goods**, making the two complementary. The two major business lines are the flow from nationwide JA -> JA Zen-Noh -> markets / major distributors -> consumers, and the **reverse purchasing flow (manufacturers -> JA Zen-Noh -> nationwide JA -> farmers)**. **It is a non-financial business entity that does not handle deposits or insurance**, but it shares the same nationwide JA customer base of 497  cooperatives as JA Bank. ^[extracted]

### Integration History ^[extracted]

| Year | Event |
|---|---|
| 1948  | **Zenhanren** (National Federation of Agricultural Cooperative Associations for Sales) established |
| 1948  | **Zenkoren** (National Federation of Agricultural Cooperative Associations for Purchasing) established |
| 1949  | Various industry-specific federations launched (rice/wheat, horticulture, livestock, etc.) |
| **1972-03** | **Zenhanren + Zenkoren merged to launch the National Federation of Agricultural Cooperative Associations (JA Zen-Noh)** |
| 1992  | With unification under the "JA" brand, **JA Zen-Noh** became the common name |
| 2002 onward | [[banking/ja-bank-system-japan|JA Bank]] integrated operations began; JA Zen-Noh and finance remained separated as businesses |
| 2014  | **Conversion of JA Zen-Noh into a joint-stock company became a political issue at the Council for Regulatory Reform** |
| 2015 onward | **JA Zen-Noh self-reform** progressed (distribution efficiency, cost reductions) |
| 2017  | Promotion of agricultural exports to the United States (establishment of rice export company, etc.) |

### Financial Complementarity

- **Flow of goods** = JA Zen-Noh; **flow of money** = [[cooperative-banks/norinchukin|農林中央金庫]] + [[cooperative-banks/ja-shinnoren|信連]] + JA Bank
- Farmers' purchasing payments and sales proceeds settle through **JA deposit accounts**, thickening JA Bank's deposit base ^[extracted]
- Major trading companies (Mitsui & Co., Sumitomo Corporation, Itochu, etc.) are both competitors and trading partners of JA Zen-Noh

### Self-Reform and External Pressure

- **2014  Council for Regulatory Reform**: strongly demanded Zen-Noh's "conversion to a joint-stock company," "business consolidation / abolition," and "production-material cost reductions" ^[extracted]
- **2015 onward self-reform annual plans**: reduction of feed / fertilizer costs by 10%, distribution efficiency, and strengthening overseas exports ^[extracted]
- Conversion to a joint-stock company was ultimately **shelved** — the federation form was maintained, while business-efficiency efforts continued

### Overseas Expansion

- **U.S. subsidiary CGB** (Consolidated Grain and Barge): major U.S. grain collection and logistics company
- **Export promotion**: overseas sales of rice, beef, and fruit / vegetables (U.S., Asia, EU markets)

### Competitive Structure

- **Major trading companies**: competes with Mitsui & Co., Sumitomo Corporation, and Itochu in production materials + food distribution businesses
- **Major food manufacturers**: competes in rice distribution with major rice wholesalers (Pearl Rice group, Shinmei, etc.)
- **Major retailers**: directly operated A-Coop stores compete with regional supermarkets and convenience stores (Seven, Aeon)

## 4. Supervision / Regulation

- **Supervisors**: Ministry of Agriculture, Forestry and Fisheries (federation supervision) + Japan Fair Trade Commission (Antimonopoly Act — concentration of handling volume)
- **Legal basis**: Agricultural Cooperatives Act (federation provisions) + restrictions on member admission to federations
- **Recent policy issues**:
  - **Production-material cost reductions** (continuing Council for Regulatory Reform issue)
  - **Promotion of agricultural exports**
  - Redefinition of JA Zen-Noh's role in the **Food Security Plan (2026 onward)**
  - Response to **digital agriculture (smart agriculture)**
