# Rokinren Bank

## Wiki route

This entry sits under [[cooperative-banks/INDEX|cooperative-banks INDEX]]. Read it against [[megabanks/mufg|三菱 UFJ FG (MUFG)]] for peer / contrast context and [[banking/INDEX|banking index]] for the broader system / regulatory boundary.

## TL;DR

The Rokinren Bank is the central financial institution for the rokin sector, whose members are the nationwide 13  labor banks. Established in 1955年 under the Labor Bank Act, it handles fund-supply adjustment for member rokin banks, investment of surplus funds, national exchange and workers' asset-formation savings processing, centralized back-office services, and the unified online system for the sector. It sits alongside [[cooperative-banks/zenshin-kumi|全信組連]] and [[cooperative-banks/shinkin-central|信金中央金庫]] as a central institution in Japan's cooperative-finance system.

The official explanation says Rokinren absorbs 58% of member rokin surplus funds as deposits, and that 80% of the deposits and negotiable certificates of deposit it takes are from member rokin banks.

## 3. Strategy / competition

- **Central cooperative-finance institution**: Rokinren is not a commercial bank; it is the central institution that supports labor banks focused on welfare finance for workers.
- **Comparison with shinkin and shinkumi**: [[cooperative-banks/shinkin-central|shinkin-central]] supports shinkin banks, [[cooperative-banks/zenshin-kumi|zenshin-kumi]] supports credit cooperatives, and Rokinren supports labor banks.
- **System sharing**: As regional financial institutions face rising costs, shared systems and outsourced operations become a source of competitiveness.
- **Policy purpose plus market investment**: Rokinren supports worker-welfare finance while investing surplus funds in financial markets, so it has both an ALM role and a social mission.

## 4. Regulation / policy

- Cooperative financial institution under the Labor Bank Act.
- Subject to disclosure, risk-management, and supervision rules including Banking Act-style application.
- Under FSA and local finance bureau supervision; AML/CFT, cybersecurity, and system-risk management are important.
