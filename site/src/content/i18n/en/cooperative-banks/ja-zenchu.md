# JA-Zenchu (National Central Union of Agricultural Cooperatives)

## Wiki route

This entry sits under [[cooperative-banks/INDEX|cooperative-banks INDEX]] as the **JA Group national-coordination layer** behind [[cooperative-banks/norinchukin|農林中央金庫 (Norinchukin)]] and the [[banking/ja-bank-system-japan|JA Bank system]]. Read it together with [[cooperative-banks/ja-zen-noh|JA 全農 (全国農業協同組合連合会)]] for the commercial-federation counterpart, [[non-life-insurers/ja-kyosairen|JA 共済連]] for the cooperative insurance federation, and [[banking/cooperative-banking-japan|cooperative banking domain]] for the broader system boundary.

## TL;DR

**The non-financial core of JA Group = the national "central union."** Established in 1954  and grounded in the Agricultural Cooperatives Act, JA-Zenchu is the **national federation responsible for JA Group representation, management consultation, audit functions, and agricultural-policy movements**. It does not operate business lines, but acts as the **political front** that faces government, political parties, and industry groups as the representative of nationwide 497  JA cooperatives (the same population as [[banking/ja-bank-system-japan|JA Bank system]]). In the **2015  agricultural-cooperative reform, prefectural central unions were dissolved and JA-Zenchu's audit authority shifted to certified-public-accountant audits**. The organization became a general incorporated association, while its representative function was maintained. It is one of the JA Group's 4  major national federations alongside [[cooperative-banks/norinchukin|農林中金]] (credit), [[cooperative-banks/ja-zen-noh|JA 全農]] (economic business), and [[non-life-insurers/ja-kyosairen|JA 共済連]] (mutual aid). JA-Zenchu itself does not have a credit, economic, or mutual-aid business layer; instead, it is the **horizontal representative organization** that ties together those 3  federations, prefectures, and local JAs. ^[extracted]

### 4  major functions ^[extracted]

| Function | Details |
|---|---|
| **Representative function** | Consults with government, political parties, and industry groups as the external representative of JA Group and 497  agricultural cooperatives |
| **Management consultation** | Management support, consulting, and governance advice for individual JAs |
| **Audit function (reduced after reform)** | Before the 2015  reform it held operational audit authority over member JAs; now **certified-public-accountant audit is legally required** |
| **Agricultural-policy movement** | Policy lobbying on TPP / EPA, rice policy, agricultural-income-doubling themes, and related issues |

### Relationship with the JA Bank Central Headquarters

- **The operating headquarters of [[banking/ja-bank-system-japan|JA Bank system]] is the separate "JA Bank Central Headquarters"**. Zenchu is not the direct operator of JA Bank; it participates as the representative of the whole group.
- Practical operation of the credit business is handled by [[cooperative-banks/norinchukin|農林中央金庫]] and prefectural [[cooperative-banks/ja-shinnoren|信連 (JA 信用農業協同組合連合会)]].
- Zenchu is JA Group's **external-facing window** for politics, regulation, and society.

### Food-security debate

- 2022-2024  Ukraine war + yen depreciation + grain-price surge -> **reassessment of food self-sufficiency and the importance of domestic agriculture**.
- Interest in agricultural cooperatives and cooperative finance has also increased internationally, and collaboration continues with global cooperative federations such as ICA / the International Cooperative Alliance.

### Impact of the 2015  agricultural-cooperative reform

- Loss of audit authority -> **greater management freedom** for individual JAs.
- Reorganization of prefectural central unions, including some mergers and integrations.
- However, **representative and management-consultation functions are maintained**, and the representative function survives even after loss of special corporate status under the Agricultural Cooperatives Act.

### Competitors / countervailing forces

- **MAFF and MoF**: reform-promotion side versus JA-Zenchu as status-quo side.
- **Agribusiness and agricultural corporation associations**: forces seeking larger-scale farms and stock-company conversion, creating tension with JA Group.
- **Regulatory Reform Council and Council on Economic and Fiscal Policy**: think-tank / advisory bodies seeking stronger competitiveness in agriculture.

## 4. Supervision / regulation

- **Primary authority**: Ministry of Agriculture, Forestry and Fisheries (main) + Cabinet Office Regulatory Reform Promotion Office (reform debate)
- **Legal basis**: Agricultural Cooperatives Act (post-reform central-union provisions) + General Incorporated Associations Act
- **Outside deposit insurance / financial regulation**: JA-Zenchu itself is not a financial institution, so it is not directly supervised by [[financial-regulators/yokin-hoken-kiko|預金保険機構]] or the FSA.
- **Recent policy issues**:
  - Food security and reconstruction of agricultural policy
  - Further reorganization / integration of prefectural JA central unions
  - Management distress at individual JAs (support for small or loss-making JAs)
  - JA Group-wide governance (coordination among the 4  major national federations)
