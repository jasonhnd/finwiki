# MONEX, Inc. - Japanese crypto-asset derivatives operator overview

## Wiki route

This entry sits under [[exchanges/INDEX|exchanges index]]. Read it with [[exchanges/fsa-vasp-registration-system|FSA crypto-asset exchange registration system]] for adjacent context and [[fintech/japan-financial-regulation|Japan financial regulation]] for the broader system boundary.

> FSA registration number: Director-General of Kanto Local Finance Bureau (Kinsho) No. 165. JVCEA member #1024. Crypto-asset derivatives trading only; spot trading is handled by subsidiary Coincheck.

## 1. Corporate entity / shareholders

- **Legal name**: MONEX, Inc. (Japanese trade name: マネックス証券株式会社)
- **Common name**: Monex Securities
- **Established**: 1999 (originally as Monex Beans Securities)
- **Head office**: 1-12-32 Akasaka, Minato-ku, Tokyo
- **Parent company**: Monex Group, Inc. (TSE Prime 8698)
- **Listing status**: Unlisted; wholly owned by Monex Group

**Key group role split**:

- MONEX, Inc. (this entry): online securities business and **crypto-asset derivatives (CFD)** business
- [[exchanges/jp-exchange-coincheck|Coincheck]] (subsidiary): **spot crypto-asset exchange business** (FSA registration number 00014, JVCEA #1017)
- Monex, Inc. (U.S. subsidiary): U.S. securities business

MONEX, Inc. is not a spot crypto-asset exchange operator. It provides crypto-asset CFDs as part of its financial instruments business. Its own public materials state that it is not a crypto-asset exchange operator and does not provide spot crypto-asset trading.

## 2. Licensing / registration status

- **Financial instruments business**: Director-General of Kanto Local Finance Bureau (Kinsho) No. 165
- **JVCEA membership**: Type 1 member #1024 (**crypto-asset derivatives trading only**)
- **Crypto-asset exchange business registration**: None

**Affiliated associations**:

- Japan Securities Dealers Association
- Type II Financial Instruments Firms Association
- Financial Futures Association of Japan
- Japan Virtual and Crypto assets Exchange Association (JVCEA)
- Japan Investment Advisers Association

## 3. Supported assets

Under Monex Bitcoin (crypto-asset CFD), the following yen pairs are publicly listed:

- BTC/JPY (Bitcoin)
- XRP/JPY (XRP)
- ETH/JPY (Ethereum)
- Other major issues; the exact number of listed issues is not publicly stated

**Product characteristics**: no physical holding of crypto-assets. All products are CFDs only. Maximum leverage is 2x. Short positions are supported.

## 4. Business scope

**Core business**: internet securities business

- Brokerage and intermediary services for domestic equities, U.S. equities, Chinese equities, investment trusts, FX, futures and options, gold and platinum, and bonds
- Supports NISA and iDeCo

**Crypto-related services**:

- Crypto-asset CFD only, under Monex Bitcoin
- No spot trading, no physical custody, no wallet, no staking
- Trading fee: free; revenue comes from spread and leverage charges
- Trading tool: smartphone app "MONEX TRADER CRYPTO" (iOS / Android)
- Margin ratio: 50% or higher, equivalent to maximum 2x leverage
- Trading hours: in principle 24 hours a day, 365 days a year including weekends, excluding weekly maintenance

**Fiat deposit channel**: through the main securities trading account, including bank transfer and quick deposit. No dedicated direct fiat deposit rail for crypto-asset CFDs is publicly disclosed.

## 5. Market share / trading volume

- No public market-share data is published specifically for crypto-asset CFDs
- It is **one of the 13 derivatives operators** affiliated with JVCEA (#1024)
- Main peers in derivatives include [[exchanges/jp-exchange-bitflyer|bitFlyer]], [[exchanges/jp-exchange-gmo-coin|GMO Coin]], [[exchanges/jp-exchange-rakuten-wallet|Rakuten Wallet]], [[exchanges/jp-exchange-sbi-securities|SBI Securities]], and [[exchanges/jp-exchange-dmm-com-securities|DMM.com Securities]]
- Customer base: domestic retail investors, especially existing Monex Securities account holders
- Monthly account figures are published in the "Monthly Overview of Account Numbers, etc.", though detailed figures were not obtainable from the inaccessible disclosure page at the time of retrieval
- The main securities business has account numbers on the order of several million, based on Monex Group monthly disclosures; exact figures require direct IR confirmation

## 6. History / major incidents

- **1999**: established as Monex Beans Securities, one of Japan's early internet-specialized securities firms
- **2000s**: period of support from Sony Financial as part of a group transition
- **2019**: acquired Coincheck, Inc. after the 2018 NEM outflow incident, bringing Coincheck's spot crypto business into the Monex Group
- **From around 2023 onward**: launched the Monex Bitcoin crypto-asset CFD service as a JVCEA derivatives member
- No public record of recent administrative dispositions or major system failures was identified as of 2026-05-18

**Important note**: the 2018 NEM (XEM) outflow incident of roughly 580 億円 occurred at Coincheck, not at MONEX, Inc. itself. Monex was the later acquirer.

## 7. Strategic developments

- **Coincheck's Nasdaq listing plan**: parent company Monex Group has continued procedures for a Nasdaq listing through Coincheck Group N.V. from 2024 onward. As of 2026-05, official confirmation of completed listing had not been identified. A briefing for individual investors as Coincheck Group N.V. was scheduled for 2026-05-20.
- No public announcement within the past 12 months was identified regarding a major expansion of Monex Securities' own crypto-asset CFD functionality as of 2026-05.
- Broader securities-business enhancements, such as d Card point linkage, continue to expand.
