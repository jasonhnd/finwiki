# Trek Labs Japan K.K. — Japan Crypto Asset Exchange Operator (Pre-Registration Entity)


## Wiki route

This entry sits under [[exchanges/INDEX|exchanges index]]. Read it with [[exchanges/fsa-vasp-registration-system|FSA 暗号資産交換業登録制度 — 番号体系・財務局管轄・登録要件]] for adjacent context and [[fintech/japan-financial-regulation|日本金融規制 — トークン・暗号資産・決済に関する法体系]] for the broader system boundary.

> FSA Registration Number: **Not registered (application in preparation)** · JVCEA Member #2034 (Type 2 Member) · JVCEA Joined 2024 年 12 月 5 日

Trek Labs Japan K.K. is the Japan market entry vehicle for Trek Labs, the operator of global crypto asset exchange **Backpack Exchange**. The company is active as a JVCEA Type 2 Member while working toward obtaining a crypto asset exchange business registration from the FSA (as of 2026-05-18, FSA registration not yet obtained).

## 1. Legal Entity and Shareholders

- **Company Name**: Trek Labs Japan K.K.
- **Brand Name**: Backpack Exchange
- **Date of Incorporation**: 2023 年 4 月 18 日
- **Registered Address**:〒160-0023  D Tower Nishi-Shinjuku, 6 -chome 11 -ban 3 号, Nishi-Shinjuku, Shinjuku-ku, Tokyo
- **Parent Company**: Trek Labs (global entity · headquartered in Dubai)
- **Listing Status**: Unlisted
- **Shareholding Structure**: Not disclosed

Trek Labs Japan is **a group Japanese subsidiary established for the purpose of entering the Japanese market for Backpack Exchange globally**, not an independent exchange entity. The company is constructing a framework to launch Japan-facing services after obtaining FSA registration, as explicitly stated on the official website.

## 2. Licenses and Registration Status

- **Crypto Asset Exchange Business (FSA)**: **Not registered** (application in preparation) — JVCEA Type 2 membership signifies that registration is pending or planned
- **JVCEA Member**: Type 2 Member #2034 (joined 2024 年 12 月 5 日)
- **JCBA Member**: Associate member (Japan Cryptoasset Business Association)
- Financial Instruments Business, Electronic Payment Instruments Exchange Business, Funds Transfer Business: none publicly disclosed

JVCEA Type 2 membership is granted to "operators who are in the process of applying for, or planning to apply for, registration as a crypto asset exchange business operator or crypto asset-related derivatives transaction business operator." The timing of FSA official registration has not been disclosed.

## 3. Supported Assets

- **Japan-facing services**: Not yet launched as of 2026-05-18 (prior to FSA registration)
- **Global Backpack Exchange** handles major tokens including BTC, ETH, SOL, and numerous Solana ecosystem tokens (see official website)
- List of tokens for Japan-facing services: Not disclosed

## 4. Scope of Operations

- **Japan-facing official services**: Not yet launched (pending FSA registration)
- Global version offers spot trading, derivatives (perpetual futures), and staking
- Official website states plans to provide "services that customers can use with confidence through mobile applications and websites" in Japan
- Backpack Exchange globally employs **non-custodial wallet technology**, emphasizing transparency in user asset management
- Fiat currency deposit channels (Japan-facing): Not disclosed

## 5. Market Share and Trading Volume

- **Japan-facing trading volume**: Not disclosed as services have not yet launched
- **Global Backpack Exchange**: Cumulative trading volume exceeding 600 億 USD (as of end of 2024 年, official announcement); monthly active users exceeding 50 万; KYC-verified users exceeding 50 万 (as of 2024 年 3 月); services operating in more than 150  countries (all figures per company website)
- Customer base (Japan-facing): Not disclosed
- Competitive positioning (Japan): Not assessable as FSA registration has not yet been obtained

**Significant event: Rebuilding after FTX losses** — Coral (predecessor entity) lost approximately 72% of its funds due to FTX's collapse, but used that collapse as a lesson to build an exchange model centered on non-custodial wallet technology, zero-knowledge proof asset attestation, and multi-party computation (MPC) custody. CEO Ferrante has publicly stated: "Backpack is doing something different."

## 7. Strategic Developments

- **Japan as primary strategic market**: The relocation to Tokyo (second half of 2023 年) and establishment of the Japan entity indicate positioning Japan as a core strategic base. The official website explicitly states: "In the future, we aim to realize these corporate principles in the Japanese market through Trek Labs Japan K.K."
- **Strengthening ties with the Solana ecosystem**: Solana founder Anatoly Yakovenko is an investor, and the Backpack wallet functions as the flagship wallet on Solana. Operations in Japan are also expected to be centered on Solana-based products
- **EU expansion (FTX EU acquisition)**: Compliance-driven service infrastructure in the European market is also being developed in parallel with Japan (from 2025 年 onwards)
- **Timing of FSA registration application**: Not disclosed. From the time of JVCEA Type 2 membership (2024 年 12 月), review processes in precedent cases often take approximately 1 to 2  years
- **Mad Lads × Saga mobile integration**: Backpack has been adopted as the official wallet for the Solana Saga smartphone, reflecting a mobile-first strategy

## 8. Key Persons

- **Armani Ferrante**: Global CEO and co-founder of Backpack Exchange. Having accumulated technology and regulatory experience during the FTX era, founded Trek Labs through Coral. Led the development of the non-custodial model. Named as CEO in statements related to the FTX EU acquisition
- **Tristan Yver**: Co-founder of Trek Labs (referenced in CoinPost and other sources). Responsible for global expansion
- **Can Sun**: Representative Director and CEO of Trek Labs Japan K.K. (as stated on official website)
- **Anatoly Yakovenko** (Solana founder): Participated as Backpack Series A investor and publicly commented
