# JVCEA — Japan Virtual and Crypto Assets Exchange Association Self-Regulatory System Overview

> Recognised Financial Instruments Business Association · Second-Type Recognised Funds Settlement Business Association · Recognised 2018-10-24

## 1. Overview

- **Name**: Japan Virtual and Crypto assets Exchange Association (formerly Japan Virtual Currency Exchange Association; renamed 2020-05 )
- **English name**: Japan Virtual and Crypto assets Exchange Association
- **Established**: 2018年4月 (formed by consolidation of predecessor 2 associations)
- **Legal status**:
  - **Recognised Financial Instruments Business Association** under the Financial Instruments and Exchange Act
  - **Second-Type Recognised Funds Settlement Business Association** under the Payment Services Act
- **Recognition date**: 2018-10-24 (dual recognition from FSA)
- **Purpose**: Developing self-regulatory rules for crypto asset exchange and derivatives trading businesses, protecting users, and promoting a sound industry
- **Location**: Chuo-ku, Tokyo

## 2. Organisation and Governance

- **Chairman**: Elected from member company representatives (based on public information)
- **Vice-chairpersons**: Multiple (representatives from major industry VASPs rotate)
- **Auditors**: Independent audit function (including external)
- **Secretariat**: Full-time staff managing day-to-day operations
- **Specialist committees**: Token review committee, audit committee, dispute resolution committee, AML/CFT committee, and others

## 3. 5  Core Functions of Self-Regulation

1. **Membership screening** — Review of the structure and compliance of VASPs applying for membership (before and after FSA registration)
2. **Token review (White List)** — Prior-review system for new listings. Members must obtain JVCEA approval before handling new tokens (two-stage process: standard prior review and simplified review; see [[exchanges/jvcea-whitelist-token-listing|JVCEA whitelist token listing]] for details)
3. **Audit and monitoring** — Ongoing checks of members' operations, finances, and asset segregation (also reflected in [[exchanges/jvcea-spot-volume-statistics-analysis|スポット取引量統計分析]])
4. **Dispute resolution (ADR)** — Handling complaints and disputes between users and members as the designated ADR institution
5. **Awareness and education** — Industry reports, statistical publications, and financial literacy activities

## 4. Membership Categories

- **Type 1 Members (First-type)**: Crypto asset exchange businesses and crypto asset derivatives trading businesses (FSA-registered VASPs)
  - Examples: [[exchanges/jp-exchange-bitflyer|bitFlyer #1002]], [[exchanges/jp-exchange-coincheck|Coincheck #1017]], [[exchanges/jp-exchange-bittrade|BitTrade #1003]], and many others
  - Number of Type 1 members as of 2024-09 : mid-to-high 30 社 range (published and updated on the official member list page)
- **Type 2 Members (Second-type)**: Associate members and related businesses (not FSA-registered / in preparation for registration / infrastructure providers and overseas partner institutions)
- **Exact total membership count**: Published on the JVCEA official member page on a rolling basis (stated as "not disclosed" when no specific date is given)

## 5. Relationship with FSA — Dual-Supervision Structure

- Crypto asset exchange businesses are supervised under a two-tier structure: **direct FSA supervision** (Payment Services Act / amended FIEA) + **JVCEA self-regulation**
- The FSA positions JVCEA as a recognised body and delegates certain regulatory details to it (e.g. White List, self-regulatory rule revisions)
- This is the core of Japan's structural characteristic of VASP regulation being called an "administration + self-regulation hybrid"
- Related: [[fintech/japan-financial-regulation|日本金融規制]] · The FSA is the supervisory authority and JVCEA provides industry self-regulation — the two form a dual framework
