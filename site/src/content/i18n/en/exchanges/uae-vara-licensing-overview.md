## Regime Overview

The UAE issued Dubai Decree No. 111  of 2022  during 2022-03 , establishing the **VARA (Virtual Assets Regulatory Authority)**. This is the **world's first independent crypto asset-dedicated regulatory authority**, completely separated from the existing securities and banking regulatory framework and operating as a single-purpose regulator — a key distinction from other jurisdictions. Its jurisdiction covers the entire Emirate of Dubai (excluding the DIFC = Dubai International Financial Centre) and all free zones, and runs in parallel with the federal-level **UAE SCA (Securities and Commodities Authority)** and **UAE Central Bank** (with clearly delineated, non-overlapping mandates). The DIFC is separately governed by the **DFSA (Dubai Financial Services Authority)**, meaning the UAE maintains 3  co-existing crypto asset regulatory tracks within a single jurisdiction.

## Licence Categories: 7  Types (VASP Activity Categories)

VARA adopts an activity-based licensing system where operators select and obtain the licences needed for their business functions:

1. **Advisory Services** — investment advice and consulting
2. **Broker-Dealer Services** — intermediation and proprietary trading
3. **Custody Services** — safeguarding of customer assets
4. **Exchange Services** — matching exchange
5. **Lending and Borrowing Services** — lending and borrowing
6. **VA Management & Investment Services** — asset management and funds
7. **VA Transfer & Settlement Services** — remittance and payments

Full-stack operators typically obtain 5-7  categories simultaneously, offering finer granularity compared to Japan's single "crypto asset exchange business" registration.

## Key Licence Holders (2024-2026)

- **Binance** — one of the largest Dubai-based operators, VARA-approved
- **Bybit** — headquartered in UAE (from 2018), globally top-tier in Dubai derivatives
- **OKX** — obtained VARA approval in 2024-09 
- **Crypto.com** — serving both retail and institutional clients
- **Kraken** — US-origin exchange expanding UAE presence
- **Coinbase International** — derivatives only (spot falls under a separate framework)

## Competitive Positioning

In the global crypto hub competition, a 3 -way structure has emerged: **Singapore MAS (institution-focused, strict)** / **Hong Kong SFC (retail permitted, limited tokens)** / **UAE VARA (full-stack permissioned, broadest scope)**. Dubai is the most flexible hub by virtue of its **corporate tax incentives** (0% corporate tax in free zones) + **retail permitted** + **diverse licence categories**. It also serves as a gateway to the Middle East, South Asia, and Africa, with stablecoin issuers such as Tether adopting UAE-routing strategies.

## Relevance to Japan

- **Bybit**, headquartered in UAE, continued operating for Japan residents through 2024-11  → fully withdrew from Japan in 2026-03  after 3  FSA warnings (details at [[exchanges/jp-foreign-exchange-bybit]])
- **OKX** holds both UAE VARA and Japan OKJ ([[exchanges/jp-exchange-okcoin-japan]]) licences simultaneously, pursuing a dual-licensing strategy
- VARA licensing has become a "global credibility floor," providing an indirect advantage when undergoing JVCEA review for Japan entry
