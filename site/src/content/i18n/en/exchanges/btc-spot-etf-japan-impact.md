## 1. Overview

2024-01-10, the US SEC approved BTC spot ETFs 11 件 all at once (BlackRock IBIT, Fidelity FBTC, Ark 21Shares ARKB, Bitwise BITB, Grayscale GBTC, Invesco/Galaxy BTCO, VanEck HODL, Valkyrie BRRR, Franklin EZBC, WisdomTree BTCW, Hashdex DEFI). This released global institutional-investor flows into the crypto-asset spot market through a regulated channel, giving hedge funds, pension funds, and RIAs (Registered Investment Advisors) low-cost exposure. The impact on domestic Japanese CEXs is observed through 3  channels: **(a) increased trading volume from spot price appreciation**, **(b) acceleration of domestic institutional accounts**, and **(c) the formalization of debate over lifting the ban on a domestic BTC ETF**.

## 2. Numbers (2024-2026)

- **IBIT (BlackRock)**: AUM on the order of $80-100B · largest spot-ETF share · reached $50B AUM at record speed
- **Total US spot-ETF AUM**: $200B+ (2026 estimate) · absorbing a few percent of global BTC circulating supply
- **Coinbase Custody**: holds custody for 9 件of 11 件(institutional-custody oligopoly) — consistent with [[exchanges/global-institutional-custody-five-pillars]]
- **Daily net inflow**: peaked above $1B/day (during the 2024 -spring and late-2024 rallies)

## 3. 3 -channel impact on domestic CEXs

- **(a) Price spillover**: BTC spot price appreciation → increased domestic spot trading volume · 1 BTC breaking above 1,500 万円 (first reached 2024-03 ). [[exchanges/jvcea-spot-volume-statistics-analysis]] also confirms a rebound in 2024 spot monthly trading volume
- **(b) Institutionalization**: acceleration of domestic corporate account openings · JVCEA statistics also show net growth in corporate accounts at 2024+. bitFlyer / SBI VC Trade / Coincheck strengthen their corporate-facing services
- **(c) Debate over lifting the domestic ETF ban**: over 2025-2026 , debate over domestic approval of BTC/ETH spot ETFs advances at the FSA + industry bodies (JVCEA/JCBA) · **not yet approved** as of 2026-05  (legal amendment required) — see [[exchanges/jp-vasp-regulatory-timeline]]

## 4. Institutional barriers to lifting the Japan ETF ban

- **Tax inequity**: crypto assets are miscellaneous income (progressive, up to 55%) · ETFs are subject to separate self-assessment taxation (20.315%) — a 35pp tax-rate gap on the same underlying asset
- **FIEA + Investment Trust Act amendments required**: adding crypto assets to the "investment-target assets" of investment trusts. The current Investment Trust Act Article 2  does not enumerate them as "specified assets," which is a cabinet-order-amendment-level matter
- **Regulatory-body allocation**: FSA supervisory guidelines + industry-response costs · custody, valuation, and liquidity rules need to be established
- **2026  amendment proposal**: debate in progress · a phased approach of sequentially lifting the ban on ETH/BTC is favored

## 5. Investor implications

- **Indirect exposure via US ETFs**: Japanese investors can buy US spot ETFs (IBIT, etc.) through Nomura Securities / SBI Securities / Rakuten Securities / Monex Securities · supported in tokutei (designated) accounts without waiting for domestic approval (20% separate self-assessment)
- **Competitive pressure on domestic CEXs**: risk of declining domestic spot-trading share when the ETF ban is lifted. In the US too, a trend of Coinbase retail spot volume flowing to ETFs was observed after spot-ETF approval → this also affects the sales-counter-model revenue of [[exchanges/jp-cex-sales-vs-exchange-model-economics]]
- **Inflow of institutional players**: increasing strategic significance for major financial conglomerates such as Monex / SBI / Nomura / Mitsui Sumitomo Trust → [[exchanges/jp-listed-cex-related-companies-matrix]]
