## 3. Listed assets

Since the company does not directly provide crypto-asset buying/selling or custody services to individuals / corporations, "listed assets" is defined as the number of supported assets through which it, as B2B infrastructure, assists client VASPs.

- **Ginco Enterprise Wallet support**: centered on Bitcoin / Ethereum, it claims a domestically top-level breadth of blockchain support, including ERC20 tokens（addable in as little as 2weeks）and proprietary-blockchain currencies（addable in as little as 1months）
- **Consumer Ginco Wallet**: a self-custody mobile wallet supporting Bitcoin / Ethereum and several other assets（offered since 2018年4月）
- A specific official list of supported assets is not posted on the official site ·「not disclosed」

## 4. Scope of business

**The company is not a crypto-asset-exchange operator（VASP）; it is classified as a B2B infrastructure-provider company for crypto-asset operators.** Rather than conducting the buying/selling, intermediation, or custody of crypto assets in its own name, it provides the systems and technical infrastructure for VASPs and others to do so.

### Main products / services

| Service | Content |
|---|---|
| **Ginco Enterprise Wallet** | An integrated cold/hot-wallet management system leveraging HSMs（hardware security modules）. No. 1 in domestic B2B crypto-asset-wallet adoption share（as of the 2021年8月 announcement）|
| **GEW Monitoring Solution** | A business-use monitoring tool that manages multiple wallets in an integrated manner |
| **Ginco Web3 Cloud** | A financial-institution-grade general-purpose blockchain development platform（public/private supported）|
| **Web3 Professional Service** | End-to-end business-creation support from planning / requirements definition through development |
| **Ginco Wallet** | A consumer self-custody mobile-wallet app（offering began 2018年4月）|

### Supported business domains
- Crypto-asset exchanges（providing wallets to FSA-registered VASPs）
- Financial institutions（securities firms / trust banks, etc.）
- NFT-service infrastructure（NFT BASE）
- Security-token（ST）custody systems
- Web3 consulting / node hosting

## 5. Market share / positioning

- **Domestic B2B crypto-asset wallets**: in 2021年8月 the company announced that "Ginco Enterprise Wallet, a business-use wallet for crypto-asset-exchange operators, holds the top domestic share." Specific figures（number of firms / percentage）are not disclosed.
- **Customer base**: domestic FSA-registered crypto-asset-exchange operators, securities firms（Mizuho Securities / Daiwa Securities Group, etc.）, trust banks（Mitsubishi UFJ Trust and Banking, etc.）
- **Competitors**: internationally, [[exchanges/jp-custody-fireblocks-japan|Fireblocks（米国）]] has already entered the Japanese market. [[exchanges/jp-custody-komainu|Komainu（Nomura系）]] is institutional custody. As domestic peer infrastructure there are system vendors of the Liquid lineage and others, but Ginco differentiates on "all in-house development" and "the most-supported chains domestically." Its positioning is detailed in [[exchanges/jp-institutional-custody-three-pillars|JP institutional custody 三本柱]].
- **Toyo Keizai "Amazing Ventures 100"**: selected in 2023年9月

## 7. Strategic trends（the last 12 months）

- **2025-09**: partnered with Membrane（UK-based lending & derivatives management）. Provides a Bitcoin holding + yield-management solution
- **2025-11**: built a partnership with UPBOND. Creating use cases in the DID/VC × Crypto domain
- **2025-11**: Min Brian appointed as the new CISO. Strengthening IT governance / external collaboration
- **2025-12**: collaborated with Babylon Labs to promote "BTCFi 2.0" in Japan（Bitcoin staking/earning functionality）
- **2025-12**: joined Canton Network（an enterprise blockchain of the Goldman Sachs / Digital Asset lineage）as a validator
- **2026-01**: concluded a Web3 -domain partnership with GVA Law Office
- **2026-03**: supported a web3 -technology-adoption verification in Ebara Corporation's "Ebara Brain Project"
- **2026-04**: management-structure change（presumed to be a representative handover from Myuto Morikawa to Ryo Sakane, but details are not disclosed）

**Strategic direction**: it is diversifying from wallets for crypto-asset exchanges toward ST and crypto-asset-trust infrastructure for financial institutions（trust banks / securities firms）, and further toward next-generation Web3  infrastructure such as BTCFi / DID. The establishment of an Indian development base is seen as groundwork for strengthening cost competitiveness and expanding talent. As Fireblocks（US）strengthens direct sales to Japan's major financial institutions, Ginco maintains a posture of keeping "the depth of domestic regulatory compliance" and "the flexibility from all-in-house development" as competitive advantages.
