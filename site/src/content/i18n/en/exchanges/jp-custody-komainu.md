## 3. Handled assets (custody scope)

No specific list of tokens is disclosed on the official site (because it is a custody specialist, there is no token list as on an exchange).

- Custody support for major crypto-assets with **BTC as the core**
- It has been confirmed that 2021 年 it served as custody for a Bitcoin ETP (an ETF-like product) on the Swiss stock exchange
- Custodial staking support: support for proof-of-stake assets such as ETH (2021 年~)
- Komainu Connect (2023 年~) covers trading · collateral use · lending and borrowing
- As of 2026 年 4 月, it is rolling out Komainu CORE (collateral-as-a-service)

## 4. Scope of business

The company's services are the 4  pillars of a custody-first (with custody as the axis) approach:

1. **Custody**: cold custody leveraging MPC (Multi-Party Computation) + HSM (Hardware Security Module) wallet technology. Independently verifiable on-chain segregated management.
2. **Custodial Staking**: an institution-oriented service to earn on-chain rewards (staking) while keeping assets in custody. Launched 2021 年.
3. **Komainu Connect (collateral management)**: connectivity infrastructure that allows assets within custody to be used as collateral for trading · borrowing · lending. Launched 2023 年. Partnered with major exchanges such as OKX.
4. **Komainu CORE**: a Collateral-as-a-Service solution that lets digital assets function as regulation-compliant collateral. Began offering 2026 年 4 月.

Retail-oriented services such as fiat-deposit channels are not offered (purely institution-oriented infrastructure).

## 5. Market position · customer base

- **Target**: institutional investors · financial institutions · sovereign nations / government-affiliated funds
- **Disclosed customer cases (primary-source confirmed)**:
  - **UK police**: 2021 年 1 月, adopted Komainu for custody of seized crypto-assets (disclosed)
  - **Bitcoin ETP listed on the Swiss stock exchange**: 2021 年 1 月, selected as the custody provider for the ETP (disclosed)
  - **UAE government / VARA-jurisdiction case**: 2023 年, involvement in a government-affiliated matter was reported around the same time as VARA license acquisition in the UAE
  - **Partnership with OKX (Komainu Connect)**: 2023 年 6 月, disclosed a custody-connectivity partnership with OKX
- **Competitive comparison**: competes with institution-oriented custody providers such as Anchorage Digital, [[exchanges/jp-custody-fireblocks-japan|Fireblocks]], and BitGo (see [[exchanges/global-institutional-custody-five-pillars|グローバル institutional custody 5 本柱]]). The company's differentiators are multi-jurisdiction compliance across Jersey · UK FCA · [[exchanges/uae-vara-licensing-overview|VARA]] + affinity with the [[JapanFG/nomura-hd|Nomura]] group.
- **Account count · AUM under custody**: not disclosed

## 7. Strategic developments (the most recent 12  months)

- **Propine acquisition (2024-10)**: acquired Propine, a Singapore-based institution-oriented custody firm. Expanding its footprint into the Asia-Pacific market is the explicit strategic objective.
- **Blockstream Series B (2025-01)**: raised $75M. Suggests strengthening Bitcoin-centric institutional custody + handling of Bitcoin-native assets. At the same time, Adam Back (Blockstream CEO) · PeterPaul Pardi · Nicolas Brand (Blockstream Capital Partners) joined the Board.
- **FCA registration (2025)**: formalization of service provision in the UK. Strengthens its appeal to institutional investors with a London base.
- **Komainu CORE (2026-04)**: SaaS-ification of regulation-compliant collateral management. Suggests entry into the digital-collateral market (connection with DeFi, repo transactions, etc.).
- **Japan expansion**: as of 2026-05 , no official announcement aimed at Japan. However, Nomura-affiliated Laser Digital Japan set up a BTC fund in 2026-01 , and there is a view that this is indirect groundwork within the group for Japan-oriented services (no official information).
