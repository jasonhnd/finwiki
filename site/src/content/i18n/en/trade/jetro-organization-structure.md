# JETRO is not a membership organization but an independent administrative agency under METI


## Wiki route

This entry sits under [[trade/jetro-members-gateway-model|JETRO Members 有料会員制は B2B 提案の入口ゲート]] and should be read with [[INDEX|FinWiki index]].

> [!info] TL;DR
> JETRO (Japan External Trade Organization) is not an industry body with "member companies" but an independent administrative agency under the jurisdiction of the Ministry of Economy, Trade and Industry (established 2003年). It has 76 overseas offices and 52 domestic offices worldwide, with 1,925名 staff. Its services target mainly export-oriented SMEs, and many services are free or low-cost. Financing and insurance for overseas export deals are handled by separate organizations, [[financial-regulators/jbic|JBIC]] and [[policy-finance/nexi|NEXI]]; within [[policy-finance/japan-policy-finance-system|日本のポリシーファイナンス体系]], JETRO is responsible for non-financial sales-channel and information support.

**Practical implication**: The right way to approach JETRO is not "selling to members" but "proposing collaboration with JETRO" (co-hosting seminars, jointly advancing PoCs, recommended-tool certification, etc.). In practice, [[trade/jetro-members-gateway-model|ジェトロ・メンバーズ入会]] becomes the entry point to the B2B proposal channel. JETRO actively supports Web3/Fintech startups through accelerator programs such as J-StarX, so a receiving channel for [[fintech/japan-stablecoin-regulatory-landscape|決済インフラ提案]] does exist. For cross-border M&A deals that involve exports or setting up local bases, the financing-side process of [[finance/cross-border-m-a-japan|cross-border M&A Japan]] must be designed in parallel with JETRO's market-research support.

Source: compilation of public information (JETRO official organization chart https://www.jetro.go.jp/jetro/profile/, METI list of agencies under its jurisdiction, JETRO list of overseas offices)
