# JETRO is not a membership organization but an incorporated administrative agency under METI


## Wiki route

This entry sits under [[trade/jetro-members-gateway-model|JETRO Members 有料会員制は B2B 提案の入口ゲート]] and should be read with [[INDEX|FinWiki index]].

> [!info] TL;DR
> JETRO (Japan External Trade Organization) is not an industry body with "member companies," but an incorporated administrative agency under the jurisdiction of the Ministry of Economy, Trade and Industry (established 2003年). 76 overseas offices worldwide · 52 domestic offices, with 1,925名 staff. Its service targets are mainly export-oriented small and medium enterprises, and most services are free or low-cost. Financing / insurance for overseas export deals is borne by separate organizations, [[JapanFG/jbic|JBIC]] and [[policy-finance/nexi|NEXI]], and within [[policy-finance/japan-policy-finance-system|日本のポリシーファイナンス体系]], JETRO handles the non-financial sales-channel / information support.

**Practical implication**: The correct approach to JETRO is not "selling to members" but "a collaboration proposal with JETRO" (co-hosting seminars, jointly advancing PoCs, recommended-tool certification, etc.). In practice, [[trade/jetro-members-gateway-model|ジェトロ・メンバーズ入会]] becomes the entry to the B2B proposal channel. JETRO actively supports Web3/Fintech startups through accelerator programs such as J-StarX, so a receiving venue for [[fintech/japan-stablecoin-regulatory-landscape|決済インフラ提案]] exists. When a cross-border M&A deal involves exports / setting up a local base, the financing-side process of [[finance/cross-border-m-a-japan|cross-border M&A Japan]] must be designed in parallel with JETRO's market-research support.

Source: compilation of public information (JETRO official org chart https://www.jetro.go.jp/jetro/profile/, METI-jurisdiction-corporation disclosure, JETRO list of overseas offices)
