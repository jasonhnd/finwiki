### 6.1 . Differentiation as the tail of the U.S. 3  major-custodian group

- Smaller scale than [[foreign-financial-institutions/bny-mellon-japan|BNY Mellon]] / [[foreign-financial-institutions/state-street-japan|State Street]].
- Differentiation axis: **integration with wealthy-client private-bank functions** in the United States, plus **deep service to selected large institutional investors**.
- In Japan, it does not roll out a full-scale wealthy-client private bank, and instead focuses on institutional investor services for GPIF, major life / non-life insurers, and major asset managers. ^[likely]

### 6.2 . Position as a GPIF / public-pension mandate provider

- GPIF overseas-asset custody is outsourced across multiple foreign custodians, including [[foreign-financial-institutions/state-street-japan|State Street]] / [[foreign-financial-institutions/bny-mellon-japan|BNY Mellon]] / Northern Trust.
- Rising overseas allocation by public and corporate pensions is a tailwind for the foreign-custodian sector overall.

### 6.3 . Division of labor with Japanese-bank trust banks

- **Japanese-bank trust banks** such as [[trust-banks/master-trust-bank|Master Trust Bank of Japan]], [[trust-banks/mitsubishi-ufj-trust-bank|Mitsubishi UFJ Trust and Banking]], and [[trust-banks/sumitomo-mitsui-trust|Sumitomo Mitsui Trust]] have overwhelming share in custody and management mandates for domestic assets.
- Foreign custodians such as Northern Trust specialize in **overseas-asset custody mandates**.

### 6.4 . Wealthy-client private bank, United States

- Northern Trust's **core business since founding** is private banking for wealthy Chicago clients, dating from its 1889  opening.
- It has deep client relationships in wealth management, trust, and estate administration for ultra-high-net-worth U.S. clients.
- In Japan, it does not directly compete with private-bank players such as UBS [[foreign-financial-institutions/ubs-japan]] / [[trust-banks/lgt-wealth-management-trust|LGT]] / [[trust-banks/smbc-trust-bank|SMBC Trust (PRESTIA)]] and instead focuses on the institutional investor channel.
