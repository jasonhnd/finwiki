# JFC Agriculture, Forestry And Fisheries Division Operating Model

## Wiki Route

This entry sits under [[policy-finance/INDEX|policy-finance index]]. Read it with [[JapanFG/jfc|JFC]], [[JapanFG/norinchukin|Norinchukin]], and [[policy-finance/agriculture-credit-guarantee-system|Japan agriculture credit-guarantee system]] for the cooperative-finance perimeter.

## TL;DR

JFC's Agriculture, Forestry and Fisheries Division is the policy-finance lane for Japan's primary-sector borrowers and related food-industry companies. It was formed when the former Agriculture, Forestry and Fisheries Finance Corporation was folded into JFC during the 2008 institutional merger. Its product set is long-tenor, low-rate policy lending for agriculture, forestry, fisheries, and downstream food-industry investment.

## 1. Mandate And Counterpart System

The division is unusual because its private-sector counterpart is not fragmented commercial banking but a cooperative pyramid: JA Bank, fisheries cooperative finance, forestry cooperatives, prefectural cooperative federations, and [[JapanFG/norinchukin|Norinchukin]]. MAFF policy plans and subsidy programs shape the product menu and underwriting overlay.

| Lane | Main product family | Typical use | Counterpart channel |
|---|---|---|---|
| Agriculture | Super L Loan and management-entity development loans | Capex, refinancing, scale expansion, certified-farmer support | JA and agricultural cooperative credit channels |
| Forestry | Forestry infrastructure and forest-management loans | Forest roads, planting, thinning, and long-cycle forest capex | Forest cooperatives and prefectural federations |
| Fisheries | Fisheries modernization and coastal-fisheries improvement loans | Vessels, equipment, port-related capex, and working-capital resilience | Fisheries cooperative finance channels |
| Food industry | Food-industry competitiveness and safety-net loans | HACCP, cold chain, processing, export capacity, restructuring | Food processors and distributors |
| Cross-sector | Subordinated quasi-equity loans | Rehabilitation, growth capital, and balance-sheet strengthening | Direct JFC underwriting plus public-program coordination |

## 2. Credit Philosophy

The post-2000s policy frame is management-entity development: support incorporated farms, community-based farming organizations, and certified business farmers rather than relying only on aging individual operators. JFC lending fills tenor, risk, and policy gaps that ordinary cooperative or commercial lenders are reluctant to hold on balance sheet.

Guarantee and insurance support commonly appears through prefectural agricultural and fisheries credit-fund associations, with central support from JAFFIC. These guarantees usually support bank-originated loans, while direct JFC loans remain on JFC's own balance sheet.

## 3. Underwriting Process

1. **Front-end intake.** Borrowers approach an AFF branch directly or, more often, arrive through JA, fisheries, forestry, prefectural extension, or certified-farmer channels.
2. **Eligibility screen.** Staff confirm product eligibility, MAFF policy fit, borrower designation, and whether grant or subsidy blending is relevant.
3. **Branch credit review.** Underwriters assess seasonal cash flow, biological yield risk, weather risk, market-price risk, and repayment capacity.
4. **Collateral and guarantee coordination.** Land, equipment, cooperative guarantees, and public-program support are reviewed together.
5. **Committee escalation.** Larger loans, subordinated quasi-equity, food-industry M&A, and high-policy-salience cases move to regional or headquarters credit committees.
6. **Execution and monitoring.** JFC tracks covenants, project completion, policy-use compliance, and restructuring needs.
7. **Default and restructuring.** Losses on direct loans flow to JFC; parallel guarantee routes act only on the covered cooperative or bank-originated loan.

## 4. Co-Financing With Private Institutions

The division's main coordination partners are JA Bank, fisheries cooperative finance, forestry cooperatives, Norinchukin, regional banks, shinkin banks, and local governments. JFC often provides the policy-tenor or subordinated slice while private lenders provide ordinary working-capital or shorter-tenor loans.

## 5. Policy Objectives

The division supports food security, farm consolidation, forestry renewal, fisheries modernization, disaster recovery, rural employment, export-capacity building, and food-industry productivity. Its product menu should be read against MAFF master plans and JFC's medium-term management plan.

## 6. Recent Policy Shift

Recent emphasis has moved toward food security, climate and disaster resilience, export-oriented food supply chains, farmland consolidation, succession planning, and capital-like support for incorporated primary-sector borrowers.
