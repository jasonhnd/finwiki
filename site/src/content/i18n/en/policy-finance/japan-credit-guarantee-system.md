# Japan credit guarantee system

## TL;DR

Japan's credit guarantee system is the public-credit infrastructure that supports SME and small-business funding in Japan. It is a layered system: (1) 51 prefectural / city-level **Credit Guarantee Corporations (CGCs)** guarantee eligible SME borrowing from financial institutions; (2) the **Japan Federation of Credit Guarantee Corporations (JFG)** coordinates the 51 CGCs and pools the responsibility-sharing burdens; (3) the **credit insurance** layer is operated by [[JapanFG/jfc|Japan Finance Corporation (JFC)]], which reinsures CGC guarantees so that final residual loss falls on the public-credit infrastructure rather than collapsing the CGC layer. Together this is the credit-supplementation system. It is not a single lender; it is a public-credit architecture. The 2020-2022 ZeroZero loan special program, effectively interest-free and unsecured, ran through this system and continues to unwind into 2024-2026 in subrogation cycles.

The system is therefore a **vertical public-credit pipeline**: borrower → lender → local CGC (first loss) → JFG coordination → JFC credit insurance (reinsurance) → METI policy backstop.

## 3. Main guarantee products: system-level menu

- **General guarantee**: standard SME bank-loan guarantee under the responsibility-sharing system, typically 80% guarantee and 20% lender retention for ordinary programs.
- **Small-business management-improvement guarantee**: guarantee for chamber-of-commerce recommended small-business improvement loans.
- **Startup-related guarantee**: startup and new-business guarantee.
- **Business-succession special guarantee**: guarantee for ownership and succession transitions.
- **Management-innovation support guarantee**: management-innovation and business-transformation guarantee.
- **Refinancing guarantee**: refinancing and debt-consolidation guarantee.
- **ABL guarantee**: accounts-receivable and inventory-collateralized guarantee.
- **Safety-Net Guarantees, categories 1-8**: designated-event guarantees with enhanced terms. See section 5.
- **Crisis-related guarantee**: national-emergency framework; up to 100% guarantee in a declared crisis.
- **Disaster-related guarantee**: disaster-recovery guarantee.
- **COVID-era ZeroZero loan guarantee**: effectively interest-free and unsecured special guarantee, with ramp-up from 2020-05, new origination closed in 2021-03, and servicing / unwind continuing through 2026+. See section 6.

## 4. Guarantee-ratio mechanics: responsibility-sharing system

The **responsibility-sharing system**, introduced in 2007-10, leaves a defined share of risk with the lending financial institution rather than offering a 100% guarantee:

- **Standard programs**: CGC guarantees **80%** of the loan; lender retains **20%** residual risk, or uses a pari-passu loss-sharing variant. This is the post-2007 default.
- **Full guarantee (100%) exceptions**: certain policy programs, including Safety-Net categories 1-3, crisis-related guarantees, qualifying startup guarantees, qualifying small-business management-improvement guarantees, and ZeroZero crisis designs, retain 100% guarantee with no lender retention.
- **Pari-passu loss-sharing variant**: under the responsibility-sharing system, lenders can choose either (a) 80% guarantee structure or (b) loss-sharing structure where CGC and lender share losses pari-passu under a defined formula.

The 2007 introduction of responsibility sharing was a deliberate policy shift to restore lender screening discipline after concerns about moral hazard in the pre-2007 100%-guarantee regime.

## 5. Safety-Net Guarantee programs

The **Safety-Net Guarantee** framework has eight numbered designations, each covering a different distress scenario, and provides enhanced terms, often including 100% guarantee and a separate quota outside the borrower's normal guarantee cap:

- **Category 1**: large-enterprise restructuring impact, designated by METI based on a major-firm collapse or restructuring affecting downstream SMEs.
- **Category 2**: large-enterprise operational shock, such as a factory closure with supply-chain impact.
- **Category 3**: bank failure or financial-institution liquidation affecting borrower SMEs.
- **Category 4**: natural disaster or wide-area emergency, frequently activated for typhoons, earthquakes, and floods.
- **Category 5**: industry-wide structural distress, with specific industries designated quarterly by METI based on macro and financial conditions.
- **Category 6**: failure of a financial institution, with a specific scope distinct from Category 3.
- **Category 7**: large-enterprise restructuring with cooperative-finance impact.
- **Category 8**: impact from Resolution and Collection Corporation demand.

The **crisis-related guarantee**, separate from Safety-Net Guarantees, is the broader national-emergency framework with 100% guarantee, activated only on rare nationwide-crisis designations such as COVID-19 or crisis conditions comparable to the 2008 global financial crisis.

## 6. ZeroZero loan unwind status (2020 → 2026)

The **ZeroZero loan** program, effectively interest-free and unsecured, was the central crisis-finance response to COVID-19:

| Phase | Period | Status |
|---|---|---|
| Origination ramp | 2020-03 → 2020-07 | METI launches private-sector ZeroZero via the credit guarantee system in 2020-05; rapid uptake reaching trillions of yen in months. JFC operates a parallel ZeroZero program directly. |
| Sustained origination | 2020-08 → 2021-03 | Continued large-scale guarantee origination via 51 CGCs nationwide. |
| Origination close | 2021-03 → 2022 | Private-sector ZeroZero closed to new applications (with limited extensions for narrow categories). JFC ZeroZero closed similarly through 2022. |
| Grace period | 2020 origination + 3y grace | Borrowers typically had a principal-grace period (commonly up to 5 years for principal, with interest subsidy during initial period). The bulk of 2020-vintage loans entered repayment-start through 2023–2024. |
| Subrogation wave | 2023-2026 | As repayment starts, subrogation by CGCs ramps. Multiple business-trade-press reports show a clear nationwide uptick from late 2023 onward. |
| Post-COVID exit framework | 2022-2025 | METI / SME Agency coordinated with JFG for unified guidance on workout / refinancing of ZeroZero borrowers, including refinancing guarantees, business-improvement products, and patient support for individual operators. |
| Continued tail | 2026+ | Subrogation tail extends as remaining 2020-vintage loans complete repayment; bankruptcy statistics show ZeroZero-related bankruptcies as a tracked sub-category through 2024–2026. |

The ZeroZero unwind is the **defining recent stress event** for the credit guarantee system and is visible in CGC subrogation statistics nationwide. Local-level coverage of the unwind appears in [[policy-finance/hokkaido-credit-guarantee-corp|Hokkaido CGC]] and other local pages where strategic.

## 7. Funding source

Each local CGC is capitalized through a mix of:

- **National contribution**: METI / SME Agency capital and subsidy.
- **Prefectural / city contribution**: local government capital; reflects local SME-policy priority.
- **Lender contribution**: partner banks, shinkin banks, and credit cooperatives contribute capital to their local CGC.
- **Guarantee fee income**: annual rate on guaranteed principal, typically in the low single-digit-percent range, with policy-program subsidies.
- **Federation pooled fund** — JFG-administered reserves backing system-level coordination.
- **JFC credit insurance**: reinsurance reimbursement on subrogation events through the [[JapanFG/jfc|JFC credit-insurance operations]] account.

The **public-credit risk-bearing waterfall** is: borrower recovery → CGC reserves → JFC reinsurance → national fiscal backstop.

## 8. Default / subrogation flow

1. Borrower defaults on guaranteed loan.
2. Lender claims under the guarantee; CGC pays **subrogation** to the lender for the guaranteed portion, typically 80% under responsibility sharing and 100% under crisis schemes.
3. CGC pursues **recovery** against the borrower, collateral, or personal guarantor.
4. CGC files insurance claim with JFC under the credit-insurance contract; JFC pays insurance proceeds to the CGC.
5. Residual loss (after recovery + JFC insurance) is absorbed by CGC reserves; if reserve depletion threatens, federation / national capital injection backstops.

Recovery rates vary by region, sector, and collateral type — urban CGCs (Tokyo, Osaka) typically have higher recovery rates than rural CGCs (Hokkaido, Tōhoku, Kyūshū rural) because of thicker secondary markets for commercial real estate and equipment.

## 9. Year evolution

| Year | Event |
|---|---|
| 1937 | Tokyo Credit Guarantee Corporation, the first credit guarantee corporation, established. |
| 1953 | Credit Guarantee Corporation Act enacted; nationwide CGC framework created. |
| 1958 | SME Credit Insurance Corporation established, later integrated into JFC. |
| 2007-10 | Responsibility-sharing system introduced, moving the default from 100% guarantee to 80% / 20% lender retention. |
| 2008-2009 | GFC emergency response; Safety-Net category 5 widely activated; emergency guarantee program used a near-100% framework. |
| 2011 | Tohoku earthquake; disaster-related guarantees and category 4 designations widely activated. |
| 2020-05 | ZeroZero private-sector program, effectively interest-free and unsecured, launched through the CGC system. |
| 2021-03 | ZeroZero private-sector origination closed (with limited extensions). |
| 2023+ | ZeroZero subrogation wave ramps as 2020-vintage grace periods end. |
| 2024-2025 | Workout and refinancing product mix expanded under a patient-support policy framing. |
| 2025 | Continued unwind; bankruptcy statistics show ZeroZero-related sub-category tail. |

## 10. Boundary cases

- **System vs federation** — this page explains the mechanism; [[policy-finance/national-federation-credit-guarantee-corporations]] explains the national coordinating body.
- **Guarantee vs loan** — the CGC guarantees credit; the loan is made by a financial institution. The CGC is not the lender.
- **System vs single CGC** — this page covers the system; local CGC pages cover specific prefectural / city corporations.
- **SME vs agriculture / fisheries** — agriculture uses [[policy-finance/agriculture-credit-guarantee-system]]; fisheries uses [[policy-finance/fisheries-credit-guarantee-system]].
- **CGC vs JFC direct lending** — JFC also lends directly to SMEs; the CGC system is a guarantee-on-private-lending mechanism rather than a direct-lending program.
