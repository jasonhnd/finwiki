Taiwan Business Bank Japan sits under [[foreign-financial-institutions/INDEX|foreign-financial-institutions INDEX]] and is a Taiwan-Japan foreign-bank branch route in [[foreign-financial-institutions/foreign-bank-branches-japan-index|the foreign-bank branch registry]]. ## Overview

Taiwan Business Bank's Japan route is listed in the FSA bank-license workbook and in public Japanese banking-directory material.^[source:fsa-ginkou-2026][source:jba-bankdb-tbb-2026] JBA's English member list includes Taiwan Business Bank under Associate Members / Foreign Banks, so this page should be treated as a foreign branch route, not a Japan-incorporated domestic bank.^[source:jba-members-2026]

The page is worth standalone treatment because Taiwan Business Bank describes its 1976 reorganization as the first specialized bank established by the government for financing assistance and guidance to small and medium-sized enterprises.^[source:tbb-history-2026] That makes it a useful comparator to Korea's [[foreign-financial-institutions/industrial-bank-of-korea-japan|Industrial Bank of Korea Japan]] and to Japanese SME-policy finance routes.

## Public Context

Taiwan Business Bank should not be treated as only another Taiwan registry row. In FinWiki, it is the Taiwan SME-bank route. TBB states that its domestic branch network covers Taiwan and that it operates global networks including Tokyo Branch in Japan, plus Hong Kong, Los Angeles, New York, Sydney, Brisbane, Shanghai, Wuhan and Yangon.^[source:tbb-history-2026] The useful question is how a specialized home-market SME bank supports Japan-facing trade, corporate accounts, remittance, and supplier flows.

The current page intentionally avoids unverified claims about individual borrowers or industry deals. Add those only when a public transaction source exists.
