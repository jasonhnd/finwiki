##Overview

Clearstream Banking S.A. Japan Branch is best read as a Luxembourg ICSD bank branch inside the Deutsche Boerse / Clearstream post-trade stack. Its Japan significance is securities settlement, custody, collateral management, disclosure routing, and JASDEC / JGB access, not consumer banking.

Registration is observed in the checked FSA source as of 2026-05-13; this page does not independently opine on current licensing beyond the cited public source.

## Why It Is Not Registry-only

Clearstream and [[foreign-financial-institutions/euroclear-bank-japan|Euroclear]] are the two natural ICSD comparison pages in the Japan wiki. A registry row is not enough because Clearstream's public materials are used to route:

- Japanese securities settlement and custody;
- disclosure requirements for Japan-market holdings;
- institutional access to Japanese corporate, municipal, and government securities;
- collateral and global-liquidity management;
- post-trade infrastructure links between Japanese CSD / CCP routes and offshore investor accounts.

## Research Checklist

1. Identify the security type: JGB, corporate bond, municipal bond, equity, investment trust, or another instrument.
2. Confirm whether the question is settlement, custody, collateral, disclosure, or tax / beneficial-owner documentation.
3. Check Clearstream's current market coverage page before using any service-level statement.
4. Cross-read local infrastructure with [[securities/japan-securities-depository-center|JASDEC]], [[securities/japan-securities-clearing-corp|JSCC]], and [[securities/japan-market-infrastructure-map|market infrastructure map]].
5. For custody-bank comparisons, open [[banking/japan-trust-bank-custody-map|trust-bank custody map]] and the global custodian pages.

## Caveats

- ICSD presence does not mean every Japanese security is equally available through every account type.
- Disclosure and withholding documentation can depend on investor type, account chain, and instrument.
- Public registry status be refreshed from the FSA source when current licensing matters.
