## 1. Company Profile

**Ultimate parent company**: Citigroup Inc. (U.S. Delaware corporation, NYSE: C, head office New York City)
**Establishment** (Parent): 1998 (Citicorp + Travelers Group merger, later Travelers Insurance was separated)
**Predecessor**: 1812 City Bank of New York → National City Bank of New York (1865 national law bank conversion) → Citicorp (1967 holding company) → Citigroup (1998 merger)
**Regulatory Capital Status**: FSB G-SIB **Bucket 3** (one of the world's largest categories, additional CET1 +2.0% requirements)^[extracted]

## 2. History (excerpt from chronology)

| Year | Event |
|---|---|
| **1902** | **Opening of National City Bank Tokyo Branch** (one of the earliest foreign presences in the history of a Japanese bank)^[extracted] |
| 1960s〜70s | Expanding corporate and transaction banking during Japan's period of high growth |
| 1980s | **Citibank Retail Development** (Unique branding as a foreign personal bank, attracting wealthy urban customers with 24h ATMs and foreign currency deposits) |
| 1998 | Parent Citicorp + Travelers Group merger → **Citigroup launched** |
| 2007〜2008 | Subprime crisis → Citi posting large-scale losses |
| **2008-11/2009** | In the aftermath of the Lehman Shock, Citi **U.S. government public funding injection** (250 億 dollars + additional support via TARP, subject to stress test) / Effective nationalization phase |
| **2009-10** | **Sold Nikko Cordial Securities to SMFG** (→ [[securities-firms/smbc-nikko]] after). Part of Citi Japan Wholesale Reconstruction |
| 2010s First half | Japan Retail to be subject to review under global "Citi Holdings" non-core asset restructuring scheme |
| **2014-09** | **Citibank Retail → Announcement of sale to SMBC Trust Bank** (individual customers, branches, PB business) |
| **2015-11** | **Retail sale completed** → Continued under the ``PRESTIA'' brand** under SMBC Trust Bank. Citibank (corporate personality) disappears |
| 2014〜 | Refocus our base in Japan to **Corporate Banks + Investment Banks + GTS + Institutional Investors**. Complete withdrawal from retail |

## 5. Supervision and regulation

- **Supervising**: Financial Services Agency (FSA)—As a bank branch/finance merchant
- **U.S. Supervision**: FRB (Federal Reserve Board) / OCC (Office of the Comptroller of the Currency) / FDIC, Global Stress Test DFAST Target
- **Current issues**:
- Global organizational reorganization of Citi in the US (separate management of Services / Markets / Banking) → Linked to governance of Japan base
- **U.S.-China/geopolitical risks**: Flows from Japanese bases to China and South Korea are strongly influenced by U.S. compliance.
- Foreign Exchange Act/Inward Direct Investment Examination (2020  revision) → Regulations on advisory services of foreign IBs in sensitive industries
