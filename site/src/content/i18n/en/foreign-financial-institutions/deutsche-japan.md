## TL;DR

A collective name for the Japanese offices of Deutsche Bank AG (headquartered in Frankfurt, **G-SIB Bucket2**). **Tokyo branch (opened 1972 ) + Deutsche Securities (corporate IB)**2 axis is the core. After experiencing financial difficulties in the home country (2016 - 2019), operations in Japan were also reduced due to2019-07 global investment bank restructuring (reduction of18,000 personnel worldwide). Currently focused on **IB + Markets (bonds/FX strengths) + Asset Management (DWS) + Private Banking**, with no retail development. Competitors are[[citigroup-japan]]/[[hsbc-japan]]/ UBS / Barclays.

## 5 . Regulation/Policy

- **Administrator (Japan)**: Financial Services Agency (FSA)
- **Home jurisdiction**: BaFin (German Federal Financial Supervisory Authority) + ECB (Single Supervisory Mechanism)
- **G-SIB Bucket2**: FSB certified, +1.5%CET1 Additional capital requirements
- **Tokyo branch regulations**: Foreign bank branches and capital regulations based on the Banking Act are based on the head office.
- **Deutsche Securities Regulation**: Financial Instruments and Exchange Act Type 1, obligation to maintain capital adequacy ratio of200%or higher
- **Money laundering**: Past large fines from the US Department of Justice, NY DFS, etc. (parent company in home country) → Strengthening compliance remains an issue
