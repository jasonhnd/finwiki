# Toyota Financial Services (TFS)

## Wiki route

This entry sits under [[leasing-firms/INDEX|leasing-firms INDEX]]. Read it against [[megabanks/mufg|三菱 UFJ FG (MUFG)]] for peer / contrast context and [[banking/INDEX|banking index]] for the broader system / regulatory boundary.

## TL;DR

**A 100% subsidiary of Toyota Motor Corporation and the global automotive finance control company** (established 2000-07-03). It provides auto loans, leases, insurance, and credit cards in more than **30  countries** worldwide. **Toyota Financial Services Americas in the U.S., formerly TMCC, is a major U.S. auto-finance company**, comparable in scale to Ford Motor Credit and Ally Financial. The domestic core is **Toyota Finance Corporation**, covering the TS CUBIC Card and auto loans. By integrating with the dealer network through a **total package** of new-car sales + loans + leases + insurance + credit cards, it is a revenue source for the Toyota brand and has been shifting since 2024 年 toward EV / connected-car-linked finance. It is unlisted and consolidated as a Toyota Motor subsidiary.

### Business categories

- **Auto loans**: credit for new-car and used-car purchases
- **Leasing**: operating leases and residual-value-set leases / residual credit
- **Insurance**: agency sales / holding of auto insurance and life insurance
- **Credit cards**: TS CUBIC Card for Toyota dealer members
- **Corporate fleet finance**: fleet-management leasing for large corporate vehicle users

### Total-package strategy

Toyota's **dealer-financed sales** model proposes **vehicle + loan + lease + auto insurance + TS CUBIC Card** in one stop at the time of new-car purchase. Through a customer-referral model integrated with the dealer network, it maintains a **sales-channel advantage** over bank auto loans and independent finance. Residual-value-set loans are linked to the used-car market, creating a structure in which TFS bears resale-value risk.

### Global diversification

- **Americas (TMCC)**: largest scale, comparable to Ford Motor Credit and Ally Financial in the U.S. auto-finance market
- **Europe (Toyota Kreditbank)**: Germany-centered, covers the whole EU
- **Asia, Australia, Latin America**: linked to Toyota distributor expansion, early deployment in emerging markets
- Currency and regional economic diversification reduce dependence on any single market

### EV / connected-linked finance (from 2024)

As EVs spread, **residual-value assessment models diverge from conventional ICE vehicles** because of battery degradation and rapidly changing technology generations, making more precise residual-value setting a key issue. TFS is also trialing **usage-based loans / insurance** using connected-car driving data, including telematics UBI. Financial products linked with charging-infrastructure providers are also being explored.

## 5. Regulation / policy

- **Domestic lead authorities**: FSA — Money Lending Business Act, Installment Sales Act, Insurance Business Act
- **United States**: Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB) and state financial regulators
- **Europe**: national financial regulators such as Germany's BaFin + ECB / European banking supervision
- **Asia**: each country's financial regulators, such as Thailand BoT and Indonesia OJK
- **Recent policy issues**:
  - From 2024: transparency in EV residual-value assessment models, a consumer-protection issue
  - From 2025: personal-information protection for connected-car data × finance
  - From 2025: U.S. CFPB trend toward stronger APR disclosure for auto finance
