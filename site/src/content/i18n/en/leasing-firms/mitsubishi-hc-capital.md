# Mitsubishi HC Capital

## Wiki route

This entry sits under [[JapanFG/INDEX|JapanFG index]]. Read it against [[leasing-firms/orix-corp|オリックス株式会社 (ORIX Corporation)]] for peer / contrast context and [[banking/INDEX|banking index]] for the broader system / regulatory boundary.

## TL;DR

Mitsubishi HC Capital is a major leasing and asset-finance company in the Mitsubishi / MUFG lineage. Official materials describe it as having FY2024  net income of 1,351 億円, total assets of 11.7 兆円 at the end of 2025-03 , 8,380  employees, and operations in more than 20  countries. When comparing it with [[leasing-firms/orix-corp|ORIX]], read it as a bank- and trading-house-adjacent asset finance platform.

## Business map

- **Customer Solutions**: Leasing / finance for domestic companies and public-sector customers.
- **Vendor finance**: Sales-finance support for manufacturers and distributors.
- **Aviation / Logistics**: Leasing of international assets such as aircraft, engines, containers, and rail freight cars.
- **Environment & Energy / Real Estate / Mobility**: Asset finance related to energy, real estate, and mobility.

## Strategic read

Mitsubishi HC Capital plays the role of a leasing company that holds and operates equipment, aviation, logistics, and energy assets over long horizons, including assets that are not easily handled on bank balance sheets alone. It is an adjacent asset-finance function within [[megabanks/mufg|MUFG]]-linked finance, and should be compared with [[leasing-firms/orix-corp|ORIX]], [[leasing-firms/tokyo-century|東京センチュリー]], [[leasing-firms/fuyo-lease|芙蓉総合リース]], and [[leasing-firms/smfl|SMFL]].

The difference from ORIX is that ORIX has a broad independent conglomerate profile spanning insurance, banking, real estate, and U.S. private equity, while Mitsubishi HC Capital has a stronger fit with large corporations, global assets, and Mitsubishi / MUFG networks.
