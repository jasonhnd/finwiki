## TL;DR

Iyogin Leasing Co., Ltd. is the regional-bank-affiliated leasing company of the Iyogin Holdings group. It draws on Iyo Bank's middle-market relationship base in Ehime and across Shikoku, while also serving operating points in Osaka, Tokyo, Hiroshima, and other Honshu markets. Its public business lines include equipment leases, finance leases, operating leases, municipal leases, medical-equipment leases, marine-cluster equipment leasing linked to Imabari shipbuilding and shipping, agricultural-machinery leases, auto leases, and factoring. Within the four Shikoku prefectures, it is one of the larger bank-affiliated leasing platforms and was repositioned as a directly held group subsidiary after the 2022-10-03 holding-company formation at Iyogin Holdings.^[extracted]

### Competition With Major and Specialist Lessors

- **Major lessors**: [[mitsubishi-hc-capital|Mitsubishi HC Capital]], [[smfl|SMFL]], [[mizuho-lease|Mizuho Leasing]], [[fuyo-lease|Fuyo General Lease]], [[tokyo-century|Tokyo Century]], and [[ricoh-leasing|Ricoh Leasing]] have advantages in large-scale, nationwide, and complex transactions. Mitsubishi HC Capital is also relevant in marine leasing linked to Imabari shipbuilding.
- **Manufacturer-affiliated lessors**: [[toyota-finance|Toyota Finance]], [[nec-leasing|NEC Capital Solutions]], [[showa-leasing|Showa Leasing]], and [[ja-mitsui-leasing|JA Mitsui Leasing]] are advantaged where leases are tightly connected to product sales.
- **Prefectural competition**: Ehime Bank-affiliated leasing and local branches of major leasing firms compete in Ehime.

### Strengths and Constraints

- **Strengths**: Iyo Bank's No. 1 market share in Ehime, broad Shikoku sales coverage, differentiation in the marine cluster around Imabari shipbuilding, and exposure to Ehime-specific sectors such as paper manufacturing and agriculture.

### Regulatory Framework

- **Supervisors**: Financial Services Agency and Ministry of Economy, Trade and Industry.
- **Legal basis**: Leasing activity is outside ordinary banking-license requirements; money-lending registration may apply where factoring or lending-like functions are used.
- **Parent governance**: As a consolidated Iyogin Holdings subsidiary, the company sits within holding-company governance and the Banking Act framework for non-bank subsidiaries, ancillary businesses, and permitted business scope.
- **Regional finance bureau**: Shikoku Local Finance Bureau in Takamatsu.

### Regulatory Issues

- **Lease-accounting revisions under IFRS 16 and Japanese GAAP**: Support for lessee balance-sheet recognition of right-of-use assets and lease liabilities.
- **SME lease tax incentives**: Support for investment-promotion and business-strengthening tax programs for small and medium-sized enterprises.
- **Environmental leasing**: Green-lease product development for renewable-energy equipment, EVs, and decarbonization-related tax incentives.
- **Installment Sales Act / Money Lending Business Act**: Boundary analysis for factoring and related receivables-finance functions.
- **Anti-social-force checks and AML controls**: Conducted in coordination with the Iyogin Holdings group.
- **EDINET and securities reports**: Standalone disclosure is limited because Iyogin Leasing is unlisted; indirect disclosure appears through Iyogin Holdings consolidated segment information.

### Accounting Treatment

- **Standalone financial KPIs**: Partial disclosure appears through parent securities-report notes and segment information. The official company profile reports sales of JPY 16.7 billion for the fiscal year ended 2022-03.
