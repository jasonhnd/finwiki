## 2. History

| Year / month | Event |

### Financial-institution audits (largest strength) ^[extracted]

- Audits many **parts of megabank groups**, **major regional-bank groups**, **major life / non-life insurers**, and **major securities firms**
- Mature methodology for **J-SOX (internal control reporting system under the Financial Instruments and Exchange Act)**
- Framework for issuing **SOC 1  / SOC 2  reports**

### Crypto assets / digital assets ^[extracted]

- **"KPMG Japan Digital Asset" group**
- **Linked audits of internal controls (J-SOX) + crypto custody controls**
- **SOC2  / ISMS preparation support**
- Advisory for [[exchanges/jp-crypto-bank-credit-facilities|銀行系クリプト与信検討]] (business knowledge on both financial institutions and crypto assets)
- As referenced in [[exchanges/japan-crypto-audit-firm-landscape]], strength in advising megabank- and regional-bank-affiliated crypto-asset-related companies

### Regional banks / local governments

- Also handles comprehensive external audits for local governments

### Sustainability assurance

- SSBJ / ISSB-related assurance through **KPMG AZSA Sustainability Co., Ltd.** ^[extracted]

## 4. Major listed-FG client relationships (public basis)

Azsa has strengths in audits of major financial institutions, but each company's latest auditor should be confirmed each time from the primary source: the "status of auditor" section in the latest annual securities report on EDINET. This entry therefore does not list specific client names.

## 5. Regulation / supervision

- **Primary statute**: Certified Public Accountants Act ^[extracted]
- **Registration**: Japanese Institute of Certified Public Accountants (JICPA) ^[extracted]
- **Supervision**: **FSA Certified Public Accountants and Auditing Oversight Board (CPAAOB)** conducts quality-control reviews / inspections ^[extracted]

### Past administrative action history ^[extracted]

- **Olympus improper accounting case** (surfaced in 2011 ): Olympus audits were handled by Azsa through the 2009-03  period, then by ShinNihon. In 2012-07-06, citing issues including audit handover, the FSA issued **business improvement orders to both Azsa and ShinNihon**. Azsa was also criticized for not subjecting a major acquisition matter to senior-level review
  - The administrative monetary penalty (1 億 9181 万円) was imposed on **Olympus itself**; it did not result in a monetary-penalty order against Azsa itself
