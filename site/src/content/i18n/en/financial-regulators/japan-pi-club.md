## TL;DR

The Japan Shipowners' Mutual Protection & Indemnity Association (Japan P&I Club, JPI) is **the only P&I club in Japan** (Protection & Indemnity mutual marine insurance association). It is a mutual-association entity established under the Shipowners' Mutual Insurance Association Act (enacted 1950 年), and it covers **shipowners' liability** — such as personal injury, oil pollution, and wreck removal — for ocean-going and coastal shipping companies. It is **one of the 13  full members of the International Group of P&I Clubs (IG)**, sharing catastrophic-accident risk through the global reinsurance pooling mechanism.

## 2. Business lines

- **Shipowners' liability (P&I cover)** — personal injury (crew, passengers, third parties), oil-pollution damage, cargo-damage liability, wreck removal, the excess portion of collision liability, etc.
- **FD&D (Freight, Demurrage and Defence)** — legal costs and defence relating to charterparties and contracts of carriage
- **Claims-handling services** — accident response and indemnity negotiation through a network of correspondents at the world's major ports
