# Trust Companies Association of Japan (Shintaku Kyokai / 信託協会)

## TL;DR

Shintaku Kyokai (信託協会) is Japan's industry body for trust banks and trust companies operating under the Trust Business Act. Its membership covers both major trust banks and the independent trust-company universe. Like other industry bodies, it is a general incorporated association rather than the FSA or a statutory SRO, but its model rules and joint guidance on trust-product disclosure, beneficiary protection, and trust administration work as de-facto industry standards.

## Legal route / statutory position

Trust banks are regulated under both the Banking Act for deposit-taking and the Trust Business Act for trust business. Standalone trust companies are regulated only under the Trust Business Act, in operating-type and management-type license categories, as referenced in the FSA list at [[trust-banks/trust-companies-japan-index|Japan trust companies registry index]].[^fsa-licensed] Shintaku Kyokai is a general incorporated association of trust-business operators and has no statutory rulemaking power. The binding perimeter is the FSA's trust-business supervision guideline,[^fsa-trust] while authorized FIEA self-regulatory organizations are listed separately by the FSA.[^fsa-licensed]

The association's practical authority is contractual and industry-convening: model templates, joint statistics, financial ADR contact for trust customer complaints, and common industry positions in FSA / METI / Justice Ministry consultations on trust law, succession, asset management, and tokenized-trust topics.

## Function / scope

The association's published outline organizes its work as:[^shintaku-profile]

1. **Policy advocacy and research**: joint positions on Trust Business Act amendments, Trust Act interpretation, tax treatment of trust products, accounting and disclosure standards, and FSA / BOJ consultation responses.
2. **Industry self-discipline and model rules**: model templates for trust-product explanation, beneficiary protection, fiduciary-duty operations, AML/CFT for trust business, elder-customer-care frameworks, and financial ADR contact for customer complaints.
3. **Statistics and disclosure**: quarterly statistics on trust assets under management by trust type, including money trusts, pension trusts, securities trusts, real-estate trusts, and tokenization-related trusts.
4. **Public communication and education**: consumer-facing information through the shintaku-kyokai.or.jp portal, succession-trust use cases, and joint statements on trust-industry digital transformation.

The association also coordinates closely with [[financial-regulators/zenginkyo|Zenginkyō]] on themes affecting trust banks as banking-law entities, and with the Japan Investment Advisers Association / [[financial-regulators/jsda|JSDA]] when trust-based products intersect investment-management and securities regulation.

## Membership / governance

Membership covers two strata:[^shintaku-members]

- **Trust banks**, including [[trust-banks/mitsubishi-ufj-trust-bank|Mitsubishi UFJ Trust Bank]], [[trust-banks/smtb|Sumitomo Mitsui Trust Bank (SMTB)]], Mizuho-group trust banks, [[trust-banks/smbc-trust-bank|SMBC Trust Bank]], [[trust-banks/nomura-trust-bank|Nomura Trust Bank]], [[trust-banks/sbi-shinsei-trust-bank|SBI Shinsei Trust Bank]], and [[trust-banks/custody-bank|Custody Bank of Japan]] for trust-bank custody business.
- **Trust companies**, drawn from the FSA list of 38 trust companies as of 2026-03-31 referenced in [[trust-banks/trust-companies-japan-index|Japan trust companies registry index]]. Examples include [[trust-banks/fpg-trust|FPG Trust]], [[trust-banks/asahi-trust|Asahi Trust]], [[trust-banks/alterna-trust|Alterna Trust]], [[trust-banks/fintech-global-trust|Fintech Global Trust]], [[trust-banks/sekisui-house-trust|Sekisui House Trust]], and other registered operators.

Governance follows the general-incorporated-association model: a rotating chair from senior executives of major trust banks, standing committees on legal, tax, accounting, IT, AML/CFT, beneficiary protection, and statistics, and a Tokyo secretariat.

## Related industry adjacency

Trust is cross-cutting. Trust banks are also banks and are therefore in scope for [[financial-regulators/zenginkyo|JBA]] standards. Trust-based investment products intersect [[financial-regulators/jsda|JSDA]] rules when they involve securities-form products. Tokenized-trust structures connect to digital-asset infrastructure such as [[payment-firms/progmat|Progmat]]. On failure resolution, trust banks fall under [[financial-regulators/yokin-hoken-kiko|Deposit Insurance Corporation of Japan]] for their deposit-taking side, while trust companies do not take deposits and have a different resolution surface because segregated trust assets remain with beneficiaries by trust-law construction. For the rest of the industry-body stack, see [[financial-regulators/zenchugin-kyo|Regional Banks Association of Japan]], [[financial-regulators/dai2-chigin-kyo|Second Association of Regional Banks]], [[financial-regulators/zenshin-kyo|Zenshin-kyō]], [[financial-regulators/seiho-kyokai|Life Insurance Association of Japan]], and [[financial-regulators/sonpo-kyokai|General Insurance Association of Japan]].
