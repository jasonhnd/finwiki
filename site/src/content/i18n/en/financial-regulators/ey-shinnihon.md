## 3. Financial Services Office (FSO)

EY Ernst & Young ShinNihon's **Financial Services Headquarters** is a specialized FS organization that covers **banking, insurance, securities, asset management, and crypto assets**. ^[extracted] in collaboration with EY Global's FSO network.

### Main Sector ^[extracted]

| Subsector | Main target |
|---|---|
| **Mega banks/major banks** | [[megabanks/mufg]] related accounting audit-related services, part of regional bank groups |
| **Life and non-life insurance** | Major life and non-life insurance companies, foreign life and non-life insurance companies |
| **Securities/Asset Management** | Major securities, management companies, trust banks |
| **Crypto Assets/Web 3 ** | Financial statement audit/segregation management audit of FSA registered VASP (see [[exchanges/japan-crypto-audit-firm-landscape]]) |
| **Regulatory compliance** | IFRS, US standards, J-SOX, SOC reports |

### Service line

- Statutory audit (Company Law + Financial Instruments and Exchange Law)
- Separate management audit (for VASP, Payment Services Act)
- Internal control audit (J-SOX)
- SOC 1  / SOC 2  report
- IFRS/US-GAAP advisory
- IPO support (stock exchange preparation)
- Sustainability Assurance (SSBJ/ISSB related) ^[extracted]

## 5. Regulation/Supervision

- **Supervising law**: Certified Public Accountants Act (昭和 23 年 Law 第 103 号 ) ^[extracted]
- **Registration**: Registered as an auditing corporation with the Japan Institute of Certified Public Accountants (JICPA) ^[extracted]
- **Supervision**: **Financial Services Agency Certified Public Accountants and Audit Oversight Board (CPAAOB)** conducts quality control review/inspection ^[extracted]
- **Industry self-regulation**: JICPA Industry Committee Practice Guidelines (第 61 号 for crypto assets) ^[extracted]

### CPAAOB inspection/disposal history ^[extracted]

- ** 2015 - 12  Business improvement order + approximately 21 億円 surcharge** (triggered by Toshiba's inappropriate accounting)★
- Disposition announced by the Financial Services Agency. Largest administrative punishment in New Japan history
