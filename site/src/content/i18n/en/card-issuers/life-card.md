# Life Card (ライフカード)

## TL;DR

Life Card Co., Ltd. is the card issuer of the [[consumer-finance/aiful|アイフル (AIFUL)]] group. Its official company outline lists credit cards, prepaid cards, bank guarantees, and insurance as its business lines, and card issuance / installment is conducted as **comprehensive credit purchase intermediation** under the Installment Sales Act. This is a product-boundary page that makes explicit the structure in which a consumer-finance group holds both lending and card, so that AIFUL is not misread as a "lending-only" business.

## 2. Operating model

Life Card gives AIFUL a card and prepaid payment surface in addition to unsecured lending. The substance of installment is lump-sum, split payment (分割払い), and revolving (リボルビング), plus businesses such as bank guarantees and insurance agency that sit at the intersection of consumer credit and payments. Prepaid (prepaid value) is a separate license lineage (Payment Services Act) from installment card issuance, and must be read as distinct from the lending core (Money Lending Business Act). It serves as a good comparator to [[consumer-finance/acom|アコム]] and bank-affiliated card issuers.

## 3. Why this page matters

- A consumer-finance group often holds both lending and card in subsidiaries. Life Card makes that product boundary visible and shows that [[consumer-finance/aiful|アイフル]] is not loan-only.
- It provides an anchor for the structure in which three license lineages — installment (Installment Sales Act), prepaid (Payment Services Act), and lending (Money Lending Business Act) — sit side by side within a single company / single group.
- It is an entry point for reading alongside independent-issuer peers ([[card-issuers/credit-saison|クレディセゾン]] and others).
