# JCB Co., Ltd. (JCB)

## Wiki route

This entry sits under [[JapanFG/INDEX|JapanFG index]]. Read it against [[card-issuers/orico|株式会社オリエントコーポレーション (Orico)]] for peer / contrast context and [[banking/INDEX|banking index]] for the broader system / regulatory boundary.

## TL;DR

**Japan's only international card brand.** In 1961-01 , it was founded as Japan Credit Bureau through investment from the former Sanwa Bank, Japan Credit Bureau-related issuers, and others; the abbreviation "JCB" later became established as both the company name and brand name. It is one of the international 5  major card brands (**Visa / Mastercard / American Express / Discover / JCB**) and is strong in **Asia (Japan, China, Korea, Taiwan, and Southeast Asia)**. It is unlisted, with major shareholders centered on banking groups including [[mufg]] and [[smfg]]. Through mutual acceptance alliances with Discover in the United States and UnionPay in China, it covers more than 200  countries and regions worldwide. In Japan, it competes with Sumitomo Mitsui Card under the [[smfg]] group (Visa) and with Rakuten Card and others.

## 1. Company Overview

**Official name**: JCB Co., Ltd.
**English name**: JCB Co., Ltd.

**Listing**: **Unlisted** (major shareholders are bank-affiliated)

**Business type**: Credit-card company + international card brand, combining 3  functions: issuing, merchant acquiring, and brand licensing.

### Origin of the abbreviation

- **JCB** = **J**apan **C**redit **B**ureau.
- The initials came from the English name of the founding company, Japan Credit Bureau. The abbreviation later became established as both the company name and the brand name.

### International brand differentiation

- **Positioning as an "international brand strong in Asia"**: stronger merchant-network presence in Japan, China, Korea, Taiwan, and Southeast Asia including Thailand and Vietnam.
- **United States**: mutual acceptance through Discover Card, allowing JCB cards at U.S. Discover merchants.
- **Europe**: limited, centered on tourist areas and major hotels.
- **One of the 5  major brands (Visa / Mastercard / Amex / Discover / JCB)**: Japan's only, and Asia's only, international card brand.

### Inbound strategy

- **Greater China visitors to Japan**: UnionPay alliance (from 2006 onward) captures payment demand from Chinese tourists visiting Japan.
- **Asian travel destinations**: merchant networks in Thailand, Korea, and Taiwan capture Japanese outbound travel payments.
- **Cross-border payments**: advantageous on both sides of international travel flows, inbound and outbound.

### Domestic competitive structure

- **Visa partner cards**: Sumitomo Mitsui Card ([[smfg]] group; largest share), Rakuten Card, Aeon Card, and others.
- **Mastercard partners**: Rakuten Card, d Card ([[ndfg]] group), au PAY Card, and others.
- **JCB brand**: estimated around second place behind Visa in domestic share, though public numeric data is limited (2 位).
- **Digital-payment competition**: code payments such as PayPay, Rakuten Pay, d払い, and au PAY are eroding part of the card + mobile-payment demand.

### Digitalization response

- **JCB Contactless** (NFC tap payment): expanded from 2020 onward, capturing demand for contactless payments during and after COVID-era behavior changes.
- **Mobile payment support** for Apple Pay, Google Pay, Garmin Pay, Fitbit Pay, and others.
- **E-commerce and 3D Secure 2.0** support.
- **Cooperation with Bank of Japan digital-yen trials** (inferred from public Wikipedia-based information).

## 4. Regulation and policy

- **Main authorities**: METI for the Installment Sales Act, plus the FSA for the Payment Services Act and amended installment-sales framework.
- **Main legal bases**: Installment Sales Act for credit-card-company regulation, and Payment Services Act for prepaid payment instruments and related routes.
- **Personal information protection**: Personal Information Protection Act, including credit-use history and identity-verification information.
- **Merchant management**: amended Installment Sales Act, from 2018 onward, strengthened merchant investigation duties.
- **International standards**: PCI DSS compliance.
- **Recent policy topics**:
  - Cashless-promotion policies in the 2020 年s, including government targets of 2025 年 40% and 2030 年s 80%.
  - Invoice-system changes from 2023-10 onward affecting card-statement handling.
  - Stablecoin and digital-yen trial response.
