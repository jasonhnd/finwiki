# Credit Saison

## Wiki route

This entry sits under [[card-issuers/INDEX|card-issuers INDEX]]. Read it against [[card-issuers/jaccs|株式会社ジャックス (Jaccs)]] for peer / contrast context and [[banking/INDEX|banking index]] for the broader system / regulatory boundary.

## TL;DR

A major domestic credit-card company. Established 1951 年 as Midoriya Credit → 1980  Seibu Credit → renamed Credit Saison in 1989 . Against a backdrop of industry-top-tier card issuance with **over 4000 万 cumulative members**, it is a non-bank that earns along 4  axes: **US American Express partnership premium**, the **Eikyu-Fumetsu (永久不滅) loyalty-point** ecosystem, **Saison Asset Management** cross-selling, and **Indian consumer finance** (Kisetsu Saison Finance) overseas expansion. After the breakup of the old Saison Group, it was repositioned as an independent card company within the Mizuho FG orbit ([[megabanks/mizuho-fg]] affiliated). Securities code TSE PRIME 8253.

### Major card brands

- **Saison Card**: core brand, many co-branded cards
- **American Express Card** (Saison-issued): a separate license from proprietary Amex
- **UC Card**: inherited via the 2006  integration (former UC Card, with a Mizuho / Dai-Ichi Kangyo affiliate history)
- **PARCO Card / Seibu / Sogo Card / Tobu Card**: retail-affiliated
- **MUJI Card / Lawson Ponta Plus**, etc.: distribution / retail partnerships

### Eikyu-Fumetsu loyalty-point ecosystem

**A proprietary program that pioneered "points that never expire" ahead of the industry.** Long-term-holding motivation → continued card use → improved customer LTV loop. Points can also be diverted to investment-trust purchases and management, building an asset-management funnel through **point × Saison Asset Management** cross-selling.

### US American Express partnership premium

- One of the rare non-banks holding an **Amex card-issuing license** in the Japanese market
- A separate line from proprietary Amex (American Express Japan)
- Premium appeal to affluent and business-owner segments via products such as the **Saison Platinum Business American Express Card**

### Overseas (India) strategy

- **Kisetsu Saison Finance (India) Pvt. Ltd.**: established 2019 , with a local non-bank (NBFC) license
- Built a **fintech-origin consumer-finance** model through domestic Indian digital lending and B2B SaaS integration
- Lateral expansion into ASEAN (Vietnam, Indonesia, Singapore) proceeds in parallel
- A two-axis strategy of **domestic mature market vs emerging-market growth**

### Asset accumulation via Saison Asset Management

- Made a subsidiary in 2017 
- Long-term diversified-investment brands such as the "**Saison Global Balanced Fund**" and the "**Saison Asset Formation Master Fund**"
- A strength in **accumulation investing in the NISA / iDeCo era** through a direct-sales customer base + card-customer funnel
- Accelerated asset accumulation since the start of the new NISA system in 2024 年

### Competition and differentiation

- Competitors: [[megabanks/mufg]] Mitsubishi UFJ Nicos, JCB, Sumitomo Mitsui Card ([[megabanks/smfg]]), Rakuten Card, PayPay Card ([[megabanks/paypay-fg]])
- As a **leading player in retail-affiliated cards**, it has a positioning distinct from both megabank-affiliated (JCB / NICOS / Sumitomo Mitsui) and tech-affiliated (Rakuten / PayPay) players
- The combination of the Seibu / PARCO commercial-flow legacy + Amex premium + Eikyu-Fumetsu points is **unique**

## 4. Regulation / policy

- **Supervisor**: Financial Services Agency (FSA) / Ministry of Economy, Trade and Industry (jurisdiction over the Installment Sales Act)
- **Governing laws**: Installment Sales Act (credit business), Money Lending Business Act (card loans), Financial Instruments and Exchange Act (Saison Asset Management)
- **Regulatory trends**:
  - From 2018: revised Installment Sales Act → merchant-investigation obligations, strengthened security
  - From 2022: revised Payment Services Act → stablecoin regime
  - From 2024: new NISA system → a tailwind for Saison Asset Management
  - Indian NBFC regulation (under RBI supervision)
- **G-SIFI / TBTF designation**: none (non-bank, quasi-major)
