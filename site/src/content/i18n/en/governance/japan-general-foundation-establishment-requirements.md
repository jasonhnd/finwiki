# Japan General Incorporated Foundation: Establishment Requirements & Process


## Wiki route

This entry sits under [[governance/japan-research-grant-map-2026|日本 研究助成金 map（2026 年度）]] and should be read with [[INDEX|FinWiki index]].

> [!info] TL;DR
> Often confused with "public-interest incorporated foundation" and "NPO corporation," but the **general incorporated foundation** holds its own distinct position as a neutral corporate form. A form suited to research / advocacy-type organizations.

## Legal Basis

- **Act on General Incorporated Associations and General Incorporated Foundations** (平成18年Act第48号)

## Required Organs (all mandatory)

| Organ | Number | Governing article |
|------|------|---------|
| Councillors | **3  or more** | Article 173 , paragraph 3  |
| Directors | **3  or more** (including directors at establishment) | Article 160 , paragraph 1  · Article 177  (applied mutatis mutandis) · Article 65 , paragraph 3  |
| Auditors | **1  or more** | Article 170  · Article 177  |
| Council of councillors | Mandatory | Article 170  |
| Board of directors | Mandatory | Article 170  |

### Prohibition on Concurrent Service (important)

- A **councillor** may not concurrently serve as a director, auditor, or employee of the foundation or its subsidiary (Article 173 , paragraph 2 )
- An **auditor** may not concurrently serve as a director or employee of the foundation or its subsidiary (Article 177  applied mutatis mutandis · Article 65 , paragraph 2 )

## Property Requirement

- **Total property contributed at establishment must be 300 万円 or more** (Article 153 , paragraph 2 )
- Articles of incorporation below 300 万円 are void

## Terms of Office

| Organ | Term | Extension by articles? | Basis |
|------|------|-------------|------|
| Director | Until the conclusion of the ordinary council of councillors for the final fiscal year within 2  years after appointment | Not allowed | Article 66  applied mutatis mutandis |
| Auditor | Within 4  years after appointment | May be shortened to 2  years | Article 67  applied mutatis mutandis |
| Councillor | Within 4  years after appointment | May be extended to 6  years | Article 174 , paragraph 1  |

## Substantive Requirement for Board Meeting Frequency

Executive directors must report their own performance of duties to the board of directors **at least 1  time(s) every 3  months** (Article 91 , paragraph 2  applied mutatis mutandis).

→ In substance, holding the board of directors **at least 4  time(s) per year** is legally required.

## Establishment Costs (minimum)

| Item | Amount | Basis |
|------|------|------|
| Articles certification fee | **5 万円** | Ordinance on Notary Fees, Article 35  |
| Establishment registration / registration and license tax | **6 万円** | Registration and License Tax Act, Appended Table 1, 第 24 号(1)(a) |
| Property at establishment | **300 万円 or more** | Same Act, Article 153 , paragraph 2  |
| Total | **Minimum 311 万円** | |

## Standard Establishment Period: 1–2  months

The Ministry of Justice does not publish an official "standard processing time" for a general incorporated foundation on its own, but in practice:

1. Drafting articles + notary certification = **2–3  weeks**
2. Contribution of basic property + appointment of directors at establishment + investigation = **approximately 1  weeks**
3. Establishment registration application–completion = **3–7  business days**

### ⚠️ Note

- A general incorporated foundation is **outside the scope of the 24/48/72 -hour special processing for stock companies**
- As an individual case it may sometimes be completed within 1  months, but the standard should be assumed to be 1–2  months

## Public-Interest Authorization (additionally obtainable)

After establishment, "public-interest authorization" may be applied for under the Act on Authorization of Public-Interest Incorporated Associations and Public-Interest Incorporated Foundations. Once authorized:
- Corporation-tax preference (the public-interest-purpose business portion is non-taxable)
- Tax preference for donations (on the donor side as well)
- Permission to use the name "public-interest incorporated foundation"

However, the authorization requirements are strict:
- The total number of directors who have a "special interest relationship (spouse, relatives within the third degree, etc.)" with each director must not exceed **1 /3 ** of the total number of directors (Article 5 第 10 号)
- The 令和 6 年 amendment (effective 令和 7 年 4 月 1 日) strengthened the exclusion of special interest relationships between directors and auditors

## Application Scenarios

- A neutral organization whose primary purpose is research / policy advocacy
- The route of initially launching as a general incorporated foundation → accumulating 3-5  years of track record → applying for public-interest authorization
- Initial director composition: scholars (research) + former bureaucrats (institutions) + on-the-ground practitioners (stakeholder perspective)
- Auditors: legal / governance experts
- Councillors: a supervisory function independent of the directors (concurrent service prohibited)
