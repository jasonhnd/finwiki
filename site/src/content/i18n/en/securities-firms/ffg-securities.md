# FFG Securities (FFG証券)

## Wiki route

This entry sits under the [[securities-firms/INDEX|securities-firms INDEX]] as the FG-subsidiary securities operating-company anchor for [[regional-banks/fukuoka-fg|ふくおか FG (FFG)]]. ## TL;DR

FFG Securities Co., Ltd. is a regional-bank-affiliated securities firm, a 100% subsidiary of [[regional-banks/fukuoka-fg|ふくおか FG (FFG, 東証 PRIME 8354)]], providing asset management, public equity underwriting, private-placement-bond intermediation, and business-succession advisory to mid-tier-corporate main-bank customers in the Kyushu base ([[regional-banks/fukuoka-bank|福岡銀行]] / [[regional-banks/18-shinwa-bank|十八親和銀行]] / [[regional-banks/kumamoto-bank|熊本銀行]]). With a Type I Financial Instruments Business (securities business) license, it is a member of [[financial-regulators/jsda|日本証券業協会]]. A representative example of the Kyushu-based bank-securities cooperation model. ^[extracted]

### FFG establishment and group integration

- **2007-04-02**: [[regional-banks/fukuoka-fg|FFG]] established (joint holding-company conversion of [[regional-banks/fukuoka-bank|福岡銀行]] + Kumamoto Family Bank)
- **2007-10**: Shinwa Bank made a wholly-owned subsidiary of FFG (entry into Nagasaki Prefecture)
- **2020-10-01**: 18th Bank + Shinwa Bank → [[regional-banks/18-shinwa-bank|十八親和銀行]] (completion of the Nagasaki-Prefecture base consolidation)
- **2021-05-28**: Minna Bank launched (Japan's first fully-cloud-native, smartphone-complete digital bank)

### FFG Securities establishment / development

- 1940-06-06 Maeda Ryozo Shoten founded → 1944-07-07 Maeda Securities Co., Ltd. established
- 2012-04 Fukuoka Bank made it a wholly-owned subsidiary and changed its name to "Fukuoka Securities"
- 2018-05 Changed its name to "FFG Securities Co., Ltd."
- **2022-04 Transferred from a 100% subsidiary of Fukuoka Bank to a direct 100% subsidiary of Fukuoka Financial Group** (reorganized as the sole securities function of the FFG group)
- As an FFG group securities firm, it aims for linkage with the Kyushu 3 -prefecture base + the digital strategy of the parent [[regional-banks/fukuoka-fg]]

### Position within the FG

- **Core of bank-securities cooperation**: The securities window for [[regional-banks/fukuoka-fg]] customers (individuals / mid-tier companies)
- **Asset-management cross-sell**: Customer migration from bank deposits → investment trusts / fund wraps
- **Business succession / M&A**: Advisory services for Kyushu mid-tier-company owners

### Competitive landscape

- **Major securities firms** ([[securities-firms/nomura-hd|野村]] / [[securities-firms/daiwa-sg|大和]] / [[securities-firms/smbc-nikko]] / [[securities-firms/mizuho-securities]]): Major firms have the advantage on large Tokyo deals; with Kyushu local customers it counters through bank-securities cooperation
- **Online brokers** ([[securities-firms/sbi-securities]] / [[securities-firms/rakuten-securities]] / [[securities-firms/matsui-sec]]): At a disadvantage in individual digital trading, differentiates through face-to-face advisory
- **Regional-bank-affiliated securities firms**: Same-type models such as [[securities-firms/mebuki-securities]] (northern Kanto) / [[securities-firms/okasan-sg]] (Chukyo)
- **Kyushu competition**: Regional wide-area competition with [[regional-banks/nishi-nippon-fhd|西日本シティ銀]]-affiliated / [[regional-banks/kyushu-fg|九州 FG]]-affiliated securities subsidiaries / [[regional-banks/hirogin-hd|広島]]-affiliated securities

### Strengths and weaknesses

- Strengths: [[regional-banks/fukuoka-fg]] a 3 -prefecture 21.9 兆円 deposit-customer base (FY2025) + a Kyushu mid-tier-corporate main-bank network

## 7. Regulation / policy

- **Supervision**: Financial Services Agency (FSA) / Fukuoka Local Finance Branch Bureau (registration as a Type I Financial Instruments Business operator)
- **Industry association**: A regular member of [[financial-regulators/jsda|日本証券業協会]]
- **Regulatory issues**:
  - Expansion of the bank-securities cooperation scope under the relaxation of bank-securities firewall regulation (2022-)
  - Compliance with the customer-first business-operation principles (FD principle)
  - Strengthening of investment-solicitation rules for the elderly
  - Pressure for transparency / low levels of investment-trust sales fees
