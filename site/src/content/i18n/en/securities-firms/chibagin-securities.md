## TL;DR

Chibagin Securities Co., Ltd. is a regional-bank securities company wholly owned by [[regional-banks/chiba-bank|Chiba Bank (TSE PRIME 8331)]]. It serves high-net-worth retail clients and mid-sized main-bank corporate customers in Chiba Prefecture and the eastern Tokyo metropolitan area, offering retail brokerage, public and private bond intermediation, investment trusts, fund wrap products, business-succession advisory, and related services. It is licensed as a Type I Financial Instruments Business Operator and is a regular member of [[financial-regulators/jsda|Japan Securities Dealers Association]]. **Its predecessor was Chuo Securities**, which had a Yamaichi Securities lineage; after Yamaichi's collapse, Chiba Bank became the largest shareholder and brought it into the group. The company changed its trade name to Chibagin Securities in 2011-01 and became a wholly owned Chiba Bank subsidiary through a share exchange in 2011-10. Because Chiba Bank remains a single bank without a holding-company structure, Chibagin Securities sits directly below the parent bank, unlike the bank-securities sibling-company model under holding companies such as [[regional-banks/mebuki-fg|Mebuki FG]] and [[regional-banks/fukuoka-fg|Fukuoka FG]]. ^[extracted]

### Competition with major and online brokers

- **Major securities firms** ([[securities-firms/nomura-hd|Nomura]] / [[securities-firms/daiwa-sg|Daiwa]] / [[securities-firms/smbc-nikko|SMBC Nikko]] / [[securities-firms/mizuho-securities|Mizuho Securities]]): majors have the advantage for large Tokyo corporates and high-net-worth clients, while Chibagin Securities competes through bank-securities cooperation for local Chiba customers.
- **Online brokers** ([[securities-firms/sbi-securities|SBI Securities]] / [[securities-firms/rakuten-securities|Rakuten Securities]] / [[securities-firms/gmo-click-securities|GMO Click Securities]]): Chibagin Securities is structurally weaker in retail digital trading and fee competition, but differentiates through face-to-face advisory and bank referrals.
- **In-prefecture competitors**: securities functions affiliated with [[regional-banks/chiba-kogyo-bank|Chiba Kogyo Bank]], Keiyo Bank-related securities functions, and Chiba branches of major Tokyo securities firms.

### Strengths and weaknesses

- Strengths: [[regional-banks/chiba-bank|Chiba Bank]]'s No. 1 position in Chiba Prefecture, deposit base in the JPY 18-20 trillion range, faster decision-making under the single-bank model, and deep bank-securities cooperation.

### Regulatory framework

- **Supervisor**: Financial Services Agency and Kanto Local Finance Bureau, Type I Financial Instruments Business Operator registration.
- **Industry bodies**: regular member of [[financial-regulators/jsda|Japan Securities Dealers Association]] and participant in the Japan Investor Protection Fund.
- **Legal basis**: Financial Instruments and Exchange Act and related cabinet-office ordinances.
- **Parent-bank governance**: because [[regional-banks/chiba-bank|Chiba Bank]] is a single bank, Chibagin Securities is positioned under Banking Act restrictions on non-bank subsidiaries, ancillary businesses, and business-scope rules.

### Regulatory issues

- **Bank-securities firewall relaxation from 2022**: eased restrictions on officer overlap and information sharing expand the cooperation perimeter with parent [[regional-banks/chiba-bank|Chiba Bank]].
- **Customer-oriented business conduct principles**: investment-trust and fund-wrap sales require conflict-of-interest disclosure and cost transparency.
- **Senior-investor solicitation rules**: Chiba's aging population raises the compliance burden for solicitation of wealthy senior clients.
- **Fee transparency and low-cost investment-trust pressure**: spread of no-load funds compresses fee revenue.
- **EDINET / annual securities reports**: Chibagin Securities is unlisted and has limited standalone disclosure; information appears indirectly in [[regional-banks/chiba-bank|Chiba Bank]] consolidated annual securities report segment disclosure.

### Accounting treatment

- **Standalone financial KPIs**: precise standalone metrics for Chibagin Securities are not separately disclosed and are only partially visible through notes and segment information in parent [[regional-banks/chiba-bank|Chiba Bank]] securities reports.
