# Morgan Stanley Japan

This entry sits under [[JapanFG/INDEX|JapanFG index]]. Read it against [[securities-firms/goldman-sachs-japan|ゴールドマン・サックス・ジャパン (Goldman Sachs Japan)]] for peer / contrast context and [[securities/INDEX|securities index]] for the broader system / regulatory boundary.

## TL;DR

Morgan Stanley Japan is the Japan base of U.S.-based Morgan Stanley (NYSE: MS). Through its **strategic alliance with MUFG [[mufg]] (2008-09)**, it built one of the deepest global investment-banking tie-ups with a Japanese megabank. In 2010-05 , the Japan securities businesses were integrated and now operate under a **2 社 structure**: Mitsubishi UFJ Morgan Stanley Securities = MUMSS, and Morgan Stanley MUFG Securities = MSMS. MUMSS covers face-to-face retail plus ECM / DCM, while MSMS covers wholesale, including M&A and institutional investors. The group is one of Japan's 5  major securities firms and has a presence in global investment banking comparable to Nomura [[nomura-hd]].

## 1. Company Overview

**Parent company**: Morgan Stanley (NYSE: MS) — New York-based global investment bank, wealth manager, and investment manager  
**Japan holding company**: Morgan Stanley Japan Holdings Co., Ltd.  
**Business type**: Foreign-capital investment bank, expanded in Japan through the strategic alliance with MUFG  
**Relationship with MUFG**: MUFG continues to hold about 20% of Morgan Stanley common shares; after acquisition in 2008-09 , the ratio was also around 24% at times after dilution adjustments.

The **dual securities-license structure** separates face-to-face retail under a Japanese bank brand from wholesale under a foreign-capital brand, supporting regulatory handling and customer-segment optimization.

### Before Integration (1970s-2007)

- **1970 年代** Morgan Stanley entered Tokyo, moving from a representative office to branch operations.
- **1980 年代** During the Japanese equity-boom period, the firm expanded significantly in Tokyo.
- **1990 年代** It partnered with Mitsubishi Trust Bank, now MUFG Trust, in investment trust and pension fields.
- **2007-2008** The subprime crisis surfaced, leading into the global financial crisis.

### MUFG Capital Alliance and Integration (2008-2010) - Core

- **2008-09-15** Lehman Brothers collapsed; Morgan Stanley shares fell sharply and liquidity concerns intensified.
- **2008-09-22** **MUFG acquired 21% of Morgan Stanley common shares for about 90 億 dollars, or more than about 9000 億円**.
  - The original plan was voting preferred stock, but immediately before closing it changed to a hybrid of common and preferred shares.
  - The deal ran in parallel with U.S. Treasury TARP arrangements and became a symbolic post-Lehman transaction.
  - For MUFG, it was the largest outbound investment by a Japanese bank.
- **2009-06** Preferred shares were converted into common shares.
- **2010-05-01** **MUFG and Morgan Stanley integrated their Japan securities businesses**.
  - Mitsubishi UFJ Securities + Morgan Stanley Japan Securities became **MUMSS (MUFG 60% / MS 40%)** plus **MSMS (MUFG 49% / MS 51%)** in a 2 社 structure.
  - MUMSS covers face-to-face retail plus domestic investment banking, while MSMS covers wholesale.
  - This is one of the deepest Japanese-bank global IB tie-ups, comparable in symbolism to Nomura's Lehman acquisition.

### After Integration (2011-Present)

- **2011-06** MUFG began receiving dividends from Morgan Stanley common shares.
- **2013** MUMSS entered a phase of system trouble response and governance strengthening.
- **2016 onward** MUMSS retail-integration effects became more visible, with account numbers expanding.
- **2020 onward** During COVID-19, M&A and ECM mandates expanded, and MSMS performance stayed firm.
- **2023-2024** Rising Japanese equities and an increase in large IPOs supported strong results at both MUMSS and MSMS.
- **2024** The strategic alliance had continued for 16  years, and Morgan Stanley continued to position MUFG as a "Strategic Alliance Partner."

### JV Structure with MUFG (Core Strategy)

- **Dual-license structure**: MUMSS, with Japanese-bank majority control, plus MSMS, with foreign-capital majority control, optimize regulatory handling and customer segmentation.
- **Cross-function model**: MUFG's bank-customer base multiplied by MS global IB products.
- **Capital efficiency**: MUFG incorporates Morgan Stanley earnings through equity-method investment income, contributing profit on the scale of hundreds of billions of yen annually.
- **Competitive differentiation**: The combination of a Japanese megabank and a foreign-capital IB is close to unique; [[smfg]] = Nikko independent, while [[mizuho-fg]] = Mizuho Securities independent.

### Wholesale Strategy (MSMS)

- **Cross-border M&A**: Both Japanese outbound acquisitions and acquisitions of Japanese companies by overseas buyers.
- **Institutional-investor trading**: Provides global liquidity.
- **Equity research**: Institutional-investor trust through globally integrated coverage.

### Retail Strategy (MUMSS)

- **Focus on high-net-worth individuals**: Face-to-face sales plus product diversity.
- **MUFG bank referral route**: Bank customers are referred into securities services.
- **Overseas products**: Provides access to Morgan Stanley global funds and products.

### Overseas Coordination

- Coordinates with Asian bases such as Hong Kong and Singapore, as well as U.S. and European bases.
- Operates as part of the Morgan Stanley global organization.
