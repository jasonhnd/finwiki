# Monex Group

## Wiki route

This entry sits under [[JapanFG/INDEX|JapanFG index]]. Read it against [[securities-firms/nomura-hd|野村ホールディングス (Nomura HD)]] for peer / contrast context and [[securities/INDEX|securities index]] for the broader system / regulatory boundary.

## TL;DR

Monex Group is a TSE Prime listed holding company, ticker 8698, that bundles a major Japanese online securities firm, crypto assets through Coincheck, and the U.S. online broker TradeStation into a **global financial platform for individual investors**. Founded in 1999-05-19 as the former Monex Securities through joint investment by Sony and Oki Matsumoto, it is still led by founder **Oki Matsumoto**, a former Goldman Sachs partner and current chairman and CEO. Its defining feature is a 3 -axis structure: domestic securities, the U.S. business, and crypto assets. This is a "retail-investor platform specialization" model that contrasts with megabank-style comprehensive finance. In 2024 年, NTT Docomo announced investment in and consolidation of Monex Group; Monex Securities is expected to be positioned as the securities core business of [[ndfg]], the Docomo-affiliated financial group, with public reorganization scheduled for 2026 年.

### Business form

A pure holding company. Under it sit securities, crypto assets, the U.S. online broker, asset management, and venture-investment businesses. Unlike megabank financial groups, it is a **non-bank personal-investor platform** without a banking business.

### 3 -axis structure (official Monex Group concept)

The strategy repeatedly described in public materials is a **global personal-investor platform**. In practice it consists of:

- **Domestic**: Monex Securities, online securities retail.
- **United States**: TradeStation, for U.S. active traders.
- **Crypto assets**: Coincheck, domestic exchange.

The 3  axes cover different geographies and asset classes and are designed to respond horizontally to the full range of personal-investor needs. In contrast with megabank groups such as [[mufg]] / [[smfg]] / [[mizuho-fg]], whose model combines banking, trust, securities, and overseas operations, Monex narrows its segments around **personal-investor platform specialization**.

### NTT Docomo alliance (announced 2024 年)

In 2024 年, NTT Docomo publicly announced investment in and consolidation of Monex Group. Monex Securities is expected to become the **securities core business of the Docomo-affiliated financial group "NDFG"** (scheduled for public disclosure in 2026 年). This implies:

- **Possible integration with the d economic sphere**, including d POINT, d Pay, and d Card.
- **Connection to a Docomo user base of more than 9000 万 people**.
- **A telecom x finance conglomerate model**, competing structurally with KDDI au Financial and SoftBank / PayPay groups.

Concrete integration schemes and capital ratios change by timing, so this page records only publicly sourced statements. It avoids inferred business judgments or strategic intent.

### Strategic position of Coincheck

- **The 2018  NEM outflow incident**, about 580 億円 equivalent, was followed immediately by Monex making Coincheck a wholly owned subsidiary for roughly 36 億円. At that time, it was a symbolic rescue acquisition of a crypto-asset exchange by a major financial group.
- After becoming a subsidiary, Coincheck rebuilt internal controls, security, and compliance systems, and operates as a licensed domestic exchange participating in JVCEA.
- The U.S. SPAC listing plan in the early 2020 年s through Thunder Bridge Capital was ultimately withdrawn, and Coincheck remains a wholly owned Monex subsidiary.

### TradeStation position

- Occupies a niche for **professional / active traders** in the U.S. online-broker industry.
- Competes with Charles Schwab, Fidelity, Robinhood, Interactive Brokers, and E*TRADE under Morgan Stanley.
- Monex Group's exposure to U.S. revenue and connectivity is heavily tied to this business.

## 4. Regulation and policy

- **Domestic supervisor**: Financial Services Agency / Kanto Local Finance Bureau.
- **Securities business**: Type I financial instruments business operator under the Financial Instruments and Exchange Act, through Monex Securities.
- **Crypto assets**: Crypto-asset exchange service provider registration under the Payment Services Act, through Coincheck, plus JVCEA membership.
- **United States / TradeStation**: SEC / FINRA supervision.
- **Listed holding company supervision**: Financial Instruments and Exchange Act listed-company disclosure.
- **Current policy issues**:
  - From 2025: NISA expansion and inflow of younger investors through the new NISA, intensifying retail-securities competition.
  - From 2024: crypto-asset taxation, separate-taxation debate, and stablecoin framework.
  - From 2024: disclosure and fairness issues around consolidation schemes in telecom x finance alliances.
