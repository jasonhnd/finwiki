# SMBC Nikko Securities (SMBC 日興証券)

## Wiki route

This entry sits under [[securities-firms/INDEX|securities-firms INDEX]]. Read it against [[securities-firms/nomura-hd|野村ホールディングス (Nomura HD)]] for peer / contrast context and [[securities/INDEX|securities index]] for the broader system / regulatory boundary.

## TL;DR

**One corner of the domestic major 5  large securities firms, a 100% subsidiary of Sumitomo Mitsui FG ([[megabanks/smfg]])**. SMBC Nikko Securities Inc. (unlisted, a consolidated subsidiary of [[megabanks/smfg]]). It has a turbulent lineage: **originating from the founding of "Kawashimaya Shoten" in 1918 年, the former Nikko Securities was established in 1944 年 → made a wholly-owned subsidiary by Citigroup in 2009 年 → acquired by SMFG for about 7700 億円 in 2010-10  → renamed SMBC Nikko Securities in 2011-04 **. Among the **domestic major 5  large securities firms** ([[securities-firms/nomura-hd]] / [[securities-firms/daiwa-sg]] / SMBC Nikko / Mizuho Securities / Mitsubishi UFJ Morgan Stanley Securities), it is the only "megabank 100% subsidiary model" (Mizuho Securities and Mitsubishi UFJ Morgan Stanley Securities go through an intermediate holding company or JV form). Following **the 2022 年 market-manipulation incident (block-offer transactions)**, it received a business-improvement order + a business-suspension order, and governance rebuilding is ongoing. Its strategic axis is "retail-affluent focus + [[megabanks/smfg]] group / wholesale collaboration + Olive brand integration".

### In-group position (within [[megabanks/smfg]])

The securities bearer of [[megabanks/smfg]]'s **"banking, trust, securities, cards" four-pillar strategy**. A core subsidiary alongside SMBC (Sumitomo Mitsui Banking Corporation), SMBC Trust Bank, and SMCC (Sumitomo Mitsui Card). It is promoting a strategy to turn securities accounts into an integrated channel within the **Olive brand** (from 2023, [[megabanks/smfg]]'s super-app). ^[extracted]

### Retail strategy

- **Affluent focus**: redefining the approx. 130  sales branches as "affluent-facing touchpoints for face-to-face asset-management consultation". The mass segment is steered toward Olive / online
- **Olive integration**: integrated management of banking, card, and securities accounts via Olive, [[megabanks/smfg]] group's personal-finance app → differentiating itself as a megabank-lineage player from [[megabanks/mufg]] (somewhat behind) and [[megabanks/mizuho-fg]]
- **Against online brokers**: against the online low-cost offensive of [[megabanks/sbi-hd]] / [[securities-firms/monex-group]] / [[payment-firms/rakuten-fg]]-lineage securities firms, defending with "face-to-face + group-bank-account linkage"

### Wholesale / IB strategy

- **[[megabanks/smfg]] IB collaboration**: a simultaneous-proposal structure of SMBC (bank) and SMBC Nikko (securities) in large M&A, ECM, and DCM deals
- **Against [[securities-firms/nomura-hd]]**: to counter Nomura in the domestic IB rankings, the key is an integrated proposal of syndication / lending + securities leveraging the bank-lineage base
- **Against Mitsubishi UFJ Morgan Stanley Securities**: against MUMSS, which holds a Morgan Stanley JV, SMBC Nikko appeals with the agility of a "standalone, independent IB" ([[megabanks/smfg]] separately holds a business-and-capital alliance with Jefferies, complementing IB)

### Overseas strategy

- **The Americas**: based on SMBC Nikko Securities America / SMBC Capital Markets, partly inheriting personal networks from the Citi-lineage era
- **Europe**: London base
- **Asia**: Hong Kong + Singapore
- Overseas deployment is mid-scale among megabank-3 行-lineage securities firms; the group as a whole complements global IB through the business-and-capital alliance of [[megabanks/smfg]] and Jefferies (from 2023)

### Governance rebuilding (after the 2022 年 incident)

- **Receipt of a third-party-committee report** (2022 年) + implementation of recurrence-prevention measures (from 2023)
- **Strengthening the independence of the compliance division** and rebuilding the transaction-monitoring system
- **Renewal of officer personnel** and strengthened intervention into group governance from the [[megabanks/smfg]] holding side
- Continuing dialogue with the FSA, advancing the normalization of operations ^[extracted]

## 4. Regulation / policy

- **Supervisor**: Financial Services Agency (FSA); a Type I financial instruments business operator registered with the Securities Dealers Association ([[financial-regulators/jsda|日本証券業協会]])
- **US subsidiaries**: US SEC / FINRA regulation
- **European subsidiaries**: UK FCA / various European national regulation
- **Hong Kong subsidiary**: Hong Kong SFC regulation
- **Recent policy issues**:
  - 2022-10  business-improvement order + business-suspension order → continued implementation of recurrence-prevention measures
  - 2024~ review of retail strategy accompanying the asset-management-nation policy (new NISA expansion / iDeCo reform)
  - 2024~ responding to the revision of listed companies' Corporate Governance Code (IB operations / tender-offer operations)
  - Tokenized securities (security tokens, ST) domain: parent [[megabanks/smfg]] invested 20% in the ST private exchange "Osaka Digital Exchange (ODX, an SBI-led PTS)", and SMBC Nikko Securities also participates as a bearer of issuance / distribution. On the other hand, [[megabanks/smfg]] participates as a co-investing shareholder in the ST issuance / management platform "Progmat, Inc." (established 2023-10 , largest shareholder Mitsubishi UFJ Trust Bank 49%) (SMBC Nikko Securities itself is not a registered shareholder)
