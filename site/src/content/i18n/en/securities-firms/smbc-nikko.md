# SMBC Nikko Securities

## Wiki route

This entry sits under [[securities-firms/INDEX|securities-firms INDEX]]. Read it against [[securities-firms/nomura-hd|野村ホールディングス (Nomura HD)]] for peer / contrast context and [[securities/INDEX|securities index]] for the broader system / regulatory boundary.

## TL;DR

**One of Japan's major domestic 5  securities houses and a 100% subsidiary of Sumitomo Mitsui FG ([[megabanks/smfg]])**. SMBC Nikko Securities Inc. is unlisted and a consolidated subsidiary of [[megabanks/smfg]]. Its turbulent lineage runs from the **1918 年 founding of Kawashimaya Shoten to the 1944 年 establishment of former Nikko Securities, 2009 年 full acquisition by Citigroup, 2010-10  acquisition by SMFG for about 7700 億円, and 2011-04  renaming as SMBC Nikko Securities**. Among the **5  major domestic securities firms** ([[securities-firms/nomura-hd]] / [[securities-firms/daiwa-sg]] / SMBC Nikko / Mizuho Securities / Mitsubishi UFJ Morgan Stanley Securities), it is the only "megabank 100% subsidiary model" (Mizuho Securities and Mitsubishi UFJ Morgan Stanley Securities use intermediate holding-company or JV forms). It received a business-improvement order and business-suspension order over the **2022 年 market-manipulation case involving block-offer transactions**, and governance rebuilding continues. Its strategic axis is "retail high-net-worth specialization + [[megabanks/smfg]] group wholesale collaboration + Olive brand integration."

### Position inside the group (within [[megabanks/smfg]])

The securities arm of [[megabanks/smfg]]'s **"banking, trust, securities, and card" four-pillar strategy**. It is a core subsidiary alongside SMBC (Sumitomo Mitsui Banking Corporation), SMBC Trust Bank, and SMCC (Sumitomo Mitsui Card). Within the **Olive brand** (2023 onward, [[megabanks/smfg]] super-app), it is pursuing a strategy of making securities accounts part of an integrated channel. ^[extracted]

### Retail strategy

- **High-net-worth specialization**: redefining about 130  branches as face-to-face asset-management consultation points for affluent customers; mass customers are guided to Olive / online channels
- **Olive integration**: integrated management of bank, card, and securities accounts through the [[megabanks/smfg]] group personal-finance app Olive, differentiating it from [[megabanks/mufg]] (somewhat behind) and [[megabanks/mizuho-fg]] as a megabank group
- **Against online securities firms**: defends against the low-cost online push from [[megabanks/sbi-hd]] / [[securities-firms/monex-group]] / [[payment-firms/rakuten-fg]] securities by using "face-to-face + group bank-account linkage"

### Wholesale / IB strategy

- **[[megabanks/smfg]] IB collaboration**: simultaneous SMBC (bank) and SMBC Nikko (securities) proposal structure for large M&A, ECM, and DCM transactions
- **Against [[securities-firms/nomura-hd]]**: to compete with Nomura in domestic IB rankings, it relies on bank-originated syndication, lending, and securities-integrated proposals
- **Against Mitsubishi UFJ Morgan Stanley Securities**: compared with MUMSS, which has a Morgan Stanley JV, SMBC Nikko presents itself as a "standalone / independent IB" with speed ([[megabanks/smfg]] separately has a business-capital alliance with Jefferies to supplement IB)

### Overseas strategy

- **Americas**: based around SMBC Nikko Securities America / SMBC Capital Markets, inheriting part of the Citigroup-era network
- **Europe**: London base
- **Asia**: Hong Kong + Singapore
- Overseas expansion is mid-sized among megabank 3 行 securities firms; at group level, [[megabanks/smfg]] and the Jefferies business-capital alliance (2023 onward) supplement global IB

### Governance rebuilding (after the 2022 年 case)

- **Receipt of third-party committee report** (2022 年) + execution of recurrence-prevention measures (2023 onward)
- Strengthening independence of the compliance function and rebuilding trade-surveillance systems
- Renewal of executive appointments and stronger intervention from the [[megabanks/smfg]] holding-company side into group governance
- Continuing dialogue with the FSA and progressing toward business normalization ^[extracted]

## 4. Regulation and policy

- **Main authorities**: FSA; securities association ([[financial-regulators/jsda|日本証券業協会]]); registered Type I Financial Instruments Business Operator
- **U.S. subsidiaries**: regulated by U.S. SEC / FINRA
- **European subsidiaries**: regulated by the U.K. FCA / European national regulators
- **Hong Kong subsidiary**: regulated by Hong Kong SFC
- **Recent policy topics**:
  - 2022-10  business-improvement order + business-suspension order -> continued execution of recurrence-prevention measures
  - 2024 onward: review of retail strategy under Japan's asset-management-nation policy (expanded new NISA and iDeCo reforms)
  - 2024 onward: response to listed-company corporate-governance-code revisions (IB / tender-offer operations)
  - Tokenized securities (security token, ST) area: parent [[megabanks/smfg]] invested 20% in Osaka Digital Exchange (ODX, SBI-led PTS), a private trading venue for STs and similar products, with SMBC Nikko Securities also participating in issuance and distribution. Meanwhile, [[megabanks/smfg]] participates as a co-investor shareholder in Progmat, Inc. (established 2023-10 ; lead shareholder Mitsubishi UFJ Trust and Banking 49%), the ST issuance / management platform, though SMBC Nikko Securities itself is not a shareholder of record
