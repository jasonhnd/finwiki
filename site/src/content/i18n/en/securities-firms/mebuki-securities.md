## TL;DR

Mebuki Securities Co., Ltd. is a regional-bank-affiliated securities company and 100% subsidiary of [[regional-banks/mebuki-fg|めぶき FG]] (TSE Prime 7167). It was based on the former **Joyo Securities** ([[regional-banks/joyo-bank|常陽銀行]] group) and was integrated / reorganized within the group after the establishment of [[regional-banks/mebuki-fg]] (2016-10). It provides asset management, public offering underwriting, private placement bond brokerage, and business-succession advisory for mid-sized corporate main-bank customers in Ibaraki, Tochigi, and the Tokyo metropolitan area. It holds a Type I financial instruments business (securities business) license and is a member of [[financial-regulators/jsda|日本証券業協会]]. ^[extracted]

### Former Joyo Securities lineage

- **2007-11-30**: Joyo Securities Co., Ltd. established (Mito City, Ibaraki Prefecture, [[regional-banks/joyo-bank|常陽銀行]] securities subsidiary)
- Main businesses were retail brokerage in Ibaraki Prefecture and public / private placement bond brokerage for mid-sized companies.
- From the 2010 年 period onward: expanded its asset-management and private placement bond brokerage roles as a regional-bank-affiliated securities company under securities-bank collaboration.
- **2017-04-03**: trade name changed to Mebuki Securities Co., Ltd.

### Mebuki FG establishment and group integration

- **2016-10-01**: [[regional-banks/mebuki-fg|めぶき FG]] established (joint share transfer by [[regional-banks/joyo-bank|常陽銀行]] + [[regional-banks/ashikaga-bank|足利銀行]])

### Position inside the FG

- **Core of securities-bank collaboration**: securities window for [[regional-banks/mebuki-fg]] customers, both individuals and mid-sized companies.
- **Asset-management cross-sell**: customer migration from bank deposits to investment trusts / fund wrap.
- **Business succession and M&A**: advisory services for North Kanto mid-sized company owners.

### Competitive structure

- **Major securities firms** ([[securities-firms/nomura-hd|野村]] / [[securities-firms/daiwa-sg|大和]] / [[securities-firms/smbc-nikko]] / [[securities-firms/mizuho-securities]]): majors have the edge for large Tokyo-based companies and high-net-worth customers; Mebuki counters through securities-bank collaboration for local customers.
- **Online brokers** ([[securities-firms/sbi-securities]] / [[securities-firms/rakuten-securities]] / [[securities-firms/matsui-sec]]): weaker in individual digital trading, differentiated through face-to-face advisory.
- **Regional-bank-affiliated securities firms**: same-model securities-bank collaboration partners such as [[securities-firms/ffg-securities|FFG 証券]] / [[securities-firms/smbc-nikko|SMBC日興]].
- **In-prefecture competition**: securities firms affiliated with [[regional-banks/77-bank|七十七銀行]] (Miyagi) and [[regional-banks/chiba-bank|千葉銀行]].

### Strengths and weaknesses

- Strengths: [[regional-banks/joyo-bank]] / [[regional-banks/ashikaga-bank]] customer base + North Kanto mid-sized corporate main-bank network.

## 7. Regulation and Policy

- **Supervisors**: Financial Services Agency (FSA) / Kanto Local Finance Bureau (Type I financial instruments business operator registration)
- **Industry body**: regular member of [[financial-regulators/jsda|日本証券業協会]]
- **Regulatory issues**:
  - Expansion of securities-bank collaboration scope through relaxation of securities-bank firewall regulation (from 2022 onward)
  - Compliance with customer-oriented business conduct principles (FD principle)
  - Stricter elderly customer investment-solicitation rules
  - Pressure for greater transparency and lower levels in investment trust sales fees
