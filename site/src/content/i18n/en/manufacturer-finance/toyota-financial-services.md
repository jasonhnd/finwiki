## TL;DR

Toyota Financial Services (TFS) is **one of the world's largest auto captive finance operations**. As a 100% subsidiary of Toyota Motor, it offers one-stop **auto loans, leasing, insurance, cards, and mobility finance** across 30+ countries in the Americas (Toyota Motor Credit Corporation = TMCC), Europe (Toyota Kreditbank GmbH), domestic Japan ([[card-issuers/toyota-finance|Toyota Finance]]), and emerging markets. As a FinWiki manufacturing anchor, read it around 4  axes: **(1) the meaning of Toyota Motor (7203) holding finance outside the parent body, (2) the design of concentrating residual-value (RV) risk on the captive side rather than the OEM body, (3) its scale as an auto-ABS issuer in global markets (connecting to [[structured-finance/auto-loan-abs-japan-toyota-honda|Japan auto-loan ABS]]), and (4) the repositioning of the captive in the BEV / connected-mobility era**. Alongside Ford Motor Credit, Ally Financial, and GM Financial, it is one corner of the top tier of global auto captive finance.

### Relationship with the Toyota Motor body

- Because TFS is a consolidated subsidiary, it is disclosed in aggregate as Toyota Motor's **"financial-services segment"** in the securities report (standalone IR is limited).
- Through the **separation of capital efficiency** between the manufacturing body and the financial subsidiary, a structure in which vehicle-sales earnings and financial-business earnings can be evaluated independently.
- In residual-value-setting loans (zankure) and leasing, the Toyota Motor body **first confirms the "new-car sales profit,"** and the risk passes to the TFS side. By design, the used-car price uncertainty of the BEV era concentrates on the TFS balance sheet.

### Connection with auto ABS

TFS is one of the world's largest auto-loan / auto-lease ABS issuers. In the US, TMCC regularly issues ABS in the **Toyota Auto Receivables Trust** series, and in domestic Japan, Toyota Finance regularly issues auto-loan ABS. For details, see [[structured-finance/auto-loan-abs-japan-toyota-honda|Japan auto-loan ABS (Toyota Finance, Honda Finance, Nissan Credit)]]. A structure in which the auto receivables that would otherwise pile up on the Toyota Motor body's B/S are securitized / taken off balance sheet by TFS via ABS, thereby restraining the group's overall leverage.

## 4. Strategy (Residual Value + EV + Mobility)

- **Residual-value risk management**: zankure / leasing is directly tied to used-car market prices, and the rapid technological renewal of BEVs (battery degradation, generational change) demands refinement of residual-value valuation models. TFS is updating its RV models by utilizing a generation-by-generation used-car price database and driving data (connected).
- **Packaging sales finance comprehensively**: the dealer model that **proposes "vehicle + loan + lease + insurance + card" all at once** at the time of new-car purchase is the core of Toyota's competitiveness. The advantage over bank-affiliated auto loans is **the power to direct customers at the point of sale**.
- **The scale of US TMCC**: at a scale on par with Ford Motor Credit and Ally Financial in the US market, it is a major issuer in the US auto-ABS market. TMCC is SEC-registered and discloses in detail via Form 10-K.
- **Repositioning the captive in the EV / connected era**: in line with the shift from vehicle ownership to usage, it is redesigning captive products such as BEV subscriptions (Toyota's KINTO), car-sharing, and usage-based insurance (UBI).
- **Full-scale mobility finance**: in tandem with Toyota's "mobility company" declaration (2018), TFS is rebranding toward "Mobility Financial Services." It connects with Toyota Wallet (domestic payments), the KINTO subscription, and the Woven City experiment.
- **Emerging-market deployment**: advance deployment linked with Toyota sales companies in Thailand, Indonesia, Australia, Brazil, South Africa, etc. Absorbing FX / country risk through regional diversification.

## 5. Regulation / policy

- **Domestic**: Financial Services Agency (FSA), Money Lending Business Act, Installment Sales Act, Insurance Business Act, Banking Act (partly within the TFS group).
- **US**: the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB), each state's financial supervision. TMCC has continuous disclosure obligations as an SEC-registered issuer.
- **Europe**: each country's financial supervision (Germany's BaFin, etc.), the ECB / EBA's bank-subsidiary regulation (Toyota Kreditbank holds a German banking license).
- **Asia / emerging markets**: each country's financial supervision (Thailand BoT, Indonesia OJK, etc.).
- **Recent policy topics**:
  - 2024〜 transparency of EV residual-value valuation models (consumer protection)
  - 2025〜 personal-information protection of connected-car data × finance
  - 2025〜 trends in strengthening US CFPB auto-finance APR disclosure
  - 2024〜 taxation / consumer-protection topics of domestic subscriptions such as KINTO
