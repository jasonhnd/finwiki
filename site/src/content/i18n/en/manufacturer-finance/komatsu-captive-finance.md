## TL;DR

Komatsu (Komatsu Ltd., TSE PRIME 6301) is a global heavyweight in construction and mining equipment, the global No.2 behind Caterpillar. Its captive finance centers on **Komatsu Financial L.P. in the Americas**, **Komatsu Finance in Europe (in-house captives in Germany, France, and Italy, with partnerships in other regions)**, and **domestic and regional sales finance**, forming **B2B equipment finance (vendor / captive finance)**. From a manufacturing standpoint, the axes worth reading are **(1) the significance of Komatsu Ltd (6301) holding its "retail finance" business outside the parent's operating segment / in a consolidated subsidiary; (2) the point that, unlike auto captives, this is B2B vendor finance whose customers are corporations (construction and mining operators); (3) the residual-value risk of leasing and the accounting distinction between true-lease / finance-lease; and (4) the point that the captive carries the wholesale (floorplan) credit on distributor inventory (connecting to the [[manufacturer-finance/floorplan-finance-mechanism|floorplan mechanism]])**. Together with Caterpillar Financial and Volvo Financial Services, it forms the top tier of construction-equipment captives.

### Relationship with Komatsu Ltd parent

- Retail finance is consolidated and disclosed as Komatsu Ltd's **"retail finance (sales finance)" business**. It is a structure that separates financial assets and receivables from the core construction-equipment sales (industrial) and manages them apart.
- In leasing and installments, Komatsu's parent **books the "machine sale"**, while the machine's residual-value risk and customer credit risk are concentrated on the captive's (Komatsu Financial, etc.) balance sheet. This is the same **vertical integration of "selling the product" and "lending / leasing the price"** as in auto captives, but it differs in that the customers are **corporations (B2B)**. For the general mechanism, see [[manufacturer-finance/vendor-finance-mechanism|captive / vendor finance mechanism]].
- The captive also extends credit on the distributor's (dealer's) **inventory (floorplan / wholesale)**, supporting everything from upstream distribution inventory to downstream customer credit. Komatsu Financial L.P. provides wholesale / retail / operating lease to 30+ contracted distributors and their customers. For details, see [[manufacturer-finance/floorplan-finance-mechanism|floorplan / wholesale finance mechanism]].

## 2. Product / business lines × significance from a manufacturing standpoint

| Business line | Details | Significance from Komatsu's manufacturing standpoint |
|---|---|---|
| New-unit retail installment | Installment for new construction equipment (flexibility in term and payment design) | Maintaining the machine-sales channel, stimulating demand |
| Used-equipment finance | Installment for used / certified-used equipment (zero down payment / payment-deferral programs, etc.) | Activating the used market, controlling the replacement cycle |
| full-service / operating lease | Residual-value lease (choose return, purchase, or extension at maturity) | Residual-risk concentration, off-balance-sheet treatment, usage-based provision |
| parts & service finance | Finance for parts, maintenance, repair (tied to genuine parts) | Aftermarket revenue, longer customer relationships |
| Distributor inventory finance (wholesale / floorplan) | The captive extends credit on the dealer's inventory | Supporting distribution inventory, smoothing production and shipment |
| Multi-brand support | Komatsu-affiliated brands (e.g., road machinery, crushers) also offered finance via distributors | Letting finance follow the expansion of the product line |

### Connection to equipment-lease ABS

Construction-equipment lease and installment receivables can become subjects of equipment lease ABS / securitization. The distinction between true-lease (operating lease, where the vendor bears the residual-value risk) and finance-lease (effectively installment) splits accounting, risk allocation, and securitization eligibility. For details, see [[structured-finance/japan-equipment-lease-abs|Japan equipment lease ABS (residual-value, true-lease vs finance-lease)]].

## 4. Strategy (Residual Value + B2B + Lifecycle)

- **Residual-value risk management (construction-equipment RV)**: full-service / operating lease depends on the used-equipment price at maturity, and Komatsu supports its residual-value model with global used-machine market data and its own certified-used network. For the general mechanism, see the residual-value section of [[manufacturer-finance/vendor-finance-mechanism|captive / vendor finance mechanism]].
- **Lifecycle finance**: from new-unit purchase → lease → used machine → parts, maintenance, retrofit, it attaches finance across the entire machine lifecycle, pitching a lower total cost of ownership (TCO). This is the core of a construction-equipment captive's competitiveness.
- **Region-by-region use of captive / partnerships**: in the Americas and major European countries it runs in-house captives (Komatsu Financial / Komatsu Finance), while partnering with specialist financial institutions in other regions. Rather than holding a captive in every region, it combines in-house and partnership according to market size.
- **Operation of wholesale (floorplan)**: it supports the distribution network with wholesale credit on distributor inventory. For details, see [[manufacturer-finance/floorplan-finance-mechanism|floorplan / wholesale finance mechanism]].
- **Funding needs for mining and large machines**: mining equipment has large unit prices and long terms, and depending on the deal, export finance ([[manufacturer-finance/export-finance-mechanism|export finance mechanism]]) or syndicated bank lending may be combined.

## 5. Regulation and policy

- **Domestic**: Financial Services Agency (FSA), the Money Lending Business Act, the Installment Sales Act. Domestic sales finance is under their supervision. Lease accounting (Japan's new lease standard) governs the true-lease / finance-lease distinction and on/off-balance-sheet treatment.
- **US and Europe**: each country's financial supervision and lease regulation. The captive handles both distributor wholesale and customer retail.
- **Accounting standards**: IFRS 16 / each country's lease standards advance the on-balance-sheet treatment of operating leases, directly affecting the product design of vendor finance.
- **Recent policy topics**:
  - Uncertainty in residual-value assessment accompanying the electrification of construction equipment (EV / hydrogen machines)
  - Trends in capital-investment subsidies and green leasing tied to GX / decarbonization
  - The combination of environmental-social considerations and export finance in large mining deals
