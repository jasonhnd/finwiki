TL;DR

Pacific Life Re is the global life and health reinsurance arm of Pacific Life Insurance Company (Pacific Life), a US-headquartered mutual life insurer founded in 1868 and based in Newport Beach, California. Pacific Life Re operates internationally with subsidiaries and branches in the UK, Australia, Singapore, Hong Kong, Bermuda, Canada, and Japan, focused on life and health reinsurance. Pacific Life Re Japan participates in the Japan life reinsurance market alongside larger reinsurers such as Swiss Re Japan, Hannover Re Japan, and the domestic Toa Re / Japan Earthquake Reinsurance ecosystem.

2. Operating model

Pacific Life Re’s business model is wholly focused on life and health reinsurance — providing reinsurance capacity to direct life insurers for mortality, morbidity, longevity, and protection products. Distinctive features:

  • Life specialty focus: Unlike multi-line global reinsurers such as Swiss Re Japan or Hannover Re Japan that span life and non-life, Pacific Life Re concentrates on life and health
  • Parent mutual structure: Pacific Life is a mutual life insurer; Pacific Life Re thus inherits the long-horizon orientation typical of mutual ownership, comparable in structure principles to those covered in mutual vs. stock insurer
  • Cedent-partnership orientation: Reinsurance treaty work tends to involve longer-term advisory partnerships with ceding direct life insurers across new product launches and in-force transactions
  • Asia growth focus: Asia, including Japan, has been a strategic growth region

In the Japan life reinsurance market, Pacific Life Re Japan supports ceding insurers in the Japan life insurance big four and smaller carriers across mortality, medical, and longevity risk transfer.
