Taipei Fubon Bank Japan sits under JapanFG index and is a current Taiwan-Japan foreign-bank branch route in the foreign-bank branch registry. Read it with First Commercial Bank Japan, E.SUN Bank Japan, CTBC Bank Japan, Bank of Taiwan Japan, and cross-border finance.

Use this page for recent Taiwan-Japan corporate finance, syndicated loans, plant-construction financing, trade finance, and Taiwan semiconductor supply-chain expansion into Japan.

Overview

Taipei Fubon Bank is the most time-sensitive remaining Taiwan branch promotion. Fubon Financial announced on 2026-02-10 that Taipei Fubon Bank obtained a Tokyo branch license from Japan’s FSA. The branch page states that Tokyo Branch was established on 2026-05-01.

The page is worth standalone treatment because the public materials explicitly connect the Tokyo Branch to Japan-Taiwan trade, Taiwanese enterprises investing in Japan, semiconductor-related factory / capacity expansion, syndicated loans, plant construction financing, and trade finance.

Public Context

Taipei Fubon Bank’s Tokyo branch is a strong example of how the Taiwan-Japan banking surface is changing after semiconductor supply-chain relocation and expansion. The bank’s news release explicitly frames the branch around Taiwanese enterprises and Japan’s role as a trading partner and import source.

This makes the page a peer of First Commercial Bank Japan and an update to the earlier Taiwan branch map that already includes Bank of Taiwan, Mega ICBC, CTBC, and E.SUN.

Research Checklist

  1. Check FSA announcement and current bank-license list for branch status.
  2. Use the branch page for product categories; do not infer all Taiwan parent-bank products are available in Japan.
  3. For semiconductor finance, verify the borrower, project, loan type, bank role, and public deal source.
  4. Separate corporate loans, trade finance, deposits, remittance, and any personal finance product.
  5. Compare with First Commercial and E.SUN when mapping Taiwan banks in Japan.
