Agriculture credit guarantee system in Japan

Agriculture credit guarantee system in Japan

Wiki route

This entry sits under policy-finance index. Read it against Japan credit guarantee system for SME-side contrast, fisheries credit guarantee system for the sister sector system, and Japan policy finance system for the broader public-credit perimeter. The deposit-and-lending counterpart for farmers is JA Bank system and the cooperative apex is Norinchukin.

TL;DR

Japan’s agriculture credit guarantee system (農業信用保証保険制度) supplements farmers’ and agriculture-related borrowers’ credit through a two-tier layered structure: (1) prefectural-level 農業信用基金協会 (Agriculture Credit Fund Associations, 47 prefectural bodies) that guarantee farmer / agriculture-corporation debt to lending institutions, reinsured by (2) the national-level 独立行政法人農林漁業信用基金 (Agriculture, Forestry and Fisheries Credit Foundations, JAFFIC) agriculture-credit-insurance business. It is adjacent to JA Bank system but it is a guarantee-insurance route, not a deposit / lending route. Compared with the general SME credit guarantee system, the agriculture system is sector-specific, MAFF-supervised, and integrated with the agricultural-cooperative system (系統金融) rather than with general regional banks.

4. 主要保証商品

  • 農業近代化資金保証 — modernization-loan guarantee for farm-equipment investment, facility modernization (greenhouses, dairy parlors, storage).
  • 農業改良資金保証 — improvement-loan guarantee for innovation, new-crop adoption, organic conversion.
  • 農業経営基盤強化資金 (スーパーL資金) 保証 — guarantee for large-scale farm-management consolidation loans channeled through JFC’s agriculture-forestry-fisheries arm.
  • 就農支援資金保証 — new-entrant farmer support guarantee.
  • 担い手・農業法人向け保証 — large-scale farmer / corporate farm guarantee.
  • 災害復旧保証 — disaster-recovery agriculture guarantee (typhoon damage, flood damage, snow damage, livestock disease).
  • 6次産業化 / 加工流通関連保証 — guarantee for farmer-led food processing and direct distribution (the “6th-industrialization” policy lane).
  • 借換 / 経営改善資金保証 — refinancing and management-improvement guarantee.

5. Operating logic — guarantee, subrogation, reinsurance

  1. Application — A farmer or agriculture corporation borrows from a lending institution (typically JA / 信連 / Norinchukin / regional bank / JFC’s agriculture arm). The lender requires guarantee credit and applies to the prefectural 農業信用基金協会.
  2. Guarantee — The prefectural association evaluates the loan, accepts the guarantee, and charges a guarantee fee (保証料 — typically a low single-digit annual rate on outstanding principal, varying by product).
  3. Lending — The lender disburses the loan with the guarantee in place.
  4. Default — If the borrower defaults, the lender claims under the guarantee; the prefectural association pays subrogation (代位弁済) to the lender, then pursues recovery (求償) against the borrower / collateral.
  5. Reinsurance — JAFFIC’s agriculture-credit-insurance business reinsures the prefectural association’s guarantee exposure; insurance payouts flow from JAFFIC to the prefectural association under the applicable insurance contract.
  6. Residual loss — After recovery and JAFFIC insurance, residual loss is absorbed by the prefectural association’s reserves, supplemented by national / prefectural / cooperative contributions.

The system thus has three loss-absorption layers: (a) borrower recovery, (b) prefectural association reserves + national JAFFIC reinsurance, (c) public / cooperative capital contributions backstopping the system.

6. 保証料 economics

Guarantee fee schedule is set by each prefectural 農業信用基金協会 within MAFF guidance, typically structured as:

  • Annual rate on outstanding principal, in the low single-digit basis-point range for senior policy loans (e.g., スーパーL資金) and higher for higher-risk products.
  • Tiered by product (近代化資金 / 改良資金 / 就農支援 etc.) and borrower category (担い手 priority discounts).
  • Subsidized for priority policy categories (new-entrant farmers, disaster-recovery, 6th-industrialization).
  • The fee economics are deliberately below market-equivalent commercial guarantee pricing — the spread is absorbed by public / cooperative subsidies.

The two systems are parallel public-credit guarantee infrastructures with different sector targets, different supervisors, and different cooperative-banking interfaces. They do not compete for borrowers — a loan to a farmer routes through the agriculture system, a loan to a small manufacturer routes through the SME system.

9. Boundary cases

  • Not JA Bank — JA Bank is a lender route; this page is the guarantee-insurance mechanism layered on top.
  • Not the SME guarantee system — SME guarantees route to japan-credit-guarantee-system.
  • Not fisheries — fisheries guarantee-insurance belongs to fisheries-credit-guarantee-system (sister sector under the same JAFFIC parent).
  • Not 林業 (forestry) — forestry credit support is also a JAFFIC business but routed separately under MAFF forestry policy.
  • Not JFC’s agriculture lending — JFC’s agriculture-forestry-fisheries arm lends directly to farmers; this page is about guarantee insurance for farmer borrowing, which may overlap when guaranteed loans are originated by JFC.
