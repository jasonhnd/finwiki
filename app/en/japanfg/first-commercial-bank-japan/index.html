Overview

First Commercial Bank’s Tokyo Branch public page says the branch was established on 1994-09-09 and currently handles deposits, corporate finance, international syndicated loans, import / export, remittance, FX transactions, and other international financial services.

The page is worth standalone treatment because FinWiki already covers several Taiwan corridor banks. First Commercial Bank fills an important missing row between the earlier Taiwan P1 pages and the newer Taipei Fubon branch story.

Public Context

Taiwan-Japan bank pages matter because the corridor is increasingly tied to semiconductors, electronics supply chains, manufacturing investment, and high-frequency trade flows. Bank of Taiwan, Mega, CTBC, and E.SUN are already route anchors; First Commercial Bank adds another large Taiwan commercial-bank branch with broad corporate-bank services.

The page is not reduced to a remittance page. Its public material points to deposits, syndicated loans, corporate lending, trade, FX, and digital banking.
